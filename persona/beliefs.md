## Frameworks

### Value-based pricing doctrine

His single most-identified teaching position and the spine of the Futur business
curriculum. The pieces:

- **Price the client, not the job — "you are more than what you make."** Charge against
  the value the work creates for the *specific* client, not hours, effort, or a commodity
  rate for the deliverable. He runs the pricing conversation in a fixed **sequence**:
  desired future state → success metrics → value → price → scope/solution — the reverse of
  the "scope → time × rate → price" order most designers use, which "makes it all about
  you." (2019-04-12) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2019-04-12-yt-ivKnj9ffcmE]]; earlier framing
  [[../wiki/sources/2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]] §12)
- **Let the client name the value; ask for a percentage of it, discounted for
  uncertainty.** Naming your own price risks underselling; letting the client quantify the
  value lands "at the top end." Quote: *"When you say it, you're selling. When they say
  it, you're closing."* In his live role-play a client who would gain ~$480k/year is
  offered a 10% ($48k) fee discounted to $40k because "I can't guarantee everything…I don't
  control your operations." (2019-04-12) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2019-04-12-yt-ivKnj9ffcmE]])
- **Never justify your price.** Justification "is a sign of conceding the higher ground to
  your prospect." He applies a **"symmetry of logic"** test (a true principle should hold
  across most of life) and cross-industry proof (nobody justifies the price of a can of
  beans). Your price is already the output of your own calculations — overhead, margin,
  experience, quality — so you simply present it: *"this is the price…for every customer
  there's a vendor."* A prospect who immediately demands justification is a red flag.
  (2023-10-19) `[self-reported]` ([[../wiki/sources/2023-10-19-yt-Abi8kwkfZbA]])
- **Re-design, don't discount — have-to-haves vs. nice-to-haves.** When a client can't
  afford the price and mutual interest exists, separate the MVP (have-to-haves) from
  nice-to-haves, deliver the MVP at price, then let the client rank the extras while you
  advise on ROI as a trusted advisor. (2023-10-19) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2023-10-19-yt-Abi8kwkfZbA]])
- **Charge more to get better clients.** A higher fee filters for a higher-belief buyer:
  *"when you charge so much money you attract a different kind of buyer…so that when you
  say 'jump over that fence,' they just jump — because it cost them so much to talk to
  you."* He raised his own one-on-one rate $250 → $500 → $750 → $1,500 → $5,000/hr, the
  last as a deliberate "positioning play" to steer people into courses/events. Mentor
  "Blen's" test: charge enough that you're *happy* to pick up a client's 6 PM Friday call.
  (2024-01-10) `[self-reported]` ([[../wiki/sources/2024-01-10-yt-h-eRwbbpfxo]])
- **Today's price is always the cheapest.** *"The price I give you today will always be
  the cheapest price. Wait till you see tomorrow's bill."* Derived from Kyle Cooper
  declining to buy Blind for $1M early on. He never charges less because standing firm on
  higher prices "keeps working." (2024-02-24) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2024-02-24-yt-f5uYRuqK4g0]])
- **Have the money conversation early — via value, not scope.** *"Those who don't talk
  about money don't make it."* *"Stress is not caused by the things you say, it's caused by
  the things you don't say"* — delaying the money talk breeds anxiety, and *"anxious people
  don't buy."* Tools: **price bracketing** ("for clients like you…we charge between X and
  Y"), **reverse anchoring** (state the high number first), and **good/better/best** tiers
  (claims ~80% pick the middle). (2022-12-27) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2022-12-27-yt-o8IdDEJym1U]])
- **Sell project/value fees, not units of time; delegate; focus.** Selling hours says your
  time is the valuable thing and invites the client to manage it; clients only care about
  outcomes. Scaling beyond a solo operator requires **delegation** (the essential role is
  owning the client relationship, not doing every task). **Focus** narrows the offering —
  "pressure = force / area," so reducing the number of things you do produces a
  breakthrough (he credits Blair Enns' "difficult business decision"). (2022-12-27)
  `[self-reported]` ([[../wiki/sources/2022-12-27-yt-o8IdDEJym1U]])

> ⚠️ Tension across years (evolution, not reversal): in the 2019 value-based-pricing
> method he *co-determines* value with the client and lets them name the number
> ([[../wiki/sources/2019-04-12-yt-ivKnj9ffcmE]]); by 2023 he stresses the price is the
> output of *your own* calculations, to be presented and not defended
> ([[../wiki/sources/2023-10-19-yt-Abi8kwkfZbA]]). These are reconcilable as different
> phases (discovery/closing vs. objection-handling), but the posture hardens over time
> toward "hold your ground."

### Serve, don't sell — "selling is helping"

- **Stop selling, start serving.** The way to close more work is to shift from selling to
  serving: *"How can I be of service to you?"* Amateurs give advice; experts diagnose —
  *"Amateurs give advice. Experts diagnose."* Turn your thinking into questions; *"whoever
  asks more questions is in control of the conversation."* (2019-04-12) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2019-04-12-yt-ivKnj9ffcmE]])
- **Selling is change management, and it's for introverts too.** Selling is helping
  someone move from a current state to a desired future state by finding the obstacle in
  the way; it does not require the loud "Wolf of Wall Street" archetype. His acronym
  **SALES = Serve, Ask, Listen, Empathize, Summarize.** *"Sales is not convincing,
  persuading, or manipulation. Selling is helping…an act of generosity."* Key premises: an
  inbound prospect has already qualified you; clients always have three options (you,
  someone else, nothing); spend is proportionate to the size of the *problem*. (2023-12-23)
  `[self-reported]` ([[../wiki/sources/2023-12-23-yt-B-NxAZiDkKo]])

> ⚠️ Position evolution: after years of teaching detailed selling frameworks, by the 2023
> Q&A his stated current strategy is *"I don't think I'm selling anymore"* — make content
> so good and give it away free ("no funnels, no email traps") so it sells for him. Selling
> mechanics remain his *teaching*; his *personal* practice has moved to content-as-selling.
> ([[../wiki/sources/2023-12-23-yt-B-NxAZiDkKo]])

### "What you believe becomes reality" — the belief cycle

- **Success is learnable, not innate**, and **belief drives results more than tactics.**
  His coaching client "Carrie" broke an $8,000 pricing ceiling to bill more in six months
  than her prior full year, *"with nothing changed but her belief in what was possible."*
  (2019-08-12) `[self-reported]` ([[../wiki/sources/2019-08-12-yt-f7T1Zs28Deo]])
- **The five-spoke belief cycle:** what you think → what you say → what you do → your
  behavior → your identity, deepening like a record groove — *"the longer you play the
  track, the deeper the groove gets, the harder it is to move the needle."* If you dislike
  your results, re-examine your belief system — *"that's just your BS."* (2019-08-12)
  `[self-reported]` ([[../wiki/sources/2019-08-12-yt-f7T1Zs28Deo]])
- **Interpretation sits between input and output** — the same input has infinite possible
  outputs depending on how you look at it: *"If you want to be angry you will find a way…I
  just happen to choose to be happy."* (2019-08-12) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2019-08-12-yt-f7T1Zs28Deo]])
- **"War Jitsu" — strip limiting words of their power:** impossible → "I'm possible";
  fail → "first attempt in learning"; fear → "fantasized experience appearing real"; and
  invert positives (safe → "stay average forever"). When bad things happen, say
  *"congratulations"* and hunt for the learning. *"Failure is the tuition I pay for future
  success."* (2019-08-12) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2019-08-12-yt-f7T1Zs28Deo]])
- **The ultimate enemy is the critical inner voice.** Childhood "armor" that helped you
  survive becomes a heavy adult burden; a three-column journaling exercise (drawn from his
  own therapy and *Conquer Your Critical Inner Voice*) separates the internalized critical
  voice — often a parent's — from your true, compassionate voice. (2019-08-12)
  `[self-reported]` ([[../wiki/sources/2019-08-12-yt-f7T1Zs28Deo]])

### Obstacles are opportunities — reinvention

- **Obstacles are a gift; how you respond determines the outcome.** *"I've always looked
  at obstacles as a gift…a call to action, an opportunity to transform."* Formalized as
  **OREO** — *"how you Respond to an Obstacle Equals your Outcome"* — and grounded in Ryan
  Holiday's *The Obstacle Is the Way* and Stoic virtue. (2021-03-16) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2021-03-16-yt-XH-wD0Jbo5Y]])
- **Reinvention is 80/20, not all-or-nothing.** Keep 80% of what works; invest 20% into
  new initiatives as "personal R&D" and an insurance policy against being made obsolete.
  Redefine identity by the *value you create for others, not what you make* — *"You are
  more than what you make."* Companies that defined themselves by their product died
  (Kodak, Blockbuster, Tower Records). (2021-03-16) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2021-03-16-yt-XH-wD0Jbo5Y]])

### Personal branding — "unbland yourself"

Deepens the v1 "Unbland Yourself" and "the more you, you are…" entries
([[../wiki/sources/2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]] §9):

- **Branding is self-discovery, not creation.** *"Personal branding is not so much an act
  of creation, it's an act of self-discovery."* It requires **vulnerability**, not polish —
  in a world where "corporations are trying to be more human and humans are trying to be
  more like corporations," leaning INTO your weaknesses is what makes you magnetic. Admired
  figures share three traits: self-confident, self-aware, vulnerable. (2023-12-19)
  `[self-reported]` ([[../wiki/sources/2023-12-19-yt-MVz9mm0Z-7g]])
- **The two-word brand.** Reduce how you describe yourself (not what you do) to two words,
  because *"complexity is the enemy of comprehension, retention and execution."* Often a
  **shadow word** (a hidden, uncomfortable truth) + a "transformer" word (e.g. "loud
  introvert," "attention broker"). A good brand story can be told by others when you're not
  in the room. (2023-12-19) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2023-12-19-yt-MVz9mm0Z-7g]])
- **Persona vs. Shadow; adversity is your ally.** The Persona is the front-facing self; the
  Shadow is the private real self — *"you pretend to be somebody long enough and you forget
  who you are."* Share your **5% high-highs and low-lows** (the EO rule), because *"if
  there's no conflict, there's no story"* and *"adversity is your ally."* (2023-12-19)
  `[self-reported]` ([[../wiki/sources/2023-12-19-yt-MVz9mm0Z-7g]])
- **Make enemies to gain fans — "your complaint can become your calling."** *"True fans
  need true critics."* Pick a fight, stand against something, name the enemy to own it
  ("we're in business to end WHAT?"). Reclaim what your worst critics call you (the
  naysayer exercise). (2023-12-19) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2023-12-19-yt-MVz9mm0Z-7g]])
- **Karmic equity / the infinite game.** Doing good expecting nothing back compounds
  exponentially; every ask resets the account to zero. Play the infinite game over
  short-term performance marketing. Closing thesis: *"Weak is strong. Perfect is boring.
  Real is relatable. Stop selling, start serving. Complexity is an enemy. Adversity is an
  ally."* (2023-12-19) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2023-12-19-yt-MVz9mm0Z-7g]])

### Brand & marketing fundamentals

- **A brand is a gut feeling; a logo is only a container for meaning.** *"Brand is a
  person's gut feeling. It's not what you say it is, it's what they say it is"* (credits
  Marty Neumeier). Branding is **impression management** — you can *influence* perception
  but not *control* it. (2024-01-10) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2024-01-10-yt-h-eRwbbpfxo]])
- **Positioning = reframing relative to competitors; premium is a positioning play.**
  Positioning is "the space you occupy in the hearts and minds of your customers relative
  to your competitors" (credits Ries & Trout); you win it by reframing a competitor's
  strength into a weakness ("pick a fight"). *"When you're a company of one, you don't
  compete on price anymore, you command a premium"* — price *communicates* position.
  (2024-01-10) `[self-reported]` ([[../wiki/sources/2024-01-10-yt-h-eRwbbpfxo]])
- **Marketing is generous; sell benefits, not features; target psychographics.**
  *"Marketing is the generous act of helping people get what they want — not what you want
  — with no expectations"* (credits Seth Godin). Sell the emotional benefit ("sleep better
  at night"), not the feature; focus on psychographics (beliefs, values) over demographics;
  the hardest, decisive step is behaving *consistently* across the whole customer journey so
  customers become advocates. (2024-01-10) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2024-01-10-yt-h-eRwbbpfxo]])

### How to learn anything

- **Learn, unlearn, relearn** (Toffler): the illiterate of the 21st century are those who
  can't. The three essential 21st-century skills are **self-learning, critical thinking,
  and articulation**; *"asking the right question is 90% of the work."* (2020-06-09)
  `[self-reported]` ([[../wiki/sources/2020-06-09-yt-ujxvy5NjeRQ]])
- **Output beats input; the cycle is input → reflect → implement → share.** Consuming
  without applying is *"the illusion of knowledge"* — *"I watch a ton of cooking shows…I've
  yet to make a single dish."* Teaching drives learning deepest — *"when one teaches, two
  learn"* — and *"if you can't explain it simply, you just don't know it."* Love being
  wrong: *"I love to be wrong."* (2020-06-09) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2020-06-09-yt-ujxvy5NjeRQ]])
- **The Five Ingredients method.** To learn to reproduce any work, deconstruct it into
  ~five discrete, observable, *repeatable* attributes in the sweet spot between "too
  specific" and "too broad." *"You can't draw what you don't know; you don't know what you
  haven't seen"* — learning is learning to see. (2020-06-09) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2020-06-09-yt-ujxvy5NjeRQ]])

### Negotiate from non-neediness — "the more I say no, the more they say yes"

The earliest-dated form of his pricing/selling posture; predates and grounds the pass-1
"never justify your price / say a price before you show a price" cluster.

- **Leverage is set in the first few minutes; neediness can be smelled.** *"The terms of
  engagement and the relationship and leverage and power is established within the first few
  minutes of your dialogue… If you're needy, if you're desperate… people can smell it on
  you."* If your stated rate rises into a question mark, the client knows it isn't your real
  rate and cuts it down. (2016-08-25) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2016-08-25-yt-aJzY8P4bWb0]])
- **"Don't want the work" — try to kill the engagement three times.** A tactic he credits
  to Blair Enns: genuinely not needing the job removes the leverage a client holds over you
  (portfolio, exposure). *"The more I say no, the more they say yes."* Creatives are
  "other-validated" people-pleasers who hand their self-worth over "on a silver platter," so
  a "mastery mindset" of not-wanting is the corrective. Caveat: you must not be "eating
  Wonderbread for dinner" — financial stability underwrites the ability to walk. (2016-08-25)
  `[self-reported]` (influence: Blair Enns) ([[../wiki/sources/2016-08-25-yt-aJzY8P4bWb0]])
- **You can be too tough a negotiator — "winning the negotiation but losing the job."** A
  fine line and an art; practice non-neediness by role-playing it on the next job you don't
  actually want. Cross-ref his 2016 rule to *state your price like a fact, then stay silent*
  ([[../wiki/sources/2016-10-28-yt-gm66Etsi2-c]]). (2016-08-25) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2016-08-25-yt-aJzY8P4bWb0]])

### Design education is broken — value over credentials

His earliest sustained thesis and the intellectual seed of The Futur ("a private art school
without the crippling debt"). All 2016–2017, all Chris-attributed:

- **Cost ≠ value; the degree is "a myth."** *"Value talks about what you're getting out of
  it; cost is an expense."* A $200k education returning $500k of value is a bargain; one
  returning $100k is just an expense. As a hiring manager he never looks at school, degree,
  attendance, or GPA — *"It's a myth."* Core line: *"the amount of education you get is not
  proportionate to the value you get out of it."* (2016-12-06, 2017-02-15)
  `[self-reported opinion]` ([[../wiki/sources/2016-12-06-yt-tOr4DoDYXaA]];
  [[../wiki/sources/2017-02-15-yt-kWZ1qtvcVHc]])
- **"Teach them not to be makers."** Design schools train "maker mentality" (production),
  but in a globally connected world the value of making is "going really down… really fast."
  Elite schools should elevate students into strategic, client-facing, entrepreneurial work
  that is "hard to offshore." He frames leading this reform as a near "moral obligation" —
  while still respecting, paying, and loving the makers he employs. (2016-06-30)
  `[self-reported opinion]` ([[../wiki/sources/2016-06-30-yt-veG3WbwwaGk]])
- **Practice beats credentials; the alternative is apprenticeship, not tuition.** Field
  practice matters far more than degrees for teaching design — his own Art Center title
  stayed "assistant adjunct instructor" after ~15 years teaching / 20 in the field because
  he has no master's and won't get one. Alternative model: spend the tuition paying industry
  masters ~$10k each to apprentice under — *"for $100,000, how many rock star… industry
  titans can you get… the number is 10."* And flip the classroom: record the best authority
  once, do the hard problem-solving at school with teachers as "coaches." (2016-12-06,
  2017-02-15) `[self-reported opinion]` ([[../wiki/sources/2016-12-06-yt-tOr4DoDYXaA]];
  [[../wiki/sources/2017-02-15-yt-kWZ1qtvcVHc]])

> Continuity note (not a reversal): this 2016–2017 education-reform stance is the same
> position he still holds in 2024 ("believe in education, distrust the institutions,"
> Opinions below) — evidence the thesis is long-standing, not a late pivot.
> ([[../wiki/sources/2024-02-24-yt-f5uYRuqK4g0]])

> ⚠️ Tension (reconcilable): in 2016 he asserts creativity itself *"cannot be taught because
> it's innate in all of us"* — it gets "beaten out" by culture, parents, and teachers
> ([[../wiki/sources/2016-06-30-yt-veG3WbwwaGk]]); by 2019 he stresses that *success* is
> learnable, not innate ([[../wiki/sources/2019-08-12-yt-f7T1Zs28Deo]]). Reconciles as
> innate *creativity* vs. learnable *business/success skills*, but the framing shifts.

### Culture is hired and fired for — values over talent

- **Hire and fire on values, not talent alone.** At Blind he first "let creativity be my
  guiding force," hired the best regardless of fit, and the culture became "unmanaged" until
  he "wasn't happy to show up to work anymore." The fix (assigned by his coach Kier
  McLaren): define core values, then *"hire based on your values and… fire based on those
  values too."* Purging the misaligned took ~1.5 years and left a company that "moved really
  fast." Having no words for his own values at first, he adapted them from Zappos' published
  list (Tony Hsieh, *Delivering Happiness*). (2016-11-03) `[self-reported]`
  (influence: Tony Hsieh / Zappos) ([[../wiki/sources/2016-11-03-yt-kmZkRkxWj2c]])
- **Never fire for a first mistake — only for not learning.** *"We will never fire somebody
  because they made a mistake… if you make the mistake repeatedly, now that's an issue
  because you never learn from it."* Fear of mistakes makes people reuse the same safe
  tricks. (2016-11-03) `[self-reported]` ([[../wiki/sources/2016-11-03-yt-kmZkRkxWj2c]])
- **Autonomy, mastery, purpose — and time over money.** He credits Daniel Pink for the three
  drivers of a creative culture, and notes younger staff rebelled against cash incentives,
  preferring shared time (pizza parties, bowling): *"the one resource that you can't get
  more of is your time."* And *"there's no such thing as an uncreative job, there's just
  uncreative people."* Culture is safeguarded by hiring only people who share the published
  values — a "weirdest people in the company" interview vets fit. (2016-11-03, 2017-02-15)
  `[self-reported]` (influences: Daniel Pink, Simon Sinek)
  ([[../wiki/sources/2016-11-03-yt-kmZkRkxWj2c]];
  [[../wiki/sources/2017-02-15-yt-kWZ1qtvcVHc]])

### Sell what the world can do — the designer→entrepreneur transition

- **"Sell what the world can do, not what you can do."** *"You guys sell what you do. I sell
  what the world can do… I sell ideas."* He prices a job to afford the best specialist and
  marks it up; the "gap" between their cost and his price is where profit and contingency
  live — *"the bigger the gap the better off you are."* (2016-10-28) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2016-10-28-yt-gm66Etsi2-c]])
- **Entrepreneur vs. "want-to-preneur" — financial IQ is client power.** Most creatives like
  the "sexy" idea of running a company but won't learn its mechanics (gross vs. net,
  pricing, asking for the sale). *"Your financial IQ allows you to engage with clients on a
  higher level."* He teaches the "runway / worry date" cash-flow tool — receivables ÷
  monthly overhead = months of runway; the worry date is one month sooner — which he credits
  to Mike Janda. (2016-10-28) `[self-reported]` (influences: Mark Cuban framing; Mike Janda)
  ([[../wiki/sources/2016-10-28-yt-gm66Etsi2-c]])

### Success is how much you grew, not how close to the vision

- **Redefine the end result as growth.** *"Most creative people measure success as to how
  closely what they produce resembles what they had imagined. I measure success by how much
  I've grown."* Nothing is ever as good as you imagine, so measuring against the vision
  guarantees disappointment — measure risk taken and distance stretched instead. A stated
  greatest fear: *"to look back and still recognize the person from a year ago."* (2016-06-16,
  2017-01-05) `[self-reported]` ([[../wiki/sources/2016-06-16-yt-6ZtbgCbcyeo]];
  [[../wiki/sources/2017-01-05-yt-rP2iDwSN_v8]])
- **"Absent a narrative, people invent one."** Without status updates, clients fill the
  silence with an unflattering story; so *"under promise, over deliver"* and keep the client
  visible on progress. *"Your reputation is all you have."* (2016-06-16) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2016-06-16-yt-6ZtbgCbcyeo]])
- **"I just sucked less."** Growth is incremental, not a switch — on his year of "torture"
  learning to speak in public: *"it wasn't that it was good, I just sucked less until I
  figured out my own trick."* Pairs with his learn-by-copying method (deconstruct good work
  into its ~17 "ingredients," repeat, then move "less away from the formula and more into
  intuition… all built on fundamentals") — the 2016–2017 antecedent of the pass-1 "Five
  Ingredients" method. (2017-01-05) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2017-01-05-yt-rP2iDwSN_v8]]; cf.
  [[../wiki/sources/2020-06-09-yt-ujxvy5NjeRQ]])

### Radical self-belief — hijack your mentors, own the key to your sanity

- **From "prove them wrong" to genuine self-belief.** Negative fuel (anger, self-pity) can
  be channeled into "prove them wrong," but that state is still negative because it's rooted
  in anger at others; the mature endpoint is doing the work because you believe in who you
  are. He describes a personal "dark night of the soul" — *"the boy was killed and the man
  emerged."* (2016-12-24) `[self-reported]` ([[../wiki/sources/2016-12-24-yt-a5VMFo_Axdc]])
- **"Hijack your mentors."** Credited to Michael Bierut: you can have mentors without them
  knowing they're mentoring you — Chris counts Jim Rohn, Tony Robbins, Jack Canfield, and
  Blair Enns among mentors he's never met. *"We live in the information age… anything and
  everything you want to know is already there, and most of it… free."* (2016-12-24)
  `[self-reported]` (influence: Michael Bierut) ([[../wiki/sources/2016-12-24-yt-a5VMFo_Axdc]])
- **Own the key to your sanity; overcome fear by "modeling."** Don't let praise or criticism
  govern your emotional state — *"would you give the key to your sanity to any person on the
  street?"* Assess your own work honestly before showing it: *"I'm not looking for your
  validation, I'm not looking for your criticism."* Overcome fear by "modeling" — copying the
  behavior and cadence of people you admire (he copied a man named Bart to learn casual

## Values

- **Mission: teach 1 billion people to make a living doing what they love.** The stated
  purpose of The Futur and the frame through which he describes his own work. (2016,
  evergreen) `[self-reported]` / `[press-corroborated]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]] §8;
  [[../wiki/sources/2019-08-12-yt-f7T1Zs28Deo]])
- **Radical accountability.** *"I'm a big believer in radical accountability…take
  responsibility for everything that happens to you, for you, with you. It's the only way
  you grow."* He'll take the blame even when it isn't his fault, because *"I want to learn
  versus pushing that responsibility on somebody else"* — *"success has many fathers but
  failure has one mother."* (2025-03-11) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2025-03-11-yt-f6YiuU-P-6E]])
- **Delayed gratification / play the long game.** *"Think in decades and not in days."*
  Relays his father's advice: make the sacrifices and work extremely hard in your short
  window to "make it," then decide from that platform whether to coast. (2025-03-11)
  `[self-reported]` ([[../wiki/sources/2025-03-11-yt-f6YiuU-P-6E]])
- **Pursue skills, not money or things.** *"Look at life as a pursuit or acquisition of
  skills, because the skills compound in you and no one can take them away."* Investing in
  yourself "raises the basement" of your value — the fallback that kept him in the game
  through the dark days of entrepreneurship. Shortcuts fail because they try to get the
  result without acquiring the skill. (2025-03-11, 2022-12-27) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2025-03-11-yt-f6YiuU-P-6E]];
  [[../wiki/sources/2022-12-27-yt-o8IdDEJym1U]])
- **Time is the most precious, non-renewable resource — trade money for time.** *"People
  underestimate the value of their time…successful entrepreneurs trade their money for
  their time."* Meetings are the biggest time-suck. (2025-03-11) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2025-03-11-yt-f6YiuU-P-6E]])
- **"Hell yeah or hell no."** *"Every time you say yes to something, you're saying no to
  something else…saying no more often gives power to your yes."* As a self-described middle
  child and people-pleaser, saying yes to everything was his failure mode. (2025-03-11)
  `[self-reported]` ([[../wiki/sources/2025-03-11-yt-f6YiuU-P-6E]])
- **Love applying more than learning; guard against energy vampires.** Learning for its own
  sake is *"some form of mental masturbation"* — before reading, ask why and how you'll
  apply it. You become the average of the five people you spend the most time with (Jim
  Rohn), so cut off negative people. Compare only against your old self — *"comparison is
  the thief of joy."* (2025-03-11) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2025-03-11-yt-f6YiuU-P-6E]])
- **Money mindset: risk over safety; invest in yourself, people, and tools.** *"In business
  all profit comes from risk"* (Drucker) — millionaires make nine bad bets for one that pays
  1,000x; playing it safe yields only "safe" (small) returns. Reinvest profit into people,
  systems, and the best tools; underpowered equipment "artificially caps" a team's

- **Give knowledge away — teaching is the model** (v1, deepened). Publish the substance for
