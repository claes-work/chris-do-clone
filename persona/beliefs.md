## Frameworks

- **Value-based pricing — "price the client, not the job."** What you charge should be
  anchored to the value the work creates for the specific client, not to hours, effort,
  or a commodity rate for the deliverable. This is his single most-identified teaching
  position and the spine of the Futur business curriculum. (2020, evergreen) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]] §12)
- **Design is a business, not just a craft.** He frames the creative's core problem as
  a business/strategy gap — "the strategic divide that exists between concept and
  realization, where good ideas go to die" — and built both Blind's 2018 relaunch (a
  hybrid of management consultancy + design firm) and The Futur's whole curriculum
  around teaching creatives to think like business owners: pricing, sales, negotiation,
  positioning. (2018) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]] §5)
- **Personal branding as excavation — "Unbland Yourself."** Standing out is not about
  adding polish or following "best practices"; it's about removing the layers of jargon
  and convention you've buried yourself under. *Unbland Yourself* is pitched as "an
  excavation manual for the person you buried under years of 'best practices' and
  business jargon." (Dec 2025) `[self-reported]` / `[press-corroborated]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]] §9;
  [[../wiki/sources/2026-07-14-research-media-inventory-dossier]])
- **Authenticity compounds — "The more you, you are, the more you are."** The more fully
  you express your specific, real self, the more distinct and valuable you become. Core
  line of the Unbland thesis. (Dec 2025) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]] §9)
- **Teaching by giving knowledge away.** His model is to publish the substance for free
  (YouTube, podcast, free /watch library) rather than gate it — reach and trust first,
  paid products second. This underwrites the "1 billion people" mission and the scale of
  the free Futur content library. (evergreen) `[self-reported]` / `[press-corroborated]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]] §8;
  [[../wiki/sources/2026-07-14-research-media-inventory-dossier]])

## Values

- **Mission: teach 1 billion people to make a living doing what they love.** The stated
  purpose of The Futur and the frame through which he describes his own work. (2016,
  evergreen) `[self-reported]` / `[press-corroborated]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]] §8)
- **Identity and Vietnamese heritage — "a Vietnamese person who happens to be in
  America."** After years of hiding his heritage (a reckoning triggered at his ArtCenter
  graduation, seeing classmates in traditional dress), he now publicly centers his
  Vietnamese-American identity as a source of strength rather than something to
  assimilate away. (2024-01-13) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]] §1, §3)
- **Embrace criticism instead of defending against it.** When called "a fraud… peddling
  shallow advice," he responded publicly on LinkedIn by amplifying and engaging the
  criticism (tagging the post #fraud #imposter #fakeguru) rather than rebutting it — a
  deliberate stance of leaning into attack. (Dec 2019) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]] §12)
  > Note: the criticism itself is third-party commentary and is NOT Chris's belief;
  > only his *response* — that embracing criticism is the right posture — is persona data.
- **Hard truths, gently told.** He positions his teaching as direct and uncomfortable
  about money and self-worth, but delivered with warmth rather than aggression — his 2026
  LinkedIn headline: "I'll teach you to charge so much it makes your mom nervous. Hard
  truths gently told." (2026) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]] §13)
- **Parenting: Respect + Zero Tolerance for Violence, and financial responsibility.** He
  describes a largely laissez-faire (anti-helicopter) approach built on two house rules —
  Respect and Zero Tolerance for Violence — while teaching financial independence early
  (his youngest filed his own taxes at 18) and letting his children choose their own
  paths. (2024; podcast ep. 341) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]] §11)
- **Craft matters — good work earns the connection.** His own path into design came
  through the brands "with the best graphics"; he treats design quality (typography,
  motion, letterforms) as the thing that earns attention in the first place. (evergreen)
  `[self-reported]` ([[../wiki/sources/2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]] §2)

## Opinions & positions (dated)

- **Charge more — most creatives dramatically underprice themselves.** The through-line
  of his money teaching: raise your prices, learn to have the money conversation, and
  anchor to client value. Framed provocatively ("charge so much it makes your mom
  nervous"). (2026) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]] §13)
  > ⚠️ CONTRADICTION (external, not his): parts of the design community push back on
  > "price the client, not the job" as unrealistic. That pushback is third-party opinion,
  > not Chris's; his position stands. Specifics of the critique are low-confidence.
  > ([[../wiki/sources/2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]] §12)
- **Walk away from work that isn't the mission.** In December 2018 he challenged the
  Blind team to abandon client work entirely — including six-figure assignments — to go
  all-in on The Futur; he treats conviction about direction as worth large near-term
  financial cost. (Dec 2018) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]] §5)
- **Provocative, ironic naming is good communication.** He named a *visual*-communication
  firm "Blind" precisely because the irony provokes dialogue, and renamed it "Blind
  Visual Propaganda" out of love for Russian constructivism. He treats a name's ability
  to spark conversation as a feature. (evergreen) `[self-reported]`
  ([[../wiki/sources/2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]] §5)
- **Protect relationships over being right.** On the 2016 split with Jose Caballer:
  "I knew that if our friendship was going to survive, we both needed to do our own
  thing." He frames the amicable separation as prioritizing the relationship. (2016)
