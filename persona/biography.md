- **~1977 — Moves to San Jose, California** to join extended family (more of his father's siblings were there); he frames the San Jose choice as largely logistical/"random." Grows up in a striving suburban middle-class household that moved every few years to better neighborhoods chasing the "American dream"; as a first-gen immigrant kid he always **identified the family as poor**, then was shocked to learn from school paperwork that their income didn't match that self-image. 🗣️ ([[2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]], [[2024-02-24-yt-f5uYRuqK4g0]])
- **Family structure & the two-poles origin (self-reported; name-free).** Middle child of **three brothers**: an **older brother** (4 years older), highly logical, **master's from Stanford**, computer-science/software engineer in Silicon Valley; a **younger brother** (1 year younger), free-form **artist who never went to college**. Chris positions himself literally in the middle, with a BFA in art & design. His **father is an engineer** (father's side: technical, stable Silicon Valley engineers); his **mother is a self-taught artist** (oils and pastels) who worked at **IBM as a designer/drafter on chip / chipboard design**, drawing by hand at high magnification; mother's side are artists (photographers, painters) — emotional, creative, financially unstable, "many divorces." Chris frames his identity as the fusion of these two poles. He credits his own path not directly to his mother but to an early "obsession with emblems and logos." 🗣️ (video corroborates and greatly enriches the dossier's father-engineer / mother-artist contrast) ([[2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]], [[2024-02-24-yt-f5uYRuqK4g0]])

Got A's easily but was bored; graduated high school with a **3.8 GPA**, gravitating to electives (wood/metal shop). **Report-card forgery anecdote:** once got a C in science, intercepted the mailed carbon report card, used his mother's professional drafting eraser to redraw it up to a B (deciding an A "would look too suspicious") and smudged carbon over it — "that's how I learned how to do forgery, pre-Photoshop." He references hearing his father's voice as the internal disciplinarian. 🗣️ (voice/character detail; corroborated across two sources) ([[2024-02-24-yt-f5uYRuqK4g0]], [[2019-08-12-yt-f7T1Zs28Deo]])

- **2016–2017 — The design-education-reform thesis crystallizes (the intellectual root of The Futur).** Across a run of *The Process* episodes (some recorded at ArtCenter, debating faculty) he lays out the argument that becomes The Futur's founding mission — a **"private art school without the crippling debt."** Core moves: **separate cost from value** (a $200k education is worth it only if it returns more than $200k of value); art schools over-index on **"maker"/production hard skills** whose economic value is "going down really fast" in a globally connected world, while the **soft/business skills** that actually get creatives hired and paid — communication, leadership, pricing, negotiation, entrepreneurship — are learned on the job, if at all; he reframes a 90-second grad-show demo reel as **"$1,000 a second,"** and floats an alternative model — spend the same tuition paying ~$10k each to **apprentice under ~10 industry masters around the world.** He casts leading this reform as a near **"moral obligation"** of top schools. 🗣️ ([[2016-06-30-yt-veG3WbwwaGk]], [[2016-12-06-yt-tOr4DoDYXaA]], [[2017-02-15-yt-kWZ1qtvcVHc]])
