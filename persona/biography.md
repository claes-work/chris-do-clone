## Timeline

- **1972 — Born in Saigon, Vietnam.** 📰 Year and place consistent across independent bios. The exact date "January 13" is aggregator-only — treat as low confidence. Vietnamese surname Đỗ; "Christopher Do" in formal records. ([[2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]])
- **1975 (April 30) — Family flees the Fall of Saigon** when Chris is three; father had served in the South Vietnamese military. Arrive in the US as refugees, first landing in Kansas City, Missouri via a Catholic-church sponsorship program. 🗣️ ([[2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]])
- **~1977 — Moves to San Jose, California** to join extended family; grows up there until college. Father rises from busboy to chief engineer at a Silicon Valley semiconductor company. Middle child; father's side technical engineers, mother's side artists — a contrast he cites as formative. Introverted immigrant kid, finds refuge in drawing and comic books. 🗣️ ([[2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]])
- **~1989 (age 17) — Discovers graphic design through skateboarding graphics** ("The companies I found the greatest connection with had the best graphics"). Rejected by UCLA, UC San Diego, and Cal Poly SLO; freelances at a silk-screen shop, where meeting a working designer reveals design as a profession. His conservative father initially opposes the career (mother intervenes; father takes ~10 years to come around). High school name: not public. 🗣️ ([[2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]])
- **1990 — San Diego City College**: completes the graphic design program in two semesters; instructor Candice Lopez helps him build the portfolio that gets him into ArtCenter. 📰 (institutional alumni page) ([[2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]])
- **1995 — BFA, ArtCenter College of Design, Pasadena** — with honors, Graphics and Packaging major, scholarship recipient. ✅/🗣️ (ArtCenter gallery + own resume) At graduation, seeing international classmates in traditional dress triggers a reckoning with his hidden Vietnamese identity. 🗣️ ([[2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]])
- **1995–1996 — Early freelance work**: R/GA (main titles for *Eraser*, *The Island of Dr. Moreau*, *Celtic Pride*; Taco Bell campaign), NOVOCOM, Epitaph Records (Pennywise, Rancid artwork), Cole & Webber (Seattle), intern at Praxis under Simon Johnston. 🗣️ (resume; exact months low confidence) ([[2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]])
- **December 1995 — Founds Blind** in Santa Monica — print roots, quickly a motion-design pioneer ("first and a half generation" of motion design). Legal entity **Blind Visual Propaganda, Inc.** ✅ (D&B/Bloomberg registries); renamed from "Blind Visual Communication" out of love for Russian constructivism and ironic names. 🗣️ Clients over ~23 years: Microsoft, Nike, Xbox, Sony, Showtime, MTV, Fox, Toyota, NYSE; music videos for Justin Timberlake, Coldplay, The Raveonettes. "$80M lifetime billings" 🗣️ only. ([[2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]])
- **2000–2015 — Teaches at ArtCenter** (part-time instructor: Advanced Motion Communication Design, Dynamic Type, Film Title Design, Sequential Design 1). **2001–2008 — Otis College** (assistant adjunct professor). Guest lectures 2002–2017: Siggraph, CalArts, TypeCon, Chinese art academies, Adobe Video World keynote. 🗣️ (resume) ([[2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]])
- **2010 — Emmy, "Outstanding Individual Achievement in Animation"**, as Art Director of The Raveonettes' "Heart of Stone" music video (VEVO.com / Blind, Inc.; he also directed it). ✅ Television Academy record. (Forbes 2026 misprints it as a "Grammy" — press error.) ([[2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]])
- **2012–2017 — Industry service**: AIGA advisory board/events co-chair (2012–14), TV Academy Motion & Title Design executive committee (2013–17), Otis Board of Governors (2013–14), Saleshood advisory board (2013–), SPJA/Anime Expo board (2014–, later chairman), Woodbury & Santa Monica College advisory boards (2014). 🗣️ (resume) ([[2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]])
- **October 2013 — Co-founds The Skool with Jose Caballer** (per his own resume; public content launches August 2014 — sources saying "2014" refer to the launch).
