## Who he is

Blair Enns is the founder of **Win Without Pitching** and the author of two books that
are canonical references in Chris Do's pricing/positioning teaching:

- **_The Win Without Pitching Manifesto_** — a set of "proclamations" for creative firms
  on how to win business without free pitching / spec work.
- **_Pricing Creativity: A Guide to Profit Beyond the Billable Hour_** — value-based
  pricing for creative work (Chris cites it by page, e.g. "page 125").

He co-hosts the **_2Bobs_** podcast with **David C. Baker** (already tracked on
[[influences]]). Biographical detail beyond his authorship/business role is **not
established in this corpus — the wiki is silent** on his nationality, dates, and
history; do not fabricate.

## Blair's frameworks — HIS, not Chris's (ATTRIBUTION GUARD)

These are **Blair Enns's** ideas. They are the exact material most at risk of being
mis-routed into Chris's persona because they sit right next to Chris's own pricing
themes. **Keep them fenced.**

- **MLE — Minimum Level of Engagement.** Declare a minimum deal size early as a hurdle
  the client must clear, so both sides can walk away before wasting time. Model line: *"answer
  the phone — ABC Agency — if you don't have $50,000, hang up."* Facetious, but the point is
  you can hardly talk money too early.
  [[../sources/2021-06-01-yt-JeJMBCbHs_k|2021-06-01]]
  - **MLE math / the "$50k hurdle":** start the minimum at **10% of your annual fee
    target**, because a healthy firm has ~10 clients each averaging ~1/10 of revenue.
    $200k target → ~$20k MLE; a $400k firm → ~$40k MLE. The "$50k, hang up" is the
    signature hurdle line. [[../sources/2021-06-01-yt-JeJMBCbHs_k|2021-06-01]]
  - MLE is a **guideline presented as a hurdle** — you needn't actually hold the client
    to it and may waive it (declared to filter and anchor, but revocable). Same session
    flags this as a nuance/contradiction. [[../sources/2021-06-01-yt-JeJMBCbHs_k|2021-06-01]]
- **"Blairtopia" — the 8–15 (call it 10) client constraint.** A thought experiment: cap
  yourself at ~10 clients. The hard constraint feels impossible, then survivable-with-pain,
  then becomes the *source* of the breakthrough — it forces the MLE conversation and forces
  you to drop your least-profitable client to add a better one.
  [[../sources/2021-06-01-yt-JeJMBCbHs_k|2021-06-01]]
- **"Retreat and follow."** The half-step withdrawal mechanic within the win-without-pitching
  frame — your only real power is to **withhold your expertise**; if you can't walk away you
  relinquish it. Clients smell neediness (repulsive) and smell discernment (attractive).
  [[../sources/2021-06-01-yt-JeJMBCbHs_k|2021-06-01]]; the "retreat and follow" half-step is
  named/reinforced in [[../sources/2022-06-16-yt-4kVo2YEzPqQ|2022-06-16]].
- **Pricing is positioning / price-signaling.** A price carries information (economists'
  "signaling") about confidence, demand, and supply; fixing prices "destroys the signaling
  and supply cannot meet demand." To signal the market to slow you down, raise the price.
  [[../sources/2021-04-29-yt-rM3nB6pLOPs|2021-04-29]]
  - **Round numbers as a "hermetically sealed box":** a round $50k resists being decomposed
    into a rate/hours, unlike a specific number. (Contrast pole: **Chris Voss** favors precise
    numbers — Chris flags this cross-reference.)
    [[../sources/2021-04-29-yt-rM3nB6pLOPs|2021-04-29]]
- **Commodity = inability to support a price premium** (_Pricing Creativity_, "page 125"):
  if you can't win the job while being near the highest price, you're selling a commodity.
  Plus **"profitability drives loyalty"** and the **"prison cell" of your own making**
  (self-imposed pricing band you can learn to expand).
  [[../sources/2021-04-01-yt-sfyHSbfUCrQ|2021-04-01]]
- **Positioning / claim-of-expertise:** clients hire the **least risky** option, not the
  best; codify a methodology (the "ditch-digging" metaphor), make the claim before you're
  comfortable, and let competitive pressure force you to pay it off.
  [[../sources/2021-04-20-yt-5a488th9fkM|2021-04-20]]
  - ⚠️ Note: the **"clients hire the least risky option"** line is a long-standing **Chris**
    theme (Chris jokes "I don't think I've stolen this from you, Blair") — that specific
    restatement is Chris-attributable; the surrounding book material is Blair's. See the
    source page for the exact split.
- **Anchoring & performance pricing:** always open with the highest number; put compensation
  at risk to charge beyond your experience ("all profit is derived from risk," quoting
  Drucker). [[../sources/2021-06-01-yt-JeJMBCbHs_k|2021-06-01]]
