# Blind, Inc. (Blind Visual Propaganda, Inc.)

Chris Do's design agency — the company that made his professional reputation. Motion-design
pioneer turned brand-strategy consultancy, run by Do from 1995 until it wound down client work
in late 2018. Now formally dissolved by merger.

## Registry record [registry-verified]

California Secretary of State business search (bizfileonline.sos.ca.gov, checked 2026-07-14):

- **Legal name:** BLIND VISUAL PROPAGANDA, INC. — entity **C1988167**
- **Initial filing:** 07/18/1997, Stock Corporation – CA – General, formed in California
- **Agent for service:** CHRISTOPHER DO (individual)
- **Principal/mailing address:** 1702 Olympic Blvd, Santa Monica, CA 90404
- **Status:** Merged Out; **inactive date 12/31/2020**. Filing history shows a Legacy Merger
  dated 12/31/2020: "OUTGOING-MERGED INTO C3630045 PALISADES INVESTMENT MANAGEMENT, INC."
  (legacy number D1608500). Standing SOS/FTB/Agent all Good at closure.

Note the founding-vs-incorporation nuance: Do consistently says he started Blind in **1995**
straight out of ArtCenter ([self-reported], corroborated by press, e.g. Motionographer's
20-year retrospective, 2015-10-22: https://motionographer.com/2015/10/22/blind-looking-back-at-20-years/);
the California corporation was filed in **July 1997**. Both can be true (operating before
incorporating), but only the 1997 date is registry-verified.

## History

- **1995 — founding.** Chris Do founds Blind in Los Angeles/Santa Monica immediately after his
  BFA at ArtCenter College of Design [press-corroborated: Motionographer 2015; The Logo
  Creative interview, thelogocreative.co.uk/designer-interview-with-chris-do/]. Early work:
  print designers "dabbling in motion design" — title design, animations for car commercials
  [press-corroborated: Motionographer 2015].
- **Late 1990s–2000s — motion-design era.** Hundreds of commercials, music videos and broadcast
  promos combining design, typography, animation, live action and VFX [press-corroborated:
  Motionographer 2015]. Chris Do won a **2010 Emmy** (Outstanding Individual Achievement in
  Animation) for the music video "Heart of Stone" produced through Blind [press-corroborated:
  ArtCenter alumni gallery, artcenter.edu/gallery/detail/569d6bda4d9b6f81553a151e/10093].
- **2010s — pivot to brand strategy.** Services expanded to brand strategy, identity design,
  messaging, UX/UI and web; blind.com later relaunched explicitly as a "brand strategy design
  consultancy" [press-corroborated: Motionographer 2015; blind.com relaunch post,
  blind.com/blog/blind-relaunches-as-brand-strategy-design-consultancy/].
- **December 2018 — end of client work.** Do and team "walked away from client work",
  including six-figure assignments, to focus on [[the-futur]] [self-reported: School of Motion
  interview, schoolofmotion.com/blog/the-uncertain-future-of-mograph-with-chris-do; Creator
  Science #111, 2022-08-02, podcast.creatorscience.com/chris-do/].
- **12/31/2020 — corporate dissolution.** Blind Visual Propaganda, Inc. merged into Palisades
  Investment Management, Inc. (C3630045) [registry-verified].
- **April 2022 — blind.com domain for sale.** Do publicly offered the blind.com domain (owned
  since 1995) for sale, with proceeds intended to finance The Futur [self-reported: Chris Do
  LinkedIn post, linkedin.com/posts/thechrisdo_blind-domainforsale-activity-6924017326073806848-7G2M,
  April 2022]. Current ownership/state of blind.com not verified (site returned HTTP 403 on
  2026-07-14). Do not confuse blind.com with teamblind.com (the anonymous workplace app
  "Blind" — unrelated).

## Scale and clients

- Clients across the years: Microsoft, Xbox, Nike, Sony PlayStation, Google, Intel, Audi (UK),
  Mitsubishi Motors, American Airlines, plus artists such as Justin Timberlake and Coldplay
  [press-corroborated: Motionographer 2015]. Wider lists also name Honda, NFL, MLB, Showtime,
  Snapchat, Riot Games [self-reported in bios].
- Revenue: "$4 million on the low end, as much as $7 million" per year, averaging ~$5M; best
  year 2007 at almost $7M [self-reported: Creator Science #111, 2022-08-02; millo.co/chris-do-interview].
- Team: ~15–16 core staff, expanding to 50–60 with freelancers; 9,000 sq ft studio with three
  editing bays [self-reported: Creator Science #111, 2022-08-02].
- **"$80M lifetime billings"** — combined gross billings over ~23 years (1995–2018)
  [SELF-REPORTED only; repeated in podcast titles, e.g. Fearless Business podcast,
  podcast.fearless.biz/e/chris-do-the-futur-podcast-interview/. No independent audit or registry
  corroboration exists; always mark as self-reported].

## Relationship to The Futur

Blind and [[the-futur]] were legally separate companies that shared the 1702 Olympic Blvd
office and collaborated on content; several Blind staff ([[futur-instructors|Matthew Encina,
Ben Burns, Greg Gunn]]) worked across both [press-corroborated: Motionographer,
"The future of The Futur", 2017-09-28, motionographer.com/2017/09/28/the-future-of-the-futur/].
The Futur, LLC's registered principal address is the same 1702 Olympic Blvd [registry-verified].

> claims are **self-reported** on-camera by Blind employees or their client unless noted.

**Confirmed positioning line.** The recurring series narration describes Blind as "a brand
strategy design consultancy based in Santa Monica, California, since 1995" that "has used the
power of design to help diverse clients reach their customers and stand out in the marketplace"
[self-reported, repeated verbatim across episodes: [[../sources/2019-07-17-yt-KcnslNdyFI0]];
[[../sources/2019-07-24-yt-pRDfov1dbj0]]]. This corroborates the "since 1995" founding date Do
gives elsewhere (still self-reported, not registry-verified — see the registry nuance above).

### The "Building A Brand" documentary (a real Blind rebrand)

Season one of *Building A Brand* (episodes published mid-2019) filmed Blind executing a full,
real rebrand of **Hamilton Family Brewery** (Rancho Cucamonga, CA) from start to finish.

- **Origin & funding.** Creative director Ben Burns pitched Chris Do on the idea over a year
  earlier: document a rebrand for a couple who "can't afford Blind services." Do greenlit and
  **funded it as a "test experiment"** — Burns says the resources, team and money made available
  were "out of this world" [self-reported, Burns in closing credits:
  [[../sources/2019-07-31-yt-pOdBeS43W0A]]]. This is the only reference to Chris Do in that
  episode; he does not otherwise appear.
- **Education-funnel → agency pipeline (demonstrated live).** Client Josh Hamilton found The Futur
  on YouTube while trying to DIY a cleaner logo himself; he "got pretty far" until videos on letter
  **kerning** made him realize "I am out of my depth here," at which point he called the parent
  company, Blind [self-reported, client: [[../sources/2019-07-31-yt-pOdBeS43W0A]]]. A concrete case
  of Futur's free education funneling a paying client to Blind.
- **Retail outcome (self-reported).** After the rebrand and retail launch, the brewery's cans went
  from 3 to **5 BevMo locations** (selling out fast), the owners hired a full-time salesperson and
  a second brewer, and projected **~20% growth for the quarter**
  [self-reported, client: [[../sources/2019-07-31-yt-pOdBeS43W0A]]].

### Blind process / culture (as filmed)

- **Style-scapes as conversation-starters.** Blind presents "style scapes" (mood/direction boards)
  explicitly framed as *not finished design* — "none of this stuff is designed for you… nothing is
  designed yet," used to "set the compass direction" and give the client something to react to,
  rather than selling a finished answer [context, Burns/Encina presenting:
  [[../sources/2019-06-19-yt-54o73NXCfTk]]].
- **Brand-style-guide deliverable + the "clients warp it" fear.** The final rebrand deliverable is a
  brand style guide ("our rules of how to use the logo, the brand, all the visual guidelines"),
  normally handed to a big client's internal design team. Blind voices a recurring designer fear:
  "whenever you're handing something off to a client… there's always a risk that the client is
  gonna take it and ruin it" / warp it "beyond recognition" — heightened when there is no internal
  design team to hold the standard [context, Burns/Encina:
  [[../sources/2019-07-31-yt-pOdBeS43W0A]]].
- **QA / "check three times" culture.** Ben Burns' war story: on a past project his team shipped
  packaging with the wrong barcode — "almost $100,000 in packaging" printed wrong, with millions in
  revenue/opportunity at stake; they lost the client (but survived), which is why he is now "hyper
  vigilant" and checks packaging "three times over" [self-reported, Burns:
  [[../sources/2019-07-17-yt-KcnslNdyFI0]]].
- **Three-stage video production.** Blind's brand-video method breaks into pre-production (define the
  communication goal; plan locations/crew/gear/schedule), production (capture picture and sound,
  overshoot because "we only have one shot"), and post-production (edit to a tight story, layer music
  to dictate feeling, final color/graphics) [context, Encina/Burns/crew:
  [[../sources/2019-07-24-yt-pRDfov1dbj0]]].
- **Packaging-as-sales for alcohol.** Encina's principle: some products "rely pretty much exclusively
  on their packaging to make the sale, and alcohol is one of them" — on a crowded retail shelf the
  design catches the eye first, then the shopper reads the label; the chosen can even moved the flavor
  name above the logo because the bottom of a shelved can is often hidden [context, Encina:
  [[../sources/2019-07-17-yt-KcnslNdyFI0]]].

### The "Design From Scratch" documentary (rebuilding thefutur.com)

A second 2019 series filmed the Blind/Futur team rebuilding thefutur.com from scratch in Webflow —
turning the agency's tools on its own brand.

- **The on-record Ben-vs-Chris tension.** The series names a standing internal conflict: Ben Burns
  optimizes for money/conversions ("trying to make the company money") while Chris Do pushes the
  "why" story ("trying to tell the story of why we're doing what we're doing") — "those two things
  have always collided" across the company. Both are framed as necessary (no money = no Futur; no
  *why* = buying becomes about price) [context, narrator/team:
  [[../sources/2019-09-25-yt-DGGUB_XlH8k]]].
- **Chris Do as internal client / creative director.** On the same project Do acted as the reviewing
  client of his own team: he rejected the near-finished build for not matching the approved style
  scape ("it doesn't quite look like the style scape to me"), weighed the man-hours and money against
  the design phase — "not seeing a great return on investment of time or resources… a little painful"
  — and stopped the review after only two pages before later signing off on the reworked MVP
  [context, Do as reviewing client: [[../sources/2019-10-09-yt-KJfB1GLGZPQ]]].
