# Danny Yount

> ⚠️ **CONTEXT — NOT PERSONA TRAINING DATA.** Danny Yount is a peer / influence in
> Chris Do's title- and motion-design world, **not the subject of this clone**. Nothing
> on this page trains the Chris Do persona. His origin story, design process, and
> leadership opinions are recorded here as **his** (context for understanding Chris Do's
> lineage and network). Only Chris Do's own words train the persona.

Renowned **main-title / motion designer**, best known for feature title sequences on
*Blade Runner 2049*, *Iron Man*, *Sherlock Holmes*, and *Kiss Kiss Bang Bang*, and an
**Emmy** for the *Six Feet Under* main title. Chris Do interviewed him across a two-part
episode on The Futur; Yount sits in the same title-design influence cluster around Chris
as [[kyle-cooper]] and [[karin-fong]]. Sources:
[[wiki/sources/2018-06-06-yt-vJ7qXaZW-KY]], [[wiki/sources/2018-06-22-yt-X-IZG-5ccWA]].

## Who he is

- **Main-title / motion designer** with a body of feature and television title work
  including *Blade Runner 2049*, *Iron Man*, *Sherlock Holmes*, and *Kiss Kiss Bang Bang*,
  plus an **Emmy for the *Six Feet Under* main title**.
  ([[wiki/sources/2018-06-06-yt-vJ7qXaZW-KY]])
- **Self-taught**, with no formal design pedigree; he came up by force of will rather than
  through art school. ([[wiki/sources/2018-06-06-yt-vJ7qXaZW-KY]])
- **Early mentor:** a Seattle **annual-report designer, John Van Dyck**, who took him under
  his wing around **1994** and shaped his design foundation.
  ([[wiki/sources/2018-06-06-yt-vJ7qXaZW-KY]])
- **Peer / friend:** designer **Ash Thorp** is named as a friend and part of his circle.
  ([[wiki/sources/2018-06-06-yt-vJ7qXaZW-KY]])
- **The *Se7en* catalyst:** seeing Kyle Cooper's *Se7en* main titles was a turning point
  that pulled him toward title design. His hands-on entry point was **Paintbox / broadcast
  news-graphics** work before he moved into film/TV titles.
  ([[wiki/sources/2018-06-22-yt-X-IZG-5ccWA]])

## Career path

- **Imaginary Forces** → **Digital Kitchen** → **Prologue** → **Prodigal Pictures** (his
  own studio) traces the arc from working inside major title shops to leading and then
  founding his own. ([[wiki/sources/2018-06-22-yt-X-IZG-5ccWA]])

## His design & leadership philosophy (HIS — context, not persona)

Everything in this section is **Danny Yount speaking**, captured in the two-part 2018 Futur
interview. It is context for understanding him and his connection to Chris — not persona data.

- **"Give yourself a no option — die doing this or succeed":** his framing of the
  self-taught path is total commitment with no fallback, an all-in bet on the craft.
  ([[wiki/sources/2018-06-06-yt-vJ7qXaZW-KY]])
- **Lead from the front / "riding the beast":** his leadership stance is to be out ahead of
  the team doing the work, not directing from behind — staying on top of a demanding,
  fast-moving process. ([[wiki/sources/2018-06-22-yt-X-IZG-5ccWA]])
- **Emotional work gets noticed:** work that carries genuine emotion is what stands out and
  gets remembered, over work that is merely technically clean.
  ([[wiki/sources/2018-06-22-yt-X-IZG-5ccWA]])
- **Experience over ageism:** he pushes back on age bias in the design/motion industry,
  arguing accumulated experience is an asset rather than a liability.
  ([[wiki/sources/2018-06-22-yt-X-IZG-5ccWA]])

- **Same media generation / title-design lineage:** Yount is a contemporary who was pulled
  into title design by the same Kyle-Cooper-*Se7en* moment that anchors Chris Do's own
  title-design world, placing him squarely in the influence cluster around Chris alongside
  [[kyle-cooper]] and [[karin-fong]].
  ([[wiki/sources/2018-06-06-yt-vJ7qXaZW-KY]], [[wiki/sources/2018-06-22-yt-X-IZG-5ccWA]])
- **He interviewed him on The Futur:** Chris Do hosted Yount across a two-part episode
  (2018-06-06 and 2018-06-22), framing him as a title-design peer whose career and craft he
  admires. ([[wiki/sources/2018-06-06-yt-vJ7qXaZW-KY]],
  [[wiki/sources/2018-06-22-yt-X-IZG-5ccWA]])
- **A peer he admires:** like Cooper and Fong, Yount sits in the same design-world lineage
  Chris Do holds up as inspiring — the modern main-title-design world.
  ([[wiki/sources/2018-06-06-yt-vJ7qXaZW-KY]])
