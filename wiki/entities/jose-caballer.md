# Jose Caballer

> ⚠️ **CONTEXT PAGE — NOT THE SUBJECT.** Jose Caballer is documented here only as context for
> Chris Do's story. Nothing on this page trains `persona/` files. In early Skool/Futur videos
> he is a co-host: **his statements must never be attributed to Chris Do** during ingest.

Designer and brand-strategy facilitator; co-founder (and originator) of [[the-skool]], the
2014–2016 venture that became [[the-futur]].

## Background

- Founded **The Groop** in 2001, a Los Angeles digital agency he led for ~11 years; clients
  included Al Gore, Jamie Oliver, Thomas Keller, Alice Waters, Disney, Nike and Myspace
  [press-corroborated: idnworld.com/creators/JoseCaballer; creativelive.com/instructor/jose-caballer].
- Creator of the **CORE** framework (rapid brand-strategy discovery/facilitation), distilled
  from The Groop's discovery process; released as "CORE Discovery 1.0" in 2015; CORE kits later
  became top sellers in The Futur's shop [self-reported: josecaballer.medium.com, "Core
  Discovery is Back", May 2026].
- Taught UX process courses on CreativeLive ("Fast and Effective UX Design")
  [creativelive.com/class/fast-and-effective-ux-design-learn-the-process-jose-callaber].

## The Skool / Futur era (2014–2016)

- The Skool "was the brainchild of Jose Caballer"; he and Chris Do partnered in 2014 to produce
  videos, events and training materials toward an online design school [press: Motionographer,
  "The future of The Futur", 2017-09-28, motionographer.com/2017/09/28/the-future-of-the-futur/].
- Co-hosted the early YouTube content, including the channel's oldest surviving uploads
  (Aug 2014, e.g. "How To Ask for the Sale Pt 1") — see [[thefutur-youtube]].
- His signature on-camera contribution: whiteboard CORE workshops (brand attributes,
  stakeholder alignment exercises) opposite Chris Do's sales/pricing material.

## The 2014 "board of directors" process conflict (per Chris Do, self-reported)

- While the two were building the company together (Jose as CEO; Chris's own title
  uncertain), Jose pushed Silicon-Valley-style process — stand-up meetings, Agile/Scrum —
  that Chris found alien and unproductive. Chris proposed a one-week test running it Jose's
  way, measured against a baseline: Chris says he individually finished his own week's tasks
  in a single day, while collectively, after a week of meetings, "no one finished anything."
  When Jose then proposed formalizing a board of directors and writing a business plan,
  Chris resisted — this episode (told from Chris's side only) is one thread in the "differing
  ideas about content, direction, and leadership styles" split described below.
  [self-reported, Chris Do's account only — Jose's side is not represented here]
  ([[../sources/2023-10-29-yt-m4OB_5wyWa8]])
- **His own ADHD-adjacent coping rituals** (per Chris's account): wearing headphones playing
  loud metal music for sensory deprivation/focus, taping Post-it notes to the sides of his
  glasses as physical blinders, and setting a recurring 15-minute alarm listing his day's
  three priority tasks — described by Chris as deliberate, impressive self-regulation
  systems, not eccentricity. ([[../sources/2023-10-29-yt-m4OB_5wyWa8]])

## Departure (2016)

- Split with Chris Do over "differing ideas about content, direction, and leadership styles"
  (Chris Do's words); described by both sides as an amicable separation to preserve the
  friendship; the company then rebranded as The Futur [press: Motionographer 2017].
- He retained CORE and continued as an independent educator/facilitator; The Futur's site kept
  a profile page for him (thefutur.com/people/jose-caballer, now largely empty).

## Current whereabouts (as of 2026)

- LinkedIn lists him at "**The Systm**" (linkedin.com/in/josecaballer) [self-reported].
- Running CORE Discovery cohorts ("MyCORE" cohorts 2025–26); relaunched the program publicly in
  a May 2026 Medium post, positioning CORE for the AI era ("making designers leaders, not just
  practitioners") [self-reported: josecaballer.medium.com/core-discovery-is-back-d9b56c2e3727].
