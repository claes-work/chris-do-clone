# Karin Fong

> ⚠️ **CONTEXT — NOT PERSONA TRAINING DATA.** Karin Fong is a peer / influence in
> Chris Do's design world, **not the subject of this clone**. Nothing on this page
> trains the Chris Do persona. Her design philosophy, process, and opinions are recorded
> here as **hers** (context for understanding Chris Do's lineage and network). Only
> Chris Do's own words train the persona.

Emmy-winning **main-title and motion designer**, a founding member / creative director at
**Imaginary Forces**, and AIGA 2018 medalist. Chris Do interviewed her on The Futur; she
is a peer whose title-design career he admires, and the two share a formative connection
through Kyle Cooper and R/GA. Source: [[wiki/sources/2018-05-19-yt-D-i9yPcEW28]].

## Who she is

- **Main-title / motion designer**, founding member of **Imaginary Forces**; her title
  work includes Boardwalk Empire, Black Sails, God of War, Counterpart, and Lost in Space.
  ([[wiki/sources/2018-05-19-yt-D-i9yPcEW28]])
- **Worked under Kyle Cooper at R/GA (Robert Greenberg Associates)** and Imaginary Forces —
  the same shop and mentor through which Chris Do first encountered title design (see
  connection below). ([[wiki/sources/2018-05-19-yt-D-i9yPcEW28]])
- **Background:** childhood super-8 cut-paper films; a Yale thesis "animated alphabet book"
  that professor **Michael Rock** called "cinematic"; a formative early stint at
  **WGBH Boston** (animating *Where in the World Is Carmen Sandiego?* under design VP
  **Chris Pullman**) before years under Kyle Cooper. ([[wiki/sources/2018-05-19-yt-D-i9yPcEW28]])
- **Heroes / influences she names:** Michael Rock (Yale professor), Chris Pullman (WGBH
  design VP), and Joan Ganz Cooney (Sesame Street founder). ([[wiki/sources/2018-05-19-yt-D-i9yPcEW28]])

## Her design philosophy (HERS — context, not persona)

- **Title-design process:** immerse fully in the subject matter (script, story, period,
  characters, brand history/archives), then distill it to **one central metaphor** and grow
  the whole sequence out from that single "nugget." Examples she walks through: Boardwalk
  Empire (Nucky Thompson as the eye of the storm; liquor bottles washing ashore = Prohibition
  as destiny), God of War: Ascension (ashes of his wife and daughter coating him), and
  Counterpart (a split Go game for parallel worlds).
  ([[wiki/sources/2018-05-19-yt-D-i9yPcEW28]])
- **Uniqueness test — "can it run in front of another show?":** a strong main title truly
  describes *this* show and is *unique* to it. "If this main title can run in front of a
  different show, then it's probably not specific enough" (Boardwalk Empire couldn't front
  Scarface). ([[wiki/sources/2018-05-19-yt-D-i9yPcEW28]])
- **Three traits of a good title:** (1) appropriateness to the material; (2) a clever
  metaphor / aha "surprise" moment; (3) craftsmanship down to the typography. Favorites
  cited: Dexter, Catch Me If You Can, Mad Men, Game of Thrones.
  ([[wiki/sources/2018-05-19-yt-D-i9yPcEW28]])
- **"Editing is the great unsung art"** — whether cutting film or cutting an idea down; the
  discipline and courage to hold to one central theme rather than including everything (Game
  of Thrones' board-game map; Mad Men's falling man). "It can't be everything all the time."
  ([[wiki/sources/2018-05-19-yt-D-i9yPcEW28]])
- **Constraints breed creativity** — invent your own deadlines; learn a tool by doing under
  a deadline. (She caveats an urban-myth-flavored belief that athletes get better grades
  in-season because of the rigorous schedule.) This theme is one Chris Do echoes in his own
  PSA in the same episode. ([[wiki/sources/2018-05-19-yt-D-i9yPcEW28]])

## Connection to Chris Do

- **Shared mentor and studio:** both Chris Do and Karin Fong **worked under Kyle Cooper at
  R/GA (LA)**. Chris was brought into the old Cat and Fiddle building as a freshly-graduated
  designer (~late 1990s) to work with Cooper — his first exposure to title design — and met
  Karin there ~20 years before this interview. ([[wiki/sources/2018-05-19-yt-D-i9yPcEW28]])
- **He interviewed her on The Futur** (live-stream episode, 2018-05-19), framing her as a
  "main title design legend." ([[wiki/sources/2018-05-19-yt-D-i9yPcEW28]])
- **A peer he admires:** she sits in the same design-world lineage as Chris Do — a
  fellow designer whose craft and career he holds up as inspiring.
  ([[wiki/sources/2018-05-19-yt-D-i9yPcEW28]])
