# Kier (Keir) McLaren

> ⚠️ **CONTEXT PAGE — NOT THE SUBJECT.** Kier McLaren is documented here only as context for
> Chris Do's story and for **speaker attribution**. He is Chris Do's longtime business
> consultant/coach — a distinct person whose teaching, biography, and worldview are his own.
> **Nothing on this page trains the `persona/` files.** In the many videos he dominates (the
> 2015 AMA, the 2018 MASTERMIND series Eps 1–10), his statements must **never** be attributed
> to Chris Do or flow into `persona/voice.md` or `persona/beliefs.md`. Only Chris Do's own
> attributed words train the persona. Almost everything below is Kier's own **[self-reported]**
> on-camera account — treat it accordingly.
>
> **Spelling note:** his name is rendered variously as **Kier**, **Keir**, and (in noisy
> auto-captions) "Care"/"Cristo." Title cards on The Futur credit **Keir McLaren**; the
> ledger/series convention uses **Kier McLaren**. Both refer to the same person.

## Who he is

Business consultant and coach to entrepreneurs, currently working heavily with people in the
creative and entertainment businesses (motion graphics, music, documentary); has also run a
small ad agency "back east" [self-reported: [[wiki/sources/2015-09-15-yt-F06ePJZEmK0]]]. He
prefers the label **"consultant"** to "coach" — Chris Do calls him "coach, mentor and friend,"
but notes Kier's own preference [[wiki/sources/2015-09-15-yt-F06ePJZEmK0]]. He is an author who
references "my book" on camera and describes ~20 years spent motivating people and helping them
find their journey [self-reported: [[wiki/sources/2018-05-09-yt-ktGYhw7gmMY]],
[[wiki/sources/2018-05-23-yt-Jbiyh2WbWPs]]].

## Relationship to Chris Do & Blind

- Chris Do has worked with Kier as a consultant for **over 10 years** (as stated in 2015); Kier
  frames it as roughly the last **15 years** working for "people at [Chris Do / Blind] and others
  in the creative businesses" — i.e. a **~10–15 year** coaching relationship [self-reported:
  [[wiki/sources/2015-09-15-yt-F06ePJZEmK0]], [[wiki/sources/2018-02-26-yt-J_m9-4rgpiE]]].
- Chris found him after a friend mentioned a consultant who had grown his business ~200% in
  revenue [self-reported: [[wiki/sources/2015-09-15-yt-F06ePJZEmK0]]].
- **Roughly doubled Blind's revenue:** Chris reports the company was stuck at a "glass ceiling"
  around **$2.4M**; in the first year working with Kier it went to **~$4M** and "never looked
  back." In their very first ~20–30 minute meeting Kier saved Chris from a "fatal" decision that
  "probably saved me hundreds of thousands of dollars" [self-reported (Chris Do's account):
  [[wiki/sources/2015-09-15-yt-F06ePJZEmK0]]].
- Kier characterizes Chris as someone uninterested in "how the other guy does it" — only in "the
  best way to do it," and open to a better way when one exists [self-reported (Kier on Chris):
  [[wiki/sources/2018-04-24-yt-gB7WZQNwsdE]]]. He also co-wrote an action-adventure story with
  Chris, whose edits improved a later draft [self-reported:
  [[wiki/sources/2018-05-26-yt-ittd4gieExk]]].

## Self-reported biography

All markers below are **[self-reported]** by Kier on camera; treat as his own narrative, not
registry-verified.

- **Origins:** Grew up in **Pittsburgh / western Pennsylvania**; played **high-school football**
  there (a contemporary of Joe Namath / Joe Montana as regional references) and later pursued
  **Division I college football** — "good but hardly great," turning down a smaller school's
  star-role offer to chase Division I because he "had to know how good I am"
  [[wiki/sources/2018-02-26-yt-J_m9-4rgpiE]], [[wiki/sources/2018-03-13-yt-v-dQZ7URtVc]],
  [[wiki/sources/2018-05-23-yt-Jbiyh2WbWPs]]]. Attended "a couple of colleges"; went "to learn,"
  not for a degree; a "checkered" academic record [[wiki/sources/2018-03-13-yt-v-dQZ7URtVc]]].
- **Early career / social work:** Started as a **social worker** on inner-city playgrounds;
  worked for the Boys Club; ran day camps for the Ohio government; later a **Job Corps manager in
  Pittsburgh** [[wiki/sources/2018-02-26-yt-J_m9-4rgpiE]], [[wiki/sources/2018-03-13-yt-v-dQZ7URtVc]]].
  Credits problem-solving skill to working "in the ghetto," "where everybody's life is on the
  line" [[wiki/sources/2018-02-26-yt-J_m9-4rgpiE]]].
- **Radio:** Moved to **New England** and worked in **radio** as an FM/AM DJ — including a
  country-music show he "knew nothing about" — before leaving because radio paid poorly
  [[wiki/sources/2018-02-26-yt-J_m9-4rgpiE]], [[wiki/sources/2018-05-26-yt-ittd4gieExk]]]. Once
  decided he would photocopy and re-date an expired FCC third-class endorsed license to land an
  announcer job if his renewal didn't arrive in time (the valid license arrived two hours before
  his first shift) [[wiki/sources/2018-03-13-yt-v-dQZ7URtVc]]].
- **Advertising:** Ran a small **ad agency** ("back east"); working with many small clients is
  where he says he learned to run a business — and from there became a consultant
  [[wiki/sources/2015-09-15-yt-F06ePJZEmK0]], [[wiki/sources/2018-02-26-yt-J_m9-4rgpiE]],
  [[wiki/sources/2018-05-27-yt-gy5KZlLRl2c]]]. Also worked at a family-run sporting-goods /
  firearms store (owner "Meyer") and for a company renting 35mm film cameras to the film/ad/TV
  industry [[wiki/sources/2018-03-10-yt-x8aMA-kbldo]]].
- **Writing & screenwriting:** A lifelong writer — first poem published in his hometown newspaper
  at ~14; later **published in Harper's Magazine**; wrote "my book"
  [[wiki/sources/2018-02-26-yt-J_m9-4rgpiE]], [[wiki/sources/2018-05-09-yt-ktGYhw7gmMY]],
  [[wiki/sources/2018-05-23-yt-Jbiyh2WbWPs]]]. Pursued **screenwriting** (classes in Boston, then
  UCLA in Los Angeles); with a writing partner described as a **"fairly well-known actor,"
  optioned a screenplay** (never made) that made "a little bit of money" — and, he says, "cost me
  a marriage" [[wiki/sources/2018-02-26-yt-J_m9-4rgpiE]], [[wiki/sources/2018-05-23-yt-Jbiyh2WbWPs]]].
  Applied to ten law schools despite long odds; accepted at the Detroit School of Law, declined
  for personal reasons [[wiki/sources/2018-03-13-yt-v-dQZ7URtVc]]].
- **Personal low point & later life:** In his **40s**, **broke, sitting on a curb in Burbank** in
  100-degree heat with a dead car battery he couldn't afford, after optioning the screenplay —
  and still "didn't feel okay" [[wiki/sources/2018-05-23-yt-Jbiyh2WbWPs]]]. Reports **two
  divorces** ("two failures") and having "crashed a couple businesses"
  [[wiki/sources/2018-05-27-yt-gy5KZlLRl2c]]]. Later describes a calmer, minimalist life: a small
  ("thousand square foot") **house near the beach**, and having lived in a small mountain town in
  **New Hampshire** [[wiki/sources/2018-05-13-yt-JNTqHJQp1QU]], [[wiki/sources/2018-05-27-yt-gy5KZlLRl2c]]].
  References a daughter and grandson (generically; no names disclosed)
  [[wiki/sources/2018-05-23-yt-Jbiyh2WbWPs]]].

## Coaching worldview

Kier's own frameworks (CONTEXT — not Chris Do's beliefs):

- **Name a target income.** Most people never define exactly how much money they need or what
  life they want; he coaches clients to name a specific number and build the business/life to
  meet it [[wiki/sources/2018-05-13-yt-JNTqHJQp1QU]]].
- **"You get the world you see."** The world is neutral; thinking positively (and surrounding
  yourself with positive people) changes the world you perceive
  [[wiki/sources/2018-05-13-yt-JNTqHJQp1QU]]].
- **External validation is empty.** Chasing fame/recognition never satisfies; self-worth must be
  sourced internally — the "actors vs. stars" distinction (loving the craft vs. needing the
  recognition) [[wiki/sources/2018-05-23-yt-Jbiyh2WbWPs]]].
- **Outsiders break paradigms.** A paradigm is a profession's "rules of the road"; breakthroughs
  come from people new to a field, unbound by its assumptions (Salk, Bezos as examples)
  [[wiki/sources/2018-05-26-yt-ittd4gieExk]]].
- **Failure → humility.** "You only learn from failure"; what it teaches is that you don't have
  all the answers and aren't as smart as you think
  [[wiki/sources/2018-05-27-yt-gy5KZlLRl2c]]].
- **Luck & serendipity.** Attributes much of his success to luck and "a spirit in the universe";
  "some problems the universe solves for you" if you stay calm and receptive
  [[wiki/sources/2018-05-27-yt-gy5KZlLRl2c]]].
- **12-step incremental change.** A "one day at a time" philosophy — small daily improvement
  compounding over ~six months; change threatens the people around you (Calder-mobile metaphor),
  and ~80% of people never change [[wiki/sources/2018-05-27-yt-gy5KZlLRl2c]],
  [[wiki/sources/2018-05-13-yt-JNTqHJQp1QU]]].
- **"Everything I teach I learned from someone else."** He is explicit that his method is
  borrowed, joking that what he learns from one client at 11 o'clock he'll sell to the next at
  1:00 [[wiki/sources/2018-02-26-yt-J_m9-4rgpiE]]].

## Toolkit

- **Inner-child dominant/non-dominant-hand exercise:** write a question to your younger self with
  your dominant hand, then answer with the opposite hand without thinking, to surface buried
  beliefs and fears [[wiki/sources/2018-05-09-yt-ktGYhw7gmMY]]].
- **Hero's-journey framing:** uses *The Wizard of Oz* and Joseph Campbell's hero's journey — the
  characters already possessed what they sought ("everything you need you already have"); paired
  with a diagnostic that a disproportionate emotional reaction signals something big inside you
  [[wiki/sources/2018-05-09-yt-ktGYhw7gmMY]]].
