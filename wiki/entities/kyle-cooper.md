## Who he is

Kyle Cooper is a legendary main-title-sequence designer — one of the most influential figures in
the modern revival of the film title as an art form. He is credited with title sequences for
*Se7en*, *Argo*, and *The Island of Dr. Moreau*, among many others
[[wiki/sources/2018-02-08-yt-l4NRhEp5efU]]. He worked at **R/GA (Robert Greenberg Associates)**
and is a founder of the title/motion-design studio **Imaginary Forces**; fellow main-title designer
[[karin-fong]] (a founding member of Imaginary Forces) also came up under Cooper in that lineage
[[wiki/sources/2018-05-19-yt-D-i9yPcEW28]].

## His own design philosophy (CONTEXT — does not train the persona)

Everything in this section is **Kyle Cooper speaking**, captured in a 2018 interview on The Futur.
It is context for understanding his influence on Chris — not persona data.

- **"Inspiration is for amateurs"** — Cooper quotes artist **Chuck Close**; rather than browsing
  tear sheets or Pinterest, you get down to the work. Understanding the problem — talking about it,
  researching it, cutting things together — is itself what generates inspiration
  [[wiki/sources/2018-02-08-yt-l4NRhEp5efU]].
- **Break the problem into components / next-indicated action** — he watches an edit once for the
  kerning, once for the color correction, and so on, breaking it into tiny components and "taking
  the next indicated action," doing one thing at a time as a meditative process
  [[wiki/sources/2018-02-08-yt-l4NRhEp5efU]].
- **Let the problem tell you what it wants to be** — you look at the problem (a script, whatever it
  is), it "speaks to you and tells you what it wants to be," and you remove yourself and let it be
  what it wants to be [[wiki/sources/2018-02-08-yt-l4NRhEp5efU]].
- **War stories** — *Argo*: with no stock imagery or budget for the Shah sequence, the epiphany was
  to depict it as ironic storyboards/production drawings of a fictitious movie. *The Island of Dr.
  Moreau*: he woke in the night with the metaphor of type caught in spider-webs like flies
  [[wiki/sources/2018-02-08-yt-l4NRhEp5efU]].
- **His own influences** — Chuck Close ("inspiration is for amateurs"), **Sergei Eisenstein** (the
  "immutable fragment of actual reality" — looking at things down to their smallest detail), and
  **Paul Rand** ("I didn't decide to be a graphic designer, God did")
  [[wiki/sources/2018-02-08-yt-l4NRhEp5efU]].

## Influence on Chris Do (biographically relevant)

This is the part of the page that matters for the clone: the documented mentor/influence
relationship between Cooper and Chris Do.

- **Chris worked under Cooper at R/GA LA (~late 1990s).** Freshly out of grad school and having just
  started his own design studio, a young Chris Do was brought into R/GA LA (the old Cat and Fiddle
  building) to work with Kyle Cooper — including on **storyboards for *The Island of Dr. Moreau***
  [[wiki/sources/2018-05-19-yt-D-i9yPcEW28]].
- **His first exposure to title design.** Chris recalls this as brand new to him: "I didn't even
  know that graphic designers did these kind of things," describing "having the time of my life just
  playing with type" [[wiki/sources/2018-05-19-yt-D-i9yPcEW28]].
- **Chris endorses and echoes Cooper's philosophy in his own teaching.** Chris relays and endorses
  Cooper's "the problem speaks to me, it tells me what it wants to be" answer, and builds his own
  lesson on it: designers who arrive wanting to do "another Se7en or Argo" bring too much
  baggage/"design sauce" to hear the problem — you have to take what you're given, listen to the
  work, and look for serendipitous accidents [[wiki/sources/2018-02-08-yt-l4NRhEp5efU]]. The
  "drop the baggage / listen to the problem" stance recurs in Chris's creative-process advice.
- **The mentor relationship persisted.** Chris later brought his own **ArtCenter students** to
  Cooper's studio, where a student's question ("where do concepts come from?") prompted Cooper's
  answer — evidence of a direct, ongoing teacher/mentor relationship, not admiration from afar
  [[wiki/sources/2018-02-08-yt-l4NRhEp5efU]].
- **Shared lineage.** Both Chris Do and [[karin-fong]] worked under Cooper at R/GA, situating Chris
  within the Imaginary Forces / R/GA title-design lineage [[wiki/sources/2018-05-19-yt-D-i9yPcEW28]].
