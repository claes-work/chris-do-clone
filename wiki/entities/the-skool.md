# The Skool (2014–2016)

The online design-education venture Chris Do co-ran with [[jose-caballer]] from 2014 until
their 2016 split — the direct predecessor of [[the-futur]].

> ⚠️ **DISAMBIGUATION:** The Skool has NOTHING to do with **Skool.com**, the community
> platform founded by Sam Ovens (later associated with Alex Hormozi). Same word, unrelated
> ventures. Any post-2019 "Skool" reference in sources almost certainly means Skool.com,
> not this venture.

## What it was

- Founded **2014** as a partnership: "The Futur was originally called the Skool and was the
  brainchild of Jose Caballer"; per Chris Do (verbatim): "Jose and I partnered to produce
  videos, events, and training materials in 2014 in the hopes of building an online school to
  teach design" [press: Motionographer, "The future of The Futur", 2017-09-28,
  motionographer.com/2017/09/28/the-future-of-the-futur/].
- Output: YouTube video series (business of design, sales, [[jose-caballer|CORE]]-style
  workshops), live events, and training materials [press: Motionographer 2017].
- The YouTube channel created for it is today's Futur channel: the legacy username URL
  **youtube.com/user/TheSkoolRocks** still resolves to the channel (checked 2026-07-14), and
  the oldest uploads date to August 2014 ("How To Ask for the Sale Pt 1", 2014-08-20, with
  Jose Caballer) — see [[thefutur-youtube]].
- The Skool era is where Chris Do learned YouTube mechanics and developed his on-camera
  presence [press-corroborated: millo.co/chris-do-interview and other origin-story interviews].

## Who ran it

- **Jose Caballer** — originator ("brainchild"), co-host, brought the CORE workshop
  methodology from his agency The Groop [press: Motionographer 2017; josecaballer.medium.com].
- **Chris Do** — co-host and partner, running [[blind]] in parallel.

## Money (self-reported)

- Year one (2014): grossed ~$15,000; 2015: ~$42,000 [self-reported:
  millo.co/chris-do-interview]. A hobby-scale side project next to Blind's ~$5M/year.

## The split and rebrand (2016)

- Per Chris Do (verbatim): "Ultimately, Jose and I had differing ideas about content,
  direction, and leadership styles." To preserve the friendship "we both needed to do our own
  thing"; friends helped negotiate an amicable separation; "We rebranded as the Futur and have
  been charging forward ever since" [press: Motionographer 2017].
- THE FUTUR, LLC was filed with the California SOS on **07/25/2016** [registry-verified,
  bizfileonline.sos.ca.gov, checked 2026-07-14] — consistent with a mid-2016 split/rebrand.
- Caballer kept his CORE framework and continued independently; CORE kits were later sold
  through The Futur's shop [self-reported: josecaballer.medium.com, "Core Discovery is Back",
  May 2026].
- No separate "The Skool" California entity was found; a definitive SOS negative search could
  not be completed (search UI became unresponsive, 2026-07-14). It may have operated as a DBA
  or under one of the partners' existing entities [unverified].
