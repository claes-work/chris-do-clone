# The Futur — main YouTube channel

The primary public corpus for this clone. Channel ID **UC-b3c7kxa5vU-bnmaROgvog**, handle
**@thefutur**; legacy URLs **youtube.com/user/TheSkoolRocks** (the original Skool-era username,
still resolving to this channel, checked 2026-07-14) and **youtube.com/c/thefuturishere**.

## Size (yt-dlp enumeration, 2026-07-14)

- **2,810,000 subscribers**
- **1,177 long-form videos** (431 ≥20 min, 188 10–20 min, 558 <10 min; ≈475 h)
- **737 shorts**; **235 streams** (≈320 h)
- Oldest uploads: Aug/Sep 2014 ("How To Ask for the Sale Pt 1", 2014-08-20, with
  [[jose-caballer]])

## History and eras

1. **The Skool / Caballer era (2014–2016).** Channel launched Aug 2014 under the
   [[the-skool]] brand (username "TheSkoolRocks"). Co-hosted whiteboard workshops (Caballer's
   CORE exercises) and sales/pricing talks (Chris Do). ATTRIBUTION WARNING: two hosts on
   screen — per-speaker attribution required before anything trains the persona.
2. **Rebrand + instructor era (2016–~2019).** Renamed The Futur after the 2016 split
   [[the-futur]]. Mix of Chris-hosted business content and tutorials/role-plays by non-Chris
   instructors — Matthew Encina, Ben Burns, Greg Gunn, Melinda Livsey (see
   [[futur-instructors]]); ~25 videos explicitly name them, more unnamed pre-2019 tutorials
   [phase-0 enumeration]. Much pure-tutorial content later moved to the separate Futur Academy
   channel (UCqHuGF2axS8Yf89r1tUVS_A, started 2019-01-23).
3. **Chris-hosted era (post-2017 → present).** Chris Do becomes the dominant on-camera voice:
   client-call role-plays, live brand-strategy consultations, pricing/negotiation
   breakdowns, podcast episodes, livestreams. Highest persona value.

Milestones: **1M subscribers reached Dec 2020** — celebration video "1 Million YouTube
Subscribers (Community Celebration)" uploaded 2020-12-16 (youtube.com/watch?v=I_SswL6QSUI,
upload date via yt-dlp 2026-07-14). 2.81M by 2026-07-14.

Monetization stance: early on the channel deliberately avoided AdSense to prioritize audience
growth [self-reported: Creator Science #111, 2022-08-02, podcast.creatorscience.com/chris-do/].
