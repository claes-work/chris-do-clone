# Defining the New Agency Model w/ Sue Parker, CEO Frank Creative

## Summary

Early **The Skool Live** episode (Skool era, before the "The Futur" rebrand),
co-hosted by **Chris Do** ("Christo") and **Jose Caballer**, with **guest Sue Parker**,
CEO and co-founder of **Frank Creative** (a "work group"/collective in Portland,
Oregon). The nominal topic is "design trends in 2015," but the conversation is really a
case study of Parker's **alternative agency model**: a small management/production core
plus a pool of freelance specialists assembled per project.

Jose drives segment one (the collective model and brand-strategy process); Chris takes
segment two, framing himself as a designer trying to move up into brand/strategy work
and pressing Parker for concrete business numbers (revenue, margins, metrics).

**Attribution note:** Sue Parker is the GUEST — her frameworks, agency model, and
sector expertise are **context, not persona training data**. Only Chris Do's own
statements feed the persona; Jose's and Sue's do not. Some fast back-and-forth in the
transcript has ambiguous speaker labels; those are flagged `attribution: uncertain`.

## Key claims

### Sue Parker (GUEST — context only, not persona)
- Frank Creative began ~1999–2000 as a **collective / "work group"** rather than a
  traditional agency: a small core plus best-fit freelancers assembled per project
  ("alchemies based on the client's needs"). (paraphrase, 2015-01-28)
- Winning bigger projects requires a **"big dig"** — sitting down with all stakeholders
  to surface their "wishes, dreams and desires" before proposing. (paraphrase)
- Her branding process: find the brand's **soul**, embrace its **position**, know its
  **consumers**, then audit whether products/packaging live up to that soul.
  (paraphrase)
- Cited work includes **Timberland** (new lines — the Ring collective, Abington Shoe
  Company) and clients across athletic / outdoor / action-sports (Adidas, Reebok, Vans,
  Deckers, Teva, Polartec). You must "sell into the organization first" (reps, buyers,
  employees) before selling to the end consumer. (paraphrase)
- She positions the model as a **"strategic hit team"**: full-service capability without
  full-service overhead, delivered at "about half the cost," because there are no
  full-time creatives — ~10 consistent management/production staff plus hired artists.
  (paraphrase)
- **Effectiveness = sales, not awards.** Ad agencies measure success by awards they give
  each other; if the client's sales go up, that is effective communication. Social media
  now gives measurable reach/impact that finance teams like. (paraphrase)
- Business numbers (Parker volunteered): ~**$1.5–2M annual billings**, held steady
  through the "creative recession" but by doing **more work at the same/lower pricing**;
  project sizes shifted from a few ~$500K projects/year to many $50K–$200K projects.
  Margins: service is "horrific" (every brand rebuilt from scratch); product can hit
  50–60 points; overall roughly **25–35%**. She runs on a deliberate "maintenance"
  schedule for work-life balance — "wealthy vs rich." (paraphrase; self-reported)
- **"Money is respect"** pricing stance and **pro bono** philosophy (Dharma): pursue
  aligned clients; give back to community (Friends of Trees, Street Roots homelessness
  work); would never charge homeless people. (paraphrase)

### Chris Do (persona-eligible)
- Self-positions as a designer **~12+ years behind** Parker, "embarking on the whole
  brand strategic aspect of my business" — a dated marker of Chris in early 2015
  transitioning from design execution toward brand/strategy. (paraphrase, 2015-01-28)
- Praises the reframe that **awards/awareness/"how cool it looks" have no impact on the
  client's bottom line**; designers should reframe toward measurable results. (paraphrase)
- Notes designers are often **uncomfortable being measured by sales** and need to get
  comfortable with it. (paraphrase; `attribution: uncertain` — rapid crosstalk with Jose/Sue)
- In closing, frames the takeaway: to graduate from designer you must talk **strategy,
  branding, marketing** and focus on tangible results, "because ultimately clients are
  not going to pay you to help them help you win awards for yourself." (paraphrase)
- Closing paraphrase of Sue on **packaging yourself**: people want to work with people
  like them, and graphic designers are "really good at putting together a nice package,"
  so "you need to elevate your own packaging." (paraphrase; `attribution: uncertain` —
  could be Jose)
