# Branding: Brand Messaging W Hotel (2015-07-21)

/ [[../../SUBJECT|SUBJECT]] · [[../../index|index]] /

A short "The Process"-era vlog. Chris Do films alone while on vacation at the W Hotel
in Taipei, Taiwan, using the hotel as a live case study in brand messaging, brand
voice, and consistent identity application. Co-host [[../entities/jose-caballer|Jose Caballer]]
is absent (referenced only via a scripted intro bit).

## Summary

Chris Do praises the W Hotel Taipei as an example of a fully thought-out brand
experience and zooms in specifically on **brand messaging** — the small, playful,
on-brand copy the hotel places on ordinary amenities. He notes that branding is "a hot
topic within graphic design" but reiterates the show's recurring thesis: branding is
not the logo or the identity, it's the **entire experience** and how you treat
customers. He gives examples of the hotel's whimsical, slightly cheeky messaging (a
spare toilet-paper roll labeled "backup plan"; a comb "for those that like a kinky or
straight"; a makeup remover that says "take it off"), noting the copy carries a light
sexual innuendo that stays in the spirit of the brand. He then covers the identity
system: the strong "W" logomark used cleverly (right-side-up = women's restroom,
upside-down = men's) and a consistent diagonal graphic bias applied across monitors,
menus, and fitness-club signage. His takeaway: the more ways you can apply a logo and a
visual motif consistently across the whole system, the stronger the brand.

## Key claims

- **[Chris, 2015-07-21, paraphrase]** Branding is not about the logo and the identity —
  it is about the entire experience and how you treat your customers. (Recurring "The
  Process" thesis, restated here.) See [[../topics/branding/branding|branding]].
- **[Chris, 2015-07-21, paraphrase]** Brand messaging means treating every ordinary
  touchpoint (toilet paper, toothbrush, makeup remover, sewing kit, comb) as an
  opportunity to "candle" the customer — i.e. to place on-brand copy that reinforces the
  brand's personality.
- **[Chris, 2015-07-21, paraphrase]** Good brand copy has a consistent voice/tone: here,
  whimsical, playful, and lightly sexual — and every message stays "in the spirit of the
  brand" rather than being generic.
- **[Chris, 2015-07-21, paraphrase]** A strong identity comes from a versatile logomark:
  the W is reused as a bathroom sign — right-side-up it reads "women," flipped upside
  down it reads "men." Reusing one designed mark in more ways strengthens the brand.
- **[Chris, 2015-07-21, paraphrase]** A brand should carry a consistent visual motif; the
  W's strong diagonal bias is applied consistently across lobby monitors (animated
  graphics on a diagonal), menus, and the fitness club.
- **[Chris, 2015-07-21, paraphrase]** General principle: the more consistently a logo and
  its graphic system are applied throughout the entire experience, the stronger the brand
  becomes.
