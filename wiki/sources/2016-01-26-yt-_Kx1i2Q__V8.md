# Books To Read to Learn UX (The Futur AMA)

## Summary

A short "The Process"-era (2016) AMA on UX design, framed as answers to
audience questions. The narration is **not Chris Do** — the speaker refers to
Chris in the third person ("Chris answered..., I'm going to recommend...") and
the topic (UX / customer journeys) is Jose Caballer's area within the early
Futur/Skillshare content. Best inference: **Jose Caballer is the speaker**

Because the persona-relevant content here is thin and secondhand, only one book

## Key claims

- [2016-01-26] (speaker, likely Jose) Anyone can become a UX designer even if
  they are not a "visual designer." UI = designing the interface elements
  (buttons, color, backgrounds, on-screen states); UX = user experience, i.e.
  how a user gets from point A to point B, gets what they need, and how the
  business goals are met. UX is about thinking through the process, not
  necessarily drawing every screen. (paraphrase)
- [2016-01-26] (speaker) To do UX you must learn to: define customers, build
  customer stories / customer journeys, and translate those journeys into
  discrete screen "states." Existing tools let you do this without being a
  visual designer, but you must become very client-facing and collaborative.
  (paraphrase)
- [2016-01-26] (speaker) A four-step method to define a customer: (1) identify
  the customer type (e.g. "the busy executive"); (2) find their story /
  demographics (age, gender, location, job); (3) figure out what they do and
  what challenges they face doing it; (4) determine what they need to overcome
  those challenges. (paraphrase)
- [2016-01-26] (**attributed to Chris Do**, secondhand via speaker) Chris's book
  answer was *Win Without Pitching* (by Blair Enns). Reported by the speaker, not
  said by Chris on camera. (paraphrase; attribution secondhand)
- [2016-01-26] (speaker) He personally likes *Rework* by Jason Fried
  ("Jason freed/fried"). (paraphrase)
- [2016-01-26] (speaker) His own recommendation: *Business Model Generation* by
  Strategyzer (Alexander Osterwalder et al.) — a great, visual book for thinking
  through a business. Ties to his reframe: "I'm designing a website — no you're
  not, you're designing a business for somebody." (paraphrase)
- [2016-01-26] (speaker) On a more personal tip, he recommends *The Power of Now*
  by Eckhart Tolle ("edart toi") for designers who feel worried/anxious about the
  future or about being liked / doing a good job. Calls it "a little fluffy" but
  worthwhile. (paraphrase)
- [2016-01-26] (speaker) Closing thesis: UI and UX have different tools and
  techniques, but fundamentally it is about solving a **business problem** — the
  app is secondary. Many people can build and design apps; few can actually build
  and design businesses. (paraphrase)
