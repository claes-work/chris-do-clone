# Online Marketing: Conversion Optimization Funnel CRO

## Summary

Episode of **"The Process"** (2016 era). This is a **guest-led** technical marketing
session. **Alan Martinez** (a.k.a. "Al" / "Allen") of **Noble Digital** is the invited
conversion-rate-optimization (CRO) expert and drives almost all the technical content:
sales funnels, cost-per-acquisition (CPA), personas from real data, and pricing a
funnel top-to-bottom. **Jose ("Hose")** is the co-host and here plays the *client* whose
business (thefutur's own products: `core 360`, modules, subscriptions, `AIB`, monthly
membership) is being funnel-mapped live. **Chris Do** hosts and interjects business /
pricing framing but does **not** lead the CRO material — this is outside his usual
teaching lane.

Because the transcript has **no speaker labels** and three voices overlap (Chris, Jose,
Alan), most attribution is inferred from context and some lines are `attribution:
uncertain`. Only clearly Chris-Do-attributed statements are marked as persona voice;
the bulk of the CRO substance is **CONTEXT** (Alan / Jose).

## Key claims (dated 2016-05-14, paraphrased unless quoted)

- **[Alan — CONTEXT]** A sales funnel is the journey from top ("never heard of you") to
  interest, to consideration, to purchase, then to delight/referral/sharing. Direct
  marketers work backward from a revenue goal to an ad budget, then drive CPA (cost per
  acquisition) as low as possible.
- **[Alan — CONTEXT]** Ideal is *not* breaking even (spend $2M to make $2M); the whole
  game is spending materially less than you make (e.g. $500K to make $2M). It will never
  be zero.
- **[Jose — CONTEXT]** thefutur's product ladder at the time: `core 360` = $1,200 (most
  expensive); individual modules = $500; annual subscription = $250; `AIB` ≈ $200;
  monthly membership = $25 (lowest paid entry); YouTube = $0 top-of-funnel. Best sellers
  split between `core` and `AIB`. Monthly recurring revenue ≈ $8K, goal to triple to
  $20K by end of 2016/2017.
- **[Jose — CONTEXT]** Biggest traffic driver is YouTube → landing page; audience is
  US, India, then UK; skews late-20s to 40s. YouTube analytics and Shopify data are
  compared manually (no automation).
- **[Alan — TIP]** "Tip number one": tie Shopify analytics to YouTube analytics to see
  patterns automatically instead of manual comparison.
- **[Alan — CONTEXT]** Personas built from limited internal data must be challenged
  against real marketplace data; iterate messaging per segment (e.g. an India-specific
  video or influencer to light up that audience). Illustrated with a pizza-delivery
  anecdote: the actual buyer ("a guy on the sofa" / a couple) differed from the assumed
  persona.
- **[Alan/Chris — CONTEXT]** Worked example of assigning a CPA per funnel stage:
  roughly "spend about half" to acquire a sale (2:1 conversion); averaging the paid
  products gives ≈ $507, rounded to a **$500 CPA budget** to distribute down the funnel.
  Lower stages (lead, trial) get progressively smaller allocations (illustrative
  numbers: ~$200 for a trial-subscription buyer, ~$25 for a lead, ~$1 for awareness).
- **[Jose — CONTEXT]** Total site conversion improved from sub-1% to ≈ 2.5%; ~80 people
  hit the Shopify page on average. Trial→product conversion estimated at ~50% within
  10–20 days (some in 3 days — usually existing business owners who already know the
  brand via the community).
- **[Alan — CONTEXT]** 2–3% is the world average conversion for e-commerce; above that
  is doing well. Each channel (Facebook, YouTube, TV) has its own funnel before the
  website; the website funnel is the most controllable, upper funnel less so ("the art
  of advertising").
- **[Alan — CONTEXT]** While not upside-down / losing money, increase ad budget every
  month until it stops making sense; start at a $500 CPA and drive it down toward $250
  over time by shifting spend to what performs.
- **[Chris Do — persona]** How to productize Alan's expertise: position specialists as a
  team of experts (conversion-funnel strategist, social strategist, brand/top-of-funnel
  strategist) and bring the right one in per engagement.
- **[Chris Do — persona]** Pricing a specialist into an engagement: bring Alan in only
  for the e-commerce/conversion portion (an hour or two on day two of a two-day
  strategy); his rate ~$5–10K, negotiable down to ~$3K for a leaner engagement. Rule of
  thumb: a specialist sub-fee should be about a quarter of the total — e.g. on a $12K+
  strategy engagement, paying out ~$3K is the threshold, because you still must get paid
  for development and time.
- **[Chris Do — persona]** For an e-commerce prospect, Chris raises his own strategy
  rate because he'll bring the specialist in to facilitate that part.
