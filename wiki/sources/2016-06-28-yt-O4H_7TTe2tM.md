# What Do I Need to Learn In Design School?

★ L3-candidate — Chris Do's design-education / lifelong-learning philosophy is a
distinct, substantial core theme (he taught 15+ years at ArtCenter/Otis). Worth
promoting into `wiki/topics/` + `persona/beliefs.md` on the next synthesis pass.

## Summary

Short talk answering a student question: how will the motion (design) industry
change, and what should Art Center students do to adapt? Chris Do declines to give
a prescriptive answer and instead argues that the real skill is meta: staying aware
that change is constant, seeking out many points of view, forming your own opinion,
and — above all — being a lifelong learner who deliberately puts themselves in
uncomfortable situations. He frames himself as one strong-but-personal viewpoint,
not an authority to be copied. He illustrates lifelong learning with a personal
example: two years earlier he threw himself into learning "digital," took low-paying
work to do it, and found it horribly uncomfortable — which to him is the marker that
real learning is happening. He closes with a "you find the answer when you're
actively looking for it" idea (the world seems to line up and hand you answers only
once you're tuned into the problem).

The clip appears to be Chris Do speaking throughout; no distinct co-host speech is
present in the transcript. Attribution is therefore treated as Chris Do.

## Key claims

- [2016-06-28] The first thing to do about industry change is to be aware it is
  happening and not take for granted that whatever you're studying or the path
  you're on is the right one. — Chris Do (paraphrase)
- [2016-06-28] You should seek out multiple sources and a variety of opinions so you
  can form your own; talking to someone like him is just one input. — Chris Do
  (paraphrase)
- [2016-06-28] He positions himself as "just one person with a very strong point of
  view" — not claiming to be right in general, only "right for me"; the listener has
  to find what's right for them. — Chris Do (paraphrase)
- [2016-06-28] When you're tuned into a problem, answers seem to appear magically
  (an article, something someone sends you) — because you're now actively looking;
  before that you "walk around like a dumb person" and don't see the answers.
  — Chris Do (paraphrase)
- [2016-06-28] Don't be complacent and expect someone to just hand you the answer;
  it doesn't work that way. — Chris Do (paraphrase)
- [2016-06-28] You have to be a lifelong ("life") learner, and that's what he is.
  — Chris Do (paraphrase) [self-reported]
- [2016-06-28] The way you know you're a lifelong learner is that you constantly find
  yourself doing things you're extremely uncomfortable with (his examples: he can't
  sing and can't dance, so a dance/singing class means he's learning something new).
  — Chris Do (paraphrase) [self-reported]
- [2016-06-28] ~2014 (two years prior), he deliberately threw himself into learning
  "digital," took on really low-paying work to do it, and it was horrible and
  horribly uncomfortable — but the information only revealed itself because he was
  actively seeking it. — Chris Do (paraphrase) [self-reported]
