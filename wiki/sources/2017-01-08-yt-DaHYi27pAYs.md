## Key claims

_All dated 2017-01-08 (publication). Paraphrase unless quoted. Speaker attribution
uncertain — see Summary._

- **[self-reported, Blind narrator]** Blind pursued a music video for Coldplay; the
  narrator says he "originally reached out to Phil Harvey," Coldplay's creative director,
  and that a person named "Toin" was a champion in helping Blind find the opportunity.
- **[self-reported, Blind narrator]** He was initially reluctant to pitch — he did not
  want to pitch "unless I have a great idea." In the last two days before the treatment
  was due, the concept shifted from a traditional music video toward an **interactive
  experience** — a deliberate stretch into unfamiliar territory.
- **[self-reported, Blind narrator]** Process: ideation started with "really loose
  Post-It note sketches" and became "a really tightly organized story matrix of all the
  outcomes and paths"; the team then built characters, artwork, and backgrounds using a
  mix of **2D, 3D, and cel animation**.
- **[self-reported, Blind narrator]** Narrative concept: a **lost traveler** given
  multiple choices — to keep chasing his long-lost love, or to find his own place in the
  world — with the **viewer choosing his path**, which decides his fate. The branching
  (two-to-three paths per story point) is meant to drive repeat viewing.
- **[context, Blind team]** The team valued having Coldplay come in, sit with them, and
  walk through the process end to end — described as validation of Blind's expertise.
- **[self-reported, Blind narrator]** Reception metrics claimed: social media (Twitter,
  Facebook, Instagram) "buzzing" within a minute of release; ~30 websites/articles
  covering it; **~600,000 views in the first week** and continuing to grow.
- **[self-reported / studio positioning]** Blind framing: "we're not afraid of taking on
  daunting challenges" — making an interactive video with a real storyline delivered on a
  fixed timeframe is the kind of challenge the studio embraces.
- **[context, Coldplay-side speaker]** A client/band voice credits Blind's "real great
  passion" and "desire to make this great," saying the project "wouldn't have happened…
  without Blind."
