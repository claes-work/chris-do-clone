# Blind / Futur Studio Tour — Facebook Live with Art Center (2017-02-17)

## Summary

A live, unedited Facebook Live walkthrough of the Blind / The Futur studio at 1702
Olympic, Santa Monica, filmed for Art Center College of Design. **Chris Do narrates
throughout in the first person** ("my name is Christo... I graduated Art Center graphic
design in 1995"), guiding viewers room-by-room and fielding audience questions relayed
by an off-camera host and team member Matt.

Although the format is a physical tour, it is **more than a walkthrough**: Chris
repeatedly explains *why* the space and culture are built the way they are, and the
segment ends with a personal rapid-fire Q&A. The tour is therefore a **context page
with moderate persona value** — it surfaces Chris's management philosophy, the
Blind→Futur media-company thesis, his teaching style, and biographical color, all
first-person and clearly Chris-attributed.

Key framing Chris gives early: two companies share the space — **Blind** (the service
company: brand strategy, motion graphic design) and **The Futur** (media / education
platform: YouTube, podcasting, Instagram, SlideShare). The through-line of the tour is
that these two worlds are beginning to "collide."

## Key claims

Only Chris-attributed, persona-relevant statements are listed. All are self-reported
unless noted.

- **Two companies, one space.** Blind = service/client work (brand strategy, motion
  graphics); The Futur = media/entertainment/education platform. Blind works "for
  people," The Futur works "for ourselves." [business]
- **Management philosophy — get out of the way.** "The way that part of my management
  philosophy is just let the people do what they need to do and give them the space and
  the tools to do that and get out of their way." [business, mindset]
- **Deliberately imperfect space.** "I'd rather worry about creating ideas and trying
  new things and making the space perfect I've let that part of my personality go." The
  space is "in transition... because we're a company that's in transition." [mindset]
- **Fitness as company culture.** Gym gear "is not for show... Being Fit and being
  active is a big part of our culture." Notes this is recent — "the last 6 years," not
  lifelong — and the team does group workouts (150 pushups the prior day). [fitness,
  mindset]
- **5pm no-work ritual.** "At 5:00 I ask everybody to get out of their chair and go do
  something... you're not allowed to work" — workouts, ping pong, video games — to keep
  people active and connected. [business, mindset]
- **Exercise → well-being (not a productivity claim).** "I don't know if there's a
  direct correlation between exercise and creativity but... when I exercise I feel
  better I have more energy and I'm more focused and it also reminds me that I have some
  control over my life." [mindset, fitness]
- **The media-company thesis / crossover.** Clients now discover Blind via The Futur's
  YouTube videos and hire them; conversely, content shot for Blind gets used on the
  YouTube channel. "My dream world is these two worlds collide in a very beautiful and
  elegant way and I think that's the future for us." [business, content-strategy]
- **The Futur's origin.** The channel was **Jose Caballer's idea** (originally "The
  School"); Chris credits Jose, not himself. Started as marketing to show their process,
  then evolved into sharing content "to help people level up." [content-strategy]
- **What drives him now.** Direct messages from people saying the content changed their
  lives are "worth... I'll take one of those over any of these [awards]." Frames teaching
  / making a difference as his "last career... this is what I work for." [mindset]
- **Awards are hollow.** "You win an award your life is exactly the same... we have tons
  and tons of awards and my life is exactly the same." Still admits wanting the hardware
  sometimes just to say "I got that." Also: "when you do good work it stands out... if
  you do good work and you enter you're going to win." [mindset]
- **Teaching style — sink or swim.** Described (by Chris and confirmed by student/CD
  Matthew) as in-your-face: challenge students, then stay silent and let them defend
  their work — "he'll just going to let you drown in class." Chris: "I sprinkle a heavy
  dose of sarcasm and... sardonic comments... but I'm trying to change my ways."
  [mindset]
- **Visual communication transcends language.** Believes his teaching principles travel
  across cultures because they're about visual communication, "of course you have to be
  sensitive to cultural context." [content-strategy]
- **Closing advice — shift from maker to author/content person.** The tour's most
  substantive persona statement (full quote below): creatives should stop thinking about
  working for others and instead "create our own economy," because labor work will be
  "eaten by software and robots." "The content we create becomes power to build an
  audience and people want our audience." [business, content-strategy]
- **Slay your fears.** "One of my philosophies is try to slay all your fears — whatever
  you're scared of move towards that." Names public speaking as his greatest fear, on
  which he's "made a lot of progress." [mindset]

Biographical color (self-reported): Art Center graphic design grad, 1995; comic-book
collector ("I owe a debt of my career to comic books"); two Emmys (art direction/design),
several Clios, Telly/broadcast awards; motion-design roots (Gnarls Barkley "Crazy," Jet,
Coldplay, Jeff Buckley interactive videos); wife is also an Art Center grad; two sons
(13 and 10 in 2017); currently reading *The Power of Self-Confidence*; identified as an
artist at ~7 when an uncle gave him a $3 pad of paper and markers; home office/"man cave"
in the master bedroom optimized to record an episode in five minutes.

> "The way that part of my management philosophy is just let the people do what they
> need to do and give them the space and the tools to do that and get out of their way."

> "I'd rather worry about creating ideas and trying new things and making the space
> perfect — I've let that part of my personality go."

> "You win an award your life is exactly the same... we have tons and tons of awards and
