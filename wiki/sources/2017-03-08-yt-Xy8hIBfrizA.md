## Summary

A step-by-step Photoshop technique tutorial published on The Futur channel. The
instructor introduces himself on camera as **"Matthew"** (Matthew Encina, a Futur
instructor / creative director) — Chris Do does not teach or appear in this video.

Matthew demonstrates how to build a double-exposure image: superimposing two photos so
the underexposed (shadow) areas of an "over image" (a side portrait with strong dark
shadows and a white background) act as a window through which an "under image" (e.g. a
landscape) shows. The mechanical core is setting the over image's blend mode to
**Screen** (an additive blend where white knocks out and dark values let the second
image through), plus prep work like extending the white background so it blocks
cleanly. He closes with four "fail-proof" design tips: (1) juxtapose the two images to
create a new third meaning/story, (2) add contrast in size and content, (3) give the
over image a clear silhouette and strong shadows (side portraits, rim or single-source
lighting work well), and (4) line up the images in interesting places (e.g. aligning
horizons inside businessmen's silhouettes for a financial client). Examples shown come
from past Futur/Blind commercial work (an energy company, a financial institution).

All content is Matthew's technical instruction and design opinion. Nothing is
