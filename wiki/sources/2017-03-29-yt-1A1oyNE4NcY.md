# How To Handle Clients with BAD TASTE in 1 minute!!

A ~1-minute The Futur short. It is a clipped excerpt of the longer 2016-04-01 talk
([[2016-04-01-yt-03lX4hn7tJ0]]) — the same value-exchange framing and the same
refund-and-refer close appear near-verbatim. Light restatement; adds nothing distinct,
so not an L3 candidate.

## Summary

Chris Do reframes designer/client work as a commercial value exchange: the client pays
to have a problem solved, not to fund the designer's art. If the idea of being paid for
your art feels cheap, become a fine artist — commercial art is a service. And when a
client engagement is genuinely destroying you, the honest move is to own it, refund the
money, apologize, and refer other designers rather than resent the client and the work.

## Key claims

Chris Do (subject):
- Agreeing to do work for someone is an implied or explicit value exchange: you give a
  creative solution to their problem, they give money. The key question is whether they
  are paying you to make your art or to solve their problem — it is the latter. (2017-03-29)
- We are in a business, a transaction; commercial art is a service like anything else.
  If being paid for your art feels cheap or crass, go be a fine artist — that is okay,
  but this is a commercial art exchange. (2017-03-29)
- When a client engagement is "destroying every fabric of who you are," own up, tell the
  client they are not the right fit for you, refund the money, apologize, and refer other
  designers you think can help. (2017-03-29)

## Notable verbatim quotes

Chris Do (persona voice):

> "When you agreed to do something for somebody you make either an implied or an explicit exchange in value."

> "Are they giving you money for you to make your art, or are they giving you money to solve their problem?"

> "If the idea of receiving money for your art feels cheap to you and feels crass, go be a fine artist — that's totally okay. But we're in a commercial art exchange, and it's a service just like anything else."

> "You know what, I don't think you're the right client for me. I'm gonna refund your money, I'm sorry. I'll refer some other designers I think I know who can help you, and I'm not the person."
