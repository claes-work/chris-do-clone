## Key claims

### William Walczak (guest, Hiilite — CONTEXT, not persona)

- SEO is not a "black box" or "snake oil"; search engines are in the business of surfacing the
  best results, so the work is legible and measurable.
- "Everything communicates" — file names, page titles, alt text, descriptions, links all tell
  Google what a thing is; describe everything accurately (don't ship `IMG_15000.jpg` or a page
  titled "Home").
- Meta *descriptions* / keyword meta *tags* are no longer used by Google (~2009); "metadata"
  broadly (how you describe your data) is still critical. Original content matters — wholesale
  copying without attribution gets you penalized.
- Page authority / domain authority: reputable inbound links (Chamber of Commerce, news,
  governing bodies, Huffington Post) build credibility; social media rapidly multiplies links.
  Hard to get above 40 on domain/page authority.
- Keyword research (Google Keyword Planner): pick the terms people actually search, not
  industry jargon. Hiilite ranked #1 for "marketing agency" but pivoted to "web design"
  because that's what clients searched. Market-by-market lexicon differs ("hair salon" vs
  "hair salons," Fresno vs Santa Monica).
- Measurement via Google Analytics: sessions vs users, bounce rate, behavior flow / drop-off,
  real-time traffic to attribute offline ads (radio/billboard/magazine).
- Content marketing IS the SEO engine (e.g. "balayage hair," "denim hair" posts for salon
  client Plan B); Hiilite writes/produces client content because ~95% of clients won't do it
  themselves. Case study: **Alpha Doors** (garage-door company, Nanaimo BC) — ~3.5 hrs of work
  → +274% sessions, +250% organic month-over-month, #1 map result within a month.
- **Hiilite productized SEO packages (context / competitive-intel):** minimum engagement
  **~$345–350/mo** (≈3–3.5 hrs), tiers up to **$1,200/mo** (~7.5 hrs), studio rate **$110/hr**;
  largest retainer client (museum society) **>$3,000/mo**; ~12 staff; no client contracts;
  positions SEO as a "port of harbor" loss-leader-adjacent relationship that upsells. Open to
  white-label / consultant arrangements with Futur members.
