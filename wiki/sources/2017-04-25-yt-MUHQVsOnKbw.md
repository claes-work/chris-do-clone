# How Your Beliefs and Behavior Define Your Personal Brand

> ★ L3-candidate — adds a distinct personal-branding framework (beliefs/behavior → brand,
> and the "think–say–do harmony" model of character) beyond a generic branding overview.

## Summary

Live Q&A / teaching session with **Chris Do** and co-host **Dave** fielding questions from
design students. The through-line: everyone already has a personal brand whether they manage
it or not, and that brand is built from beliefs and behavior — not a logo. Chris argues a brand
is the gut feeling people have about a person, that everything you do and say shapes that
perception, and that people trust those who believe what they believe (leaning on Simon Sinek's
*Start With Why*). He connects this to portfolios (show what you believe, not just the work),
to "character" as internal harmony between what you think, say, and do, and to authenticity
(brand attributes must align with behavior — using the 2017 United Airlines passenger-removal
incident as a cautionary example). Dave adds team-dynamics and mentorship angles; Chris uses
designer "Meg's" magazine-cover exploration as proof that depth of work signals passion.

Speakers: **Chris Do** (subject / persona), **Dave** (co-host), plus unnamed students.

## Key claims

- **[2017-04-25] (Chris)** Everyone already has a personal brand, and most people are not
  managing it well — so you need to start managing it deliberately. *(paraphrase)*
- **[2017-04-25] (Chris)** Your logo is not your brand; a brand is a person's gut feeling about
  a person, product, or service. *(paraphrase — restating Marty Neumeier's definition, though
  Neumeier is not named in the clip)*
- **[2017-04-25] (Chris)** Everything you do and say impacts the perception people have of your
  brand. *(paraphrase)*
- **[2017-04-25] (Chris)** A strong brand is defined by what it stands for and its values —
  not what it makes; Apple's "Think Different" communicates who it is for (the crazy ones, the
  rebels) rather than describing products. *(paraphrase)*
- **[2017-04-25] (Chris)** We trust people who believe what we believe more than people who are
  merely qualified — illustrated with the babysitter example (known neighborhood teen "Sally"
  chosen over the more-qualified professional nanny "Mary"). Attributed to Simon Sinek's
  *Start With Why*. *(paraphrase)*
- **[2017-04-25] (Chris)** In a portfolio, most designers only show the work and never
  communicate what they believe; to move into real branding you must start articulating what
  you stand for. *(paraphrase)*
- **[2017-04-25] (Chris)** Character is internal harmony: when what you think, what you say, and
  what you do are aligned. A "fractured sense of self" is when those parts fight each other;
  self-awareness is what brings them into harmony. *(paraphrase — Chris loosely references
  Freud/psyche but disclaims psychology expertise)*
- **[2017-04-25] (Chris)** When a brand's promises (attributes) don't align with its behavior,
  it reads as disconnected and inauthentic — the biggest problem. Cited the United Airlines
  incident and its reported ~4% (~$800M) stock drop; "what you do says more about what you
  think." *(paraphrase)*
- **[2017-04-25] (Chris)** Depth of exploration is proof of passion — e.g. designer "Meg" did
  ~35 different magazine covers (not iterations) plus multiple full spreads over ~2 weeks before
  refining; a student spending 10 weeks and producing one logo + one application signals a lack
  of passion. *(paraphrase)*
- **[2017-04-25] (Dave, co-host)** Character in a studio/business context is about team
  dynamics and mentorship — knowing when to lead and when to take a back seat, fitting people
  together like a sports team toward a common objective. *(paraphrase — co-host, not persona)*
- **[2017-04-25] (Chris)** Teams fail when individuals lack a defined common purpose, or when
  one or two people do the work of five; program teams for diversity of strengths and
  personality styles (don't put two "Alphas" together), and rotate roles. *(paraphrase)*
