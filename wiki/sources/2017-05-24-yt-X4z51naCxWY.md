## Summary

Nils Lindstrom introduces himself and the Lettering 01 course published on the Futur
(@thefutur) channel. He describes himself as a letter form artist who has taught type and
letter form for 30 years at the Art Center College of Design in Pasadena, California, and
distinguishes a letter form artist from a calligrapher or sign painter — same medium,
different tools. He frames the course for practicing and aspiring graphic designers who
want to improve their drawing skills and understanding of typography. His central teaching
premise: type has always been drawn by the hand and arm, letter form parts are named after
parts of the body, and the key skill is learning to see ("you can't draw what you can't
see, and you can't see what you don't know") — what he calls "visual athleticism," matching
a shape or proportion precisely. He then previews the course structure: 8 videos covering
the origins of the Roman alphabet and the evolution into modern typography; two chalk demos
(an Old Style Serif and a Black Letter style); sketching, ideation and refinement of a
script logotype with flourishes; scanning and vectorizing/digitizing that logo in Adobe
Illustrator; handouts and assignments; and demonstrations of drawing type precisely and
manipulating type properly (weighting, changing character width, slanting) without the
pitfalls of doing it on the computer. He closes on the rationale that ~90% of graphic
design deals with typography, so designers must understand its origins, rationale and
logic, and the best way to do that is to draw type.
