# Tell your Brand Story. Increase your value exponentially — Chris Do LIVE @ NAB 2017 Las Vegas

A short keynote clip from Chris Do at NAB 2017 (Las Vegas) arguing that
creative professionals fail to tell their own brand story, and that adding
story to any object or service multiplies its perceived value. The captured
transcript is brief (~37 lines) — likely an excerpt/highlight of the full
talk. All content is Chris Do speaking, first person, addressing an audience
of creatives ("storytellers").

## Summary

Chris Do opens by challenging the audience: are they good storytellers of
their *own* brand? He argues that when a prospect visits a website and sees
undifferentiated logos or videos with no accompanying story, the work looks
like the "tens of thousands of kids" doing the same thing — and
undifferentiated work invites heavy competition and easy replacement. He
frames this as the "shoemaker's kids have no shoes" problem: creatives are so
busy working on clients' businesses that they neglect the business of their
own business.

To prove story creates value, he cites the **Significant Objects experiment**
by Joshua Glenn and Rob Walker: the researchers bought insignificant/junk
objects (avg. ~$1.25 each), hired writers to invent a story ascribing meaning
to each, and resold them on eBay. Roughly $128.74 of objects sold for ~$3,625
— which Chris rounds to a "30X multiplier" from adding story alone. His
closing takeaway: story turns a commodity (or junk) into something valuable,
so if you want to make more money, tell your story.
