# Designer as Entrepreneur, Going Beyond Design: CSUN Portfolio Review

## Summary

Short clip from a CSUN (California State University, Northridge) portfolio review
where [[../entities/chris-do]] gives one-on-one critique to design students. The
featured segment is Chris reviewing Juliana, a graphic design major / marketing minor,
who presents a class assignment she pushed beyond the brief: **Time Swap**, a tutoring
app where students exchange tutoring time with one another or purchase tutoring, with
quality signaled by user-generated information (ratings/reviews).

Chris's critique operates on two levels. On **pitch/business framing**, he coaches her
to present the concept like a Shark Tank pitch — lead with the problem story, the
frustration that motivated it, then the solution — treating design as support for the
concept rather than the point. On **design craft**, he gives specific notes on the
logo/mark (wants it to communicate the *swapping* of time, not just time) and color
(purple skews feminine/cosmetic; use it as an accent, make the palette more neutral).
His closing frame is the video's thesis: her work goes "way beyond aesthetics" — it
solves a problem, has a business model, and opens onto running a company and managing
teams, which he calls a wonderful arc for her career.

The clip closes with student reaction testimonials (context) and a brief personal
aside from Chris about having skipped his own portfolio review and high school
graduation.

Co-host presence: the transcript shows only Chris speaking as reviewer; no co-host
lines are attributable. (Task noted Dave Moon as possible co-host = context — not

## Key claims

Speaker attributions: **Chris Do** = persona-eligible. **Juliana (student)** = context.

- **[Juliana, 2017-05-31, paraphrase, context]** Her project Time Swap is a tutoring
  app where students exchange time with one another or purchase tutoring; it started as
  a class assignment ("come up with an app") that she extended into a case-study
  format. Tutor quality is conveyed through user-generated information.
- **[Chris Do, 2017-05-31, paraphrase]** A designer should pitch a concept the way one
  would on Shark Tank: state the problem you saw, the fact that no solution existed,
  the concept you built, and what it does — the design is secondary and exists to
  support the concept.
- **[Chris Do, 2017-05-31, paraphrase]** Storytelling beats feature-listing in a pitch:
  narrate the lived frustration ("I got frustrated, I needed help and couldn't get it")
  including doubts about price and tutor quality, then resolve it with the product.
- **[Chris Do, 2017-05-31, paraphrase]** Logo critique: the mark needs work and should
  communicate not just *time* but the *swapping/exchange* of time — a graphic device
  expressing the exchange.
- **[Chris Do, 2017-05-31, paraphrase]** Color critique: this hue of purple skews
  feminine/cosmetic; use purple as an accent rather than everywhere, and make the
  palette more neutral.
- **[Chris Do, 2017-05-31, paraphrase]** The strongest thing about the project is that
  it goes beyond aesthetics — it functions, solves a problem, and contains a business
  model; that opens onto learning to run a company and manage teams, which he frames as
  a strong career arc (the "designer as entrepreneur" thesis).
- **[Chris Do, 2017-05-31, paraphrase]** What he values in this student is a business
  mindset: identifying a problem and using design as a means to solve it.
