# How & Why To Use Layer Masks vs Multiply? Adobe Photoshop Tutorial

Context page. Low persona value: this is a step-by-step Adobe Photoshop technique

## Summary

**Who teaches:** Chris Do himself — the video opens "hey guys, Chris here" and he
narrates the entire hands-on demo in first person (a Wacom-tablet Photoshop screen
recording). This is a follow-up to a "Spartan logo design challenge" video, answering
viewer questions about why he built a "paint splotchy layer" the way he did.

The tutorial demonstrates a single craft technique: instead of applying a grayscale
watercolor texture with a Multiply (or Screen) blend mode — which only darkens (or only
lightens) the layer below and is tied to the underlying colors — Chris copies the
texture into a **layer mask** on a solid-color fill layer, inverts it (Command+I), and
cleans up edges with the burn tool (O) or an Overlay brush (B). He then uses Levels
(Command+L) on the mask to control how much of the texture's whites and darks show
through. The payoff he stresses: the layer-mask method preserves both highlights and
shadows independently of the background, giving a more "three-dimensional," "crusty /
caked-over" grunge look and letting the color be changed freely (Hue/Saturation) — an
effect Multiply or Screen alone cannot produce. He notes the two approaches are not
mutually exclusive and can be combined.
