# Win More Clients — Build Empathy to Earn Trust

★ L3-candidate — adds a distinct, memorable empathy/trust framework (the "haircut" analogy, "start empty and listen," and the personal definition of success) beyond generic sales-clients material.

## Summary

In this clip (@thefutur, 2017-06-14), Chris Do uses a live audience member named Ben — who twice got a haircut he did not want because he "couldn't articulate to the lady what [he] wanted" — as an analogy for the creative-client relationship. The core argument: clients (like Ben) often cannot articulate what they want because they lack the industry vocabulary, so the professional's job is to build empathy and trust, not to impose their own artistic agenda. Chris frames winning and keeping clients around understanding the world through the client's eyes, deciding honestly whether you are a good fit, and — once paid — treating the work as being about the client's happiness rather than the creator's self-expression. He closes with practical advice ("start empty and listen") and his personal definition of success: doing good work for clients who appreciate it, and continuing to grow.

## Key claims

- (Chris, 2017-06-14) Clients often cannot articulate what they want because they aren't in your industry and lack the terminology (like Ben, who couldn't describe his haircut visually with words); the professional must recognize this rather than fault the client. [paraphrase]
- (Chris, 2017-06-14) An ideal client is one who is very discerning — they know what they want and have good taste. [paraphrase]
- (Chris, 2017-06-14) You must honestly assess fit: is this a client that's a good fit for me, and am I a good fit for them? If not, be honest and refer them elsewhere. [paraphrase]
- (Chris, 2017-06-14) Once you accept the money, you have a professional obligation to serve the client; the work is no longer about you — it's about making the client happy. [paraphrase]
- (Chris, 2017-06-14) When a client is happy, they come back and trust is built — that trust/rapport is what lets you later propose new ideas together. [paraphrase]
- (Chris, 2017-06-14) When working with a client, "start empty and listen" — come in with nothing and focus on what the client wants. [paraphrase]
- (Chris, 2017-06-14) If you feel frustrated and want to make your own art, do it on your own time, just for yourself; don't impose it on client work. [paraphrase]
- (Chris, 2017-06-14) He does not define his success or happiness by how much of his own artwork he gets to do; he defines success by (1) doing good work for good clients who appreciate it, and (2) whether he is growing. [paraphrase]
- (Ben, audience/context, 2017-06-14) Paid ~$20 for a walk-in haircut, got a stylist assigned, and did not get the haircut he wanted because it's hard to explain something visual with words. [paraphrase]
