# How Will Customers Find You — Build Awareness by Informing or Inspiring

★ L3-candidate — introduces a distinct awareness/marketing framework (the conversion funnel traced backwards from profit, plus the "inform or inspire" content mandate) that goes beyond the current content-strategy topic pages.

## Summary

Chris Do (speaking to a live creative-professional audience at The Futur) argues that a good portfolio does not automatically deliver profitable work — that is a "fantasy." To reach profit you must reverse-engineer the chain: profit ← profitable work ← closing sales ← proposals ← RFPs ← meetings/calls ← first contact. That chain leads back to the **conversion funnel** (also called purchase funnel / user journey), which he says marketers know but creatives usually do not.

He walks the funnel top to bottom: a customer has a **need**; the top is **awareness** ("how will customers find you"); then **return/validation** (people compare and validate before buying); the dashed conversion line ("where the magic happens") where a passive looker becomes a customer; then **delight** (do what you said, then exceed expectations) which triggers **share** — the word-of-mouth that feeds reach again. He notes the funnel also involves **micro-conversions** (small escalating commitments — email signup, signing a petition) and a **resistance/doubt barrier** the seller's job is to reassure past.

The core prescriptive message: to get more leads, focus on the **top of the funnel**. Word of mouth is powerful but unpredictable and effortless-for-you, so it can't be relied on. Instead, find where your audience is most active, tell a repeatable story, and empower clients to share it. He criticizes creatives who treat social media like advertising ("your license to spam") and who only talk about themselves ("me me me"). His rule: **provide value freely and give it away** — a boomerang where what you put out comes back. The takeaway action: **inform or inspire** by sharing your knowledge, wisdom, experience, techniques, and process to create content that attracts the clients you want, aligned with the problems and challenges they currently have.
