## Summary

A short fly-on-the-wall documentary episode from The Futur / Blind, showing life inside **Blind**,
Chris Do's design studio (see [[wiki/entities/blind]]). The central thread is a **mentorship
moment**: Chris coaches an **intern** who has been handed a real client brief — a "Spartan Race"
challenge project — and is jumping straight into designing. Chris pushes back on designing before
understanding the subject, laying out his research-first, "think more make less" approach and
framing every project as a chance to learn something new (here, Greek/Spartan culture).

**Chris's presence: substantial for the runtime.** Chris is the dominant on-camera voice in the
teaching passage (lines 17–36) and appears again in the casual studio-culture footage (lines 38–60).
Other voices — the **intern** describing his Spartan concept (lines 2–15, context) and **Aaron**, a
Blind team member filming (context) — are studio/context material, not persona. Team = CONTEXT;
only Chris-Do trains the persona.

Attribution is mostly clear from content: the design-philosophy lines match Chris's known framing
and are attributed to him. The opening voice describing the Spartan concept ("as an intern he told
me to…", "that's the thing referencing whatever Spartan stuff") is the **intern** speaking about
himself — context, not Chris.

## Key claims

Speaker labels: **[Chris Do]** = persona-eligible (self-reported unless noted); **[Intern]**,
**[Aaron / team]** = CONTEXT (Blind studio), not persona.

- **[Chris Do]** (2017-07-22, self-reported design philosophy): Designers too often "jump and start
  designing"; his own approach is to slow down — "think more, make less." (paraphrase + quote, lines 17–20)
- **[Chris Do]** (2017-07-22): Design must come "from a place of Truth," and reaching that truth
  requires first *learning* about the subject — for a Spartan brief, the Spartans' armor, era, and
  culture ("get into Athens and Sparta… to know design motives"). (paraphrase + quote, lines 20–27)
- **[Chris Do]** (2017-07-22): He treats "every project as an opportunity to learn something new,"
  wanting the intern to walk away not as "a better designer but a person who knows more" about the
  subject matter (Greek culture / the Spartans). (quote, lines 27–34)
- **[Chris Do]** (2017-07-22): Challenges the intern for designing before doing the research —
  "so you don't know anything yet, you're already designing." (quote, lines 35–36)
- **[Intern]** (CONTEXT, 2017-07-22): Was told, as an intern, to download "The Challenge brief"
  (a Spartan Race project) and "just try it out / figure it out"; his concept plays up that a
  Spartan Race is "grueling… hardcore," and Sparta as "fast moving… hard." (paraphrase, lines 2–15)
- **[Blind / studio culture]** (CONTEXT, 2017-07-22): The studio runs on hands-on mentorship — interns
  are given real briefs and coached in person. Informal, "keeping it real" atmosphere: Chris notes he's
  "walking in my slippers and eating pretzel… but this is real." Team member **Aaron** is filming
  despite Chris joking he told him not to. (paraphrase, lines 38–60)
