# Motivation, Focus & Grind - 7 Days Till Delivery

A short first-person video diary narrated by Chris Do documenting the final week of a high-pressure studio project (Blind / The Futur era) — from the grind of the last few days, through a 21-hour delivery shift, to the live premiere and later reflection. The whole piece is Chris speaking in the first person about motivation, focus, effort, and what makes work worth it.

## Summary

Chris narrates the closing stretch of a demanding client project. His team is attempting things they have "almost never done before" — highly technical, challenging work where individual processes can take hours to render before you know if they even worked. He frames roadblocks as an inherent part of the process, and argues the bigger the hurdle you clear, the more satisfying the result. He describes a "bottomless" to-do list, uncertainty about whether there's enough time/manpower, and the physical toll (second wind vs. creeping delirium). He observes teammates dropping off in motivation (not focus) in the final push, and reflects that good work can happen without extra effort, but truly *better* work requires the late nights and the pace. On delivery morning he reports a 21-hour shift — the first office sunrise he's seen in ~8-9 years. The long-time client has repeatedly seen the team perform under pressure and deliver. At the live premiere in front of thousands, the crowd erupts and Chris exhales, releasing the tension. Weeks later he reflects on the endless revisions and finessing of details "no one will really even see or appreciate except for us" — and concludes it was all worth it.
