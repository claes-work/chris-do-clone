## Key claims

### Chris Do (persona — art-direction / ideation method)

- `[2017-08-15]` (paraphrase) When designing a symbol for a concept like protection,
  start by generating a large volume of metaphors — imagine asking people on the
  street for the top hundred symbols for protection — and just draw them quickly
  without over-thinking.
- `[2017-08-15]` (paraphrase) Avoid the obvious cliché — the padlock. The lock is
  where everyone's mind goes first ("the lock will lead you somewhere"), so it should
  not be the starting or default answer.
- `[2017-08-15]` (paraphrase) Icons of this kind are usually built by **combining two
  things** — the interplay of two symbols is often more revealing about the idea than
  either alone.
- `[2017-08-15]` (paraphrase) Keep **boiling the idea down** to its essence — reduce a
  concept (e.g. a weapon, lightning) to the simplest form that still reads.
- `[2017-08-15]` (paraphrase) These logos/icons should work as an **idea**, not as a
  detailed illustration — they don't need to be rendered heads or scenes.
- `[2017-08-15]` (paraphrase) A distinctive real-world texture — e.g. Kevlar's weave
  pattern — can itself become a recognizable geometric container that letters or
  forms sit inside.
- `[2017-08-15]` (paraphrase) Organize exploration systematically — e.g. put each
  variation on its own so they can be grouped, and lay out a page of just weapon
  symbols scaling from small to big — so the range can be compared.
- `[2017-08-15]` (paraphrase) On interviewing a candidate (Elaine): if a person is in
  the room, their skill is already assumed; the interview is really about checking
  personality and culture fit.

### Blind team members (CONTEXT — `attribution: uncertain`, NOT persona)

- `[2017-08-15]` (paraphrase) One team member wants a new hire who is a "Swiss Army
  knife" — some copywriting, heavy digital-experience skill, able to pick things up
  and run with many different tasks.
- `[2017-08-15]` (paraphrase) Another emphasizes the **motion** side — sequencing
  images to tell a story and solve a problem — as their main need.
- `[2017-08-15]` (paraphrase) Another wants someone really sharp with **typography**
  who also knows **corporate identity**, so work can be shared when Blind gets busy.
