# How To Build Self Confidence & Overcome Imposter Syndrome (7 mins)

## Summary

★ **L3-candidate** — This is a compact, single-topic mindset teaching that lays out
Chris Do's signature framework on confidence, ego, and imposter syndrome (including his
"formula for happiness" and the ego-as-gap model). Imposter syndrome and creative
confidence are recurring core themes, so this talk is a strong candidate for promotion
into `wiki/topics/mindset/` and `persona/beliefs.md`.

Solo talk by Chris Do (The Futur), delivered to a live audience. Chris argues that
creative confidence is a **skill that can be cultivated**, not an innate trait. He opens
by confessing he is an "extreme introvert" for whom public speaking is a lifelong
challenge — and that the fact he is standing on stage is proof that fears can be overcome
through work (coaches, books, videos). He tells a story about meeting boxing legend Sugar
Ray Leonard, using it to illustrate the payoff of being fully transparent and authentic:
by admitting up front that he was starstruck and might get emotional, he built a genuine
relationship rather than performing a mask.

Chris then defines confidence as beginning with inner peace / peace of mind, offers a
"formula for happiness" (what you think, say, and do in harmony), and locates unhappiness
in **attachment** and **ego**. He frames the ego as the thing living in the *gap between
the conscious and unconscious mind* — the distance between "the you of today" (lacking
skills/confidence) and "the future you." He connects this directly to imposter syndrome:
the mask you think the world sees, when in fact people already see you for who you are.
His resolution is **detachment, surrender, and authenticity** — and the paradox that when
you relinquish authority/power/influence, you actually gain it.
