A ~7-minute NAB 2017 keynote delivered entirely by Chris Do. He argues that creativity is not undermined by having a process — a repeatable process yields predictable results, the same way elite athletes rely on rituals (he cites Jack Nicklaus's pre-shot routine, timed by a psychologist to within one second across every hole). He distinguishes "positive thinking" (hoping) from "positive knowing" (trusting a formula proven through repetition), and shares his own brutal pre-talk ritual. He then hands the audience four reflective prompts for discovering their own creative formula, defines the flow state via neuroscience-flavored language (optimal state of consciousness, action–awareness merging, time dilation, loss of self), and closes with a personal story: roughly five years into his company, burnt out and feeling threatened by a talented young art director, he took a three-month sabbatical, began teaching at Otis, and rediscovered how much he knew by being forced to explain his thinking to students. The teaching experience cured his self-doubt and revealed he "had a lot more to offer" than his studio work.
