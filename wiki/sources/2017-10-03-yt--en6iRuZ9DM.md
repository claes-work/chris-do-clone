# Secret Power of Introverts — Being A Great Listener (ep. 4)

★ **L3-candidate** — This is a landmark, dense articulation of Chris Do's introvert/listening philosophy and his personal self-development story (Myers-Briggs INTJ, the boardroom anxiety anecdote, the 10-year business coach relationship, the "change the lens" reframe, ego/expectation as the source of anxiety). Strong voice-bank and beliefs material for `persona/` and `wiki/topics/mindset`; promote on the next synthesis pass.

## Summary

Episode 4 of Chris Do's coaching series with mentee Rebecca Hyneman (a graphic designer / introvert working to grow her business and put herself out there). Framed as a mid-season "recap," it becomes a deep coaching session on being an introvert. Chris shares that he is "extremely introverted" and has spent ~15 years working on it, then teaches a set of reframes: introversion is not a weakness but a source of power (introverts become great listeners because they focus on others rather than on what to say next); the "loudmouth" is often given undeserved credit while the quiet, deliberate person is read as thoughtful and smart. He recounts personal stories — a terrifying boardroom moment ~20 years ago, working with a business coach ("Kier McLaren") one session a week for ten years, and reframing critical feedback as "a new tool/toy" rather than an attack. Core teaching: "change the lens" — move energy away from ego, expectation, and self-focus (what will I say, how am I judged) toward serving the other person. He connects anxiety to expectation (his wife's meditation influence) and illustrates focus-shifting with a friend's severe social-anxiety soup story. He assigns Rebecca homework: talk to five total strangers via small talk (barista, waitstaff, elevator, park) in low-stakes, no-business situations. Also touches goal-setting/vision boards, the "social contract" of public commitment, and a rowing-the-Atlantic story about fear of failure exceeding fear of death.
