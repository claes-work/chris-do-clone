## Key claims

Dated 2017-11-15. Paraphrased unless quoted below. Attributed per speaker.

**Chris Do (persona — his framing, mentoring, critique):**
- Frames the prize: The Futur flew one person to LA to celebrate crossing 100,000 subscribers, giving Sean 24 hours of open access to "ask anything."
- Reframes Sean's one-hour rule as a virtue: it is not that Sean *only* spent an hour, but that he *allowed himself* only an hour — which "means really discipline." (Chris elevating self-imposed constraint to a discipline signal.)
- On conceptual logo design generally: when combining two symbols you have to "find where the shapes overlap and how they could come together" — his stated method for conceptual thinking.
- Critique of the "sharp/knife + crown" logo: dislikes the curved bottom corner of the crown because a curved/rounded corner "creates that sense of off-balance" and "feels dull," which undercuts the sharpness the concept needs.
- Suggests a stronger execution: build the crown *out of knives* laid out so the shapes form the crown — integrating the two symbols rather than placing them adjacent.
- Praises the same logo's overall pairing: "a good pairing of a strong icon and a serif typeface," with kerning he calls nice — his objection is isolated to the one corner.

**Sean Campbell (context — his story, NOT persona training data):**
- Is 17, a high-school senior, not yet in college; self-taught; found this was his first time flying on a plane to reach LA.
- Ran a personal "30 logos in 30 days" challenge using daily briefs from an email-list service (briefs were exercises, "not real things"); constrained each logo to one hour or less as a creative warm-up and to have daily Instagram content.
- Also participated in a Local Inspirations DC Comics logo challenge.
- Got into design ~2–3 years earlier (around age 14) by making and selling t-shirts on Teespring, earning ~$800; liked the making/designing more than the money, having always drawn as a kid but not knowing "design was a thing" until then.
- On his "deadbeat" logo: brief was an online music-sharing community wanting "dead" + "beat"; he combined a tombstone with a sound wave; says he doesn't particularly like it.
