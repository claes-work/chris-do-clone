## Summary

A short, therapy-flavored conversation on self-doubt and imposter syndrome
aimed at creatives. The guest therapist draws a central distinction between
**self-confidence** (how you feel about your abilities alone, in your own skin)
and **self-esteem** (how you feel about your abilities in the face of / compared
to other people). Negative feedback absorbed early — especially from caregivers —
leaves an "imprint" or inner negative voice that isn't truly yours. The therapist
describes an "empty chair"/switch-chair technique to externalize that voice
(e.g. a parent's voice) and separate it from the self. Chris shares personal
material: caregivers who shut down his negative emotions ("don't feel that"), his
introverted temperament that turned inward ("there must be something wrong with
me"), and how he later projected an unmet need for praise ("you did a good job,
you're a good boy," "you did great on that music piece") onto his relationships
with women. The practical takeaway offered: start consciously noticing your
felt reactions around specific people — like/dislike, hurt, anger, envy, fear —
as a way in to what's actually happening.

## Key claims

### Therapist / guest (CONTEXT — not persona; the framework speaker)

- [2017-12-07] There is a difference between self-confidence and self-esteem:
  self-confidence is how we feel about our own abilities alone, "in our own skin,
  in our own room by ourselves." (paraphrase)
- [2017-12-07] Self-esteem is how we feel about our abilities in the face of / in
  comparison to other people — it introduces how others feel about us. (paraphrase)
- [2017-12-07] Enough negative feedback creates an "imprint" — like a thumbprint —
  a negative inner voice ("you're no good at this") that comes from somewhere
  outside you, not from you. (paraphrase)
- [2017-12-07] A therapy tool: the open/empty chair — you switch chairs and speak
  as the negative voice (e.g. a father's or mother's voice), so you can see that
  the person and the voice are not the same thing, and that you have a hand in
  crafting the life you want. (paraphrase)
- [2017-12-07] When you're young you need external praise to build a self —
  you start as a "blank slate" with your own temperament and personality. (paraphrase)
- [2017-12-07] Way in for non-"feeling types": notice whether you like or dislike
  something/someone, then look at what's underneath the preference (hurt, anger,
  fear, envy). (paraphrase) `attribution: uncertain` (could be Chris)
