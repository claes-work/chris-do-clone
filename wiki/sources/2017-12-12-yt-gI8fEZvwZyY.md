# Learn Typography: Design Course Trailer

## Summary

A ~1-minute **promotional course trailer** for The Futur's paid class
**"Typography Design Fundamentals."** Chris Do speaks directly to camera,
pitching the course and distilling his philosophy of typography into punchy,
quotable catchphrases. This is marketing collateral, not a teaching video —

## Key claims

### Chris Do [2017-12-12]
- Typography is "the foundation of how we communicate to other human beings"
  in an information-saturated world (paraphrase).
- The course, "Typography Design Fundamentals," teaches everything he learned
  at Art Center College of Design (described as "one of the best," a private
  art school) plus additional self-researched material, distilled into one
  course (paraphrase).
- Learning typography — rules about contrast, proportion, and relationships
  between objects — unlocked design for him broadly, applying to 2D layouts,
  3D objects, architectural spaces, building facades, and furniture (paraphrase).
- Course structure (paraphrase): four main ~90-minute modules — (1) contrast
  and repetition (his top ~10–20 typography rules), (2) grids (root 2, root 3,
  golden section, Fibonacci — overlaid on masterworks), (3) typographic details
  and letterform origins (Roman letterforms from circle/square/triangle
  geometry), (4) typographic terms and layout assignments.
- The layout assignment is "borrowed from way back from Basel, Switzerland" —
  described as how designers learn to use typography — delivered as a 7-week
  self-study exercise (paraphrase).
- Bonus content (paraphrase): an Adobe Illustrator keyboard-shortcuts episode
  (with an on-screen key overlay), a live Helvetica work session coaching a
  younger designer, and a session designing a website / landing page by
  deconstructing an existing site.
- The course benefits web/digital designers too, not just print designers
  (paraphrase).
