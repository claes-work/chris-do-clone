# DIY Packaging Design Assignment Portfolio Challenge

## Summary

A short "DIY assignment" episode from The Futur. A design student (referred to as
"Somali"/Sonali on the auto-captions, working with a second person named Aaron) is set
a portfolio challenge: pick an item under $10 from a hardware store and redesign its
packaging, without changing the physical package itself. The student narrates a trip to
the hardware store comparing lightbulb and cleaning-product packaging by perceived value
and price, and selects Glass Plus glass cleaner. Back at Blind (Chris Do's studio),
Chris Do delivers a live design critique of her redesign: challenging her color choices,
pushing on value/contrast between the two colors, the "glass" vs "plus" sub-brand
lock-up, use of negative space, dynamic type cropping against a strong baseline, and a
"modern / Scandinavian / Swiss" simplicity direction.

### Chris Do (persona)

  the assignment is to show that using design raises perceived product value. (paraphrase)

  (paraphrase, restating the student's rationale approvingly)

  because "glass" and "plus" are not equal — "plus" reads as a sub-brand, so it's "Glass

- `[2017-12-26]` A plus sign can be implied rather than drawn — suggesting it be hidden
  inside existing letterforms. (paraphrase)
- `[2017-12-26]` Leave a lot of negative space; place the mark simply and figure out how to
  make the other side "be there but not be there." (paraphrase)
- `[2017-12-26]` Play off the angle and crop the type against a strong baseline alignment
  (e.g. a "27" / "two seven" treatment) to make it dynamic. (paraphrase)
- `[2017-12-26]` Keep the type very simple — "Scandinavian" / "Swiss design" — which can be
  beautiful. (paraphrase)
- `[2017-12-26]` Imagine walking down the store shelf: good packaging should "jump out"
