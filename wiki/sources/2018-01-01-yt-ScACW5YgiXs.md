# Why Being Bad Is Good For Art – 3 min edit

## Summary

A 3-minute edit clipped from a longer Futur live video chat. Host **Mark Contreras**
intros the segment; the substance is delivered almost entirely by guest **Jonah Loeb**,
a conceptual artist and illustrator for video games (e.g. Skyrim). Loeb argues that the
pressure to make "beautiful," portfolio-worthy art paralyzed him at the blank page, so
he deliberately committed to making *bad* art — buying sketchbooks with the sole
intention of producing work he wouldn't like or show anyone. The result: 3–4x more
output and much faster learning. His thesis is that art is not an object to be cherished
and admired but a process of constant exploration and study, one you "dive into and then
extract knowledge from."
