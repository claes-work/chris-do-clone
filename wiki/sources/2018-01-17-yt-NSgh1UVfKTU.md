# How do you Learn Soft Skills? Learning How to Learn.

> Auto-captions present (English). Short clip (~35s of transcript). NO-CAPTIONS: false.

## Summary

Short clip in which Chris Do answers an audience/student question about how to develop
soft and hard skills. Rather than answer directly, Chris uses the Socratic method —
turning the student's own question back on them ("how do you think you acquire it?") to
lead them to the answer. His model: hard skills are learned by looking up a tutorial and
practicing, but you first have to know roughly what the answer looks like to even search
correctly (otherwise you run "a ton of wrong Google searches" and go down the wrong
path). Soft skills (e.g. holding a meeting, delegating, managing and motivating people)
are learned by following a research trail — a topic leads to a book, the book references
case studies, you dig deeper and deeper until you've acquired the skill. He closes by
noting soft skills are much harder to teach and apply than hard skills.

material. The student asking the opening question and giving short answers ("practice",
