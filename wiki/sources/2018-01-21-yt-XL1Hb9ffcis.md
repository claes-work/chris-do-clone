## Summary

A short "Edit"/cutdown excerpted from a longer live talk or workshop (Chris references "a bunch of dudes in this room"), published on The Futur channel. Primary speaker is **Chris Do** teaching sales/marketing fundamentals to creatives. A **co-host** (unnamed in transcript — attribution: uncertain) appears near the end to summarize and hand back.

Core argument: people only hire those they **know, trust, and like**. "Knowing" is the job of marketing. Chris reframes sales away from high-pressure/scare tactics (uses a timeshare pitch as the negative example) toward creating something valuable that draws people in. He distinguishes **outbound** (pushing: ads, cold/warm emails, LinkedIn prospecting) from **inbound** (pulling: creating valuable content that draws people in — e.g. The Futur's own YouTube audience), arguing creatives succeed more with inbound because they are often socially awkward and averse to selling. He closes on consultative selling: diagnose the client's challenges as a curious professional, stay in the diagnostic phase as long as possible, get paid to think rather than make, and avoid jumping prematurely to a solution biased by what you happen to sell.

**Chris Do** (all `[2018-01-21]`):
- (paraphrase) People generally hire those they know; not knowing you means they will hire less talented people they do know instead. People hire who they trust and who they like. `[2018-01-21]`
- (paraphrase) The "knowing" part of know/trust/like is what marketing is for. `[2018-01-21]`
