## Summary

A brief anecdote told from the **employer's side** about an employee who quit the company (Blind/The Futur) gracefully. The employee had, from the beginning, been clear about their career goals; after about a year, having reached a good point, they said some of those goals could not be fulfilled at the company and they wanted to "spread their wings." The employer counter-offered with more money to retain them, the employee declined because the gap between what they wanted and what the role offered was too large, and both sides parted on good terms — the employer even offered future freelance work. The praised behavior: the departing employee did not just hand over two weeks' notice, but offered to finish active projects and negotiate a comfortable, mutually-agreed end date so the company wasn't left "high and dry."
