## Summary

Part 1 of a two-part portfolio review on The Futur's YouTube channel. Host **Greg**
walks through the first graphic-design portfolio of **Emily** ([surname redacted]), a
graphic designer who interned/freelanced at Blind (Chris Do's studio) starting September
2014 and graduated April 2016. The review is retrospective and encouraging: Greg asks
"time-capsule" questions about what Emily was thinking at the time and what she would
change now with hindsight.

Projects walked through:
- A **graphic-design showreel** (unusual for a graphic designer vs. motion designer),
  made because her grad-show wall had large moving screens.
- **Epics network rebrand** (student project) — a Netflix-style TV network; category
  system split by frame background (white = movies, red = sitcoms/comedy, black = live
  sports), reusing brand colors while categorizing.
- **Honda Research America (HRA) rebrand** (sponsored group project, ~2013; ~$2,000
  per-student budget) — connected "HRA" letterforms evoking tire tread; tagline
  "What's next" (no punctuation, works as question and statement). Emily joined the
  group after another member's logo was chosen.
- **Adler Planetarium rebrand** — a connected three-poster triptych (white-on-black,
  high contrast) mixing 2D "orbital"/cartographic graphics with abstract 3D forms
  (made in the Plexus plugin) meant to evoke the space-time continuum.
- **"Hidden Wonder" light-pollution exhibition** (extension of the Adler project) — a
  physical Kinect/Xbox-sensor installation projected onto mosquito-net fabric; walking
  across panels revealed increasing light-pollution levels (Joshua Tree → Los Angeles).
  Posters used the **moiré effect** to encode pollution intensity (harder-to-read
  letters = more pollution).

The review doubles as a light lesson on presenting work: mock-ups and in-context
presentation make design read more clearly and land with more impact.

Ends with a plug for Part 2 (Emily + Greg discussing early Blind branding projects).
