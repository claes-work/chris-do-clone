## Summary

Short conversational clip from The Futur in which Chris Do asks Kyle Cooper about his
creative process and where ideas/concepts come from. Cooper argues that "inspiration is
for amateurs" (quoting artist Chuck Close): rather than browsing tear sheets or
Pinterest, you get down to the work — start talking about the problem, researching it,
cutting things together — and understanding the problem is itself what generates
inspiration. He describes breaking an edit into tiny components (watching once for
kerning, once for color correction, etc.), "taking the next indicated action," and
treating the work as a meditative process of doing one thing at a time. He recounts
that ideas often arrive unbidden — he attributes them partly to God/prayer — with
war stories from Argo (the ironic storyboard/production-drawing concept for the Shah
sequence when stock imagery didn't exist) and The Island of Dr. Moreau (type caught in
spider-webs like flies). Chris frames and closes the piece by recalling Cooper's answer
to a student — "the problem speaks to me, it tells me what it wants to be" — and warns
that designers who arrive wanting to do "another Se7en or Argo" bring too much baggage
to "hear" the problem.

Kyle Cooper is a documented long-standing influence on Chris Do (Chris brought his Art
Center students to Cooper's studio). This clip is primarily a portrait of Cooper's
philosophy, captured and endorsed by Chris.
