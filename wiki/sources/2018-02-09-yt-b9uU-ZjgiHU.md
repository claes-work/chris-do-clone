# Young Graphic Designers Get Coached — Young Guns Ep. 1

## Summary

Series-opening / teaser episode for **Young Guns**, a multi-part The Futur series in
which **[[../entities/chris-do|Chris Do]]** (referred to on-screen as "Christo") mentors
five young designers "coming from different backgrounds and countries with one common
goal: to advance their careers in the business of design." The narrator states the
participants "will be guided by Christo as he coaches them through a series of sessions
where he will learn about our participants and their problems, evaluate their work, and
provide them with guidance to grow in their design careers."

This first episode is almost entirely **introductions**: each coachee states name, age,
location, current work, what makes them unique, why they design, their relationship to
failure, and their parents' role in their careers. **Chris Do does not speak on-camera in
this episode** — his role is established only by the narrator. There is therefore
**no direct Chris-attributed coaching content** here; the substance is the coachees'
self-descriptions (CONTEXT only) plus the framing of what the series will teach.

## Key claims

### Chris Do / series framing (narrator-stated; NOT a direct Chris quote)
- [2018-02-09] (paraphrase) The premise of the series: "it's never too late or too early
  to start a design career." Five young designers from different countries are coached to
  advance in the *business of design*.
- [2018-02-09] (paraphrase) Chris's coaching method, as framed by the narrator: he learns
  about each participant and their problems, evaluates their work, and gives guidance to
  grow their careers. Participants will learn "how to run a creative business and step
  inside the mind of Christo and the way he can analyze what people say and then put that
  into design."
- [2018-02-09] (paraphrase) What the coachees hope to gain: streamline their whole
  professional workflow, deliver more value to clients, and get more balance in life.

### Coachees (CONTEXT — do NOT train persona)
- [2018-02-09] (paraphrase) In Egypt "the general sentiment towards designers is that
  they're just good at Photoshop," so the Cairo coachee repositioned himself as a
  consultant. *(Speaker: Teddy Falco?)*
- [2018-02-09] (paraphrase) Multiple coachees frame **failure as part of learning** — "you
  have to fail to move forwards to grow"; failure is "not a big deal," you fail, do the
  thing again, and learn. *(Various coachees.)*
- [2018-02-09] (paraphrase) Parents play a big role: some are supportive as long as the
  child is happy and has "a roof over their head"; others pushed toward "more traditional"
  or technical careers (engineering) and were initially disappointed. *(Various coachees.)*
