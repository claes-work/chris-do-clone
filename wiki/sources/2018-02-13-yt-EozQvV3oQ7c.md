## Summary

A short, single-narrator teaching clip laying out technical **legibility rules for
setting body text**. The spine is the "**triadic relationship**" — the mutual harmony
between (1) type/point size, (2) line length (measured in characters per line), and
(3) leading (interline spacing) — and the claim that changing any one either helps or
hurts legibility. From there the narrator covers word-shape recognition (we read
word silhouettes before individual letters), why all-caps signage hurts recognition,
how over- or under-tracking destroys legibility, the optimal reading point size
(9–12pt), and how x-height drives both required leading and maximum line length
(character counts of 65 for small x-height, 52 for large x-height). Music plays over
the end; no Q&A or dialogue.

## Key claims

### Narrator (likely Chris Do — attribution: uncertain)
- `[2018-02-13]` There is a **"triadic relationship"** — a harmony between type/point
  size, line length (how many characters are on a line), and interline spacing
  (leading); changing any one of the three either improves or harms legibility.
  (paraphrase)
- `[2018-02-13]` We recognize the **shape of a word before** we can read its individual
  letters. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-02-13]` All-caps signage (e.g. freeway "NORTH"/"SOUTH") is harder to
  distinguish because the letters look too similar; a giant lowercase "n" vs. "s" would
  read faster because the shapes differ more — so **be aware of word/letter shape**.
  (paraphrase)
- `[2018-02-13]` **Too much or too little space between letters destroys legibility**:
  reduced tracking runs letters together and over-open tracking also impairs reading;
  there is an optimal spacing, and the **default tracking is usually fine** for most
  typefaces — messing with it tends to impair legibility. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-02-13]` The most legible **reading-text point size is 9 to 12 points**.
  (paraphrase)
- `[2018-02-13]` **Larger x-height typefaces need more interline spacing** — e.g.
  Helvetica (large x-height) should in theory be opened up (more leading). (paraphrase)
- `[2018-02-13]` **Maximum line length is measured in characters**: ~**65 characters**
  for a small x-height typeface, ~**52 characters** for a large x-height typeface.
  (paraphrase)
