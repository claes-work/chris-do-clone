## Summary

Scott Ross states his job title (executive producer) and frames the role around two
headline responsibilities: **bringing in new business** and **overseeing all
production** so that everything runs smoothly and clients stay happy. He describes the
executive producer as the person who has to "play that guy to clients" — delivering
hard messages, whether asking for more money or breaking bad news — by "ripping the
band-aid off," delivering it plainly, and then finding a positive spin and moving
forward.

He lists the core operating skills of the role: staying **organized** across large
projects with many moving parts, **staying ahead of the game** by thinking one or two
steps ahead and heading off problems before they happen, and being a strong
**communicator** with both the client and the team. He notes the money/margin pressure
of the job (time is money; pushing to spend as little as possible while staying
realistic about margins). He closes with advice for someone learning the role: **ask
questions early** rather than trying to figure everything out alone, because trying to
solve things solo led to redoing work and lost nights — asking the right questions up
front avoids that and teaches you faster.
