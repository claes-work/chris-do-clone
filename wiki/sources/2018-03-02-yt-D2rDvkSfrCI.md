## Summary

Episode 9 of a recurring coaching series on **The Futur**, in which **Chris Do** interviews his mentee **Melinda Livsey** about her progress. Since her prior (discouraged) appearance — when her leads had "dried up" after she raised prices — Melinda reports a turnaround: leads returned around Thanksgiving and she now sells strategy she formerly gave away, having gone from charging **$0 for strategy (a questionnaire she called "strategy")** to **$5,000 for brand strategy alone**, with logo/identity a separate minimum $5,000 on top. She reports 2017 gross billings of ~$80k [self-reported] and a 2018 goal of $180k [self-reported].

The centerpiece is a **live role-play**: Melinda plays a brand strategist taking a discovery call from a prospect (played by Chris) who wants "just a rebrand / just a logo." Chris coaches her through leading with strategy, qualifying budget, using a gap diagram, and — critically — **saying no and referring out** an $5–8k logo-only job that doesn't fit her process. He repeatedly breaks character to critique and teach. The back half probes *why* Melinda succeeded where other coaching-group members stalled: her answer is beliefs (identity not tied to accomplishments), a "just do it / just start" disposition, and a daily gratitude practice.

## Key claims

All dated `[2018-03-02]`. Paraphrased unless in the quotes section.

### Chris Do (persona — coaching / framing)

- Chris (paraphrase): Results take time — "prepare the soil, plant, and you don't get the harvest for some time"; people wrongly expect that doing X yields Y immediately. Melinda's lead turnaround came months after the changes, around Thanksgiving.
- Chris (paraphrase): Charging for strategy means **getting paid to think** — a "good problem to have" versus designers struggling to get $400–500 for a logo. Selling strategy earns "a seat at the table" so you're an advisor, not an "order taker."
- Chris (paraphrase): Separating strategy out as its own line item (rather than lumping it into a package) is itself an act of **validating that the value is there** and that clients will hire you for it; people who "mush it together" lack the confidence to separate it.
- Chris (paraphrase): He endorses Melinda's reframe of "selling" as **aligning with the client's goals** rather than pushing anyone into anything.
- Chris (paraphrase): You must learn to **say no** and refer out work (a logo-only engagement) that won't reach the client's goal — and do it graciously, offering referrals. Standing your ground is praised.
- Chris (paraphrase): With experience, the whole qualifying/strategy pitch can eventually be done **without visuals**, "over the phone while driving to Starbucks"; Melinda currently needs in-person/Skype meetings and visuals because her website still leads with deliverables (case studies not yet built).
- Chris (paraphrase): The initial call qualifies the price; the follow-up gets the prospect into a Skype or in-person meeting where visuals do the persuading.
- Chris (paraphrase): He selects coaching-show guests by **spotting talent**, which is not Instagram following or existing design-star status — he chose Melinda because, given a small task (drawing grids over logos), she took "a kernel" and made it "blow up." That "take a little bit and run with it" spirit is what gets rewarded.
- Chris (paraphrase): A key life edge is focusing on **what you have to gain** rather than what you have to lose — most people fixate on potential loss; few weigh potential gain, and Melinda did the latter.
- Chris (paraphrase): Being "set in your rhythms" for a decade is no barrier — "the only thing that's stopping you is you"; he cites Melinda changing sharply 10 years post-graduation.
- Chris (paraphrase): Warns against **paralysis by analysis / overthinking** — the rare, valuable trait is taking a bit of counter-intuitive advice and moving forward without over-analyzing.

### Melinda Livsey (mentee — CONTEXT; her framework as coached)

- Melinda (paraphrase): Went from charging **$0 for strategy** (a questionnaire she called strategy) to **$5,000** for in-person strategy workshops; brand strategy "starts at 5,000 and can range to 15." [self-reported]
- Melinda (paraphrase): Logo/identity is a separate **minimum $5,000 on top** of strategy; complexity affects the price.
- Melinda (paraphrase): She now **leads with strategy as the only entry point** — she will not take a deliverables-only (logo-only) job; if a prospect refuses strategy she refers them elsewhere.
- Melinda (paraphrase): Her framework hierarchy is **Brand Strategy (core) → Objectives (e.g. UX, marketing funnels, measurable goals) → Deliverables (logo, identity, website)**; starting with the logo first yields "a shell of a brand" with nothing holding it up.
- Melinda (paraphrase): Without strategy and goals, design work is **an expense, not an investment** — "we don't know if you're reaching your goal, so it always ends up being an expense."
- Melinda (paraphrase, in role-play): Qualifies budget by noting marketing efforts are **typically ~5–10% of gross revenue** depending on the goal.
- Melinda (paraphrase, in role-play): Uses a **visual gap diagram** — writes down where the client is now, their ideal, and an obtainable "aim" in the middle — so both parties look at the same picture; distinguishes tasks/vehicles (logo, rebrand) from the actual end goal (e.g. being proud of the company, feeling above competitors).
- Melinda (paraphrase, in role-play): Strategy deliverables include defining the **ideal client**, a **marketing funnel** (awareness → advocacy → referrals that re-feed the funnel), and brand attributes / **positioning statement / value proposition**. Distinguishes a "brand strategy" from a "mission statement / business plan."
- Melinda (paraphrase): A year prior her top package was a **~$6,300 branding package**; 2017 gross billings ~**$80,000**; 2018 goal **$180,000** (up from a prior stated goal of $100k). [self-reported]
- Melinda (paraphrase): Built confidence by doing free/low-stakes strategy sessions first for warm contacts (a cousin, a friend/client, previous clients), improving her strategy document each time; realized "there's magic in this."
- Melinda (paraphrase): Attributes her breakthrough to (1) **beliefs** — her identity/worth is not attached to what she accomplishes or fails at; (2) a **"just do it / just start"** personality (cites agreeing at 16 to teach piano before feeling ready); (3) a **daily gratitude practice** — texting two friends three things she's grateful for every morning — which rewired her toward noticing positives; and (4) **excitement overshadowing fear** (fear is present but the excitement is bigger).
