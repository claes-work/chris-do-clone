## Summary

A solo teaching episode on The Futur's channel in which host **Matthew Encina** walks
through his personal method for networking online — via direct message, cold email, and
social media — to create your own opportunities. His core framework is a four-step
sequence: **be genuine → be curious → add value → go for the ask**, with the first three
mandatory before ever asking for anything. He argues most cold outreach fails because it
is self-serving ("all about me"), likening a premature pitch to "going in for the kiss"
on a first date. He illustrates the framework with three real examples: (1) a Twitter DM
to an influencer podcaster to book them as a cross-promotion guest; (2) a cold email to a
Fast Company writer (Evie) to land press coverage for a Coldplay interactive video his
studio Blind produced; and (3) social-media opportunities that came inbound after he
publicly posted his own 3D design work (a Maxon Cinema 4D community manager invited him to
present at NAB/SIGGRAPH; a Philippines event organizer flew him out to speak). Recurring
themes: research the person first, make it about them, make the ask super easy, treat
online interaction like a "digital cocktail party," overcome fear of rejection, and
remember "if you don't ask, the answer will always be no."

## Key claims

_All claims below are attributed to **Matthew Encina** (context, NOT the subject)._

- [2018-03-23] (paraphrase) Everyone has unlimited potential to create their own
  opportunities regardless of where they are or how they grew up. Matthew notes he grew up
  a very introverted, shy creative and developed tools over time to socialize.
- [2018-03-23] (paraphrase) Most cold emails follow the format "hey I work for this
  company, we offer these services, get in touch" and almost never get a reply — they fail
  because they are self-serving and impersonal, feeling like one of a thousand on a list.
- [2018-03-23] (paraphrase) Premature pitching is like meeting someone on a first date and
  immediately going in for the kiss — it doesn't work because you didn't make it about the
  other person.
- [2018-03-23] (paraphrase) The four-step outreach framework: **be genuine, be curious,
  add value, then go for the ask** — you must do the first three before the ask, or it
  sounds robotic and copy-pasted.
- [2018-03-23] (paraphrase) Networking outreach mirrors client strategy work: understand
  the person's problems, needs, wants, and desires ("what are your problems, what's
  getting in the way, how can I help you") before offering anything.
- [2018-03-23] (paraphrase) DM example: Matthew DM'd an influencer podcaster on Twitter,
  opening with a genuine compliment ("devouring your show, keep killing it"), then asked a
  curious open-ended question, then offered value (guesting to reach The Futur's ~160k
  creative audience for mutual cross-promotion), then made the ask — which succeeded.
- [2018-03-23] (paraphrase) Roughly 90% of people will respond to a genuine compliment,
  even if only a thumbs-up or emoji — it is a good conversation starter.
- [2018-03-23] (paraphrase) Email differs from DM: you often get only one shot, so get to
  the point, make it super easy, and do the research up front.
- [2018-03-23] (paraphrase) Fast Company example: wanting to get published with no
  contacts, he read the site, identified ~10 authors/editors in the music section,
  researched them on Twitter and LinkedIn, and cold-emailed a writer (Evie) offering
  exclusive behind-the-scenes content of a Coldplay video his studio Blind made — packaged
  into one easy PDF with images, video, and a celebrity name-drop (Chris Martin) — closing
  by asking for a referral if it wasn't a fit. This one succeeded.
- [2018-03-23] (paraphrase) On social media, treat it as a public forum / "digital
  cocktail party": have as many conversations as possible, be genuine, curious, friendly,
  a "good human being," and don't be a troll.
- [2018-03-23] (paraphrase) Philippines example: by building relationships online with an
  event organizer (Raymond), then commenting to volunteer when they crowdsourced a sixth
  speaker, Matthew was flown to the Philippines all-expenses-paid to speak to a few hundred
  people.
- [2018-03-23] (paraphrase) SIGGRAPH/NAB example: after posting his own 3D renders on
  Instagram/Twitter (tagging software developers and using trendy hashtags), a Maxon
  Cinema 4D community manager DM'd him to invite him to present — in the very first week of
  posting.
- [2018-03-23] (paraphrase) Creatives, introverts, and business owners fear rejection and
  focus on the 99 reasons someone will say no instead of the one reason they'll say yes —
  switch the mindset to "why not" and focus on the value you can bring.
- [2018-03-23] (paraphrase) If you don't ask, the answer will always be no — 100% of the
  time.
