# Conscious Capitalism & Working With Non-Profits

> Format: The Futur LIVE event (Santa Monica). **Panel/interview** — Chris Do hosts and
> debates guest **Matthew Manos**, founder of the design-strategy consultancy *very nice*
> and author of *How to Give Half of Your Work Away for Free* (2012–2013). Audience Q&A
> throughout. Only Chris Do's lines train the persona; Matthew Manos is **CONTEXT** (a
> guest, not the subject). See `wiki/entities/` if a context page is warranted.

## Summary

A live conversation and friendly debate on conscious capitalism, pro-bono work, and
serving non-profits. Guest Matthew Manos presents his "give half" model: his firm
*very nice* donates 50% of its work to non-profits for free, powered by a ~600-person
volunteer/freelancer network managed by a small ~10-person core team. He frames a
business as balancing two axes — **impact** and **revenue** (a Venn diagram) — and argues
that a purely revenue-focused business risks burnout from lack of fulfillment.

Chris Do plays the counterpoint: he positions himself and Blind/The Futur as
profit-driven while still giving significant work away for free (the free educational
videos). The two find common ground on using free/pro-bono work as a way to **prove a new
service to yourself** and unlock more profitable positioning, and on **brand-as-filter** —
that clear, staked-out messaging attracts the right clients and repels the wrong ones.
Chris challenges Manos's model with the alternative "charge 100% and donate 50% of profit
instead," and Manos defends giving *services* over cash because talent multiplies impact
and removes trust risk over how money gets spent.

### Chris Do (SUBJECT — trains persona)

- `[2018-04-06]` The Futur / Blind also transitioned from visual design to design

  (paraphrase)
- `[2018-04-06]` Money is a measurement of success and any business should measure it that
  way; he is "still trying to find the ceiling" of how much they can charge. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-04-06]` His early (self-described "dumb") measure of success when starting Blind
  in 1995 was simply being able to have three or four employees on staff. (paraphrase)
  [self-reported]
- `[2018-04-06]` "Blind is a service company that's driven by profit and the bottom line" —
  while at the same time producing videos that cost a lot to make and giving them away free.
  (paraphrase / near-quote)
- `[2018-04-06]` The Futur's free videos are Chris's own "give back" model — people can get
  the chops they need without spending a lot of money on college. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-04-06]` Counter-model posed to Manos: why not **charge 100% and donate 50% of your

- `[2018-04-06]` Your site, work, how you speak, and what you talk about should all act as a

### Matthew Manos (GUEST — CONTEXT, does NOT train persona)

- `[2018-04-06]` US non-profits spend ~**$8 billion/year** on design and marketing
  professionals; there are ~1 million non-profits in the US. (paraphrase) [self-reported]
- `[2018-04-06]` "Pro bono" (short for *pro bono publico*) literally means "for the good of
  the public," not "for free"; the movement began in the late-1800s legal industry
  (Reginald Heber Smith, *Justice and the Poor*) and reached design/marketing via the Ad
  Council in the 1950s–60s. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-04-06]` *very nice* has given away **over $7.5 million** in services to **500+
  organizations** (clients include Google, UNICEF, NASA, MTV); it turns 10 in April 2018,
  founded 2008 from a UCLA dorm room. (paraphrase) [self-reported]
- `[2018-04-06]` The "give half" 50% figure is philosophical: anything you spend at least
  half your time on is no longer extracurricular — it's core to who you are. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-04-06]` Pro-bono work is **hard to write off on taxes** because volunteer hours
  aren't documented paid costs; giving money or physical goods is easier to deduct.
  (paraphrase)
- `[2018-04-06]` Business benefits of pro bono: networking (his largest clients came this
  way), portfolio-building in new spaces, and pivoting services — *very nice* pivoted from
  visual design to design strategy (now ~50/50) by offering strategy free first to gain
  credibility. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-04-06]` He uses **pro-bono contracts** stating the dollar value the service would
  have cost — this kills scope creep and forces the non-profit to re-value the service.
  (paraphrase)
- `[2018-04-06]` Picking non-profits: prefer **specific, tangible missions** ("end hunger
  between 5th and 6th Street") over grandiose ones ("end world hunger"); cites *dig deep*
  (water rights, one New Mexico reservation) as a model. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-04-06]` Prefers giving services over cash because talent is a multiplier and he
  distrusts how donated money gets spent (e.g., on a designer who does a bad job). (paraphrase)
