# 4 Tips To Grow As a Designer. Career Advice in 5 mins.

> Source note: this is a ~5-minute edited compilation of Chris Do answering audience
> question cards (written down "last night") on the topics of fear, expertise, the
> future of design, and work-life balance. Excerpt/edit — questioners are named on
> the cards but only Chris speaks on camera.

## Summary

Chris Do responds to four audience questions in rapid succession, delivering one piece
of career advice per question:

1. **On fear / "choosing the right path"** — reframes the premise: framing a choice as
   right-vs-wrong (binary) is what intimidates people out of trying. There is just *a*
   path; take it, evaluate as you go, and switch without judgment if it feels wrong.
2. **On standing out** — go "an inch wide and a mile deep." Don't overwhelm clients with
   everything you can do (like a 300-item menu); tell them the one thing you do and do it
   really well, so you are perceived as an expert and are easy to hire.
3. **On the future of design** — traditional/classical graphic design is "dead or dying";
   build a community around shared beliefs, values, and aesthetics, and leverage that
   audience to make money (cites calligrapher Seb Lester as an example of "printing money"
   off an audience rather than doing service work).
4. **On work-life balance** (asked by "Peter," a full-timer who freelances web design
   after-hours) — use your steady income as runway to raise freelance rates, develop
   art-director/creative-director skills, delegate work to a global network of creatives,
   and grow toward entrepreneurship. Also blur the lines: overlap work with life (Chris
   travels and vlogs with friends/coworkers he works with, and soon his whole family).

  evaluate as you go, and choose another without judgment if it doesn't feel right. (paraphrase)

  with 300 items, too much choice is confusing. Don't make it harder for a client or
  agency to hire you; make it easier. (paraphrase)
- [2018-04-14] Tell clients the one thing you do, show them one thing, and do it really
  well — "go an inch wide and a mile deep" — to become and be perceived as an expert. (paraphrase)
- [2018-04-14] Traditional/classical graphic design is "dead or dying." (paraphrase)

  longer does service work — he can make prints and sell them to his audience, "in essence

- [2018-04-14] A full-timer who freelances after-hours has steady income and runway, so
  they should start raising their freelance rates. (paraphrase)
