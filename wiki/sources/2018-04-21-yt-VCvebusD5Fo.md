# Learn Self Acceptance / Self Confidence By Letting Go Of Ego & Being Yourself

> Captions present (manual/auto `.en.txt`). Not a no-captions source.

## Summary

Solo talk by **Chris Do** (The Futur) delivered to a live audience. A single-speaker
monologue built around an original named framework Chris calls the **"Mirrormask."** The
thesis: everyone walks around holding up a mask they mistake for a mirror — an idealized
self-image (better-looking, taller, smarter) — and the effort of holding that mask up
drains enormous energy. The liberating realization is that **the world already sees you
exactly as you are** and has already chosen to accept or reject you, so the mask is wasted
effort — "ditch the Mirrormask."

Chris illustrates with two personal stories: (1) judging a broadcast-design show at a
swanky club feeling like a fraud/imposter despite ten years running his company and many
awards; (2) his acute discomfort during the first ~10 The Futur YouTube videos — clenched
jaw, hating the sound of his own voice, banning staff from editing the videos within eight
feet of him. He resolves it by accepting that he does sound "terrible" but everyone already
hears him that way and is fine with it. The final move is an **ego-kill / be-of-service**
reframe (drawn from a Zen-master anecdote his wife showed him): stop trying to be the smart
star on stage, silently wish the audience well, and enter intimidating rooms (of powerful
decision-makers) as someone there to help rather than to sell or prove himself — which is
paradoxically read as confidence.
