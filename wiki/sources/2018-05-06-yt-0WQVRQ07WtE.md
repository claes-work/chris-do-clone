# Believe In Yourself — Power of Self Confidence

## Summary

A short excerpt from a live Chris Do talk/workshop (The Futur). **Chris Do is the sole
presenter/persona voice** throughout; a few lines are brief answers shouted from the
audience during a Q&A moment (marked **[audience / context]**, attribution uncertain).
No family or private individuals are named. "Karen" is named as a professional protégé
Chris has been training, not a private/family relation, so she is retained as context.

The distinctive angle here (versus the repo's other confidence/imposter-syndrome pages)
is Chris reframing self-confidence as **not needing external validation at all**. His
thesis: in a sales/pricing conversation, "I am nothing — I'm just a mirror to you." Value
and persuasion come from making the interaction entirely *about the other person* (via a
question-based, therapy-style process), not from your credentials — portfolio, prestigious
school, awards, reputation, budget, even your voice or looks. Because none of it is about
you, you don't need any of those things to perform, which is exactly what makes the idea
"empowering." He names this the "Zen and the art of being nothing and everything" framing
(his talk title). He recounts a thought-experiment / role-play test he offered a doubting
protégé (Karen): the two of them meet the same client cold — no known portfolio, company,
reputation, awards, or budget — using "just words." He closes on the idea that the props
we demand (credentials, a good voice, the right body language) are **"crutches"** we invoke
to explain why we can't do something; the real skill is learning to ask questions and to
"stop trying to convince — just help the person realize the answer."

## Key claims

> Attribution note: All substantive claims are Chris Do's (**[Chris Do / persona]**).
> Two short prompts are audience answers during Q&A (**[audience / context]**,
> attribution uncertain).

- [Chris Do / persona] (2018-05-06) (paraphrase) Pricing, selling prestige, and mitigating
  a client's fear "has nothing to do with me" — the entire dynamic is about the client, not
  the seller. Hence his talk title "Zen and the art of being nothing and everything."
- [Chris Do / persona] (2018-05-06) (paraphrase) In the client relationship "I am nothing —
  I'm just a mirror to you," and that is empowering precisely because you can be "of no
  means, of no acclaim, with no background" and still create value; you don't need a degree
  from a prestigious school or a portfolio.
- [Chris Do / persona] (2018-05-06) (paraphrase) The model is therapy: therapists "invert
  it" to make it all about you and use a question-based process to help you self-discover.
  Persuasion should copy this — "stop trying to convince; just help the person realize the
  answer."
- [Chris Do / persona] (2018-05-06) (paraphrase) Role-play/thought experiment offered to a
  protégé (Karen) who insisted she couldn't do these things: the two of them meet the exact
  same client cold, with the client knowing nothing of either one's portfolio, company,
  reputation, awards, or budget — Chris claims he could still win using "just words."
- [audience / context] (2018-05-06) (paraphrase) [attribution: uncertain] Audience answers
  to Chris's "how do I demonstrate value in that moment?": you project confidence; it's in
  your words, how you speak, how you look at the person, and body language.
- [Chris Do / persona] (2018-05-06) (paraphrase) He strips each prop away to prove the point:
  on the phone you lose looks and body language, and even "I hate my voice" — the excuses
  keep collapsing, showing they were never the real lever.
- [Chris Do / persona] (2018-05-06) (paraphrase) The credentials and traits people cite are
  "props" / "crutches" we "put in front of us just to say why we can't do something, why we
  can't achieve something."
- [Chris Do / persona] (2018-05-06) (paraphrase) The teachable core is simple: learn how to
  ask questions and make it about them and not about you — "you'll do really well… you just
  have to learn how to ask, that's all."
