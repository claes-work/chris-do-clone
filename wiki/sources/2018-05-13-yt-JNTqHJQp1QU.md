## Summary

A short solo mastermind talk by Kier McLaren on designing your life around a concrete
financial and personal target, then removing the people and habits that block it. His
throughline: most people never define exactly how much money they need or what life they
actually want, and many of the people in their lives were "chosen for the wrong reasons."
He argues that surrounding yourself with positive people and thinking positively changes
the world you perceive ("you get the world you see"; the world itself is neutral). He
frames personal change as a threat to others — using a Calder mobile as a metaphor: when
you move, everyone connected to you is forced to move or push you out. He closes on the
"price of change": most people won't change, roughly 80% stay locked in the past and 20%
are willing, and if you need others' approval to feel okay you can't change. Aimed at
creatives/artists told their path "isn't a career."
