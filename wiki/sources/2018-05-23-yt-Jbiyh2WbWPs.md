# What's Your Motivation? The Empty Pursuit of Fame — Ep. 8 w/ Keir McLaren

Mastermind series, Episode 8. Single-speaker monologue by Kier McLaren. No Chris Do
audio present. Auto-captions available (English).

## Summary

Kier McLaren delivers a solo talk on motivation and the emptiness of chasing external
recognition. Speaking from his own life, he recounts a string of successes — athletic
scholarships, being published in Harper's, radio, business, optioning a screenplay —
each of which lost its meaning for him the moment he achieved it, because the real
drive was to feel okay about himself from the outside. He anchors the talk in a low
point: sitting on a curb in Burbank, in his 40s, broke, with a dead car battery he
couldn't afford, having just optioned a screenplay that "cost me a marriage" — and
still not feeling okay. His core message to the (creator/vlogger) audience: examine
what you actually expect to get out of your ambitions, distinguish wanting the craft
from wanting the recognition, and learn to source validation internally rather than
from fame, money, or being well-known.

## Notable verbatim quotes

All quotes are **Kier McLaren (CONTEXT)**.

- "Everything — as soon as I tried something, I had success, and I still felt crappy
  about myself. I just stopped doing it... I was doing [it] because I wanted the
  recognition. I didn't realize then I had to get it from inside of me. I thought I
  could get it from outside."
- "I still don't feel okay about myself, because everything that I did was to feel good
  about myself — to get it from somewhere else."
- "There are two kinds of people [in Hollywood]: people who want to be actors, and
  people who want to be stars... [the actors] are always acting and improving their
  talent and their skills because they want to act. There are other people who just
  want to be stars because they need the recognition."
- "The better-looking they are and the more successful they are, the more insecure they
  are, because they're looking to the outside to feel good about themselves."
- "Examine what you think you're gonna get out of it... Is it because you have a great
  message to tell that I need to hear, and that's just the vehicle for it? Is it because
  you want to be famous or you want to be rich or you want to be well-known?"
- "I'm good at motivating people and helping them find their journey. That's what I
