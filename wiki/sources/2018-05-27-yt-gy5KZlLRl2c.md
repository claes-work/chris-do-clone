# What Failure Can Teach You About Humility — Ep. 10 w/ Keir McLaren

## Summary

A short (~3.5 min) solo reflection by Kier McLaren on failure as the teacher of
humility. Kier recounts a lifetime of "enormous failures" — two divorces, crashed
businesses, big risks — and argues you only truly learn from failure, and what you learn
is humility: that you don't have all the answers and aren't as smart as you think. He
warns against believing he "has all the answers," attributes much of his success to luck
and serendipity ("a spirit in the universe that helps you"), and stresses that growth
comes from associating with smart, self-aware people and staying open to better ideas.

The centerpiece is an anecdote: years ago, worn out during the Christmas ad-business
rush and worried about an unexplained health symptom he was avoiding, Kier sat next to a
disheveled "town drunk" at a New Hampshire pizza shop. The man — half-drunk — described
how he immediately went to the doctor when he noticed skin cancer, unlike people who
avoid the doctor out of fear and die. Kier realized *he* was that avoidant person,
booked an appointment the next day (it turned out to be nothing), and learned humility
from a man the whole town, himself included, looked down on. He closes with a
12-steps-inspired "one day at a time" philosophy: small daily changes compound, many
problems the universe solves for you if you stay calm, quiet, and receptive rather than
drowning in distraction and noise.
