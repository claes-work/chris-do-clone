# Gain Clarity w/ Clients Using Objective Criteria — Dr. Samuel Holtzman Pt. 1

> ⚠️ ATTRIBUTION NOTE: This is a **guest episode**. Most of the substantive content is
> spoken by **Dr. Samuel Holtzman** (guest expert — CONTEXT only, does NOT train the
> persona). Only **Chris Do's** own intro and closing reflection are persona-relevant.
> A mid-video question from a Blind team member is of **uncertain** attribution.

## Summary

Part 1 of a series filmed at Blind (Chris Do's design studio). Chris introduces
Dr. Samuel Holtzman — described as someone who "teaches teachers how to teach better" —
whom Chris invited to Blind so the team could learn to talk to clients with empathy.
Holtzman then delivers the core teaching: everyone (designer, team, and client) views a
project through their own "conceptual framework" — a set of lenses shaped by experience,
brand, company, and expectations. To bridge the gap between the studio's framework and
the client's, he advocates a **facilitation / criterion-driven** approach: get the client
to define four or five concrete criteria for what they expect from the project. His
method is to start with a broad question, narrow down to a specific, then drill back out
to the big picture ("start big → narrow → back out"). He warns against diving into the
final deliverable too fast (pulling the project toward the studio's framework and away
from the client's original intent), and recommends phrasing discovery as open-ended but
specific "I-statements" ("I've done things like this in the past — is that what you're
looking for?") because they're easier for a client to answer than "tell me what you
expect." Chris closes by reflecting on what he took from working with Holtzman at Art
Center: how to extract what's in his own head and share it with a group so everyone views
the problem the same way.

### Dr. Samuel Holtzman (CONTEXT — guest, does NOT train the persona)

- `[2018-06-05]` Everyone perceives through a personal **"conceptual framework"** — a set
  of lenses built from experience and how one looks at and approaches things. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-06-05]` Inside an organization, additional shared filters apply — notably the
  brand, a shared icon/structure everyone in the org works within. Clients arrive with
  their own conceptual framework, brand, company, and expectations. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-06-05]` **Facilitation** is the work of figuring out the client's expectations
  and criteria — what they want from the project. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-06-05]` The easiest way is **criterion-driven**: get the client to define ~4–5
  things they really expect as criteria. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-06-05]` Structure discovery as **start big → narrow to a specific → drill back
  out to big**; this frames the space you can play in. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-06-05]` Diving too fast into the final deliverable pulls the project toward the
  studio's conceptual framework and away from the client's original intentions.
  (paraphrase)
- `[2018-06-05]` Use open-ended but specific questions, often opening with **"I"
  statements** — easier for a client to answer than "tell me what you expect." (paraphrase)
