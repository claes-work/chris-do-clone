> ★ **L3-candidate** — Contains a distinct, quotable Chris Do client-selection framework: *resign/fire a bad client, refund their money, and pay them to leave* ("here's your money back, good luck with your life"), plus the discipline to reject flattering-but-underpaying "ugly clients." ## Summary

A short Q&A clip (from a live The Futur talk/audience session) in which **Chris Do** answers two audience questions: which client work was most rewarding, and which clients he would never work with again. He names **music videos for bands** as his most creatively rewarding work — soul-filling but financially unsustainable, because the studio routinely spends as much finishing the job as the band pays. He then describes firing ("resigning") bad clients: refunding their money and telling them to leave, framed with an analogy to ending a bad romantic relationship. He closes by warning creatives against being seduced by flattery from clients who praise their talent but offer little money or only "exposure," and urges the courage to tell a client they are not a good fit.
