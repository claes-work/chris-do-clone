# Get Clear & Avoid Creative Block — Fuzzy Goals Equals Fuzzy Results

> Auto-captions available (English). Transcript is a single continuous clip (~103 lines); light word-level noise typical of auto-captions.

## Summary

Chris Do fields a live question from a design student who gets "stuck" mid-project and
can't tell whether the work is good or how to get unstuck. Chris reframes creative block
as a symptom of an unclear goal. He contrasts linear, mathematical problem-solving
(A→B→C→D toward a known Z) with creative work, where being in "the muck" feels
directionless because there is "no compass, no destination, no map." His remedy: define
the goal with increasing specificity — attaching concrete words and images to it — so a
vague "rock" becomes a recognizable "arch." He critiques the abstract language students
use for concepts ("it's bold, it's beautiful, it's innovative, it's fresh, it's modern")
as too fuzzy to guide work. Nailing the goal down to two words, translating those words
into images, and then defining a process/roadmap ("your creative recipe... a formula for
success") is the path out of the block. The talk was delivered to a student audience
(references "Jung's class" and asks the student's semester/year).
