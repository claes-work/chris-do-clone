# Advice for Students — My Life at Design College

★ **L3-candidate** — reason: rich, dense **biography** material on Chris Do's design-college years (which school, how he paid for it, what kind of student he was) plus a formative **18-to-19 turning-point** narrative. Strong feed for `persona/biography.md`. Promote at a synthesis pass.

## Summary

An interview segment where Chris Do answers audience/host questions about his own time at **ArtCenter College of Design** ("Art Center"). He describes himself as an obsessive, hardworking, overachieving student — frequent all-nighters, sleeping at the school, chasing the top scholarship-review score, and taking the hardest classes outside his major (drawing with product designers, film with film students). He debunks the myth that ArtCenter students are all rich: he came from an upper-middle-class family, his mother paid roughly half his tuition, the rest came from Stafford student loans, maxed-out credit cards, computer-lab work, and freelance jobs. He entered on a 50% scholarship and had most of his tuition covered by the school by graduation, then paid off his credit-card and student-loan debt within about a year to a year and a half of finishing. He closes on the pivotal **18-to-19 transition**: a low emotional point during a bad relationship, feeling like a "loser" who had squandered an opportunity, from which he made a hard internal decision to change his life and get himself into ArtCenter no matter how long it took. Recurring theme: comfort makes people "soft"; having no plan B forced his drive.
