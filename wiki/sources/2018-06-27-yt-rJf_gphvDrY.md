## Summary

A sponsored, crew-led how-to covering four in-house video-production hacks used at The
Futur: (1) a battery/memory-card organization system, (2) clear on-set communication via
Cobra walkie-talkies, (3) shooting better B-roll, and (4) a low-budget DIY lavalier mic
using a phone's earbud headset. Presented by Aaron and Mark; sponsored by Cobra PTT / USA
(Trekker Tech). Production mechanics only — no Chris Do teaching content.

## Key claims (by speaker)

### Aaron & Mark — Futur crew (production mechanics)
- `[2018-06-27]` Organization: keep charged and dead batteries in separate bags so you
  never fumble for a working battery mid-shoot. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-06-27]` Label memory cards using a notebook, pen, and cheap tape — note what
  camera/audio/talent is on each card so you can name folders correctly days later when
  editing. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-06-27]` Clear on-set communication is "the key to running a tight production";
  be direct and be nice about it. They use Cobra ChatTag walkie-talkies (lightweight,
  wearable, 8-mile range) to coordinate across the studio. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-06-27]` Two kinds of B-roll: "establishing" (paints a picture of where you are)
  and "transition/action" B-roll (moving from one thing to the next); catch quirky,
  candid moments — "shoot what nobody else is shooting, or shoot it like no one else is
  shooting." (paraphrase / partial verbatim)
- `[2018-06-27]` DIY lavalier: record with a phone's Voice Memos app and the mic on the
  bundled earbud headset, placed close to the mouth — the video's own audio was recorded
  this way. Credited to a crew member (Spencer). (paraphrase)
- `[2018-06-27]` Closing recap: be organized (use a system that works for you), keep clear
  communication, make the best of B-roll, and don't let budget hold you back. (paraphrase)
