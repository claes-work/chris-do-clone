# Why I Don't Work On Design Projects Anymore

★ **L3-candidate** — contains a landmark CEO/identity-shift framework ("making a difference vs. making things"; maker mindset vs. entrepreneurial mindset) plus dated biographical facts about when and why Chris Do stopped doing hands-on design work. Strong feeder for `persona/biography.md` and `persona/beliefs.md`.

## Summary

Short Q&A clip (likely from a live workshop/audience session) in which Chris Do answers an audience member ("Shane") about whether he still does hands-on design work now that he runs the business. Chris explains that most of his day is spent planning, casting vision, managing, writing task lists for his team, and reviewing/giving feedback — leaving almost no time to "make things." He frames a personal evolution: over his life he "learned to love making a difference more than making things." He describes design work now as a "guilty indulgence" he occasionally indulges, and reframes even that as learning-in-order-to-teach rather than practicing his own craft. He then narrates the historical mechanism of his transition: in his first year in business, demand exceeded his personal capacity, so he brought in friends to do the work while he art-directed, discovered he could add ~10% effort to others' work and make it "twice as good," scaled up by hiring people better than himself, and shifted into the responsibility of keeping revenue flowing to keep the team fed and elevated. He labels the two orientations the "maker mindset" vs. the "entrepreneurial mindset," insisting neither is wrong — just different paths.

Speaker confirmation: single primary speaker is **Chris Do** (referred to as running a 22-year-old business and a newer 3-year-old one — consistent with Blind founded ~1995 and The Futur founded ~2015). Questions come from an audience member named **Shane** (CONTEXT, not persona). "Jung" is mentioned only as someone wearing a monogram logo (CONTEXT).
