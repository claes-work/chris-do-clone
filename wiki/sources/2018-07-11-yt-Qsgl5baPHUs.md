## Summary

A ~90-second tactical primer on growing an Instagram profile, structured around
four categories: **strategy, following, hashtags, and stories**. The instructor
advises defining your brand (story, personality, vision), switching to a
business profile, building a social-media calendar, and producing relevant
content. On following, they stress defining a target audience (demographics,
age, gender, desires, industry) and leveraging similar/adjacent pages'
communities. Hashtags are framed as a reach tool that should be used wisely and
kept relevant. Stories are pitched as a low-risk, interactive space to
experiment with content, link out, and add calls to action — either temporary
(24h) or archived for the long term.

## Key claims (grouped by speaker)

### Unnamed Futur instructor (NOT Chris Do — context only)
- `[2018-07-11]` Instagram growth breaks into four levers: strategy, following,
  hashtags, and stories.
- `[2018-07-11]` Define your brand first — your story, why you exist, what makes
  you different, your personality (funny/serious/educational), and your vision;

- `[2018-07-11]` If you want to be seen as a brand rather than an individual,
  switch to a business profile.
- `[2018-07-11]` Use a social-media calendar to plan and split content across
  the relevant platforms.
- `[2018-07-11]` Define your target audience (demographics, gender, age range,
  desires, likes/dislikes, industry) so you can make content that suits them
  perfectly.
- `[2018-07-11]` Grow by engaging with similar pages' already-built, engaged
  communities.
- `[2018-07-11]` Hashtags are a tool to reach *relevant* people who will engage;
  use them wisely and keep them relevant (don't put #vegan on a photo of meat).
- `[2018-07-11]` Stories are temporary, so they're a low-risk place to experiment
  with content without cluttering your feed; they're interactive, good for
  linking out and calls to action, and can be kept 24h or archived long-term.
