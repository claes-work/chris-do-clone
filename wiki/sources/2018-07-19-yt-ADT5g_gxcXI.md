## Summary

Kier McLaren delivers a solo talk on why people make "bad" decisions and struggle to
decide for themselves. His core thesis: every decision you make serves your ego and
feeds a hidden need — even destructive ones give you something. Procrastination and
decision-avoidance are traced to childhood: people who once paid a high emotional or
physical price for a wrong decision adopt the strategy of not deciding at all, then
carry that strategy unchanged into adulthood ("using ten-year-old strategies to run a
forty-year-old life"). He prescribes self-examination — including a left-hand/right-hand
journaling technique to access "little you" — and neuroplasticity ("rewiring"): old
mental pathways are deepened each time you repeat a pattern, but new pathways can be
built by forcing different behavior for 30–60 days. He frames this with a spiritual
metaphor (Old Testament/Jesus references) about how the "wisdom of the ages" intuited
what UCLA science now proves, and about leaving old beliefs behind to form your own way
of being in the world.
