# How To Work Less And Make More

★ **L3-candidate** — This is a live, end-to-end demonstration of Chris Do's core value-based-pricing sales conversation: surfacing the client's desired future state, attaching a dollar value to each outcome, and reframing pricing away from hours (inputs) toward outcomes (outputs). It shows the *mechanics* of the pitch in dialogue, not just the principle, so it is a strong candidate for promotion into the value-based-pricing and delegation topic/persona pages.

## Summary

A recorded coaching session in which Chris Do role-plays as a consultant selling to a freelance client (referred to only as "Ron"). Ron runs a freelancing business (~$200K/year, aiming for $300K) plus a nascent productized/recurring-revenue business, but freelancing consumes nearly all his time. Chris deliberately runs the session as a "meta" exercise: he treats Ron as a client to *demonstrate* how he sells, while simultaneously teaching Ron to do the same.

Chris walks through his method live: (1) surface the client's "desired future state" 2–5 years out (more free time, being paid to think/consult rather than execute, building passive/recurring income); (2) quantify the value of each desired outcome to the client rather than pricing by the hour; (3) reframe pricing from "inputs" (hours) to "outputs" (defined deliverables), attributing the framing to "Peter Blair"; and (4) systematize/delegate so the expert "parachutes in" only for the high-level thinking. He also gestures at the non-financial value of mentoring/building a team. The through-line: stop selling time, start selling outcomes, so you can work less and earn more.

## Key claims

### Chris Do (subject)

- `[2018-07-23]` The dream of all creatives is to do something once, really well, and get paid for it for the rest of their life — and "it's not a dream if you know how to do it." (near-verbatim / paraphrase)
- `[2018-07-23]` Method step 1 — surface the client's "desired future state," but keep it grounded 2–5 years out; going further ("pie in the sky") makes it too dreamy to price. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-07-23]` If a client makes $300K in 30 hours/week and you halve their working time to 15 hours, you have "done twice as much work in half the time," so the first-year value of that time savings is ~$150K — and it recurs in years 2, 3, 4. (paraphrase; illustrative math applied to the client's numbers)
- `[2018-07-23]` The value is not just the money saved but the emotional value: coming in to do only "the sale, all the high-level thinking" and none of "the garbage" — running the business "on autopilot and keeping all the fun parts." (paraphrase)
- `[2018-07-23]` Stop measuring/selling time as a freelancer. Move toward a project-based model and "assign a higher dollar value to the output." (paraphrase)
- `[2018-07-23]` Distinguishes two ways of bidding/estimating projects, attributed to "Peter Blair": "Inputs" = selling time (cost = how many hours you input); "Outputs" = you don't care how many hours or people are involved, only that the final product matches spec, delivered on time. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-07-23]` One workable model: sell a defined 2–3 week sprint where you define what "done" looks like (an MVP), then use your team to run it. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-07-23]` You do NOT need to hit $500/hour before your first hire — the plan is to move off hourly entirely, not to maximize the hourly rate first. (paraphrase; corrects the client's assumption)
- `[2018-07-23]` Build a team and systems around the expert so they "parachute in" only for critical thinking work; the team preps him, handles the client, and delivers with "impeccable delivery" and smooth customer service. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-07-23]` There is real (if hard-to-account) value in mentoring: teaching a young hire something that improves their life "forever," so that even "if you get hit by a truck tomorrow" that person can sustain themselves and a family. (paraphrase)
