## Summary

A short documentary-style episode. Ben Burns and a Futur teammate are on their way to
an in-person pitch with a brand-strategy prospect ("a new lead") they've been nurturing
for ~2 years, who has just come into a round of investment and is vetting options. A
"champion on the inside" (their contact) wants them to "razzle-dazzle them but not
overwhelm them." Ben is nervous — it's his first in-person lead of this kind, versus
phone calls where body language and appearance don't matter.

The team runs a practice pitch (Ben rehearses the intro cold, "going through the
motions"), then unpacks the *source* of the nerves — imposter syndrome and performance
anxiety ("six years ago putting drug dealers in jail and here I am"). The reframe that
"takes the pressure off": the meeting is not about proving themselves or validating what
they do, it's about serving the client and finding out whether it's a mutual fit — the
team is genuinely willing to walk away. After the meeting they debrief at a coffee shop:
it went well, they communicated clearly, told stories rather than dumping data, asked
high-value questions, and shifted the pressure onto the client.

Relation to the **"Pitch This!" presenting doctrine:** same family of ideas, but the
**distinct angle here is the *mindset / pressure-relief* dimension of presenting**, not
stagecraft. The load-bearing moves are (1) reframe the meeting as *serving, not proving*,
(2) name and dissolve imposter syndrome/performance anxiety before walking in, (3) be
willing to walk away as the source of calm/leverage, and (4) stories over data because
people remember and see themselves in stories.

## Key claims

### Ben Burns / Futur teammate (context — Chris-Do philosophy channeled)
- [2018-07-31] In-person meetings add variables a phone call doesn't — appearance and
  body language communicate, so there's more to be self-aware of. (paraphrase)
- [2018-07-31] The nerves are mostly imposter syndrome and performance anxiety —
  self-imposed pressure to do a good job for the company owner, the teammate, and Chris.
  (paraphrase)
- [2018-07-31] "Any kind of power you give up, you're already losing" — the goal is to
  sit down and figure out if it's a good fit **without feeling the need to validate what
  we do.** (quote — speaker uncertain, likely Ben)
- [2018-07-31] Calm comes from not needing the job: confidence in delivery + being
  "perfectly okay with walking away" if the fit isn't right. (paraphrase)
- [2018-07-31] When you focus on serving the client, the nervousness goes away; it's no
  longer about proving yourself but about pulling something out of *them* — so the
  pressure is actually on the client. (paraphrase)
- [2018-07-31] Shift to client-centered questions: "What do you want to accomplish? What
  are your challenges? Where do you want to be in about a year — what does that look
  like?" (quote — speaker uncertain)
- [2018-07-31] Prove value with a two-part structure: the "flashy stuff" (cool work) *plus*
  high-value questions that demonstrate how you'd actually work together. (paraphrase)
- [2018-07-31] "Chris tells us this all the time — people remember stories," and people
  relate to stories because they can see themselves in them; throwing raw data / only the
  end piece isn't relatable. (paraphrase — explicitly attributed to Chris second-hand)
