## Summary

A short, single-narrator productivity explainer built around three tips for getting more
done: (1) keep a prioritized to-do list that puts high-value work at the top and
low-value maintenance at the bottom; (2) track your time to expose where it actually
goes and let measurement shift your habits; (3) track your projects in one central,
documented hub so a team can scale without hand-retraining. The narrator demonstrates the
system using Notion as their current tool and repeatedly stresses that the tool matters
far less than the habit/process. No landmark or novel framework — standard
prioritization + time-audit + shared-workspace advice, presented operationally.

**Narrator (attribution: uncertain)**

- [2018-08-07] Works at two companies, Blind and The Futur; manages ~10 people and an
  average of five projects at a time.
- [2018-08-07] Tip 1 — Create a prioritized to-do list: put high-value tasks (new
  business development, client meetings, project postings) at the top; low-value
  maintenance tasks (accounting, timesheets) at the bottom. Set realistic goals/deadlines
  so unfinished tasks don't feel defeating, and review at week's end.
- [2018-08-07] Incomplete tasks get pushed to the following day rather than dropped.
- [2018-08-07] Tip 2 — Track your time: uses a stopwatch-style phone app to log tasks

  revealed ~1/3 of time went to email, very little to channel content, and zero to
  personal development.
- [2018-08-07] After starting to log time, habits changed almost instantly; email time
  dropped to 6.3% of total, and time was intentionally carved out for content and
  personal development. Invokes "what gets measured gets improved."
- [2018-08-07] Tip 3 — Track your projects: uses a Notion workspace as a single central
  hub for the video-content team, holding creative and technical guidelines so the team
  can scale and grow without retraining people by hand.
- [2018-08-07] Overall thesis: the tool isn't as important as the habit — it's about
  creating processes and habits that you and your team can commit to.
