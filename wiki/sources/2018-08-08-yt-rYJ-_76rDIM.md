# Overcome Fear Ep.13 w/ Keir McLaren

## Summary

Solo talk by Kier McLaren (Chris Do's personal coach) on fear, stress, and the emptiness
that "success" fails to cure. His thesis: outside things can't solve inside problems.
People in creative/entertainment trades chase becoming "somebody" (actor, writer,
producer, editor, animator) to feel good about themselves, but external achievement
never fixes internal voids — and can make them worse, driving self-destructive behavior.
Most stress and blocking behavior is rooted in **fear**, often an "old message from the
past." His practical remedies: name the worst-case concretely (which drains its power),
get distance between feeling and thinking, and above all **just do it**, since fear
otherwise keeps people paralyzed and they never get what they want. He notes highly
successful creatives simply act without overthinking — citing "Christo" (Chris Do) as
someone to whom failure "doesn't occur." Closes on the classic line: no matter where you
go, when you look in the mirror, there you are.

## Key claims

_All claims below are attributed to **Kier McLaren (CONTEXT)**, not to Chris Do._

- `[2018-08-08]` (Kier — CONTEXT) Outside/external things cannot solve inside/internal
  problems; success can make the void feel worse because achieving the goal and still
  feeling bad is deeply frustrating.
- `[2018-08-08]` (Kier — CONTEXT) Most stress and unwanted behavior is underpinned by
  some form of fear — often an "old message from the past."
- `[2018-08-08]` (Kier — CONTEXT) Stress is self-generated — "the good news is it's
  self-generated; the bad news is it's self-generated."
- `[2018-08-08]` (Kier — CONTEXT) Making a fear concrete (writing down the worst thing
  that can happen) reduces its power — like turning the light on in the closet.
- `[2018-08-08]` (Kier — CONTEXT) The best advice is to "just do it" — you only figure it
  out by doing; fear keeps many people permanently paralyzed.
- `[2018-08-08]` (Kier — CONTEXT) Highly successful creatives often act without anxiety
  of failure because it doesn't occur to them that they can fail. He names **"Christo"
  (Chris Do)** as one such person — third-person reference confirming Chris is not the
  speaker.
