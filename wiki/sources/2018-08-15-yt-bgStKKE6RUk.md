## Summary

A short highlight reel of brand designer Brian Collins articulating his philosophy of
brand-building. Collins lays out a three-part model — **familiarity** (constancy),
**surprise** (novelty / being on the frontier), and **context** (when and where you are
familiar vs. surprising) — and argues that provoking strong reactions (love or hate) is
a sign the work matters, because indifference is the real failure. He reframes **poetry**
as a core strategic discipline (good poetry *is* an experience, not a description of
one), challenges the idea that brand work must be "relevant" or "timeless," and instead
prizes authenticity, potency, sincerity, and clarity. He closes on Ray Eames's line that
"a designer is like a good host, anticipating the needs of their guests," elevating
**anticipation** as the value that gets clients into the future as fast as possible.

The video is opened by a voiceover noting Collins is one of only five people to receive
