## Summary

A short The Futur clip in which **Chris Do** hosts guest **Henry Kaminsky Jr.**, a brand consultant, on the question: *"How do you choose good clients, and do you have a system for choosing them?"* The conversation lays out desirable client traits — self-awareness, coachability, having done some homework / having a clear direction, and treating the project as fun rather than overly serious. One speaker adds harder selection filters: wanting clients who **value their time** (clients with too much free time turn a 10-minute call into two hours), who are **risk-tolerant** rather than always choosing the safe/conservative path (*"if you're looking for the safe choice, we are not the safe choice"*), and who **share common values and beliefs**. It then turns to a **strategist reframe** (signature Chris Do): don't wish your clients were better communicators — *you* should aspire to be the better communicator, because that is why they hire you. Using a car-mechanic analogy, the point is that clients usually don't know precisely what they need, and that's okay; the creative's job is to become a strategist/consultant/better listener, not to assume everything the client says is true — and the more you reveal to clients what they don't know, the more valuable you become.
