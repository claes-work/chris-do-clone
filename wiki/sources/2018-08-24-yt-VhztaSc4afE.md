## Summary

Filmed as a travel-vlog wrapper around Chris Do's talk at Digital Design Days (Milan, 2018), where he delivered a keynote, ran a workshop, and moderated a panel. The core of the video is his keynote thesis: how to find your "superpower" by combining, rather than segregating, the different things you are good at, enjoy, and can get paid for.

Chris frames this through his own story — running a successful motion design firm for 20+ years and teaching for 15+ years, yet feeling unfulfilled because each pursuit had "holes" (money without full meaning, or meaning without money). Accidentally starting The Futur revealed his "latent superpower" by combining teaching, business, and design. He offers a five-prompt self-inventory and a "Matrix" for turning overlapping strengths into a product/business, closing on a Jim Rohn-derived definition of wealth and a call to take action.

A short mid-video panel clip features Danny Yount (with Ash Thorp referenced) speaking on artist vulnerability — this is guest/context material, not Chris Do's voice.
