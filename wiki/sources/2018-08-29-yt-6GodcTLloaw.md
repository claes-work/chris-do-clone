## Summary

Guest lecture by Dr. Samuel Holtzman to The Futur team on how to move "beyond the brief"
when generating creative ideas. Using a hypothetical Heineken commercial brief — "30 seconds,
internet-based, show the bottle every five seconds, theme of celebration" — Holtzman
demonstrates a **decoding method**: instead of taking a client's literal request at face
value, you unpack the *intent* behind their words. "Bottle every five seconds" is decoded
not as literally showing a bottle, but as needing something **iconic and repetitive** that
runs continuously — a "spine" or "vertebrae" holding the piece together (heartbeat/EKG,
waves, patterns). "Celebration" is treated as a loaded **code word** the client hasn't
examined; the designer's job is to draw the client's mental image out of their head with
non-threatening questions ("what does a celebration look like to you?") rather than
alienating ones ("how does it make you feel?"). The back half generalizes this into a model
of **empathy**: you cannot step into someone else's shoes (that's presumptuous/offensive),
but you can step *out of your own* — recognize your assumptions and generalities as
un-shared — listen from that position, and then "design a pair of shoes for them." Chris Do
frames the episode at the top as training for the team in client empathy.

### Dr. Samuel Holtzman (CONTEXT — guest, does NOT train persona)

- `[2018-08-29]` A client's literal brief encodes an assumed mental picture; the designer's
  job is to **decode intent, not execute the literal words**. "Bottle every five seconds"
  really means: find what is *iconic* to Heineken and make it **repetitive / continuous** —
  the "spine" of the piece — rather than literally featuring the bottle. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-08-29]` Pull the requested object apart into its elements ("what's iconic about it,
  what history of representation can we build off of") and keep those elements running,
  instead of inserting the literal image on a timer. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-08-29]` "Celebration" is a **code word** loaded with the client's un-stated image
  (they're probably picturing Spring Break); get them to paint the scene and tell a story so
  the real image surfaces. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-08-29]` Decode by asking safe, concrete questions ("what does a celebration look
  like to you?") — *not* feeling-questions ("how does it make you feel?"), which are
  near-impossible to answer and make the client feel stupid. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-08-29]` Empathy defined: you **cannot** step into another's shoes (it's alienating
  and offensive to claim you can); you step **out of your own** framework, recognize your
  assumptions are not shared, listen from that position, then design something that fits
  them — "you can design a pair of shoes for them." (paraphrase)
- `[2018-08-29]` Reaching shared understanding sometimes "takes dynamite" — intensive
  conversation or deliberately uncomfortable experiences to break someone out of their
  assumptions. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-08-29]` Example of reframing: "one of the first things that our generation blew out
  was this idea of family being blood relatives — family is the family you make," which opens
  a whole different creative direction for the commercial. (paraphrase)
