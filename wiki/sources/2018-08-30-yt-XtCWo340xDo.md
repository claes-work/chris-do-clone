# How To Get Work — Reach Out Ep. 18 w/ Keir McLaren

Auto-captions present (English). Source is the YouTube auto-caption track; speaker

## Summary

Kier McLaren delivers practical advice, aimed at people (especially those under 40 and
those just starting out), on how to land a first *meeting* with a buyer rather than a
first job. Core message: be honest about your own talents and level, study the
marketplace, and use research + creative, human outreach to open doors. He argues that
selling is not everyone's gift — if you can't do it, partner with someone who can. All
content is Kier's; Chris Do is only mentioned in passing.

## Key claims

_All claims are **Kier McLaren (CONTEXT)** — not Chris Do._

- `[2018-08-30]` (Kier, CONTEXT) Be honest with yourself about where your talents lie
  and what level you can realistically play at; then match that to where the
  marketplace is buying. *(mindset)*
- `[2018-08-30]` (Kier, CONTEXT) The goal is not to get your first job but to get your
  first *meeting* with someone who can buy. *(sales-clients)*
- `[2018-08-30]` (Kier, CONTEXT) Most people use LinkedIn and the internet poorly when
  job/work hunting. Research target companies, identify the principals, look at where
  people went to school and their activity to find genuine common ground. *(sales-clients)*
- `[2018-08-30]` (Kier, CONTEXT) Establish relationships on real commonality (shared
  interests), not merely on wanting work — e.g. he referenced a prospect's fine-art
  study at the Sorbonne to build rapport. *(sales-clients)*
- `[2018-08-30]` (Kier, CONTEXT) Creative, human, unexpected outreach beats standard
  text/email/LinkedIn messages — cites his friend Joe Montalbano sending unsolicited
  ice cream as a door-opener that landed meetings at Apple and Google. *(sales-clients)*
- `[2018-08-30]` (Kier, CONTEXT) People under 40 rarely think to just phone someone; a
  phone call is a way to "do something different." *(sales-clients)*
- `[2018-08-30]` (Kier, CONTEXT) When you get in front of a buyer, lead with a solution
  to a problem they *perceive* they have — buyers care about their own problem, not your
  capabilities. Illustrated with an Amazon executive's reply email rejecting a
  "capabilities meeting" and asking instead for ideas to sell movies better. *(sales-clients)*
- `[2018-08-30]` (Kier, CONTEXT) Be honest about being new; show work (even low-paid or
  free/spec work for friends). Most people are willing to listen to an honest young
  person trying to get work. *(mindset, sales-clients)*
- `[2018-08-30]` (Kier, CONTEXT) Selling isn't for everyone; some genuinely can't do it
  and shouldn't beat themselves up. Pair the creative type with a sales/marketing type
  (analogy: Bill Gates promoting Microsoft vs. the operators like Steve Ballmer behind
  the scenes). Partner up — business partner, employee, or life partner. *(mindset)*
