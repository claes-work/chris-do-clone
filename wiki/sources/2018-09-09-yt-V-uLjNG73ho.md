# Why Do I Give So Much Away — My Content Strategy

Short (~1 min) monologue by Chris Do laying out The Futur's "give it all away"
content strategy: stop selling, create value, give as much as you can, and trust
reciprocity ("karmic equity") to return it. Relates to the broader give-first /
attraction material; what is DISTINCT here is the explicit "stop selling / your
credibility goes out the door" framing plus concrete 2018 self-reported growth and
membership numbers used as proof the model works.

## Summary

Chris Do answers a frequently-asked question — how to build communities and what
content strategy to use — with a single directive: stop selling. He argues audiences
have become "hypersensitive" to being sold to, and that the moment you start selling,
your credibility, authenticity, and whole value proposition collapse. The alternative
is to create value and give away as much as you can, short of bankrupting yourself.
He frames this as The Futur's operating model: don't pitch; when you do pitch, be
very clear about it. He describes his own work as "transferring what's up here out
there" — moving his knowledge to others so they can learn — done openly and
generously.

He anticipates the objection that this is a crazy business model and rebuts it with
self-reported proof: The Futur is a sustainable company growing 300% year over year,
in its fourth year of business, with no visible ceiling. He ties this to what he
calls "karmic equity" — the more you give, the more comes back — and cites 517 paying
members giving $5/month (a growing number) as evidence that giving generously and
without expectation prompts reciprocity. He closes on this as a truth about humanity
and civilization: "just give stuff away."
