## Summary

Short conversational clip from The Futur. A guest (a DJ, addressed as "Mike," with "Ryan" also named) raises a personal-identity worry: he feels like "a completely different person" when he is DJing versus on the mic announcing at a wedding, and asks whether wearing many "masks" for the world is disingenuous or inauthentic — "who is Mike?"

Chris Do reframes it. Being multi-faceted is not fakery: complex people show a different face in each role (dad, husband, teacher, podcaster, boss) and naturally speak differently to their kids than to their clients — all of those are still genuinely *them*. He offers his prism metaphor: a person is like brilliant white light, and when passed through a prism it diffracts into the full spectrum of visible colors. Each day you choose which shades to show; you open up fully only around the people you trust most (sometimes not even a life partner — maybe childhood friends). He closes by applying it to himself: people say he has "changed so much" over the trajectory of the show, but he says he actually hasn't changed at all — he has simply *shown more of himself*.
