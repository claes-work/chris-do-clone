# How to Create a Wax Seal Logo Mock Up in Photoshop

## Summary

A short screen-share tutorial in which Emily demonstrates how she built a convincing wax-seal
mockup of a logo in Photoshop. Chris Do prompts her to reveal her workflow step by step. The
technique layers several Photoshop effects on the logo art: **Filter > Distort > Ripple**
(large, but subtle) to break up the perfect vector edges into "scalloped, undulated" edges;
fill dropped to zero; then **Bevel & Emboss** (inner bevel) for depth with a top-right light
source; **Inner Shadow** and **Inner Glow** matched to the artwork's highlight color to
simulate reflected light; a **Gradient Overlay** to help the surface read; and finally a
**Drop Shadow** set to Linear Burn (using the darkest color in the image) as the effect that
"makes it believable." The takeaway framing: there is no single magic button — realism comes
from layering many subtle effects until it fools the eye.

## Key claims (grouped by speaker)

### Emily (The Futur team) — tool mechanics, CONTEXT only
- `[2018-09-14]` Use **Filter > Distort > Ripple** at "large" but kept subtle to break up the
  perfect vector edge, giving organic scalloped/undulated edges. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-09-14]` Drop fill to zero and build the look purely from layer effects. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-09-14]` **Bevel & Emboss** (inner bevel) supplies depth; set the light source
  top-right so shadows fall consistently with the image. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-09-14]` Use a slightly gray (not pure black) highlight/shadow so it doesn't "burn in"
  and look artificial. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-09-14]` For **Inner Shadow / Inner Glow**, pick the artwork's brightest highlight
  color (e.g. the gold/yellow of the seal) to simulate reflected light and matching catch of
  light. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-09-14]` A **Gradient Overlay** helps the surface read better. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-09-14]` The **Drop Shadow** — set to **Linear Burn** using the darkest color in the
  image so it borrows the background colors — is what makes the seal believable. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-09-14]` It's "season to taste": adjust the numbers until nothing jumps out as fake.
  (paraphrase)

### Chris Do — conceptual asides (low persona value, subject-attributed)
- `[2018-09-14]` The numbers you dial in are **relative to the size of the artwork**; on small
  art they won't work, so understand the concept rather than memorizing specific numbers.
  (paraphrase)
- `[2018-09-14]` Realism comes not from one "magic button" but from layering effects until you
  achieve the desired result. (paraphrase)
