## Summary

A first-person vlog by **Matthew Encina** documenting the ~36 hours leading up to (and
the aftermath of) his live presentation at **SIGGRAPH** in Vancouver, Canada — billed
as "the biggest computer animation conference in the world." Encina presents live at
the **MAXON / Cinema 4D booth** ("c4d live" stream) as a guest speaker, walking through
a Cinema 4D scene of a Western shootout (bandit vs. sheriff) using Pro Render / HDRI
lighting.

The narrative arc is a stress/adversity story: a last-minute prep crunch, a missing
passport stuck at the post office one day before an international flight, a hoarse
voice, and — during the live demo — multiple software crashes (Cinema 4D crashing on
open, an accidental internet-connect hang while trying to email a render to a colleague
named Rick). Encina frames it as "the universe testing me" and closes with a lesson on
composure under technical failure. He notes vlog-style videos are rare on the channel
and asks for comment feedback.

Encina's self-described background in the video: creative professional for 15 years,
~12 years in motion design, started as a designer and moved into animation. As Creative
Director he says he now mostly directs teams and spends little time in the software, so
he had to "shake off the cobwebs" to prep the live demo.
