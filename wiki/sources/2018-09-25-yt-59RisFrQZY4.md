## Key claims (tool technique — attribution uncertain)

- [2018-09-25] Establish guides first — x-height, baseline, and the governing diagonal
  angle — and base the letter proportions on a reference letter (here, the H).
- [2018-09-25] Draw monoline letters as a stroke (no fill) with a rounded cap via the
  Properties panel; bump stroke weight with the mouse wheel.
- [2018-09-25] Repeated letterforms can be "cheated": a correctly drawn stroke is
  duplicated and reused across letters that share geometry (N is the H with a shorter
  stroke; parts of the A borrow from the H).
- [2018-09-25] A "kink" in a curve means the bezier handle is too short; lengthen the
  handle in the direction the arc travels to smooth it.
- [2018-09-25] Turn off **Scale Strokes & Effects** (Command K) before scaling so the
  monoline weight stays uniform.
- [2018-09-25] Use the scissors tool to split a point so anchors move independently;
  use **Average** (Command Option J) then **Join** (Command J) to snap two anchors
  together and lock the connection.
- [2018-09-25] Aligning transitions to the established grid/angle lines is what makes
  the joins connect cleanly — "smooth" beats slavishly following the original sketch.
