# Avoid Clients Who Waste Your Time

> Auto-captions confirmed present. NO-CAPTIONS marker not applicable — transcript is the video's English captions.

## Summary

Short single-speaker clip in which Chris Do answers the question "what are the
characteristics of a time vampire in your initial communications?" He lists concrete
early-stage red flags — signs visible in the very first emails or on a sales call — that
predict a client will be a drain: talking past each other, reply emails that show the
person didn't read what you wrote, "just want to pick your brain" brainstorm requests,
and bad listeners who ignore or reject pushback. He closes with his heuristic for who to
work with: only people he'd want to have dinner with.

Speaker: **Chris Do** (single voice throughout; no guest). All lines below are
Chris-attributed.

## Key claims

### Chris Do
- `[2018-09-26]` A time-vampire red flag is when "you're talking past each other" — e.g.
  you reply to an email and their next email makes it clear they didn't read what you
  wrote; the communication lag "is just gonna be torture." (paraphrase)
- `[2018-09-26]` "Can we just jump on a call to brainstorm / I just want to pick your
  brain" is a red flag: people who have time to sit around picking someone's brain are
  not busy people. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-09-26]` What brain-pickers usually really want is *validation* from a perceived
  expert — confirmation that they're smart and have a great plan — not actual advice.
  (paraphrase; he flags this as a broad generalization)
- `[2018-09-26]` Another red flag: if you push back on someone and they just ignore or
  reject it — i.e. they're a bad listener. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-09-26]` All of these signs are discoverable very quickly, even in an email, and
  by the end of a half-hour sales call you'll know if it's a bad fit. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-09-26]` His boiled-down rule: only work with people you'd like to have dinner
  with — an early "I think I can hang out with this person" feeling means communication
  clicks, you laugh at each other's jokes, there's mutual trust, and they won't bug you.
  (paraphrase)

## Notable verbatim quotes

All quotes are **Chris Do**:

- "someone sends you an email, you reply to the email, and then they send back another
  email that makes it clear that they didn't read what you wrote — that's a big red flag,
  because the communication lag is just gonna be torture."
- "people who have the time to sit around picking someone's brain are not busy people…
  usually that's a sign that what they really want to do is get validation from an
  expert… they're not really looking for your advice, they're really looking for
  validation."
- "I only work with people I'd like to have dinner with."
- "if you have that level of even an early relationship with someone… then your
  communications [are] clicking, you're laughing to each other's jokes, there's a mutual
  trust, you understand each other, and they're not gonna bug you."
