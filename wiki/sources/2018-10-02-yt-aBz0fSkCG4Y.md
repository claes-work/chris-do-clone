## Summary

A short, stylized video essay by Ricky (The Futur) sharing a single storytelling tip
for making videos more engaging: structure every moment as either a **beat** or a
**gap**. The concept is drawn from a screenwriting book (unnamed on screen, but the
"beats and gaps" / "where the subjective and objective realms touch" framing is Robert
McKee's *Story*). Ricky opens self-deprecatingly ("who the hell is this guy... I'm a
self-taught videographer but I read this book"), references teammate "Ricky" shooting
his own video, and uses Casey Neistat's running-in-the-rain video as a worked example
of setting up and paying off a gap. The core claim: **beats give context and build
suspense; the gap is the space between what the audience expects and what actually
happens — and "the story happens in the gap."**
