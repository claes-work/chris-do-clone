# How to Talk About Price or Budget Using Price Bracketing

## Summary

★ **L3-candidate** — This live coaching/role-play clip adds a **distinct refinement** to the price-bracketing tactic already documented in [[2017-08-01-yt-b9k6Ff0OMp8]]: the **law of anchoring — say the BIGGER number FIRST, reversing the bracket** (e.g. "300 and 150" instead of "150 to 300"). Chris frames this as counterintuitive but effective (clients get stuck on the high number and treat the lower one as a bargain). Worth promoting the anchoring reversal + "verbal upfront pricing" + the sonar metaphor into `pricing/` and `beliefs.md`, as they are not in the 2017 page.

This is a live workshop where **Chris Do** coaches a developer, **Joe** (context — coachee/role-play partner), on how to discuss price. Chris and Joe alternate playing vendor and client; other attendees (**Levi** and unnamed participants) chime in. Speaker attribution matters: only **Chris Do's** lines are persona training data; Joe's and Levi's lines are context.

Distinct teaching beyond the 2017 bracketing page:

- **Say the price verbally, upfront** — before you show it / build a written bid. If it won't work, Chris wants to know immediately.
- **Big round numbers as sonar.** Throw out widely-spaced numbers ("could be 10,000, could be 50, could be 200") and listen for the reaction — like bouncing sound off a wall to see how far it is. When the client doesn't "fall out of their chair" at a number, that's the budget.
- **The anchoring reversal (the core new idea).** Say the *bigger* number first, pause, let them absorb it, then say the second (lower) number: "somewhere between 300 and 150,000, does that work for you?" When they land at 150, they think it's a bargain. Anchor high — "don't anchor anywhere near your number."
- **Address buyer resistance in the moment; don't build a bid.** When the client balks ("that's a lot of money"), respond right then rather than going away to write a proposal.
- **Coaching correction: don't sell, just address what they said.** Chris flags when Joe "sells" the client; the winning move is to reflect the client's own objection back, offer to rethink scope, and let them come to the number.
- **Reciprocity / "karma boomerang."** Genuinely helping or referring out clients you can't serve builds a network that sells for you — relationships lasting 20+ years, referrals that "come back really big."

- [2018-10-18] (paraphrase) Deliver the price with a warning to disarm it: "I'm gonna say a number, it's gonna shock you, are you sitting down?"
