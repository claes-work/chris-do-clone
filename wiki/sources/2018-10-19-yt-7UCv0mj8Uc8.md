## Summary

A short Futur clip in which Chris Do interviews Johnny Cupcakes (Johnny Earle) about
branding advice for anyone wanting to start a t-shirt company or clothing brand today.
Johnny delivers a rapid list of tips: don't game social media by follow/unfollow
tactics; keep your name short (skip the word "clothing"); network in real life to
amplify digital; do at least 12 things that separate you from competitors (name,
packaging, story, events, marketing); be transparent and tell your story constantly;
have fun because good humor and good design are contagious; and above all be original
and authentic. Chris closes by reinforcing that finding and telling your story is what
separates a brand from being "just another t-shirt company," and highlights Johnny's
custom Johnny Cupcakes Chinese-takeout-box packaging as an example of creating
meaningful customer experiences.

## Key claims

### Johnny Cupcakes / Johnny Earle (CONTEXT — does NOT train persona)

- `[2018-10-19]` Following and unfollowing people every day is a poor strategy for
  building a social-media audience; those people won't actually buy your products and
  it wastes time and looks silly.
- `[2018-10-19]` Don't add the word "clothing" to the end of your brand name — it's
  unnecessary; keep the name short and sweet.
- `[2018-10-19]` Make time to network in real life; digital is only most powerful when
  married to real-life relationships.
- `[2018-10-19]` Do at least 12 things that separate you from everyone else — name,
  packaging, story, the type of events you do, the type of marketing you do.
- `[2018-10-19]` Stay transparent and tell your story all the time.
- `[2018-10-19]` Have fun — good humor and good design are contagious and make people
  excited; a company doesn't have to be the most serious.
- `[2018-10-19]` Be original and authentic; people will call you out if you're not.
