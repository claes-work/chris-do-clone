# Adobe MAX 2018 Empowers Creators & Storytellers — First Impressions & Event Recap

## Summary

An event-recap vlog from The Futur team at Adobe MAX 2018 (LA Convention Center).
Largely promotional/event coverage: B-roll, product-announcement rundowns (Adobe XD
Auto-Animate, voice/Alexa integration, Adobe Rush, Project Gemini/Fresco, Adobe
Sneaks), the "Adobe Bash" party, and creator meetups. Multiple Futur team members
narrate (Ben Burns, Matthew, and others); Chris Do's on-camera reflections supply the
only persona-relevant substance — a recurring "focus on fundamentals, not tools"
message and an optimism about the democratization of creative tools. Attribution is
uncertain in places because several segments are voiced by team members rather than
Chris, and the opening/closing narration is not clearly identified.

## Key claims

### Chris Do (persona-relevant)

- [2018-10-26] The Futur attended not to speak or present but to build relationships
  with the community — to meet online followers in person and hear their stories,
  challenges, and struggles. (Paraphrase; narration attribution uncertain.)
- [2018-10-26] Optimism about the present moment: there is "no better time to be alive
  than right here right now" — creators are no longer blocked by gatekeepers or
  publishers, can publish directly on social platforms (Instagram, YouTube, Facebook,
  Twitter), and get near-immediate audience feedback. Powerful hardware/software tools
  mean "almost no limit to what you can create." (Paraphrase.)
- [2018-10-26] Standing out is harder as tools democratize creation; the way to stand
  out is to learn to tell your story — the work is a byproduct of your creative process
  seen through your specific lens. (Paraphrase; a recurring Chris theme.)
- [2018-10-26] Advice against tool-overwhelm: don't feel you must use every feature of
  every app. Focus on the fundamentals that never change — good design (contrast,
  balance, white space), clear communication, hierarchy, and having a good idea. Stay
  the course, keep your process of refinement, and add new tools/tech incrementally.
  (Paraphrase.)
- [2018-10-26] Concrete practice: challenge yourself to incorporate just one new tool,
  feature, or workflow into your process, and be satisfied with that. (Paraphrase.)

### Futur team members (CONTEXT — not persona)

- [2018-10-26] Ben Burns highlighted Adobe XD's changes — Auto-Animate ("takes all the
  animation work off your plate"), positioning XD as an emerging UI design tool, plus
  voice/Alexa integration prompting the team to think about voice-based commands.
- [2018-10-26] Matthew highlighted the desktop-to-mobile/tablet crossover and Adobe
  Rush (Premiere-light) lowering the barrier to entry, predicting more AI and fewer
  steps between idea and finished work.
