# How To Sell Your Design Services without Experience?

## Summary

Short-form clip in which Chris Do answers the question of how to sell a company
or a service when you have little experience or portfolio. His core argument: you
sell on confidence and on WHO you are, not on your body of work. He reframes the
seller as an executive producer / sales agent who manages the relationship between
client and maker rather than doing the work himself — and positions himself as a
"problem solver" and "business designer" rather than a technician. He closes with a
ranked list of what NOT to sell: mistake #1 is selling the portfolio, #2 is selling
the process; instead you should sell the people.

Single speaker throughout (Chris Do, monologue). No coachee/other voices present.

## Key claims

### Chris Do

- [2018-10-29] You sell your company or service mostly on your confidence, not your
  body of work — a portfolio that has won every award won't matter if you don't
  represent yourself as someone who knows what they're doing and can take care of it.
  (paraphrase)
- [2018-10-29] The seller is like an executive producer or sales agent: you don't do
  the work, you manage the relationship between the client and the person who gets it
  done — and the head of a company isn't much different. (paraphrase)
- [2018-10-29] What he actually sells is himself as a problem solver and business
  designer, not as the technician on the software (he notes he had gotten rusty in
  After Effects). (paraphrase)
- [2018-10-29] Because he sells who he is and what he can do — not the work — he could
  sell all the company's assets including the portfolio and still go out and get work.
  (paraphrase)
- [2018-10-29] Ranked selling mistakes: #1 is to sell the portfolio; #2 is to sell the
  process. Instead, sell the people. (paraphrase)
