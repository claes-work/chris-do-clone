# How To Get More Clients: A Challenge To Get More Leads

## Summary

First episode of Chris Do's "Commute Challenge" series (The Futur) — short, low-production
videos that set the viewer a one-week action challenge, followed a week later by an
educational follow-up. Chris addresses the channel's most-asked question — how to find
more / better / bigger clients — and reframes it around a single diagnostic: "How many
people do you talk to in a week?" (usually the answer is none). His thesis: early-stage
creatives wrongly wait on lead funnels and automated marketing; instead they should do
direct human outreach. The challenge, credited to Jim Rohn, is to reach out to 30 new
people in one week, split across two target types: (1) bigger agencies / design studios
to model after and receive their overflow/misfit leads and referrals, and (2) "fringe
service providers" — adjacent, non-competing creatives (e.g. a designer contacting
photographers) — to build a referral network. Chris asks 200 viewers to commit in the
comments; if they do, he promises a follow-up with his email template and phone script.
Viewers are asked to share results on Twitter/Instagram with #futurcommutechallenge.
