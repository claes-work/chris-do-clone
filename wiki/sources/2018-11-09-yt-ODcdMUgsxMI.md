# How Positioning Your Brand Can Increase Your Value

★ **L3-candidate** — Contains a compact, canonical statement of Chris Do's
positioning-increases-value framework (value = maximum willingness to pay;
Rolex/Timex commodity contrast; hourly billing = self-commoditization; become the
go-to person). This is landmark positioning/pricing doctrine and a strong candidate
for promotion into `wiki/topics/` (branding + pricing) and `persona/beliefs.md`.

## Summary

A short monologue on how positioning raises perceived value. The speaker defines value
as the maximum a consumer will pay, uses the wristwatch market (Timex vs. Rolex / Patek
Philippe) to argue that people pay for story and difference rather than the commodity
function, and applies this to creative professionals: you must become the "go-to person"
for something specific. Billing by the hour is framed as self-commoditization (it makes
you an apples-to-apples price comparison); differentiated positioning is the "salvation."
Branding is acknowledged as a long game, but focused positioning plus marketing aimed at
one thing is what brings qualified leads in the door — assuming an audience exists that
cares about that thing.

## Key claims

### Chris Do (attribution: uncertain on speaker ID — no on-screen label; content strongly consistent with Chris Do)

- `[2018-11-09]` Value is the maximum amount a consumer is willing to pay for an item. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-11-09]` A wristwatch is a commodity — you can get a Timex for five dollars — yet people still buy Patek Philippe and Rolex because those brands are different and carry a different story. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-11-09]` Buyers of a Rolex are not buying a watch, they are buying "a Rolex" — i.e., the story and identity, not the function. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-11-09]` You must become the go-to person for something — a particular specialized skill (example given: hand lettering / calligraphy). (paraphrase)
- `[2018-11-09]` Billing by the hour sets you up as an apples-to-apples commodity, because it makes it easy for a buyer to compare a $10/hour provider against a $100/hour provider and pick the cheaper one. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-11-09]` Positioning yourself as the go-to person is "your salvation, not something to fear." (quote fragment / paraphrase)
- `[2018-11-09]` Branding is a long game, but positioning as the go-to person and focusing all marketing on that one thing brings leads in the door — provided there are people who care about that thing. (paraphrase)
