# How Strategy Changed My Design Business

★ **L3-candidate** — Significant BIOGRAPHY source: Chris Do gives a first-person, dated account of Blind's near-collapse and reinvention (revenue peak/decline, the 2013 inflection point, the pivot from motion design to branding consultancy). Includes [self-reported] financial figures found nowhere else in the wiki. Should be promoted to `persona/biography.md` and the Blind entity page.

## Summary

Live "Step Inside the Dojo" deep-dive on The Futur channel. Chris Do (host) with Matthew (creative director at Blind, co-presenter) and team members Sina and Erica in the booth. Chris tells the story of how learning **strategy** rescued and transformed his motion design studio, Blind. He frames the whole episode as "meta": teaching Russell Brunson's *DotCom Secrets* funnel concept (cold/warm/hot leads, problem→solution→product) while simultaneously applying it to the audience to sell two Futur products (the CORE brand strategy kit and the Brand Messaging Kit).

Structurally the talk moves through: (1) Blind's history and financial trajectory; (2) the 2013 crisis and a failed $5,000 web project; (3) the "strategic divide" problem — being seen as a vendor/executor, not a consultant; (4) learning strategy from Jose Caballer and adopting the CORE framework; (5) results — repositioning as a branding consultancy; (6) live Q&A pulled from Instagram/YouTube; (7) product pitch and recap.

### Chris Do (speaker: Chris Do)

- `[2018-11-09]` Blind is a motion design firm he has run for 23 years, started in 1995. (paraphrase) — BIOGRAPHY
- `[2018-11-09]` Blind is located at 1702 Olympic, Santa Monica. (paraphrase) — BIOGRAPHY
- `[2018-11-09]` Blind has worked with big brands including Coldplay, Sting, and Xbox, plus car companies and electronics companies; won many awards. Much of the reel work was done by creative directors (including Matthew and Greg), not Chris personally. (paraphrase) — BIOGRAPHY
- `[2018-11-09]` 2007 was the company's Zenith/highest point, "almost $7 million" in revenue for a relatively small West Coast firm, just before the economy crashed. [self-reported] (paraphrase) — BIOGRAPHY
- `[2018-11-09]` The business was built almost entirely on making commercials for ad agencies; as audiences moved from TV/commercials to social platforms, revenue declined. (paraphrase) — BIOGRAPHY
- `[2018-11-09]` 2013 was "the big inflection point" in the company. (paraphrase) — BIOGRAPHY
- `[2018-11-09]` Attempts to pivot (explainer videos, corporate videos, documentaries) failed because the team was used to high budgets — directors couldn't do projects under $150,000; explainer videos naturally run $20k–$50k. One client said "you do too much, it's going to be too expensive" before Blind could even pitch. (paraphrase) — BIOGRAPHY
- `[2018-11-09]` Chris asked his older brother (an advisor to many companies) for web work; the brother found him a $5,000 client. The project went badly — Chris didn't know how to design websites, goals/process were unclear, many revisions, and neither client nor Chris was happy. Net result: no money and bad work. (paraphrase) — BIOGRAPHY
- `[2018-11-09]` The core problem: for 20+ years Blind lived on "the execution side" — agencies did strategy/marketing/art direction/copy, then handed off. This left a "strategic divide": lack of clarity/alignment, over-focus on aesthetics, and being seen as a vendor rather than a consultant. Chris admits he "wanted to surrender and totally give up." (paraphrase) — BIOGRAPHY
- `[2018-11-09]` His friend Jose Caballer taught him strategy, sending over documents; the CORE framework is something Jose spent "almost 20 years learning, developing himself and distilling down into the three key exercises." (paraphrase)
- `[2018-11-09]` Strategy = a plan on how to get from A to B: define the goal, ask why they aren't there today (the challenge), then deeply understand the customers/users. "A lot of user experience design to me is just anticipating needs." (paraphrase / partial quote)
- `[2018-11-09]` Trojan Storage was the first branding project where someone (Jose) led the strategy and Chris designed; it grew from "a new logo and a website" into signage, ads, and a showroom — because Chris was repositioned from vendor to consultant/advisor. (paraphrase) — BIOGRAPHY
- `[2018-11-09]` Oles was the first project where Chris applied the CORE framework on his own without Jose — done remotely for a remote fishing resort off the coast of Vancouver. (paraphrase) — BIOGRAPHY
- `[2018-11-09]` Other repositioned clients named: Tango (identity, keynote decks, website), Mastro, EYRC (local star architects), plus a commercial real-estate developer and an editorial company. (paraphrase) — BIOGRAPHY
- `[2018-11-09]` Result: Blind moved from a narrow motion design company to a branding consultancy sought by diverse clients; "paid for making" → "paid to think"; from designing identities to designing businesses. The branding projects were ~50x the size of the $5,000 website ("a 50x return on investment in scale"). (paraphrase) [self-reported]
- `[2018-11-09]` "Within two years we're able to reverse the trend of a 20-year-old company." (paraphrase) — BIOGRAPHY
- `[2018-11-09]` A brand is not just a look — it has a voice and a behavior; you shape it *with* the client, not *for* them. (paraphrase) — recurring belief
- `[2018-11-09]` Sales technique for low-budget clients: tell them the endeavor "probably has little impact on your business," which forces them to either agree (and hire someone cheaper like Fiverr/99designs) or insist it's important (opening a real strategy conversation). Then ask about goals, challenges, and customers. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-11-09]` On ageism / being taken seriously as a young strategist: people judge you by how you dress and how you speak/think; the CORE framework gives a young designer a high probability of success. (paraphrase)
- `[2018-11-09]` CORE has been The Futur's best-selling product for four years and continues to bring in "big numbers." [self-reported] (paraphrase)
- `[2018-11-09]` The Futur puts products on sale only twice a year — July 4th and November (Black Friday). (paraphrase)
- `[2018-11-09]` Chris does not consider himself a copywriter/writer; he developed the Brand Messaging Kit after doing brand messaging many times and seeing patterns. He can "freestyle" an anthem spot live once he knows the client's customers. (paraphrase) — BIOGRAPHY / self-characterization
