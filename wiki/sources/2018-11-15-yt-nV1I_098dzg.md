## Summary

A short explainer arguing that "user experience design" lacks a single agreed
definition, then surveying a few existing definitions before offering the narrator's
own. The narrator cites Dan Mackowski (VP of Design, Walmart), Mary Kay McCluskey
(Director of Research, UserTesting), and Don Norman (credited as the person who coined
the term "user experience"). The narrator then proposes their own definition and
argues that UX is distinguished from other design disciplines by two requirements:
interaction and iterative validation. Recurring motif: the UX designer controls
"51%" of the experience — the extra 1% over a 50/50 split coming from the ability to
iterate and learn across users. Closes with a sponsor read for the domain registrar
Porkbun. Design-craft content only; no biographical or business-strategy material.

## Key claims

### Narrator (unidentified — attribution: uncertain)

- [2018-11-15] There is no single concrete, agreed-upon definition of what user
  experience design is; the video sets out to define it.
- [2018-11-15] Cites Dan Mackowski (VP of Design at Walmart): "you can't design
  experience but you can design for them" — narrator agrees you can influence but not
  fully design someone's experience.
- [2018-11-15] Cites Mary Kay McCluskey (Director of Research at UserTesting), from a
  Skillshare usability class: UX design is "a commitment to building products with the
  customer in mind." Narrator likes it but thinks UX is bigger than the product.
- [2018-11-15] Cites the person who coined the term (Don Norman): "human interface" and
  "usability" were too narrow; he wanted to cover all aspects of a person's experience
  with a system — industrial design, graphics, the interface, physical interaction, and
  the manual. Narrator thinks he "nailed it."
- [2018-11-15] Narrator's own definition of UX design: "influencing controllable
  variables to cause a positive emotional response when a person interacts with a
  product, environment or brand."
- [2018-11-15] Simplicity and ease of use are a factor, but not the whole goal —
  sometimes people want confusion, frustration, panic, or challenge (panic-room games,
  roller coasters, horror movies, puzzles); whether that is loved or hated depends on
  the person.
- [2018-11-15] TL;DR of the definition: "do what you can to make people feel great when
  they use your stuff."
- [2018-11-15] Requirement 1 — UX design requires **interaction**; without interaction
  "it's just design." You control only 51% of the experience / relationship.
- [2018-11-15] Requirement 2 — UX design requires **iterative validation** (paraphrased
  as "repeated testing"); without it you are "simply guessing." Throw away tidy
  waterfall / Gantt-chart planning; ship something, observe whether it gets the desired
  result, then bring it back and readjust.
- [2018-11-15] The "extra 1%" (why 51% not 50%) comes from iteration: repeating the
  loop lets the designer learn, execute, and build a better version for future users.
- [2018-11-15] Sponsor read: episode sponsored by Porkbun, a domain registrar based in
  Portland, Oregon; promo of $5 .design domains for the first year.
