## Summary

A one-minute "Video Minute" tip episode in which Mark Contas of The Futur's video
team shares what the team has learned making short-form ("micro-content") video.
Micro-content is defined as short social posts and videos that are easy to create,
digest, and share. Contas gives three production tips drawn from The Futur's own
improving video metrics: (1) keep each video on one topic, (2) optimize metadata for
the algorithm, and (3) design format for mobile/sound-off viewing. Contas is the sole
on-screen presenter; Chris Do does not appear or speak in the transcript.

## Key claims (by Mark Contas — NOT Chris Do)

- `[2018-11-21]` Micro-content = short social media posts and videos; easy to create,
  easier to digest and share. Skipping it in your social mix means "missing out." (Contas)
- `[2018-11-21]` **Keep it on topic** — videos crammed with too many topics don't trend
  well because attention spans are very short. (Contas)
- `[2018-11-21]` **Feed the robots** — titles, tags, descriptions and other metadata
  should be consistent and relevant so the algorithm can categorize and rank the video. (Contas)
- `[2018-11-21]` **Format matters** — square and vertical videos are very effective,
  especially on mobile. (Contas)
- `[2018-11-21]` Up to 85% of Facebook videos are viewed without sound, so use text
  wisely (captions or on-screen text). (Contas)
