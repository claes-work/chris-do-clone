# Going From Identity Design to Brand Strategy — A Deep Dive w/ Melinda Livsey (Ep. 12)

★ **L3-candidate** — Chris live-teaches several landmark Futur frameworks in one sitting
(questionnaire → strategy workshop, order-taker vs. consultant, cold-vs-hot leads,
symptom-vs-root-cause diagnosis, and knowledge → capital/equity conversion). Note: the
actual identity-design → brand-strategy *transition* is Melinda's story and is explicitly
**deferred to another episode** ("that's not this episode"), so this video is stronger as a
source of Chris's coaching/positioning frameworks than of a step-by-step transition playbook.

## Summary

A deep-dive coaching episode. Melinda opens by asking how "specializing" and being a
"consultant who can solve any problem" coexist. Chris refuses to answer for a hypothetical
"person not in the room" and insists they solve for Melinda specifically. The conversation
then walks through: (1) why the client questionnaire is inferior to a live strategy workshop;
(2) how a questionnaire turns the designer into an "order taker" and removes them from the
conversation; (3) the difference between cold and hot inbound leads, and how positioning as a
strategist (vs. a deliverable-maker) changes lead temperature; (4) diagnosing the client's
*why* / root cause rather than treating self-prescribed symptoms (the rash-and-carrots
analogy); and (5) a long career-strategy arc where Chris steers Melinda from "build a service
business big enough so I can later do the thing I love" toward "why aren't you doing the thing
you love now?" He introduces the lifestyle-business vs. bigger-mission choice, the Dan Sullivan
three-year question, converting knowledge/experience into capital and equity (books, courses,
speaking, licensing royalties instead of one-to-one time), and the art-vs-work distinction
(borrowed from a Seth Godin conversation). The episode is framed as the pilot of a recurring
remote coaching series (Melinda's OC→Santa Monica commute made in-studio taping impractical).

## Key claims

### Chris Do (persona) — [2018-11-22]

- **Refuse to coach "the person not in the room."** When Melinda frames the question as being
  for other people who ask her, Chris redirects: solve for the actual person present, not a
  hypothetical. [2018-11-22]
- **Definition of branding endorsed:** helping a brand develop its personality (knowing who
  they are and making it attractive to their ideal client) and uncovering who that ideal client
  is and how to communicate to them. [2018-11-22]
- **Questionnaire → strategy workshop.** A questionnaire is "homework" / a chore the client
  rushes; it only surfaces top-of-mind answers. A live workshop with founder and key
  stakeholders uncovers insights they don't see on the surface. [2018-11-22]
- **The questionnaire makes you the order taker.** Filling out a form is like ordering off a
  menu — it removes the designer as a participant and defines them as an order taker ("here are
  the questions, I don't really want to talk to you, I'll make what you asked for"). [2018-11-22]
- **Clients lack the visual vocabulary.** Broad words like "optimistic, innovative, cool" mean
  many things; the client doesn't have the designer's training. If they could specify precisely,
  they'd just hire a low-level designer and art-direct them. So you must be in the conversation
  to reshape it ("maybe the word isn't 'cool,' it's 'conservative'"). [2018-11-22]
- **Same questions, different answers.** A strategist asking the *same* questions gets better
  answers because she shapes the conversation, probes ("why do you say that, give me an
  example"), and builds rapport so the client feels understood. [2018-11-22]
- **Not every inbound lead is hot.** Assuming every caller/emailer is a hot lead is a mistake;
  many are colder than you think because they aren't even aware of their real problem — you have
  to make them aware. [2018-11-22]
- **Symptoms vs. root cause (rash/carrots analogy).** Clients self-diagnose and self-prescribe
  a deliverable ("you do this, do it for me"). Treating the symptom (ointment for a rash) never
  solves the real problem; a doctor finds the allergy (carrots) and the symptom clears. Pause
  and understand the deeper problem before accepting the brief at face value. [2018-11-22]
- **Fear = "Fantasized Experiences Appearing Real."** Designers' fears about transitioning to
  strategist (what if they ask hard questions / demand proof / guarantees) are negative
  self-talk; acting through it reveals the pushback often isn't there. [2018-11-22]
- **Selling ≠ convincing.** When it's the right fit it's not persuasion — it's a conversation;
  the client convinces themselves. [2018-11-22]
- **Being a hyper-focused strategist is fine.** Doing strategy first, delivering only
  logo/identity/messaging, and referring out websites/collateral is a legitimate, focused model
  — you can be a "generalist strategist" who is nonetheless very narrow in deliverables. [2018-11-22]
- **Two kinds of scaling.** You can scale by hiring/offloading time, OR scale by charging more
  and more for your expertise (fewer clients, higher prices, less work). Melinda wants the
  latter. [2018-11-22]
- **Lifestyle business vs. bigger mission.** A lifestyle business (work a few months a year, a
  handful of clients, comfortable income) is a great model *if it's the one you want*. Chris says
  he could net ~$300–400k/yr working from home with one assistant, but he's punting the lifestyle
  business because he wants a bigger mission. Be cognizant of today's decisions and their
  pros/cons for tomorrow. [2018-11-22]
- **The Seth Godin caution.** People only see the latter, lean phase of Seth's career and skip
  the first ~30 years of disruption/innovation (e.g., corporate email) that earned it. Don't
  copy the endpoint without the arc. [2018-11-22]
- **Convert knowledge and experience into capital and equity.** Selling time one-to-one caps
  your return; writing a book, vlogging/blogging, speaking, or building a video course lets you
  sell your knowledge beyond a one-to-one relationship. [2018-11-22]
- **Dan Sullivan three-year question** used as a coaching tool: "Three years from now, looking
  back, what has to have happened — professionally and personally — for you to be happy?" Chris
  says not having an answer is a problem worth fixing. [2018-11-22]
- **Maslow / "obedient machines."** People stay busy at the base of Maslow's hierarchy and never
  reach self-fulfillment; pull the emergency brake, stop autopilot, and ask what you really want. [2018-11-22]
- **Art vs. work (via Seth Godin).** With art you ask "how can I do *more*"; with work "how can
  I do *less*." Design a life that feels like play regardless of pay and your life will be well
  spent. [2018-11-22]
- **Business designer / life designer.** Just as he helps businesses design to their potential,
  Chris frames himself as helping design a person's life to their potential. [2018-11-22]
- **Golden handcuffs warning.** If the service work pays too well it becomes addictive; once you
  hit the income goal you may never start the side thing you actually love. [2018-11-22]
- **Be your own client.** Move from solving other people's problems to solving your own — the
  Jen Billick / Knock Knock example (turned her OCD into an organizational-products company). You
  can conceptualize products and license the ideas for royalties (referencing pro member "Seth")
  rather than run the business. [2018-11-22]
- **Teaching his 12-year-old son** to speak, communicate, create content, take photos, and tell
  stories so he can "skip all that" and live in the current era instead of an old playbook. [2018-11-22]
- **Meta close — the model in action.** Chris explicitly narrates that he just did to Melinda
  what a strategist does: asked questions, listened, mirrored, let her self-diagnose, then
  reframed her own bottle-and-label analogy back at her. And that by recording it publicly he
  converts a finite one-to-one exchange into scalable equity for the audience. [2018-11-22]

### Melinda Livsey (context) — [2018-11-22]

- Was a "confused graphic designer" a year prior; learned brand strategy and now offers brand
  strategy, identity, and messaging — dropped lower-paying "order-taker" clients; now hired for
  expertise. [2018-11-22] (context — her own trajectory)
- Switched from a questionnaire to a strategy workshop with founders/stakeholders. [2018-11-22] (context)
- Deliberately narrow: does strategy + identity + messaging, refers out websites and collateral;
  wants higher prices and fewer clients, not a big team. [2018-11-22] (context)
- Wants flexibility so her service income can fund side experiments/products (e.g., the "cat
  hater survival kit"), collaborating with other creatives; prefers idea/prototype over running
  a business. [2018-11-22] (context)
- Realizes her roadblock: she filtered Chris's Seth Godin story as "I must build my business to
  X before I can do what I love." [2018-11-22] (context)
