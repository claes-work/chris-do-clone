# Starting Your Business: Company Culture

## Summary

A short coaching answer in which **Chris Do** responds to a **coachee** (context —
an early-stage founder running a startup with a **scattered / remote team**) who asks
how to manage the team, keep them positive, and build good company culture. Chris's core
thesis: culture is one of the most important things to get right, and you get it right
**at the very beginning**, then build from there.

His distinct angle here is **culture-as-competitive-advantage**: candidates (freelance or
staff) weigh roughly four things — pay, quality of the work, how they're treated, and how
the company manages projects. If you can't win on **price** or **project quality** (a
startup usually can't), you compete on the other two — **treatment** and **project
management** — and that levels the playing field against bigger, better-known shops. He
frames the leader's obligation as protecting and nourishing people's **long-term careers**
rather than "using them up and spitting them out," and ties this to **burnout avoidance
through project selection**: taking jobs that are too big/complex or under-timed forces
all-nighters that burn out the team (and the founder). He closes by connecting this to
**longevity of passion** — ~20 years in and still passionate, because he learned to manage
projects, his time, and what he puts in.
