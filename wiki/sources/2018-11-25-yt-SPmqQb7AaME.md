# Logo Design Critique: Custom Lettering / How to Draw Monoline M in Illustrator

## Summary

A live Adobe Illustrator screen-share in which Chris Do critiques a student's
monoline "Motorhead" wordmark and then rebuilds the custom "M" from scratch on
camera. Chris drives the software throughout while an off-camera co-host/student
banters and asks questions. The bulk of the video is tool mechanics (scaling at a
45° angle, the measure/ruler tool, `Constrain Angle`, round caps, snapping),
interleaved with typographic critique and Chris's characteristic showman humor.
Persona value is LOW (mostly craft/tool demonstration), but Chris is unambiguously
the lead teacher, so his voice and teaching manner are usable.

## Key claims (Chris Do, unless noted)

- [2018-11-25] The monoline "Motorhead" logo "reads well" and the embellishments
  are good, but two flaws: the counter/negative shape at the top of the "M" looks
  "funky," and there is a "little kink" plus a "little divot" that should be removed.
- [2018-11-25] Fix approach: redraw the "D" without the divot — "have it come up and
  straight up" — and resolve the transition so it does not have a sharp point.
- [2018-11-25] To make a stroke transition "nicely" without a kink, align the bezier
  handle exactly to the guide line: "if you make this handle in the exact same line
  there then it won't have a kink in it."
- [2018-11-25] Optical adjustment: a segment can *feel* too thick even when it is not,
  because the tapering changes the perceived weight — "you would move that thing down"
  optically. (Optical correction over mathematical correctness.)
- [2018-11-25] Illustrator technique demonstrated: measure an existing stroke's angle
  with the measure/ruler tool (got 67.79°, rounded to 67.8°), then set that value in
  `Command+K` → Constrain Angle so the pen and rectangle tools snap to that exact
  angle while holding Shift.
- [2018-11-25] Other mechanics shown: paste-in-front + scale-at-45° to mirror a line,
  `Shift+X` to swap fill/stroke, the new Illustrator stroke-properties palette
  (round cap option), and turning off Snap to Pixel / Snap to Point when points won't
  land where intended.
- [2018-11-25] Typographic vocabulary taught in passing: open counter (the M), closed
  counter (inside the D and A), the "eye" of a lowercase e — "just trying to drop some
  typographic terms on you guys."
- [2018-11-25] On the ruler/measure tool: it has "been there forever but nobody uses
  it" — offered as one useful way to work, while acknowledging he "shouldn't have drawn
  it this way."
