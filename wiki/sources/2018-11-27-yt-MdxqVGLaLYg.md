# What Would You Do Differently If You Had To Start Over?

Single-speaker monologue by **Chris Do** (The Futur), answering an audience/interviewer
question about what he would do differently if he had to build his career from scratch.
All lines are Chris Do (first person; references "Mark and I," The Futur team, and the
YouTube creation class). Solo attribution throughout.

## Summary

Chris Do reflects on how he would rebuild if starting over: he would skip having a
business partner, get over his fear of being on camera, iterate fast, and work from a
home studio — the same path he observes 99.9% of YouTube influencers take (solo, finding
their voice before scaling a team). He frames the real advantage of a "legitimate
company" as being able to be more than one person and manage a team. He then lays out his
core competitive philosophy: he deliberately seeks hard, high-walled markets ("the feast
with gun turrets, barbed wire, and a moat with man-eating fish") because once he scales
those walls there is zero competition. He argues the internet is full of "undiscovered
country" and that the design-education niche is wide open because few design
professionals who have actually accomplished something are also passionate, dedicated,
and charismatic teachers. He closes with the tactical method he and Mark teach: Google
search a topic everyone wants to learn, find that nothing good exists, make the video,
and dominate the top-10 search results (they did this with branding, typography, logo
design, critique, starting a creative business).
