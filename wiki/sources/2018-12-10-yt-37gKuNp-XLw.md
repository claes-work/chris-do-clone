## Summary

The thirteenth episode of an ongoing on-camera coaching series in which Chris Do publicly mentors brand strategist Melinda Livsey. Melinda arrives with a specific question — "who am I talking to when I create content, and in what voice?" — as she pivots from design toward strategy, consulting, and content creation. Chris uses the session to teach a full content-marketing philosophy for attracting ideal clients.

His core moves: (1) write content aimed at a *previous version of yourself* — the person you were 12–18 months ago — because you understand their struggle and can help them make the same transition; (2) treat content marketing not as disguised advertising but as giving away your best advice for free so you rank in organic search and build trust; (3) recognize that the creatives who consume your content become your champions, carrying your name up to the decision-makers who actually hire; (4) reframe peers as community rather than competition — generosity signals confidence and expertise and disrupts the scarcity mindset; (5) favor momentum and direction over rigid plans; (6) make many small bets, read the feedback, and adapt. Chris backs the advice with The Futur's own data (large companies now calling to train creative teams; a $130k client who was a fan first; brand-sponsor deals worth over half a million). In the final third he pushes Melinda toward building productized/creative income ("add a zero" to her $1,200/mo course revenue) and, most personally, declares that for the first time in his life he considers himself *an artist making art for the masses*, urging everyone to do work for the pure joy of self-expression.
