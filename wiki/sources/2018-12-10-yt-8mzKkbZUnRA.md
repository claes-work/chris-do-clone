## Summary

A short, edited interview clip on The Futur addressing whether AI/automation will take
"your" job. The guest's argument: competence is overrated because anything that can be
"written down" (documented, looked up, memorized) can be done more cheaply — by a lower-
paid worker or, increasingly, by a computer. Workers are therefore "squeezed" from the
top (bosses pushing ever-more menial labor) and the bottom (computers taking that same
labor). The safe alternative is to "race to the top" and become a **linchpin**: someone
who solves interesting problems no one has solved before — work that *cannot* be written
down. He ties this to a critique of industrial-era schooling (built to produce
"compliant factory workers") and argues education should instead teach kids to lead and
to solve interesting problems. A second thread contrasts the **artist** (asks "how can I
do *more*?", gives everything, does frightening work without a manual) with the **worker**
(asks "how can I do *less*?", holds back because the boss will always ask for more) —
tracing the holdback to industrial conditioning, not human hardwiring.

- [2018-12-10] People hold back not because they are hardwired to, but because they are

- [2018-12-10] Invokes the Karl-Marx tension: the boss wants unlimited effort for no

- [2018-12-10] Industrial conditioning trained us to seek deniability, authority, and
