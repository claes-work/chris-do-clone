## Summary

Short single-speaker clip (mostly Chris Do) responding to a question about
consuming lots of learning (articles, YouTube) yet feeling unaccomplished
because there's nothing to "quantify" at day's end. Chris's core answer: pure
consumption is not valued by society; value is created only when you **convert**
what you learn into something you share — a tweet, post, article, poster, song,
infographic, sketchnotes, podcast, or video. He frames content creation as
trading your time and attention with an audience in exchange for influence
(which can later be traded for sponsorship, etc.), and defines creativity as
"the ability to find a connection between two disparate ideas." He closes by
describing his own practice: distilling hours of interviews, reading, and videos
into bite-sized pieces — "I've converted hours of my life into minutes of yours."
