## Summary

Chris Do introduces a reformatted weekly series (rebranded from the "Commute Challenge" to the
"Coffee Challenge"): week 1 he issues a challenge to help viewers "succeed in life and in business,"
week 2 he responds by showing how he tackled it himself. This week's challenge is about **process /
documentation**. He argues that creatives treat their day-to-day work as ad-hoc problem-solving
rather than as repeatable systems, and that this ad-hoc mindset blocks building and scaling a
sustainable business.

The concrete assignment: pick **two things you do regularly** — one **micro** (checking social media,
email, mail) and one **macro** (how you design a logo, how you build a website, how a wedding
photographer sets up a shoot) — and **document every single tiny step**. He stresses granularity:
capture even the small steps you'd normally skip. Payoff: documenting your process makes you a
**better delegator** and makes it **easier to explain your process/approach to clients**.

He asks for engagement (200 comments committing to the challenge; post results on Twitter/Instagram
with hashtag **#futurchallenge**) and promises that if 200 people commit, next week he'll show his own
tools and process, including how he **documented creating a design system**.
