# Pros & Cons of Running A Design Business From Home

> Auto-captions used (`.en.txt`). Captions were present. NO-CAPTIONS flag not applicable.

## Summary

Short Q&A clip in which Chris Do answers a viewer question about the benefits of running a
design/startup as a "fluid model" — a small core team plus remote artists scattered around the
world ("digital nomads"). Chris lays out a balanced pros/cons list for a distributed, work-from-
anywhere design business: communication and project-management overhead go up, but you gain access
to a global talent pool, better quality of life for artists, and lower labor costs by leveraging
differences in cost of living (citing Brazil and India as sources of strong artists/animators). He
adds a caveat: the very best artists tend to migrate to the biggest markets (San Francisco, LA, New
York, London / where the big effects shops are), so a remote model may not deliver the same top-tier
talent, which in turn means more management. Generic, experience-based advice rather than a named
framework.

## Key claims

**Chris Do (subject):**
- [2018-12-16] The "fluid model" — a very small core team with everyone else working remotely from all over the world, managed remotely — is a viable way to run a design startup. (paraphrase)
- [2018-12-16] Distributed/remote teams make communication much harder and require more time spent on project management, because assets must be distributed, software versions differ, and files aren't always synced. (paraphrase)
- [2018-12-16] Tools that help manage globally distributed teams include Trello, Basecamp, and Slack, among other project-management software. (paraphrase)
- [2018-12-16] The benefits of the remote model: you can work with anybody (global talent), it's a higher quality of life for artists (they work from home on their own terms as long as the work gets done), and you'll most likely pay less. (paraphrase)
- [2018-12-16] Because US/UK cost of living and currency (dollar/pound) are relatively high, you can leverage lower-cost local economies — e.g., Brazil and India have amazing artists and animators. (paraphrase)
- [2018-12-16] Caveat: the best artists generally migrate to the biggest markets (San Francisco, LA, New York, London / wherever the big effects shops are), so a remote model likely won't get you the same quality of artists — which means more management. (paraphrase)
