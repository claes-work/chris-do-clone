# How to Make More Time: Document Delegate Automate

★ **L3-candidate** — landmark statement of Chris Do's document → delegate → automate approach to scaling. Foundational to the "codify-into-steps / delegation-margin / scaling" cluster; the process-documentation mechanics here (why document, what to document, the six-point how-to, "give them context") deserve full promotion into `wiki/topics/` + `persona/beliefs.md`.

## Summary

Chris Do argues that documenting your processes is the unlock for scaling a company. He frames two audiences for documentation — clients (covered elsewhere, in the "complete case study" product) and your **team** (the focus here). Because there are only 24 hours in a day, at some point you cannot and should not do everything yourself; delegating requires a process to be written down first. He gives concrete examples from The Futur: pointing every new designer to a public YouTube video ("How to Present Logo Designs") instead of one-on-one training, and writing step-by-step, screenshotted tutorials in Notion for website/marketing tasks that previously only he could do. Team members Matthew (Matthieu, video content team) and Jonah (intern/freelancer) corroborate the payoff: a documented Notion process let Matthew publish a site download himself in ~an hour while remote, without phoning Ben Burns, freeing Chris's time for higher-leverage work. The video closes with a six-point "how to document" list and Matthew's capstone tip: give the person **context** (the big goal / end result) before the steps.

## Key claims

### Chris Do
- [2018-12-19] Documenting your process "unlocks the door to start scaling your company." (paraphrase)
- [2018-12-19] There are two audiences who read your documented processes: your clients and your team. (paraphrase)
- [2018-12-19] There are two ways to grow a business: (1) sell the same product for more money (raise price), (2) sell more products (more clients/projects); selling more requires scaling, which means outsourcing and hiring. (paraphrase)
- [2018-12-19] There is no ceiling to what you can charge, but selling more projects to more clients requires scaling. (paraphrase)
- [2018-12-19] At some point you cannot do everything yourself — and you shouldn't; there are only 24 hours in a day. To delegate tasks you must have a process in place. (paraphrase)
- [2018-12-19] Example: instead of doing logo presentations himself or training every hired designer one-on-one, he points them to a video he made ("How to Present Logo Designs"); as long as they follow the process they need very little direction. (paraphrase)
- [2018-12-19] Earlier in 2018 he was the only person who could touch the website/marketing materials; the only thing holding the team back was knowledge, and "it's not even very technical." His brain power should be used on a more strategic level. (paraphrase)
- [2018-12-19] Fix: he wrote a step-by-step tutorial with screenshots for every process he had and shared it with the team (in Notion). (paraphrase)
- [2018-12-19] Because Matthew could put the download up himself, it saved Chris one-to-two hours to do something else (email strategy, new content). (paraphrase)
- [2018-12-19] Six tips for documenting your process: (1) start now — grab a notebook and pen, jot down what you're doing; (2) keep it simple; (3) be clear — don't give the new person fuzzy instructions; (4) be visual — designers/creatives are visual creatures; (5) use the right tool for the job — don't let the medium restrict you (typed + screenshots, or video, whichever is best); (6) make it shareable — The Futur uses Notion as a wiki anyone onboarded can access, plus public YouTube videos to train staff. (paraphrase)
