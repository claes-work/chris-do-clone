# Starting A Business in 2019? Unorthodox Marketing Techniques by Johnny Cupcakes

> ⚠️ ATTRIBUTION: This is a **guest-dominated** segment. Nearly all content is spoken by

## Summary

Johnny Earle (Johnny Cupcakes) walks through the unorthodox, low-budget marketing tactics
that built his t-shirt brand over ~17 years without traditional advertising. He describes
weekly/twice-weekly product drops tied to any excuse to celebrate (Valentine's Day,
St. Patrick's Day), reactive/nostalgia-driven "remix" merch, experiential stunts (giving
away a limited St. Patrick's Day shirt only to people who physically found him hiding in
Boston bushes dressed as a leprechaun), scarcity plays (a breakfast-themed shirt sold only
during breakfast hours), and user-generated-content contests (a water-bottle video contest
that let him sell slow-moving inventory at full price). He also details his pop-up-shop model:
500+ pop-ups, 99% rent-free, arranged via social media and barter — trading product/exposure
for space, framing it as an old-fashioned 1920s barter economy where both sides win. Chris Do
appears only at the close to affirm the tactics and reframe them as proof there is "no excuse"
for a new founder without a following to get started via barter.

## Key claims

### Johnny Cupcakes / Johnny Earle (CONTEXT — does NOT train persona)

- [2019-01-04] For 17 years in business, the brand spent little to no money on traditional
  advertising, relying on "bizarre adventures through t-shirts" — packaging, storytelling, and
  making memories around merchandise. (self-reported)
- [2019-01-04] The brand does not sell to retail stores and releases products weekly, sometimes
  twice a week, requiring a constant flow of ideas. (self-reported)
- [2019-01-04] They tie drops to any reason to celebrate (Valentine's Day, St. Patrick's Day)
  and do "reactive marketing" — e.g. a "cupcake bling" shirt within 24 hours of Drake's
  "Hotline Bling," pulling nostalgia references from everywhere. (self-reported)
- [2019-01-04] For a St. Patrick's Day "pot of gold cupcake and crossbones" shirt, he took a
  deliberate financial loss by giving it away free — only obtainable by physically finding him
  hiding in strangers' bushes in Boston dressed as a leprechaun; the weirder the stunt, the
  more inevitably customers share the story. (self-reported)
- [2019-01-04] Scarcity/experiential tactic: a breakfast-themed t-shirt you could only buy
  during breakfast hours. (self-reported)
- [2019-01-04] To move ~1,000–2,000 slow-selling custom water bottles, he ran the "Johnny
  Cupcakes water bottle contest" (submit a video of what you can do with the bottle to win a
  $500 gift card); entrants had to buy a bottle, so customers made content for the brand and
  the bottles sold through quickly at full price with no discounting. (self-reported)
- [2019-01-04] He has personally done 500+ pop-up shops, ~99% with no rent paid, arranged via
  social media by announcing tours and inviting people to host — locations ranged from a
  backyard barbecue to Facebook's headquarters to an art gallery. (self-reported)
- [2019-01-04] Pop-ups are framed as true partnerships: the host gets photos/video, cross-
  marketing, and a few hundred existing Johnny Cupcakes customers in their area brought in. (self-reported)
- [2019-01-04] For founders with no following, he advises adapting via revenue share (offer
  20/30/50% of the day's take) or bartering product for space (e.g. shirts for a frat party
  in exchange for a venue) — an "old-fashioned 1920s" barter approach. (self-reported)

### Chris Do (interviewer — trains persona)

- [2019-01-04] Chris affirms Johnny's tactics as "great suggestions" and praises that he
  "problem-solved that through a number of different ways" to conduct business.
- [2019-01-04] Chris's takeaway framing: there is "really no excuse" for aspiring founders —
  even without a social-media following, you can barter and trade your way in by making it a
  "win-win situation for both parties."

## Entity notes — Johnny Cupcakes / Johnny Earle (CONTEXT)

New context material for the existing Johnny-Cupcakes entity page (noted from 2018-10-19):
- ~17 years in business as of Jan 2019; grew with essentially zero traditional ad spend.
- Business model: no retail/wholesale; direct, weekly-to-biweekly limited "drop" cadence tied
  to holidays and pop-culture moments (reactive marketing).
- Signature tactics: experiential scavenger-hunt giveaways (leprechaun-in-a-bush), time-gated
  scarcity products (breakfast-only shirt), UGC contests to clear inventory at full price, and
  a rent-free pop-up model (500+ shops, ~99% free) sourced via social media and barter.
- Home base: Boston.
- Philosophy: build memories/stories around merchandise; barter and win-win partnerships as a
  core distribution strategy.
