## Summary

A one-minute clip from The Futur in which Seth Godin argues that writer's block (and,
by extension, creative block) is a myth. His claim: the paralysis we call "block" is
really an unwillingness to write *badly*. No one has "writing poorly block" — so if you
allow yourself to write poorly enough, your brain eventually gives up resisting and you
start writing well. He frames writing as a job like any trade (compares it to plumbing)
and challenges anyone claiming writer's block to first produce 50,000 words of bad
writing before deciding whether they are a writer.
