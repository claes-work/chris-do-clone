# Taking Risks - Changing Careers

★ **L3-candidate** — Contains a compact, reusable Chris Do framework on risk ("our relationship with risk needs to change; seek discomfort because breakthroughs are preceded by anxiety") plus a first-person career-risk chronology ($85k → $30k → no job → freelance → own business). Worth promoting into `wiki/topics/mindset/` and `persona/biography.md` + `persona/beliefs.md` (dated), not just held at L2.

## Summary

In a coaching Q&A clip, an audience member asks Chris Do how to have the confidence to switch careers into a new field without feeling they're sacrificing too much (leaving behind the benefits/security of their current job). Chris reframes the question: switching verticals *is* risky and always means starting over, so the real work is changing your relationship with risk. He argues that the moments right before a big breakthrough are filled with fear, anxiety and ambiguity — so most people avoid that discomfort and never explore the other life waiting for them. He makes it relatable by chronicling his own chain of risky career moves, and lands on a personal driver: he values freedom/writing his own destiny more than security, and would rather make far less money and work harder than work for someone else.

Speaker: two speakers. An unnamed **audience member / coachee** asks the opening question (`attribution: uncertain` — identity unknown). The rest is a monologue answer by **Chris Do** (first person — "I walked away from a job... that paid me $85,000 a year," "I'm not a guy who works well for other people"). Only Chris-attributed material trains the persona. The video opens and closes with a repeated Chris pull-quote ("what got you here won't get you there").

## Key claims

### Chris Do

- [2019-01-21] "What got you here won't get you there" — your life experiences reward you for doing the same thing repeatedly; anytime you try something new it will be horrible at first, but if you stick it out through the discomfort, wonderful things can happen. (paraphrase; also given as a direct quote below)
- [2019-01-21] Switching verticals is inherently risky and means starting over — that's just "part of how it works." You can't make it not-risky; you can only change how you relate to the risk. (paraphrase)
- [2019-01-21] Our relationship with risk and ambiguity needs to change: risk/uncertainty should be seen as a *good* thing to seek, not avoid. (paraphrase — a value statement)
- [2019-01-21] From motivation/breakthrough literature: the moments *before* a big breakthrough are filled with anxiety, fear, apprehension, nervousness, lack of clarity and ambiguity — and right after that part is "the best time." Most people avoid that nervousness and never explore the other life waiting for them. (paraphrase, cites "a lot of these books on motivation")
- [2019-01-21] Autobiographical risk chronology: came out of school into a job paying **$85,000/year**, left it for another job paying **$30,000/year**, then quit *that* for no job, went freelance, then got an opportunity to start his own business — "bouncing from one risky idea and activity to another." (paraphrase — self-reported; specific salary figures)
- [2019-01-21] He walked away from a guaranteed agency salary, moving up, where he was treated well, for basically no guaranteed income — because "the thing I loved more than what I was afraid of was the idea I could write my own destiny." (paraphrase + quote)
- [2019-01-21] Self-characterization: "I'm not a guy who works well for other people," doesn't like sharing the decision-making process, and is willing to shoulder the risk for the reward of freedom — "I'd rather make a lot less money, I'd rather work a lot harder than to work for someone else." (paraphrase + quote)
- [2019-01-21] He says he's still in front of the audience "because we keep taking those risks all the time." (paraphrase)
- [2019-01-21] Practical close: keep changing until you find the thing you love — the reward will be there for you, and if not, change again. (paraphrase)

### Audience member / coachee (`attribution: uncertain` — not the subject; does NOT train persona)

- [2019-01-21] Asks how to have the confidence to switch to a new field they're more passionate about without sacrificing too much — e.g. dropping the benefits/security of one job — and worries they'd be "making a stupid move by switching careers." (paraphrase)

## Notable verbatim quotes

All quotes below are **Chris Do**:

- "What got you here won't get you there — so all your life experiences reward you for doing the same thing over and over again, and anytime you try something new it's going to be horrible, but the reward is if you stick it out, if you go through the discomfort, wonderful things can happen for you."
- "Switching verticals is risky, so now we have to understand our relationship with risk — and is risk bad or good?"
- "The moments before a big breakthrough happen in your life are filled with anxiety, with fear, apprehension, nervousness... so a lot of us avoid that risk, that nervousness, that uncertainty, and we never explore that other life that waits for us."
- "I walked away from a job coming out of school that paid me $85,000 a year to try another job that then paid me $30,000 a year — that's super risky — and then I quit that job for no job."
- "The thing I loved more than what I was afraid of was the idea I could write my own destiny."
- "I'm not a guy who works well for other people... I'm willing to shoulder the risk for the reward of just freedom. I'd rather make a lot less money, I'd rather work a lot harder than to work for someone else."
- "Our relationship with risk and ambiguity needs to be changed — it should be a good thing, you should seek that, because the reward will be there for you. And if not, change again and just keep changing until you find the thing you love."
