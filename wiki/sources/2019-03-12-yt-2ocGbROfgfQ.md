# How to Create Engaging and Relevant Content for Instagram

## Summary

A short guest-interview clip. The guest — hand-letterer / illustrator **Stefan Kunz** — walks through how he built a viral chalk-wall lettering piece ("home is where the pants aren't"), explaining that growing on Instagram comes from making something people will *talk about* and engineering early engagement ("hype") so a post takes off before the algorithm buries it. **Chris Do**, hosting, reframes Stefan's work as conceptual, ad-like content design — praising him as a "master marketer" who happens to also be a great hand-lettering artist.

## Key claims

### Stefan Kunz (guest — Futur context only, NOT persona)

- [2019-03-12] To grow on Instagram, make something people will talk about — that's what gets you further, gets you recommended, and gets people saying "did you see this guy who…" (paraphrase)
- [2019-03-12] A post needs strong early engagement / "hype" or it can get lost in the algorithm; you want it to "take off strong" from the start. (paraphrase)
- [2019-03-12] For the "pants" piece he manufactured intrigue by framing the missing-pants detail as if it were a mistake ("I kind of forgot to put on pants for this one") so people would DM asking whether he really forgot — the confusion, then the reveal of the quote, created the buzz. (paraphrase)
- [2019-03-12] On his creative process: the idea evolved from "home is where the chalk wall is" to "home is where the pants aren't"; composition problem-solving started from two slanted lines (upper/lower line of the pants) that became a center to build the rest of the words around. (paraphrase)

### Chris Do (host — persona-eligible)

- [2019-03-12] Stefan doesn't treat Instagram the way most people do; he sits and thinks "what am I going to do that's going to get people to talk," designing a post conceptually — "almost like an ad… in a way that's really engaging." (paraphrase)
- [2019-03-12] Putting "one twist" into a piece and building interest around it — "you can call it hype, you can call it PR, marketing" — is what makes it work. (paraphrase)
- [2019-03-12] Framed Stefan as "a social media marketing person who just so happens to be a pretty darn good hand-lettering artist on the side," and as "the Johnny Cupcakes of the hand-letter world" — a master marketer. (paraphrase; Johnny Cupcakes = the streetwear brand, cited as a marketing exemplar)
- [2019-03-12] Half-serious business suggestion: Stefan could sell campaign design / creative direction — art-directing shoots — and charge $250,000. (paraphrase; the number is Chris's off-the-cuff hypothetical, not a real rate)
