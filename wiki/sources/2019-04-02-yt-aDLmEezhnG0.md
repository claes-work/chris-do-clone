# Losing A Big Job Was The Best Thing To Happen To This Photographer

★ **L3-candidate (partial)** — Chris Do's social-media-strategy points here (feed-vs-stories, vulnerability sells, gatekeepers are gone, earned vs. bought followers, brand-ambassador/branded-content shift) restate and extend his content-strategy positions and could feed `wiki/topics/content-strategy/` + `persona/beliefs.md`. The guest's story itself is context, not persona material.

## Summary

On-stage interview: Chris Do talks with photographer **Yvette** (a working commercial/celebrity photographer) about her losing a large camera-manufacturer job because her Instagram following was too small (they wanted ~50k; she was crossing above/below ~1,000). Chris reframes the loss as a gift and a growth moment, uses it to attack the "old guard" resentment of social media, and argues creators must play the new game: put yourself into your brand, be vulnerable/authentic, curate the feed but experiment in stories, chase engagement over vanity metrics, and position as a brand ambassador as ad money migrates from TV to influencer/branded content. After Yvette went public with the story it got picked up (fStoppers, PetaPixel, Pasadena Now) and her account more than doubled (~1,000 → ~2,500), with a stated goal of reaching 10,000 by year-end.

Speaker: host **Chris Do** (subject) interviewing guest **Yvette** (context, not the subject), in front of a live audience that asks questions. **Bonnie** (another photographer) is referenced as an exemplar but does not speak here. Only Chris-attributed material trains the persona.

## Key claims

### Chris Do (subject)

- [2019-04-02] Creators who spent time and money learning their craft resent that "self-taught" newcomers "own" social media, but fighting that is a losing game. (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-02] "You need to put you into your brand" — a photographer must put herself into her work to separate from a noisy landscape of image-makers. (quote + paraphrase)
- [2019-04-02] Vulnerability and authenticity are what people buy into; holding an idealized "perfect image" is unrelatable and nobody cares. When Yvette shared her real, vulnerable story her account more than doubled even though her feed barely changed. (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-02] Invokes Simon Sinek: "people don't buy what you do, they buy why you do it." (paraphrase, attributed to Sinek)
- [2019-04-02] "The gatekeepers are gone" — wonderful and horrible at once: the doors are open, nobody's minding the shop, and young people native to the now/future are "taking your lunch." (quote + paraphrase)
- [2019-04-02] The chase for relevance and for big brands to hire icons to shoot/partner is nothing new; only the media platform changed. Cites Irving Penn shooting for Clinique and Annie Leibovitz / Mark Seliger for Vanity Fair — "iconic shooters shooting icons for an iconic brand." (paraphrase, third-party examples)
- [2019-04-02] The old-guard complaint ("why should we who earned our seat play this dumb game?") is answered by Yvette's point that they always played this game — it was just called printing/binding expensive books, mailing them, meeting art buyers and reps. (paraphrase, Chris relaying Yvette's framing approvingly)
- [2019-04-02] "Change is inevitable but progress — that's a choice." (quote)
- [2019-04-02] On feed vs. stories: the feed/grid is "precious" and should be curated to a consistent aesthetic (an editorial eye + a consistent tone of voice); stories are immediate, ephemeral, and disappear, so use them to experiment and explore your voice — post your "weird stuff" there and see what resonates. Consistency and trying things are not mutually exclusive. (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-02] Consistency is about an aesthetic/editorial eye and a consistent tone of voice, NOT a split personality (grind-guy one day, vulnerable the next). (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-02] Create content for your audience, not yourself: "I'm not there for me, I'm there for them" — just ask the audience what they want and give it to them. (quote + paraphrase)
- [2019-04-02] Follower counts are vanity metrics; a thousand earned followers beats a hundred thousand bought bots. Low engagement (e.g., 200k followers, 16 likes / one retweet) exposes inauthenticity, and brand ambassadors won't work with that. (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-02] To explain suspiciously high followers with few posts: they may have deleted/archived hundreds of images that didn't work ("clean up your account, it's messy"), or bought followers. A viral meme with 200k likes on a 2,400-follower account shows likes don't convert. (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-02] "You have to be consistent... just because you have one hit doesn't make you the Beatles." Audiences decide within a couple of swipes whether to follow, and will unfollow when a feed drifts. (quote + paraphrase)
- [2019-04-02] Freelancers need thick skin — you'll lose many jobs, and losing/gaining more jobs means more momentum; some losses you stop and reflect on, others you let go and move on. (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-02] No amount of "ninja skill training" / call-qualifying would have saved this particular job — the client needed a photographer who would also give them a social-media boost; that boost was the value-add, and he doesn't blame them for the decision. (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-02] Advertising (as a career path) is "done" / a "dark path"; brands like Amex are redirecting huge media-buy budgets (cited "half a billion a year") away from TV into Facebook/Instagram, influencers, and branded content. "The fall of one Empire means the birth of another." (paraphrase; the Amex figure is Chris's own rough, self-flagged out-of-date number)
- [2019-04-02] Losing this job with "the curtain lifted" — understanding the real reason you lost — is "one of the greatest gifts of a career." (quote + paraphrase)
- [2019-04-02] Personal aside: growing up bullied, he got fired up and "fired back" on social media defending Yvette against a YouTube creator who questioned her character/story. (paraphrase, self-disclosure)

### Yvette (guest — context, NOT the subject)

- [2019-04-02] She's shot for decades, including some of the biggest celebrities; jobs come many ways — sometimes you're told "you're perfect, are you available these four days?" and you say yes; other times you bid (she's done "hundreds and hundreds" of proposals). There's no fixed step-A-B-C order. (paraphrase, guest)
- [2019-04-02] Budget hadn't been discussed yet; she didn't care about budget in that moment — only that a big manufacturer said she was perfect — and ~20 minutes later the opportunity was withdrawn for lack of followers. (paraphrase, guest)
- [2019-04-02] "We've been playing this game since the beginning, it was just called something else" — books, binding, mailing, meeting art buyers, chasing reps. (quote, guest)
- [2019-04-02] Her Instagram feed looks "schizophrenic"/unorganized across many kinds of work (cookbook, high-end weddings, advertising, personal); her takeaway from Chris: the portfolio lives in the feed/grid, the story lives in stories. (paraphrase, guest)
