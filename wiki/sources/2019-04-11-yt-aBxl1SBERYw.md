## Summary

A two-person conversation about self-image, external validation, and emotional regulation. Chris Do describes reaching a place where self-acceptance replaced the need for external approval: he treats himself as the best judge of his own work, so praise doesn't inflate him and criticism only confirms what he already knows. He recounts consciously modeling his emotional state on Spock/Vulcans — stripping away emotion because it clouds judgment — and "compressing" his emotional highs and lows like an audio sound-limiter. The second speaker adds his own arc (swinging on outside opinion, staying in a funk for months) and credits coaching from Chris with teaching him that external validation doesn't work.

## Key claims

### Chris Do

- [2019-04-11] He now lives "in [his] own universe" because he considers himself the best judge of his own work; when someone says it sucks they're only affirming what he already knows, and he'll do better next time. (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-11] Praise doesn't build him up either — he knows he worked hard and made it as good as possible, so he doesn't respond much to external judgment in either direction. (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-11] "That's why I don't really care" — people come up and say they hate him or love him, and it doesn't move him because he knows who he is. (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-11] His whole aim was to be "even-keeled," so he deliberately modeled his emotional state on Spock. (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-11] His reasoning: Vulcans stripped away emotion over millennia because emotion clouds judgment, leaving purely logical creatures with superior intellect and strength — everything he aspired to. After being hurt over relationships that didn't work out, he decided that if emotions meant the highest highs and lowest lows, he'd rather "clip off" both — like running his feelings through a sound limiter/compressor, cutting the peaks and the bass. (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-11] He jokes that compressing emotion too much may be why he "sounds a little funky" — because he doesn't want to be ruled by emotion; he wants to do what's right. (paraphrase, self-deprecating)
- [2019-04-11] Reported speech (Chris quoted by Speaker B): Chris coached him that he had to find internal happiness, because the external validation he'd been running his life on "doesn't work — look at where you are right now; if it worked you wouldn't be here." (paraphrase, reported)
