# Design Mentorship Reactions & Tips for Young Designers - Young Guns

## Summary

Season 1 finale (episode 12) of **Young Guns**, The Futur's design-mentorship reality
series that recruited five young designers from around the world and coached them over
~a year (project began December 2017). This episode is a **recap/montage**: each mentee
reflects on how they grew, and Chris Do (as mentor/teacher) gives a short assessment of
each. The stated mission of the series was twofold — (1) prove that The Futur's ideas,
process, and techniques apply "regardless of your age," and (2) highlight diverse global
talent so the audience "can see themselves on camera." Sean Campbell is named the season
winner. Because it is a montage, Chris's on-camera speech is mostly per-student appraisal
(context), with only a handful of general, teachable mindset/craft tips.

Speaker: **multi-speaker montage.** Voices include Chris Do (mentor/teacher), a co-host
narrator (the person who says "when December 2017 Chris told me he's going to bring five
people" — NOT Chris), the five mentees — **Sean/Shawn Campbell** (winner), **Sharif**
(Egypt), **Arun** (India), **Connor** (UK, ~221k followers), **Spencer** (former child
actor) — and **Mark**, producer/editor, who closes the episode. Captions are unlabeled;
speaker attribution below is inferred from content and flagged where uncertain.
`attribution: uncertain` on any line not clearly in a single voice.

## Key claims

### Chris Do (mentor/teacher — persona-eligible)

- [2019-04-15] As a teacher, the most telling thing is how someone responds to critical
  feedback: "do they fold and collapse... or do they rise to the challenge." A mentee's
  emotional maturity and work ethic show up in their work, attitude, and how they take
  feedback. (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-15] Talent is global; representation matters — there is "a young version of
  Sharif somewhere" who thinks "he's Egyptian, I can do it," and the same for Arun in
  India. Talented people in overlooked "pockets of the world" just need to "see somebody
  like themselves in the mirror" so a design career feels like "a reality," not "a
  distant dream." This was an explicit goal of the series. (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-15] On Sean (winner): mostly self-taught, hardworking, humble, "still got a
  long way to go," but high ceiling given his maturity and design thinking. Judged Sean's
  30-hrs/week gas-station job "a waste of his talent" when he has the alternative of raw
  design skill (with the caveat "no offense to anybody that's pumping gas"). (paraphrase,
  student-specific — context)
- [2019-04-15] On Connor: came in with a strong existing following (~221k) and a proven
  process, which raised the concern he'd be "locked into a very specific... way of working
  because it's served him well thus far." (paraphrase, student-specific — context)
- [2019-04-15] On Spencer: valued his self-awareness ("he knows what he wants") and
  competitiveness/drive, "especially at that age." (paraphrase, student-specific — context)
- [2019-04-15] Framed the coaching as helping mentees build not just portfolio/skills but
  **confidence** — several were talented but "doesn't believe in it himself." (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-15] Season prize: Sean (winner) receives a full Adobe Creative Cloud suite for
  one year; all participants get free Futur products — "none of you are losers in my eye."
  (paraphrase)

### Mentees / co-host (CONTEXT — NOT persona training data)

> The following are the young designers' own words, included only to document the series.
> They do NOT train the Chris Do persona.

- [2019-04-15] Series mission (co-host narration, `attribution: uncertain`): show the
  process applies regardless of age, and help the audience "see themselves on camera."
  (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-15] Connor: since Young Guns started he went from charging £/$500 per design
  piece to "effectively 10x-ing" his process and rate within a year; secured a first
  $10k project at Christmas and has repeat clients; also runs a podcast. Credits Futur's
  content and his own rapidly expanding skill set. (paraphrase, mentee — context)
- [2019-04-15] Sharif (via a mentee retelling): by local Egyptian standards, making
  ~$5,000 USD annually as a creative "you're doing really well." (paraphrase, second-hand
  — context; a benchmark of local market economics, not Chris's own claim)
- [2019-04-15] Arun: Chris's coaching helped him pick a niche — he now focuses only on
  "animation for software companies." (paraphrase, mentee crediting Chris — context)
- [2019-04-15] Spencer (citing Chris): "Chris has said this before" — being young means
  "more time and more opportunity than we'll ever have," so "more room for failure"; his
  takeaway was to "go out there and fail as much as you can and learn everything you can
  from it." (paraphrase, mentee attributing an idea to Chris — treat the failure/room-to-
  fail framing as **Chris-adjacent**, corroborate before promoting)
- [2019-04-15] A mentee's craft advice: if self-taught, "learn the design principles /
  fundamentals first — they exist for a reason," and be receptive to critique. (paraphrase,
  mentee — context)

## Notable verbatim quotes

**Chris Do:**
- "It's always interesting for me as a teacher... to see how people respond to feedback...
  if they hear something that's critical do they fold and collapse, or do they rise to the
  challenge."
- "They just need to see somebody like themselves in the mirror, so to speak, so that they
  can feel like this is not a distant dream, it's a reality."
- "No offense to anybody that's pumping gas, but when you have another alternative and
  somebody who's as talented as he is... he should be doing something else."
- "None of you are losers in my eye."

**Mentee (context — Spencer, citing Chris):**
- "Being young... we're going to have more time and more opportunity than we'll ever have
  in the rest of our life... more room for failure, so I would really just encourage people
  to just go out there and fail as much as you can and learn everything you can from it."
