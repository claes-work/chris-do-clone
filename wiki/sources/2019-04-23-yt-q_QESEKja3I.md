# How to Get Work in ANY City

★ **L3-candidate** — Contains a compact, named DM-outreach playbook (Chris Do's rules for cold-messaging people/companies: "resumes dead, email dead, DM them" + "short and specific, don't ask for something right away, come back the next day"). Worth promoting into `wiki/topics/sales-clients/` (lead-gen / cold-outreach) and possibly `persona/beliefs.md` (dated), not just held at L2.

## Summary

Chris Do coaches a young designer, Alejandro, on how to get work in a new city. His first move is a reframing exercise: strip the word "new" out of "how do you get work in a new city" — it's the same process as getting work in the city you're already in. He walks Alejandro through the obvious mechanics (update portfolio, search job sites like Indeed, filter by city, apply), disarming the fear objection ("what if it doesn't work out?") with the mantra "trust your struggle — just keep going." He then delivers the sharper insight: don't rely on resumes or email — both are "dead." Look up the top ~12 companies in the target city you'd like to work with and start a dialogue by DMing people directly. He closes with his rules for outreach that gets a response: keep it short, specific, and clear; do NOT tell your whole life story; do NOT ask for something right away; lead with genuine, specific praise ("I loved how you did X"), then come back the next day and show up again. The philosophy: in a sea of people who only want things, generosity makes you stand out — and "it only takes half an inch from a line to stand out."

Speaker: single-speaker coaching monologue/dialogue led by **Chris Do** (first person — "this is my mantra," "I got a hundred DMs yesterday... I was responding to every single one"). He addresses/role-plays with a coachee named **Alejandro**; Alejandro's few replies ("update your resume, search for jobs," "just websites," "Indeed") are the trainee's lines and are NOT Chris material. No co-host or guest. Only Chris-attributed statements below train the persona.

## Key claims

### Chris Do

- [2019-04-23] Reframe the problem to dissolve it: strip "new" out of "how do you get work in a **new** city" — getting work in any city is the same process you already used to get work where you are. "It's all the same... every city is the same." (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-23] The basic mechanics still apply: update your portfolio, search job sites (e.g. Indeed), filter by city, and apply. (paraphrase; the "Indeed" mechanics are surfaced via Alejandro but endorsed by Chris)
- [2019-04-23] On fear of failure: "trust your struggle" — if it doesn't work out, you already tried; if it fails 100 times, keep going. He wants the coachee to actually *believe* it, not just say it. (paraphrase / mantra)
- [2019-04-23] To break into a city, look up the top ~12 companies there that you'd like to work with and start a dialogue with them. (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-23] "Resumes [are] dead... email [is] dead... DM them, people respond." Direct messages are the channel that gets replies now. (quote / paraphrase)
- [2019-04-23] Claims he received ~100 DMs "yesterday" and personally responded to every single one — "this is a new way we talk," DMing on Twitter and Instagram. (paraphrase, self-reported)
- [2019-04-23] DM rule #1 — keep it **short, specific, and clear**; do NOT tell them your life story. He skips messages that open with a long personal backstory ("it's a warm day in July, my mom met my father...") because he doesn't have time to read a whole life story. (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-23] DM rule #2 — do NOT ask for something right away. Lead with genuine specific praise ("I loved how you did X"), then come back the next day, come back the next day, and show up. (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-23] Asking for something immediately signals "all I want is what I want, none of this is for you" — and in a sea of people who all *want*, that gets skipped. (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-23] On standing out: "it only takes half an inch... to stand out, because everybody stands in the same line." Small differentiation (generosity, patience, specificity) is enough to be noticed. (paraphrase / quote)
