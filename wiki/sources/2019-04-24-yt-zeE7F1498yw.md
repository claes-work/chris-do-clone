# How to Make More Content and Reach More People

## Summary

Chris Do explains his content-marketing engine: take one piece of long-form
content and "chop it up into seven different ways," reformatting each cut to
fit the platform it lands on. He illustrates leverage with a story about a
superfan (based in Croatia or Greece) who read and watched everything Chris
posted, scraped the highest-engaged pieces, and reposted them across platforms
so effectively that The Futur hired him — creating a friendly "weird
competition" between Chris and the fan over reposting Chris's own content. He
credits this repurposing approach with growing his personal following from
~1,500 to nearly 60,000.

Speaker: single-speaker monologue by **Chris Do** (first person — "my own
account," "I'm the person [who] responds to everything," "Ben Burns[,] find
this man a job with us," "how I got from like 1500 followers to like almost
60,000"). Ben Burns and the superfan are referenced, not speaking. Attribution
is confident: this is Chris himself teaching content strategy, not a team-member
production tutorial. Material trains the persona.

## Key claims

### Chris Do

- [2019-04-24] You can take one piece of content and chop it into seven
  different formats, giving the audience each in the way they want it. (quote/paraphrase)
- [2019-04-24] The Futur is a team of twelve; many people help, but on his own
  personal accounts Chris responds to everything himself. (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-24] A superfan (living in Croatia or Greece) read/watched every single
  thing Chris posted across Twitter, Instagram, and Facebook, scraped the
  highest-engaged content, and reposted it to other platforms — and was
  eventually hired and paid to do it. (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-24] This created a "weird competition": Chris would go to repurpose
  his own content and find the fan had already moved it to another platform, so
  they end up competing to repost Chris's material. (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-24] Audiences find you through unexpected doors — one person found
  The Futur via the podcast (not their main vehicle), another via a written
  post, another realized they had a YouTube channel — so distributing across
  formats/platforms widens discovery. (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-24] The repurposing pipeline: record long-form content → have it
  transcribed → cut the transcript into micro-articles → the craziest line pulled
  out becomes a tweet → that tweet coupled with an image becomes an Instagram
  story. (paraphrase)
- [2019-04-24] Don't send the identical thing everywhere — "make it work for its
  platform." Do that and you'll see tremendous growth. (quote/paraphrase)
- [2019-04-24] This repurposing approach is how he grew from ~1,500 followers to
  almost 60,000. (paraphrase, self-reported)
