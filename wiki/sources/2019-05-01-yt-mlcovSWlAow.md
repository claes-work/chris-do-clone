# Belief Systems—Unlocking Your Potential

★ **L3-candidate** — Contains a named, self-contained mindset framework (Chris Do's "belief system" model: inserting an **interpretation** step between input and output, plus a set of positive/negative word reframes — FAIL, FEAR, "no", pain, "safe"). Worth promoting into `wiki/topics/mindset/` and `persona/beliefs.md` (dated) as a distinct piece from the existing thoughts→words→actions belief-cycle material.

## Summary

A short documentary-style edit built around Chris Do's keynote and impromptu Q&A at the **AIGA National Design Conference** (his first time attending; captions garble AIGA as "HHH"). His thesis: creatives don't need secret tools or techniques — outcomes come down to your **belief system**, i.e. what you believe to be true or false. He inserts a critical, often-overlooked step between "input" and "output": **interpretation**. The same input yields infinite possible outputs depending on how you choose to see it, so he deliberately chooses an interpretation (be happy, everything is a work in progress) that "lifts him up." He then reframes fear-loaded words positively (FAIL = first attempt in learning; "no" = time for the next opportunity; FEAR = fantasized experience appearing real; pain = precursor to notable things) and inverts a comforting word negatively ("safe" = always following everyone / stay average forever). Interstitials show the team's live-stream misadventures; a guest appearance from Debbie Millman ties creativity to insecurity; and a whiteboard Q&A moment restates the "stop selling, start serving" sales idea.

Speaker: primarily **Chris Do** (first-person keynote + Q&A). **Debbie Millman** (Design Matters podcast host) speaks briefly as a guest in the booth. An unnamed **Futur team member / narrator** provides B-roll voiceover about the live-stream logistics. Audience members ("Masa," a young designer "Jody/Jodie") are referenced but their lines are Chris paraphrasing/role-playing.

## Key claims

### Chris Do

- [2019-05-01] Most creatives think a secret sauce, tool, or technique would unlock them, but the truth is it all boils down to your **belief system** — what you believe to be true and not true. (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-01] The naive model is input → output; the missing step is **interpretation**: input → interpretation → output. How you look at things determines the output. (paraphrase, framework)
- [2019-05-01] There is subjective "individual reality" (what you believe is real) which sometimes rubs against objective physical reality; your life depends on how you look at the world. (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-01] A single input has an infinite number of possible outputs — "if you want to be angry you will find a way to be angry... I just happen to choose to be happy, that works much better for me." So he built a belief system that lifts him up and reminds him that falling down is okay and everything is a work in progress. (quote + paraphrase)
- [2019-05-01] Negative self-talk / low self-esteem / low self-worth means we lack **internal validation**, so we seek it externally — and when we look for validation from others, they can take advantage of us (e.g. when a client asks you to do something). (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-01] Reframing fear-words: **Fail** = "the first attempt in learning"; **No** = "just time for your next opportunity" (the door closing); **Fear** = "a fantasized experience appearing real — hasn't happened yet"; **Pain** may be necessary for good things, "the precursor to anything notable." (paraphrase, acronym reframes)
- [2019-05-01] Inverting a positive word: **Safe** = "always following everyone" and "stay average forever" — you don't want to be safe. To insult a designer, tell them their work is "average." (paraphrase + quote)
- [2019-05-01] To believe and then do it is "the secret formula"; when your belief about what is possible changes, "that's all it takes." (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-01] Don't mistake somebody's **best** work for what they do all the time — that's a disservice to creative people. (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-01] Advice for creators: "make stuff without judging it... make it all the time and be very prolific," then someone will say "that's amazing" and you learn to listen to that — you can test this in real time via Instagram engagement numbers. (quote + paraphrase)
- [2019-05-01] Clients form **mental shortcuts** about you ("Jody does web comps") — this is heuristics, a form of bias, because we operate on too little information as a survival technique. (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-01] In a pricing Q&A: "stop selling... start **serving** — how can I be of service to you?" You can't diagnose if you've asked zero questions; the goal is to figure out whether there's a problem to be solved. (quote + paraphrase; restates prior "stop selling, start serving" idea)
- [2019-05-01] Success metric for a talk: impacting the life of even one person in a 1,400-person conference "you've done something wonderful"; other speakers told him afterward "I needed to hear that" and that he energized them. (paraphrase)

### Debbie Millman (guest)

- [2019-05-01] She has not found a common thread for how people become creative, but suspects one may be "a pervasive sense of **insecurity**" — creativity as a way to express thinking/ideas and a response to insecurities — though she notes this is anecdotal, not empirical. (paraphrase)

## Notable verbatim quotes

**Chris Do:**
> "It all boiled down to your belief system — what you believe to be true and not to be true."

> "If you want to be angry you will find a way to be angry, and if you want to feel hurt you will find a way to feel hurt. I just happen to choose to be happy — that works much better for me."

> "Fail is just the first attempt in learning."

> "Fear could be a fantasized experience appearing real — hasn't happened yet."

> "Safe could be simply always following everyone... safe could be stay average forever."

> "If you're a creator, make stuff without judging it — and make it all the time and be very prolific, and then somebody will say that's amazing... and learn to listen to that."

> "Stop selling. You want to close more jobs? Start serving. How can I be of service to you? You've asked me zero questions — how can you diagnose if you haven't asked me any questions?"

> "The thing that you need to do is to believe and then do it. That's the secret formula."

**Debbie Millman:**
> "One of the threads very well maybe [is] a pervasive sense of insecurity — that creativity is a way to express thinking and ideas, and that creativity becomes a response to the insecurities. But I don't have any empirical evidence to suggest that; it's really anecdotal."
