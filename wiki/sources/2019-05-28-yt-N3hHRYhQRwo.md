# Designers Ask How To Start on Youtube — w/ Melinda Livsey and...

★ **L3-candidate** — Chris Do's coaching here is a self-contained, rich playbook for
*starting* a YouTube channel from zero: video length, front-loading value, personality
over information, being polarizing, format archetypes, studying other creators, and
managing expectations. This is Chris speaking as a content-strategy authority to
beginners and is worth promoting into `wiki/topics/content-strategy/` and dated entries
in `persona/beliefs.md`, not just held at L2.

## Summary

A panel/coaching episode: Chris Do advises two Futur Pro Group members — Melinda Livsey
and Raoul — who want to launch a co-hosted YouTube show about brand strategy and brand
identity. Chris does the overwhelming majority of the talking (roughly 70-80%), delivering
a start-from-zero playbook: don't treat early time as wasted, be nimble and let audience
comments (with timestamps) shape you, keep videos 4-9 minutes and front-load value,
realize people tune in for *personality* not information, stand for something / be
polarizing, pick a format (contrast between co-hosts, pro/con, play-by-play + color
commentator), study and mimic other creators as training, and lower your expectations
("the first 10 episodes we're gonna be terrible"). The episode ends with Chris live-coaching
Melinda and Raoul through their show's outro, take by take.

Speaker: **Chris Do** (host/coach, dominant voice), **Melinda Livsey** (guest, context —
brand identity designer pivoting to brand strategy), **Raoul / Rahul** (guest, context —
brand identity designer; caption spells his name inconsistently). Third parties cited by
Chris: Gary Vaynerchuk, Zimri Mayfield ("Zimri"/"Zimre"), Aaron Draplin, Marques Brownlee
(MKBHD), Simon Cade (DSLR Guide), Phil DeFranco, Joe Rogan, David C. Baker & Blair Enns
(the "2 Bobs"), Gordon Ramsay, Jimmy Fallon — all as examples, not present.

## Key claims

### Chris Do

- [2019-05-28] Early effort on a channel isn't "time wasted" — it is time developing who
  you are, your personality, the technical craft, and learning to listen to your audience.
  (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-28] Go in with a plan, but stay nimble — cites Mike Tyson: "everybody has a plan
  until they get punched in the face." Your "punch in the face" is discovering the audience
  loves content you didn't plan to keep making; listen and give them more of what they want.
  (paraphrase + quote)
- [2019-05-28] When asking for comments, tell viewers to include a **timestamp** so you know
  exactly which moment they mean (credits Gary Vaynerchuk for the tip) — viewers might say
  they loved the "deep" part at what you think is minute 22 when they mean minute 4.
  (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-28] Social platforms like YouTube and Instagram are **conversational, not
  monologue** — you post, read comments, respond, and over time people feel they know you;
  one-directional broadcasting is "the old media way." (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-28] For beginners, keep videos **4 to 9 minutes**; 10 minutes is too long. The
  Futur's own average watch time is ~9 min and YouTube's average is ~5 min. Push past that
  and half your content may never be viewed. (paraphrase; note he flags his own numbers as
  approximate — "we'll have to update this video to make sure I'm not lying")
- [2019-05-28] **Front-load the video with value.** Knowledge is like medicine — wrap it in
  something digestible and "sugarcoat it." Start by telling viewers what they'll get and be
  excited to teach it. In movies they say "cut to the car chase" — bring people up into the
  moment of conflict; don't open with backstory. (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-28] The "car crash" opening principle translates across platforms: on Instagram
  it's the caption; on YouTube it's the title + thumbnail. (this connection partly voiced by
  Raoul and affirmed by Chris) (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-28] With two co-hosts, define your **roles and contrast** — don't say the same
  thing in different voices. He and Jose Caballer deliberately pulled out the contrast
  between them (like Oscar and Felix from *The Odd Couple*). (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-28] Format archetypes to consider: pro/con debate format; a sports-broadcast pair
  (factual **play-by-play** + emotional **color commentator**, cites boxing announcer Jim
  Lampley). (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-28] **People do not tune in for information — they tune in for personality** and
  how relatable/connected they feel to you. This is "really really important." (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-28] **Stand for something or you stand for nothing** — avoid being "all things to
  all people." Have a point of view / a lens on the world. It doesn't have to be perfect at
  the start; big YouTubers' first videos aren't who they are today — they grow into it.
  (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-28] **Be polarizing** — for or against something — because it incites passion and
  makes people want your specific take. Riding the fence makes people not care. (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-28] Cites Zimri Mayfield: initial videos were generic and unremarkable; growth
  (300k+ subs) came from leaning into his goofy, zany, self-deprecating personality — his
  programmatic "challenge and rules" episodes are boring, his goofy logo critiques get the
  views. (paraphrase, third-party example)
- [2019-05-28] "The 10% that you're scared to let the world see — that's the 10% that you
  need to make 100%." (quote) — reveal the personality you're hiding. (quote)
- [2019-05-28] Education-only content is a trap: watch education channels and "see if you can
  stay awake." Content sits on an **entertainment ↔ education spectrum**. LinkedIn Learning
  tutorials are technical but sleep-inducing; Aaron Draplin's Skillshare logo class feels
  like a documentary (you watch him sketch and get philosophical) and is one of the most
  viewed courses. (paraphrase, examples)
- [2019-05-28] History-of-Food-Network analogy: it started technical (measurements,
  temperatures) then moved to lifestyle/reality; Julia Child taught cooking but the
  celebrities today (Gordon Ramsay on *Kitchen Nightmares* / *Hell's Kitchen*) draw viewers
  through personality and conflict, then sneak in basic teaching so "you're not bored by the
  tactical steps." Chris advises finding your spot on that spectrum. (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-28] **Structure the show before the content** — "think about the show map first,
  the content second." A compelling structure fills itself in, like a sitcom: once the
  characters (Homer, Bart, Marge) are defined, you drop them in a situation (they win the
  lottery, go on vacation) and the story writes itself. They have 50 topics but no hook —
  the hook/structure is the missing piece. (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-28] Pitch test: if you walked into a Hollywood executive and said "we're really
  knowledgeable and have interesting stories," you'd get "next." You need a **catchphrase**
  and a clear premise. His show with Jose opened with a defined framing ("the business of
  design / the design of business") and then they'd just argue. Phil DeFranco: "hello you
  beautiful bastards… you've been filled in." (paraphrase + quote)
- [2019-05-28] **Bits and formats** make any guest work — Jimmy Fallon designs bits (karaoke,
  lip-sync, games) so it's fun no matter who shows up. That's how you know you have a format.
  (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-28] The "just throw us in a room and we'll riff" model (Joe Rogan) only works if
  you genuinely are that deeply-read, experienced, story-rich person — Rogan reads a lot,
  has done TV/UFC/comedy, so he can talk about anything. Ask honestly if you are that person.
  (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-28] The 2 Bobs (David C. Baker & Blair Enns) work because both are well-read
  expert consultants/writers/workshop-leaders with (he suspects) a format and notes; it can
  sound "too smooth," almost premeditated. (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-28] **"The art of being a professional is being able to read something that's
  premeditated or follow a show structure and make it look natural."** What looks
  spontaneous may be hours or weeks of work. (quote/paraphrase)
- [2019-05-28] Tells Melinda and Raoul directly they are **not** the loud/bombastic type
  ("Canadian and OC girl") and shouldn't force it — there's a place for chill: cites Marques
  Brownlee (MKBHD, ~19M subs, super chill, #1 tech reviewer) and Simon Cade (DSLR Guide,
  British, sits mid-spectrum — educational but held by who he is; his philosophy: never buy
  another camera, gear gets in the way of becoming a storyteller). (paraphrase, examples)
- [2019-05-28] "**Now is the time** for young people to create content" because you bring a
  peer group with you (each creator is older than the audience beneath them); you can't
  authentically be the brand-new "noob" once you have experience. (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-28] Entering a crowded space, you must **study the body of work** — "writers need
  to read, directors need to watch films," so influencers must watch other YouTubers, break
  down structure, openings, endings, catchphrases, mannerisms. (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-28] Admits (not embarrassed) that he watches enormous amounts of YouTube, Netflix
  comedy specials, and late-night shows through the lens of "what can I learn," and actively
  **mimics** performers (paces like Chris Rock, studies enunciation, Graham Norton's
  conversational style) as training — "like ballet" / muscle memory. He also notes Phil
  DeFranco's on-air voice isn't how he talks when interviewed — it's a performed "YouTube
  vlogger voice." (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-28] If your quiet/subtle personality is the thing, **lean into it and make it
  lovable** — "the world is full of people who are uncomfortable in front of the camera; if
  you can make that your thing and make it lovable" it works. (paraphrase + quote)
- [2019-05-28] On "be yourself": the flat, chill you-alone isn't the on-camera you — channel
  the amped-up version of yourself that shows up when a beloved old friend visits. Reveal
  *that* person to the world. (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-28] Self-description of his own polarizing brand: "I'm super opinionated, I like
  to critique… I'm a constant improver, I'm not going to mince my words, I'm very direct —
  that's part of my culture. If you don't like that, I'm fine with that." (quote/paraphrase)
- [2019-05-28] Resents unsolicited direction on his channel — after 23 years running his
  company and working 14-16 hour days, the channel is the first place he has "the freedom and
  autonomy to do and say and think what I want," so he reacts badly to "get your hand off my
  hand" backseat advice. "I work 80 hours not to work 40 hours for someone else." (quote)
- [2019-05-28] Accepts the cost of polarizing: haters, unsubscribes, mean tweets are "the
  consequences," vastly outweighed by the private messages, testimonials, and wins. The
  ketchup/mustard metaphor: you're for a specific type of person — "find your tribe." (paraphrase)
- [2019-05-28] **Lower expectations to zero.** Tell yourself "the first 10 episodes we're
  gonna be terrible" so you have nowhere to go but up; most people expect to be amazing and
  get depressed when views/comments disappoint. Invokes the formula **"happiness is inversely
  proportionate to expectations."** (paraphrase + quote)
- [2019-05-28] Coaching craft for co-hosts: don't "ping-pong back and forth"; when physically
  together, talk to each other and take advantage of it; in an outro you can summarize the
  episode, thank viewers, remind them who you are, and land a catchphrase; then get out —
  "don't drag it out." Energy has to "build up inside you like it's ready to burst and then
  shoot it out." (paraphrase)

### Melinda Livsey (context)

- [2019-05-28] She and Raoul met in the Futur program, became friends, worked on projects,
  and want to start a YouTube show at Chris's earlier prompting; it's a jump-off from her
  existing blogging about pivoting from brand identity to brand strategy and how strategy
  helps designers build better brands. (paraphrase — context, does not train persona)
- [2019-05-28] Their core worry: they have lots of value/stories but don't know if people
  will care, and can't decide the hook or keep it under five minutes. (paraphrase — context)
- [2019-05-28] Affirms the "car crash" opening applies across platforms — for Instagram it's
  the caption. (paraphrase — context)

### Raoul / Rahul (context)

> attribution: uncertain — captions spell the name "Raoul," "Rahul," and "Raul"; treat as

- [2019-05-28] Identifies as a brand identity designer; he and Melinda had many "aha moments"
