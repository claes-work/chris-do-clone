## Summary

Episode 9 of *Building A Brand* documents the packaging-design phase of Blind's rebrand of Hamilton
Family Brewery. Design intern Jun creates dozens of beer-can mock-ups; creative director Ben Burns
and designer Matthew Encina critique the options, present the chosen direction to owners Josh and
Kristen, then troubleshoot a pre-press knockout/metallic-ink error with the client's printing vendor
before seeing the final printed cans, coasters, and taster-flight materials in person.

Speaker: **NOT Chris Do.** Multi-speaker documentary — off-screen **narrator** (voiceover framing the
episode), **Ben Burns** (Blind creative director; packaging/barcode war story), **Matthew Encina**
(Blind designer; critique + client presentation), **Jun** (Blind design intern; caption sometimes
garbles as "June"/"Jun" — the hands-on packaging designer), and **Josh & Kristen** (Hamilton Family
Brewery owners / clients). `attribution: none-to-subject` — Chris Do is silent throughout.

## Key claims

_All statements below are attributed to Blind employees or the brewery clients, NOT to Chris Do.
Dated to the publication date; retained as Blind-process/entity context only._

### Narrator (voiceover)

- [2019-07-17] Blind is "a brand strategy design consultancy based in Santa Monica, California, since
  1995" that "has used the power of design to help diverse clients reach their customers and stand out
  in the marketplace." (quote — entity fact about Blind)
- [2019-07-17] The previous episode built Hamilton's website in Webflow (via intern Jun) so owners
  Josh and Kristen could run it without learning to code; this episode moves to can packaging. (paraphrase)
- [2019-07-17] Packaging is one of this project's biggest challenges because the brewery is moving into
  retail distribution, where the can must convey their warmth and craft to shoppers who have never
  visited the tasting room. (paraphrase)

### Ben Burns (Blind creative director)

- [2019-07-17] Ben hadn't done packaging design in ~12 years, since graduating Art Center. (paraphrase)
- [2019-07-17] War story: on a past project his team put the wrong barcode on packaging that went to
  press — "almost $100,000 in packaging" printed wrong; there were millions in revenue and opportunity
  cost at stake. They lost the client but didn't go out of business, which is why he's now "hyper
  vigilant" and checks packaging "three times over." (paraphrase — Blind-process lesson on QA/vigilance)
- [2019-07-17] On the design system's flexibility: the standard identifiable blue background + hop icon
  stays constant, while a swappable colorful label communicates the ingredients/flavor inside, with
  room behind the beer name for optional illustration Josh can create himself. (paraphrase)

### Matthew Encina (Blind designer)

- [2019-07-17] The brand attributes identified with Josh and Kristen are "heritage but also fun"; the
  packaging challenge is combining craftsmanship with fun and flavor. (paraphrase)
- [2019-07-17] Alcohol is a category that "rel[ies] pretty much exclusively on their packaging to make
  the sale" — with so many options, something that stands out catches the eye first, then the shopper
  reads the label. (paraphrase — packaging/branding principle)
- [2019-07-17] They deliberately included one weak option in the client presentation: "You gotta show
  them something bad so they know what's good for them." (quote — presentation technique)
- [2019-07-17] The chosen direction switches the hierarchy, placing the beer name/info ABOVE the
  Hamilton logo, because on a retail shelf the bottom of the can is often hidden, so the flavor name
  must sit at the very top. (paraphrase — retail-shelf design reasoning)

### Jun (Blind design intern)

- [2019-07-17] Dropping the full logo and focusing on the icon removes legibility issues and frees space
  to make key elements bigger. (paraphrase — design-craft refinement)
- [2019-07-17] Reaching the final can is "a process of constant refinement" — abstracting the pattern,
  then playing with typography. (paraphrase)

### Josh & Kristen (Hamilton Family Brewery — clients)

- [2019-07-17] Their current challenge: communicating clearly on packaging "to everybody outside of our
  tasting room" now that product will sit on shelves far from their hometown crowd who already "get"
  them. (paraphrase)
- [2019-07-17] Brand goal is transparency — remove any "masks," take the customer "behind the veil,"
  treat them as family invited into the garage rather than a "profit mark." (paraphrase)
