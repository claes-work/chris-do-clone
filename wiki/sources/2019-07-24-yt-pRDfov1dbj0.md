## Summary

Episode 10 of Blind's "Building A Brand" series documents the creation of a 60-second
to 2-minute brand video for Hamilton Family Brewery's website, meant to tell the
owners' story to site visitors who can't reach the tasting room. The Blind team (led
by Matthew Encina and Ben Burns) walks through the three-stage video production process
— pre-production, production, post-production — then shoots on location at the working
brewery (interviews with Josh & Kristen, plus slow-motion beer-pour "tasty film"
b-roll), selects music via Epidemic Sound (the episode sponsor), and presents the
finished video to the delighted clients.

Speaker: multi-speaker documentary. Voices present: an **off-camera Narrator**
(voiceover), **Matthew Encina** and **Ben Burns** (Blind creative directors, on
camera), an unnamed **sound/camera crew member** (uncertain), and clients **Josh
Hamilton** and **Kristen Hamilton**. **Chris Do is NOT identifiable in this
transcript.** No line can be confidently attributed to Chris Do.

## Key claims

_All process claims below are attributed to Blind team members (Encina/Burns/Narrator),
NOT to Chris Do. They are recorded as Blind-process context, not persona data._

### Narrator (Blind series voiceover)

- [2019-07-24] Blind is described as "a brand strategy design consultancy based in
  Santa Monica, California since 1995" that uses design to help clients stand out.
  (paraphrase — recurring series intro)
- [2019-07-24] The brand video's job is to help site visitors who can't visit the
  tasting room understand who the Hamiltons are, their passion, and what to expect
  from the beer — "like a can on the shelf." (paraphrase)
- [2019-07-24] Video production breaks into three major steps: **pre-production**
  (identify the communication goal, plan locations/crew/equipment/schedule),
  **production** (capture all needed picture and sound; overshoot because you
  typically get only one shot), and **post-production** (edit to a tight story, layer
  in music to dictate feeling, final color and graphics to make it pop). (paraphrase —
  framework)

### Matthew Encina (Blind creative director)

- [2019-07-24] The goal is to capture the brewery's story, experience, and what they
  stand for in "a nice tight sixty seconds to two minute video" that lives on the site.
  (paraphrase)
- [2019-07-24] Interview strategy split by person: Kristen was directed toward the
  family/emotional perspective ("heart and home"); Josh toward the educational/product
  angle ("business and product") — "two sides of the same story." (paraphrase)
- [2019-07-24] "Music tells you how to feel" — cites his three-year-old responding
  emotionally to a movie's score (calm until ominous tones creep in). (paraphrase;
  the three-year-old anecdote is Encina's, NOT Chris Do's)
- [2019-07-24] Self-described limit: he can talk about typefaces, colors, point sizes,
  and design "all day," but with music he's "a novice" — so he and the editor must
  align on shared language to get the audio right. (paraphrase, self-critical)
- [2019-07-24] Picking the right music is even harder than picking the right color for
  a brand, because it's hard to talk about. (paraphrase)

### Ben Burns (Blind creative director)

- [2019-07-24] Positioned as the person closest to the client (Josh) and therefore the
  "super objective" gut-check before locking the edit — Matthew shows him the cut cold
  to test whether it represents the brand. (paraphrase)

### Crew member (sound/camera — attribution uncertain)

- [2019-07-24] On shooting in an industrial space: hard surfaces cause sound
  reflections and echo, so they treated the room with C-stands and sound blankets to
  dampen it and make the voice sound rich and tight. (paraphrase)
- [2019-07-24] Used two audio sources — a boom mic and a lavalier — at slightly
  different levels for safety/coverage, because "we only have one shot to do this."
  (paraphrase)
- [2019-07-24] Lighting note: a "kick," "hair," or "edge" light behind the subject
  creates separation between the silhouette and the background; shooting into the
  shadow side gives the face more shape and definition. (paraphrase)
- [2019-07-24] "Tasty film" = shooting food or beverage to look more visually
  appealing (sweating/condensation on the glass) so the viewer can almost "taste it
  through the screen." (paraphrase)
