# Work On Yourself More Than On Your Job – 2 Minute Motivation

## Summary

A short "2 Minute Motivation" montage of Chris Do coaching moments, stitched into one theme: your career growth is capped by how much you invest in *yourself*, not just how well you do your assigned job. Chris argues that showing up and doing the job keeps you employed but doesn't get you promoted — recognition follows self-development done in your own free time. Along the way he touches on prioritization (stack and sequence tasks), emotional detachment ("attachment is a route to disappointment"), and a bias toward getting results and shipping over being right or chasing the last invisible 5–10% of polish.

Speaker: single-speaker compilation of **Chris Do** (first person throughout — "I prefer the opposite," "I became somebody different so the world will have to recognize me"). Appears to be edited from multiple coaching/critique sessions (he addresses students/team and names "Ricki," a Futur team member, as a contrast case). No other speaker's words are included. Attribution to Chris is clear.

## Key claims

- [2019-07-29] Doing your job well keeps you employed, but if you never work on yourself you have no grounds to complain that the boss doesn't recognize you — "because you haven't done jack." (paraphrase; verbatim fragment below)
- [2019-07-29] "You should work on yourself way harder than you work on your job." Give your best ~8 hours a day, then what you do in your own free time determines who you become. (quote / paraphrase) — NOTE: this echoes Jim Rohn's well-known line "work harder on yourself than you do on your job," but Chris does **not** credit Rohn in this clip.
- [2019-07-29] "Don't live up to the titles you have, live up to the title that you want" — if you want to be a partner or creative director, behave now like the person in that role. (quote)
- [2019-07-29] On prioritization: list what you have to do, "stack it in your mind and prioritize," and pick the task that makes the most sense; grouping and sequencing tasks helps. (paraphrase)
- [2019-07-29] Chris contrasts himself with a colleague ("Ricki is a big teddy bear... emotionally engaged with everything he does"): Chris prefers "to have no emotions and objectively cut everything," because "attachment is a route to disappointment and unhappiness." (paraphrase + quote) — a stated personal preference for emotional detachment in work decisions.
- [2019-07-29] "Getting things done is more important than being right" — care most about getting the results, not how you got there. (quote / paraphrase)
- [2019-07-29] The professional delivers a good product within the budget and timeline using the resources they have. (paraphrase)
- [2019-07-29] Diminishing-returns caution: you can "lose all of your life" into the last 5–10% of polish "that nobody cares about" — only invest in that final stretch when it's genuinely critical / make-or-break in the moment. (paraphrase + quote)
- [2019-07-29] "I became somebody different so the world will have to recognize me as somebody who has grown — and that could be your future." Self-transformation, not tenure, drives recognition. (quote)
- [2019-07-29] "Tell me what you do in your free time and I will tell you what you become." (quote — closing line)
