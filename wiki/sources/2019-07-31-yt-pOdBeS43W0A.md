## Summary

The season-one finale of *Building A Brand*, Blind's documentary series that films a
full rebrand "from start to finish." This episode covers the final deliverable (the brand
style guide), Josh and Kristen Hamilton's all-at-once brand reveal party at Hamilton
Family Brewery (Rancho Cucamonga), and a follow-up check-in confirming the rebrand's
commercial results (cans into BevMo/retail, ~20% quarterly growth, new hires). The
narrative arc is a designer's "closed loop": help a small client rebrand, then verify it
performed in the marketplace.

Speaker: **multi-speaker documentary.** Voiceover narration (uncredited); **Ben Burns**
and **Matthew Encina** (Blind creative directors, in interview/reaction segments);
**Josh Hamilton** and **Kristen Hamilton** (the clients). **Chris Do does NOT speak.**
All quotes below are attributed to the Blind employees or clients, none to Chris Do.

## Key claims

_None are Chris-attributed. The following are Blind-employee / client statements retained
as process and entity context only._

### Ben Burns / Matthew Encina (Blind creative directors)

- [2019-07-31] A brand style guide is "our rules of how to use the logo, the brand, all
  the visual guidelines" needed to make more assets; Blind normally hands it off to a
  big client's internal design team, but here delivered it directly to the owner. (paraphrase)
- [2019-07-31] "Whenever you're handing something off to a client as a designer there's
  always a risk that the client is gonna take it and ruin it" — a recurring designer fear
  that a shipped product/website gets "warped beyond recognition" six months to a year
  later; heightened when there's no internal design team to hold the standard. (quote + paraphrase)
- [2019-07-31] The logo breakthrough came from reworking the hop shape — keeping it but
  enlarging and pointing the stem — after Josh rejected an early mark for having "no
  meaning behind it" beyond his first initial. (paraphrase)
- [2019-07-31] Josh flipping the entire identity at once (rather than a slow drip test)
  was framed as "a courageous move" — the slow-drip approach is "a good strategy…
  especially if you already have an established customer base," but Josh went all-in. (paraphrase)
- [2019-07-31] Closing designer sentiment: seeing the work perform in retail "closed this
  big loop from the very beginning of this process… getting them something that's going
  to stand out in the marketplace"; "our work here is done." (paraphrase)

### Josh & Kristen Hamilton (clients)

- [2019-07-31] Josh found The Futur through YouTube while trying to design a cleaner logo
  himself; he got "pretty far" until videos on letter kerning made him realize "I am out
  of my depth here," and reached out to the parent company Blind. (paraphrase)
- [2019-07-31] Approached the reveal as a single hard switch — stockpiled old-logo
  merchandise to burn through, then revealed everything at once ("bam, here's the new
  one") rather than fading brands in/out — to "treat the new design with reverence." (paraphrase)
- [2019-07-31] The final icon encodes three layered meanings: a house silhouette (they
  sold their house to start the brewery), a tree (the Hamilton tree, their heritage mark),
  and a hop (favorite beer ingredient) — "a hop, a house, and our Hamilton tree." (paraphrase)
- [2019-07-31] Results after retail launch: cans went from 3 to 5 BevMo locations after
  selling out fast; hired a full-time salesperson and a second brewer; projecting ~20%
  growth for the quarter. A best compliment received: the new logo says "you're not a
  little backyard brewery anymore… we're a legit company now." (paraphrase)

### Ben Burns (closing credits)

- [2019-07-31] Over a year earlier, Ben pitched Chris Do on documenting the process with
  a couple who "can't afford blind services," and "he was willing to take that gamble";
  the resources, team, and money made available for "this kind of test experiment" were
  "out of this world." First thank-you goes to Chris. (paraphrase — this is the only
  reference to Chris Do in the episode)
