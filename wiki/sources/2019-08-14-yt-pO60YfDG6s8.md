## Key claims

### Mentor voice — probable Chris Do (attribution: uncertain / not name-confirmed)

- [2019-08-14] The mission of Young Guns season 2 was to gather seven aspiring creatives from around the world, regardless of geography, to mentor them, expose their work, and unleash their talent. (paraphrase)
- [2019-08-14] He wanted a program format flexible enough to address each designer's individual needs while assessing and nurturing their creative talent. (paraphrase)
- [2019-08-14] A core reframing coaching move: ask the designer to name what feels inadequate or missing, then "how would you reframe that? what would you tell yourself so that can become a positive?" (paraphrase, coaching technique)
- [2019-08-14] One of the biggest challenges facing designers is a lack of confidence, partly because we only ever see the very best work highlighted on social media, at award shows, and on stage — "the tail end of their journey, not the beginning" — so we compare our worst to their best. "Don't compare yourself to someone else's highlight reel." (paraphrase + quote)
- [2019-08-14] Push work conceptually: "reduce your ideas to something really concise and say the most with the least." (quote, near-verbatim)
- [2019-08-14] Designers instinctively work on what they can control (layout, rendering, illustration) because progress there feels good — but "conceptual thinking has no such measure": you can spend an hour, a day, or a week and no one can discern the effort, because coming up with magical ideas is genuinely hard. Good conceptual work brings together two disparate things, finds the common ground, and presents it elegantly and true to the project's message. (paraphrase)
- [2019-08-14] On time management: you can work really hard and have little to show for it, but don't give up — keep identifying your own process, develop methodologies and shortcuts, and "you will be like the people you look up to." (paraphrase)

### Young Guns participants — NOT Chris Do (does NOT train persona)

- [2019-08-14] A participant shifted her mindset from "I have to" to "I get to" — getting excited about what she gets to learn and the clients she gets to work with. (paraphrase, participant)
- [2019-08-14] A participant realized illustration is what makes her happy and now posts full illustrations on Instagram. (paraphrase, participant)
- [2019-08-14] A participant found her sweet spot as a strategist / "translator between client and designer." (paraphrase, participant)
- [2019-08-14] Participants' outcomes: one got a full-time job at a branding agency; another decided to pursue a full-time role over freelancing to grow; another is starting a visual-communication master's. (paraphrase, participants)
- [2019-08-14] Participant advice to young designers: don't fear failure (fastest way to learn); don't let your education hold you back; female designers especially should not let low confidence and negative thoughts stop them; you don't always have to love the work, sometimes you just have to do it. (paraphrase, participants)
