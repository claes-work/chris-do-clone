# (Don't) Question Everything — How to Learn Faster

★ **L3-candidate** — Contains a coherent learning-mindset idea worth promoting into
`wiki/topics/mindset/` and `persona/beliefs.md`: *there is a time to ask questions and a
time to stop questioning and absorb*. Note the crispest articulation of that framing is
delivered by the editor/narrator (Aaron), not Chris; the persona-relevant nugget is
Chris's own "to be a great student, do what your coach says **without question**."

## Summary

A short compilation on how to learn faster. Chris Do argues that the trait that made him
a great student was not talent or success but a willingness to do **everything** his coach
told him to do **without question** — he frames the "cynical voice that questions
everything" as Steven Pressfield's "resistance." An editor/narrator (Aaron) then adds
commentary reconciling this with the instinct to "question everything": there is a time to
ask questions (when you're searching for new mentors, books, and ideas) and a time to stop
asking and let information flow in unimpeded. The Karate Kid ("do everything I say without
question" / paint the fence) and Yoda ("empty your mind") are used as illustrations.

Speaker: **two speakers.** (1) **Chris Do** — the coaching/monologue material about being
a great student, his own coaching investment, and the "resistance." (2) **Aaron** (The
Futur editor/narrator; caption renders "Erin") — the closing meta-commentary and the
"time to ask / time to learn" framing, plus the Yoda reference and the sign-off. Only
Chris-attributed material trains the persona; Aaron's narration is context. A second
unnamed voice appears briefly in the Karate Kid exchange (`attribution: uncertain` —

## Key claims

### Chris Do (trains persona)

- [2019-08-19] Over ~10 years he spent over a quarter-million dollars on coaching to
  improve every single week, doing whatever his coach told him to do. (paraphrase,
  self-reported biography)
- [2019-08-19] What made him a great student was not that he was successful but that he
  **tried** — and that he was willing to do anything his coach said **without question**.
  (paraphrase)
- [2019-08-19] The missing component people overlook in being a good student is the
  "without question" part — willingness to do what the coach says is not enough on its
  own; doing it without resisting/second-guessing is what matters. (paraphrase)
- [2019-08-19] The real obstacle to learning is "that cynical voice that is questioning
  everything" — he names it "the resistance" (Steven Pressfield framing) and says you must
  resist it: "if I say go do this, do it." (paraphrase + quote fragment)
- [2019-08-19] Uses The Karate Kid as a model: Mr. Miyagi only agrees to teach Daniel if
  he pledges to "do everything I say without question" (paint the fence, wax on / wax off);
  Daniel gets upset not understanding, but the obedience is the training. (paraphrase,
  third-party illustration)

### Aaron / narrator (The Futur editor — context, does NOT train persona)

- [2019-08-19] Chris "has a style of his own and sometimes it can be hard to accept what
  he says"; a viewer might object "isn't it good to question everything?" (paraphrase)
- [2019-08-19] Reconciliation: yes, but there's a time to ask questions and a time to
  learn. The time to ask is when you're **searching** for new information — new books,
  new mentors — because questions guide the search and give direction. Once you've found
  a good mentor or idea, stop asking questions and focus all your energy on absorbing it.
  (paraphrase — this is the clip's central framework, narrator-articulated)
- [2019-08-19] Invokes Yoda — "clear your mind of questions," "empty your mind, let
  information flow into you unimpeded by questions" — as the best way to learn.
  (paraphrase, third-party illustration)

## Notable verbatim quotes

**Chris Do:**

- "Whatever my coach told me to do, I did [without question]... that's what made me a
  great student — not because I was successful, but because I tried."
- "I don't know why you guys are like running in molasses."
- "The real problem is that cynical voice that is questioning everything — it's the
  resistance. You resist it. If I say go do this, do it."

**Aaron / narrator (context, not persona):**

- "There's a time to ask questions and a time to learn. The time to ask questions is when
  you're searching for new information — new books to read, new mentors to follow."
- "Once you find a good mentor or idea, it's time to stop asking questions and focus all
  of your energy on absorbing the new information."
- "Like Yoda said, clear your mind of questions — empty your mind, let information flow
  into you unimpeded by questions."
