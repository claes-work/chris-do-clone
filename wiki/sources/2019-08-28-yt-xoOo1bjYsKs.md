## The 3 tips (Futur team process — NOT Chris-attributed)

1. **The five-sentence rule** — if an email would need more than five sentences,
   schedule a phone call instead. A call conveys tone and emotion and yields clarity
   faster than trying to convince someone over email. The presenter claims instituting
   it reclaimed ~12 hours per week.
2. **Drop breadcrumbs (the "decision tree")** — document every decision made with the
   client so you can retrace the path when conflict/scope-change arises. Tactics: take
   copious notes on calls and email a recap asking the client to confirm; label rounds
   explicitly ("round two of three" on the slides and in the email subject line); mark
   what's "locked" vs. what's next; and send a weekly three-sentence status check (what
   you did last week / what you'll do this week / where you are in the timeline).
3. **Build an email arsenal** — template your ~10 most frequent client emails
   (onboarding, posting work for approval, follow-ups, scheduling calls), leaving gaps
   for client-specific info, so you stop retyping the same messages.
