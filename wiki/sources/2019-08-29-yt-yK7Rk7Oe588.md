# What Is The Difference Between Branding & Marketing?

★ **L3-candidate** — Contains several clean, self-contained definitions worth promoting into `wiki/topics/branding/` (and reconciling with existing brand material): Chris Do's definition of **positioning**, the Godin-sourced modern definition of **marketing**, Fabian Geralt's definition of **branding** and the "brand platform," and the **marketing-activity taxonomy** (promotion / publicity / PR / advertising / sales / marketing) recounted from Allan Dib's *The 1-Page Marketing Plan*. The through-line thesis — branding and marketing are converging "blood cousins," and labels matter far less than moving the needle on the client's business — is a distinct, quotable Chris position.

## Summary

A three-way studio conversation reacting to a Donald Miller (StoryBrand) video that argued companies shouldn't invest in branding until they hit a large revenue threshold (the video variously said $500M, $250M, $50M). Melinda Livesey moderates and plays the working designer's confusion ("I've been calling my logo branding"); Fabian Geralt defends branding as the foundational "why + look-and-feel + positioning + differentiator" that must precede marketing; Chris Do takes the most conciliatory position — branding and marketing are deeply overlapping ("blood cousins... maybe twins"), the tribalism between the camps is counterproductive, and what actually matters is whether the work improves the client's business. Along the way they define positioning, unpack a marketing-activity taxonomy, and critique the rhetorical framing of button-pushing YouTube videos (including their own).

**Speaker:** Multi-speaker roundtable. **Chris Do** (host). **Fabian Geralt** — brand strategist / brand-identity designer, has a podcast interviewing entrepreneurs, positions branding as foundational. **Melinda Livesey** — brand-identity designer, recurring Futur guest, moderates and asks the framing questions. Sarah (audio, off-camera) is referenced. **Donald Miller** (StoryBrand) and **Seth Godin** and **Marty Neumeier** are cited as third parties, not present. Claims below are grouped by speaker; only Chris-attributed material trains the persona.

## Key claims

### Chris Do

- [2019-08-29] These provocative "X over Y" videos are engineered to push buttons and incite emotion; nobody shares "I love branding and marketing equally." Chris notes he makes such videos too, and that Miller is "really smart about positioning" — declaring "I'm for this and not for that" is itself a positioning move that rallies the marketers. (paraphrase)
- [2019-08-29] Everyone should be aware of their own bias: Fabian is a branding guy so of course he defends branding; Miller is a marketing guy teaching people to market products, so of course he champions marketing. Chris frames his own aim as looking "as robotically as I can" at what's actually being said. (paraphrase)
- [2019-08-29] **Positioning** = "the art or the strategy of occupying a space, a position in a person's mind." (quote)
- [2019-08-29] Branding and marketing are, depending on your definition, "almost the same thing... we're like blood cousins, we might even be twins." As both disciplines evolve, the overlap is growing; "if you just take the word branding and switch it out for... marketing, you'll be okay." (paraphrase + quote)
- [2019-08-29] Cites Seth Godin (from The Futur's conversation with him): the old idea of marketing was "just advertising — repetition of message over all media and controlling the dialogue," which no longer works; Godin's new definition is "marketing is the generous act of helping other people achieve their goals." Chris notes you could swap in "branding" or "sales" for "marketing" in that sentence — a healthier shared definition of a company's role toward its customers. (paraphrase + quote)
- [2019-08-29] The best brand strategists are the ones who keep up with, understand, and are genuinely interested in marketing and selling — "sparks fly in the intersection of marketing and branding." (paraphrase + quote)
- [2019-08-29] Traditional marketing doesn't work anymore because you can't say something that isn't backed up by actual experience; buyers trust 300 Amazon reviews over any ad, which is why influencer marketing and integrated branding work — trusted people vet what's good for us. Marketing today should include product design, UX, and journey mapping top-to-bottom, because if one part of the story is out of alignment the whole thing is. (paraphrase)
- [2019-08-29] Cites an unnamed multi-billion-dollar-company CEO: "Marketing is the most important thing that our company does — that's why we don't trust the marketing people to do it." (quote, third-party)
- [2019-08-29] Recounts the **marketing-activity taxonomy** from Allan Dib's *The 1-Page Marketing Plan* (circus analogy): painting a sign on the elephant = **promotion**; marching it through town trampling flowers = **publicity**; smoothing it over with the upset mayor = **public relations**; handing out flyers = **advertising**; people asking questions at the booth = **sales**; designing the whole thing = **marketing**. Marketing is the high-level strategic planning; the rest are tactics/execution. (paraphrase, third-party framework)
- [2019-08-29] Where the branding person fits into that story: upstream of all of it — sitting with the circus owner to define who they are, what audiences want, the overlap between the two, then crafting the experience, the name, the look-and-feel (e.g. deciding to drop animals and become a Cirque-du-Soleil-style human-performance show). "Let's ask first why... this is what's gonna move the needle." (paraphrase)
- [2019-08-29] Cites Marty Neumeier's *The Brand Flip* on the skills of a great brand strategist: think visually, articulate with words, think at the 30,000-foot high level, and co-create — a very small group (~"five people") can truly do all of it. (paraphrase, third-party)
- [2019-08-29] Applying labels and getting into tribalism is dangerous: marketing people look down on branding people and vice versa. The real end goal is to improve the client's business and impact their bottom line — "all the other stuff to me is tactics... what you call it, I don't really care." A designer can call themselves whatever they want; "the proof is in the pudding — what is it that you really do?" Look at function first, then design a label around it. (paraphrase + quote)
- [2019-08-29] What design schools label "branding" is usually just identity/design systems, not branding — because it ignores the name, positioning, messaging, copywriting, and voice. Real brand strategy is genuinely hard and demands mastery of multiple disciplines. (paraphrase)
- [2019-08-29] Suspects marketing curricula and much branding education are "vastly outdated... very textbook, very analytical," with little creativity. In his own education, branding and marketing were separate departments that looked down on each other; he suspects at most universities branding sits under the marketing umbrella. (paraphrase)
- [2019-08-29] Positioning check: if you polled the audience, most self-described "branding people" actually just do logo design and would drop off; few do positioning strategically; whereas a high percentage of marketing people do positioning. (paraphrase)
- [2019-08-29] Miller's influence is being over-amplified — "for every one Donald Miller talking about marketing, there's probably 35 branding people talking about branding" at design conferences. But the core objection stands: framing it as "don't brand until you're $50M/$500M" is incorrect and taken out of context. (paraphrase + quote)
- [2019-08-29] Closing thesis: our instinct is to go tribal — "this is us, this is them, and it's war" — but stepping back, "we're all the same family, it's cousins," and everyone is better off having broader, open-minded cross-discipline discussions in service of improving and impacting the client's business. (paraphrase + quote)

### Fabian Geralt

- [2019-08-29] Miller's video "made my head explode." Miller is a good guy (StoryBrand is his book and Fabian liked it), but the claim that a company needs to be $50M before it starts branding "makes absolutely no sense" — it's "the cart before the horse." You cannot market something you haven't branded yet: "you have to create the word before you spread the gospel." (paraphrase + quote)
- [2019-08-29] Rebuts Miller's "don't ask why, just send email-email-email": the email has to come "from a space of why." You must first build a brand platform to derive why the company exists — that's fundamental. And any $50M company already spent heavily on branding to get there in the first place. (paraphrase)
- [2019-08-29] **Definition of branding:** it "establishes the why behind the brand, establishes a look and feel, establishes a positioning and a differentiator in the marketplace — all of that combined is the beginning of branding." (quote)
- [2019-08-29] The deliverable is a **brand platform** ("as the industry calls it") — like a business plan but a brand plan: what do we stand for, why do we exist, how do we differentiate, how do we position to stand out. The big why is a "**because statement**" ("this is why we exist"). It also requires understanding customers before launch (who they are, what channels, reading their social comments), then deriving the verbal identity — a toolbox of the right words, tone, and how the brand wants to be seen (friend? leader?). The logo is one small component; "the rest they feel." (paraphrase + quote)
- [2019-08-29] On Miller's "30/70 rule" for a budget: not a bad rule — of a $100k budget put ~30% into branding to establish the foundation and more into marketing/advertising to spread the word, because if people don't hear about it they won't buy and you're out of business. (paraphrase)
- [2019-08-29] Brand values must "come from within" — from the entrepreneur's genuine belief — but in the end it's still all about the customer; the overlap between the founder's values and the customer's values is where the differentiator lives. He interviews entrepreneurs every two weeks and hears the same story: a strong against-the-grain belief that attracts like-minded people. When the "soul" comes from the founder, they can actually lead and "talk the talk." (paraphrase)
- [2019-08-29] Calling yourself a "marketing firm" to attract clients who are searching for "marketing" would backfire — it implies services (social media, content marketing, advertising) you may not actually deliver. Every label is a "sticker"; the real challenge is describing your practice by how you help clients. For his own practice the resonant framing is "**clarity and focus**" — clients don't come for a logo (that's an end result), they come for clarity and focus. (paraphrase + quote)
- [2019-08-29] "You're selling the problem, you're not selling a service — I learned it from Donald Miller." (quote) `attribution: uncertain` (speaker inferred from context; likely Fabian describing his own practice)

### Melinda Livesey (moderator)

- [2019-08-29] Frames the session; admits she has an unarticulated opinion and wants to hear both perspectives. As a brand-identity designer she sees a lot of overlap between branding and marketing. (paraphrase)
- [2019-08-29] Public-service-announcement takeaway for the audience: "if you make a logo, if you make a mark, you're an identity designer / logo maker — you're not a branding person yet. It's one facet, one very small facet." (paraphrase, echoing the group consensus)
- [2019-08-29] Poses the practical tension: entrepreneurs listening to people like Donald Miller are thinking "marketing, marketing, marketing — that's what I need," while designers all call their work "branding" — so would it behoove some designers to call themselves a marketing firm to connect with those clients? (paraphrase)
