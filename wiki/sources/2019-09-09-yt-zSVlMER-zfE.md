# The Difference Between Good & Great Companies

★ **L3-candidate** — This is a named, self-contained framework (Chris Do's "audience > customers" thesis and its three operating principles: be consistent, give generously, serve others). Worth promoting into `wiki/topics/content-strategy/` and `persona/beliefs.md` (dated), not just held at L2.

## Summary

Chris Do delivers a short talk (filmed around a Geneva/Milan "Digital Design Days" speaking trip) on what separates great companies from good ones: **all companies have customers, but a few companies have an audience.** A customer transacts once and moves on; an audience is a set of true fans who enroll in your tribe and evangelize your work. Because customers must be *paid* for their attention (that's what advertising is) while an audience *gives* time and attention freely, an audience is far more valuable. He distills the path to building one into three ideas he credits for The Futur's success: be consistent, give generously, and serve others. He closes on Seth Godin's "smallest viable audience" and the inversion it implies — find a market and design a solution for their problem, rather than designing a solution and hunting for a market.

Speaker: single-speaker monologue by **Chris Do** (first person — "two years ago when I was charging fifty dollars," "our success," references to "the Futur Pro group"). No co-host or guest speaks. Filippo (Digital Design Days host), Terry Crews, Gary Vaynerchuk, Austin Kleon, Bianca Challot(?), and Seth Godin are named as third-party references, not present. Note: one anecdote about going "from fifty dollars" to "two hundred thousand" twelve months later appears to be a testimonial from an audience member Chris is relaying, not Chris's own numbers — attribution uncertain (see below).

## Key claims

### Chris Do

- [2019-09-09] The difference between good and great companies: "all companies have customers, a few companies have an audience." An audience is more valuable than a customer base. (paraphrase / near-quote)
- [2019-09-09] A customer buys a product or service, a transaction and exchange of value happens, and then they move on. An audience is more of a true fan — they enroll in the community, join your tribe, and extol the virtues of your products and services. (paraphrase)
- [2019-09-09] Why an audience beats customers: with customers "you have to pay them to pay attention — that's what advertising is," whereas an audience happily gives you their time and attention. (paraphrase, with quote fragment)
- [2019-09-09] Three ideas that have been key to The Futur's success: **be consistent, give generously, serve others.** (paraphrase — the talk's spine)
- [2019-09-09] Be consistent = showing up every single day and doing the thing you think matters without worrying too much about results; the results follow if you put in the work. (paraphrase)
- [2019-09-09] "Action beats intent" — illustrated with Terry Crews, who said he hates working out; his only commitment is to put on workout clothes and drive to the gym, then do one small set. Many sets later, that compounds into results. (paraphrase, third-party anecdote)
- [2019-09-09] Cites Gary Vaynerchuk's "ideas are crap, execution is the game" — take a direction and adjust as you go. (paraphrase, third-party attribution)
- [2019-09-09] Give generously = hold nothing back; 100% transparency, be vulnerable, talk about your numbers, share everything someone else needs to know to be helped. People are hungry to find out more, relate, connect, and be part of your tribe. (paraphrase)
- [2019-09-09] "Give them a tour of the factory," go behind the scenes, and "become a documentarian of your own work" (credited to Austin Kleon's book — i.e. *Show Your Work!*). This can take any form — speaking, writing, blogging, tweeting, video — so find the medium most natural to you. (paraphrase, with quote fragments; book title inferred)
- [2019-09-09] Share the research, reference, drawings, plans, sketches — all the trials and tribulations of reaching a solution — because it lets people peek into your world and how you do what you do. (paraphrase)
- [2019-09-09] Serve others = "stop selling and start celebrating"; be a champion for the people you serve and move away from a selfish advertising/media mindset toward serving others. (paraphrase, with quote fragment)
- [2019-09-09] Relays an anecdote: hired by LinkedIn to interview a woman (name unclear in captions, "Bianca Challot"?), he asked what still gets her out of bed after making her money; she answered that her goal is to get one thank-you message a day in her inbox/DMs. Chris frames this as a wonderful purpose and a marker of serving others. (paraphrase, third-party anecdote; name attribution uncertain)
- [2019-09-09] Building your first true fans: start from Seth Godin's "smallest viable audience" (SVA) — can you pick them out in a crowd, describe them, know their challenges? Then deliver value to them. (paraphrase, third-party attribution)
- [2019-09-09] This inverts the usual approach: instead of designing a solution and then finding a market for it, look for a market, find their problem, and design a solution for them. Align what you do through that lens and you'll do well. (paraphrase)
- [2019-09-09] On The Futur Pro group: it's "the modern-day version of a meetup" — you meet up online, and the relationships formed there become lifelong relationships outside in the real world; members host their own meetups, do projects together, and hire one another. (paraphrase)
