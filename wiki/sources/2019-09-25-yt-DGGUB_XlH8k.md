## Summary

First episode of The Futur's "Design From Scratch" miniseries, documenting the ground-up rebuild
of thefutur.com (sponsored by Webflow — they will nuke the old WordPress site and rebuild in
Webflow). The episode is a behind-the-scenes look at a frustrating kickoff meeting where the team
"can't agree on a direction." Its central theme: it is *hard to design for yourself* because you're
emotionally attached, versus the relative objectivity you have on a client's project. The team names
the core tension — **Ben** (make the company money / conversions) vs. **Chris** (tell the story of
*why* we do what we do) — and offers Chris's own framing, "passionate detachment," as the way to stay
objective about work you had a hand in making.

Speaker: **multi-speaker documentary.** Narrator = a Futur team member (likely **Greg Gunn**,
`attribution: uncertain`), the self-described "Sherpa" and person responsible for the site launch.
Talking-head interviews: **Matthew** (Matthew Encina, head of content, "objective consultant"),
**Ben** (Ben Burns, marketing lead, most web-savvy), **Jamie/"Jeanne van Ward"** (returning ex-staff
freelancer, designing the site — name garbled in captions, `attribution: uncertain`), and **"Saying"**
(in-house art director developing the site in Webflow for the first time — name garbled,
`attribution: uncertain`). **Chris Do** speaks only two short direct lines and is otherwise narrated
about in the third person. Simon Sinek's "people don't buy *what* you do, they buy *why*" is spoken by
the narrator, not Chris.

## Key claims

### Chris Do (direct speech — trains persona)

- [2019-09-25] The problem with the old site isn't that it's broken — it's that even he, the owner,
  can't find things on it: "when I, the owner, tried to look for things that I can't find it myself,
  I'm about to jump out the window." (quote) He holds strong opinions about what the new site should be.
- [2019-09-25] Once the project is handed to the executing team (Greg, Jamie, and "Saying"), he wants
  them to "drive in and take it forward" — and admits that if they don't, he will "personally be very
  frustrated." (paraphrase — reveals his hand-off / delegation posture and low tolerance for stalling)

### Chris Do (reported / quoted by narrator — moderate attribution)

- [2019-09-25] "Passionate detachment" — the narrator quotes "mr. Doe himself": staying objective
  about your own work comes "through deliberate practice of passionate detachment," i.e. learning to
  **separate the create mode from the critique mode** (likened to an athlete watching game tape after
  the game). Reported second-hand, so `attribution: reported quote`, but the concept is Chris's.
- [2019-09-25] "Can you read the label when you're inside the bottle?" — offered as a Chris-style
  framing of the self-design problem (attribution ambiguous between Chris and narrator).

### Futur team / narrator (context only — does NOT train persona)

- [2019-09-25] The Futur is "a group of creative professionals on a mission to teach anyone willing to
  listen the value of design" — bridging the gap between design and business ("how to charge more money
  to design logos, but it's bigger than that"); goal to "teach 1 billion people." (narrator, paraphrase)
- [2019-09-25] The website is "the cornerstone of our business and the only reason we all stay gainfully
  employed" — a rebuild done right should raise conversions; done wrong, the business drops off.
  (narrator, paraphrase)
- [2019-09-25] Recurring internal tension: **Ben** is "trying to make the company money" while **Chris**
  is "trying to tell the story of why we're doing what we're doing" — the two goals "have always
  collided" across the company. Both matter: no money = no Futur; but without the *why*, buying decisions
  become about price. "People don't buy what you do, they buy why you do it." (narrator, paraphrase; Sinek)
- [2019-09-25] Why designing for yourself is hard: on a client project you're "somewhat divorced" from it;
  clients hire outside experts precisely because they couldn't get results on their own. For your own site
  — "a representation of us" — there's heavy emotional attachment, which kills objectivity. (narrator/team,
  paraphrase)
- [2019-09-25] Matthew's role as "objective consultant": because he's detached he can ask questions the
  people "so close to the details" have overlooked, and check "is this accomplishing what our users want?"
  (Matthew, paraphrase)
- [2019-09-25] "Saying" (art director) is building the site in Webflow for the first time and owns the
  outcome: "it's my first time going in and if it doesn't turn out well, it's on me." (paraphrase — Futur
  process/pressure context)
