# Website Ready to Launch? – Design From Scratch Ep. 3

> ⚠️ **ATTRIBUTION — does NOT train the persona.** This is a Futur *documentary-series*
> episode ("Design From Scratch," final of 3), narrated and executed by **Futur team
> members** — not a Chris Do teaching video. The lead narrator is **Greg Gunn**; other
> team members (**Sayed/"sow"**, **Craig**, **Ben**, designer **Jaime**) build and

> `attribution: uncertain`. Only material clearly spoken by Chris in the review meetings
> is tagged to him, and even that is held as Futur context, not persona training data.

## Summary

Documentary episode following the Futur team as they develop and launch the redesigned
thefutur.com in Webflow. The team (Greg + Sayed) spend ~5 weeks designing and ~9 weeks
developing 36 pages, then show the near-finished build to Chris Do for review. Chris is
underwhelmed: the build doesn't match the approved "style scape," and they only get
through the first two pages before he stops the review, questioning the return on the
man-hours and money spent. The team diagnoses their own mistake — they kept the client
(Chris) in the dark, over-focused on shipping on schedule at the expense of matching the
design vision — rework the two reviewed pages, and get Chris to sign off on the MVP
site. Webflow is the episode sponsor.

Speaker: **multi-speaker documentary, team-led.** Narration and most on-camera talking is
by **Greg Gunn** (self-identified: "my name is Greg Gunn") and teammate **Sayed** ("sow").
**Chris Do** speaks only in the client-review segments. No single-speaker Chris monologue.
Attribution of individual lines is uncertain because captions are unlabeled.

## Key claims

### Chris Do — as reviewing client (attribution: probable; Futur context, not persona training)

- [2019-10-09] After four months of team work, the built site does not match the approved
  style scape: "it doesn't quite look like the style scape to me." (quote fragment, probable Chris)
- [2019-10-09] Weighing the man-hours and money spent against the design phase, he is "not
  seeing a great return on investment of time or resources" and finds it "a little painful."
  He stops the review after only the first two pages. (paraphrase, probable Chris)
- [2019-10-09] In the second review he accepts the reworked site as a step forward: the
  design assets "are there," it "feels sophisticated enough and reflects the brand," is
  "light [and] open," and reflects Jaime's original style scapes — "a big improvement from
  what I saw previously." (paraphrase, probable Chris; MVP sign-off)

### Futur team — Greg Gunn / Sayed narration (attribution: team, NOT Chris — do not feed persona)

- [2019-10-09] Presentation tip (narrated by Greg Gunn to the audience): resist the urge to
  share work with a client without context; if you want feedback, tell the person what you
  want feedback *about* and give them structure for how to respond. (paraphrase, Greg Gunn — NOT Chris)
- [2019-10-09] The team's "fatal flaw" was keeping the client (Chris) in the dark and
  guessing at what he wanted instead of checking in. (paraphrase, team)
- [2019-10-09] Focusing narrowly on one task (shipping the build on time) made them lose
  objectivity on whether it matched the design vision — "very hard to do something like
  this for yourself." (paraphrase, team)
- [2019-10-09] "Done is better than perfect" — but they note they "weren't even aiming for
  perfect"; the real tension was finishing the website vs. finishing the video series.
  (paraphrase, team; slogan attribution unclear in captions)
- [2019-10-09] Build stats: ~5 weeks designing, ~9 weeks developing; 36 pages, 47 blog
  posts, translations, 14 [product/DLC] pages, 9 database collections in Webflow. (paraphrase, team)
- [2019-10-09] Reason to document the process: to learn how to do it better next time and
  share it so viewers in a similar position can save time/headache. (paraphrase, team)
- [2019-10-09] The website is "the most important part of our business"; the site exists both
  to communicate the mission (Chris's priority) and to rank/make money (Ben's priority) —
  a tension flagged since episode 1. (paraphrase, team)

## Notable verbatim quotes

**Chris Do (probable, unlabeled captions):**
- "It doesn't quite look like the style scape to me."
- "We spent a lot of time and if we measure the man-hours and the money against [the]
  design phase ... I'm not seeing a great return on investment of time or resources and I
  can't figure out why. It's a little painful for me right now."

**Greg Gunn (narrator, NOT Chris):**
- "Resist the urge to share something with a client without any context, and if you're
  looking for feedback let the person know what you are looking for feedback about and give
  them some structure for how to respond."
- "Done is better than perfect ... but we weren't even aiming for perfect."
- "It's very hard to do something like this for yourself."
