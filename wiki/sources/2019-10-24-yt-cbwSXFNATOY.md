# Why You Don't Need A Mentor—Find Heroes

★ **L3-candidate** — Chris Do's "find heroes, not mentors" reframe is a named, self-contained idea (heroes scale, mentors don't; use their voice in your head as a compass; "hijack" them without permission; you don't need a mentor when you can have many heroes). ## Summary

A montage-style motivational video from The Futur arguing that you don't need a formally-agreed mentor to grow. The core reframe (Chris Do's): mentors don't scale — for every successful person there are ~2,000 people asking them to mentor — so instead find **heroes** who don't even need to know you exist, and use their voice in your head as a compass ("what would X do?"). Heroes are free, unlimited, and everywhere: books, biographies, TED talks, podcasts, interviews, the person next to you in the audience. The closing line ties it together: if a mentor agrees to coach you, great, but don't let the lack of one be your excuse — "you don't need a mentor when you can have many heroes."
