# The Most Valuable Thing You Can Do For Your Client

## Summary

A short conversational clip (excerpted from a longer interview) arguing that the single
most valuable thing you can give a client early in a relationship is simply to
**understand them** — to go in with no agenda, slow down, and make the person feel heard.
The teaching: people stay in a defensive mindset (holding onto their agenda) until they
feel the person in front of them genuinely sees, cares about, and understands them; only
then does "the body relax." Rushing a client — driven by anxiety to prove you "get it"
quickly and are the expert — signals the opposite and is described as both self-harming
and a waste of time. The through-line resolves on the title point: understanding them
right now is the most valuable thing you can offer; other value comes later.

## Key claims

### Speaker A (primary teacher — identity uncertain, likely guest)

- [2019-10-29] The one goal in a first conversation is to totally understand the other
  person's world — coming in "with an agenda at all" defeats it. (paraphrase)
- [2019-10-29] The fastest way to understand someone is counterintuitive: completely slow
  down in the moment and abandon all agenda. (paraphrase)
- [2019-10-29] People have to feel heard and understood before they'll drop out of a
  defensive mindset — the state where they're "holding on to" their agenda. (paraphrase)
- [2019-10-29] For that defensiveness to release, "the body actually has to relax," which
  requires feeling that the person in front of you sees you, cares about you, and
  understands what you're going through. (paraphrase)
- [2019-10-29] This applies not only to working with a person but to clients too: can you
  appreciate the client's dilemma and go in slowly to understand it — e.g. when they're
  "waffling on spending more," what is the dilemma for them? (paraphrase)
- [2019-10-29] To strengthen client relationships you must slow down and recognize this is
  a valuable relationship in which the client holds real power and leverage; trying to rush
  through it is "probably hurting you and actually a waste of time." (paraphrase)
- [2019-10-29] The way to slow down is to anchor your mind in the idea that the most
  valuable thing you have for them right now is just understanding them; other valuable
  things come later. (paraphrase) — this is the title thesis.

### Speaker B (interjection — plausibly Chris Do, uncertain)
