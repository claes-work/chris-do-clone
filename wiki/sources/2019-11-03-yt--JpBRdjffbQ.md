# 1 Powerful Marketing Tip For Broke Artists

★ **L3-candidate** — This is a self-contained parable ("the horse painter" story) that vividly illustrates a core Chris Do pricing principle: value and price are set by *context and audience*, not by the work alone. Worth promoting into `wiki/topics/pricing/` and `persona/beliefs.md` (dated) as a canonical illustrative story.

## Summary

Chris Do tells a single story to make one point: a broke artist can command far higher prices by changing *where* and *for whom* the work is seen, not by changing the work itself. An illustrator dropping out of school (couldn't pay tuition) painted stunning animals. Chris had her bring her easel and a half-finished horse painting to the Paddock Riding Club — the most exclusive riding club in Los Angeles — and finish the horse on-site, in front of wealthy horse owners. As predicted, a passerby asked for a commission; when the artist was about to charge $100, Chris told her to charge $1,000 per sitting / $2,000 for two. She landed a two-sitting commission and, by day's end, four commissions worth close to $10,000. He caps it with the Wayne Dyer line, "if you change the way you look at things, the things you look at will change."

Speaker: single-speaker monologue by **Chris Do** (first person — "an illustrator came to me in class," "I said to her," "I told her"). No other speaker; the artist's lines are quoted by Chris within the story.

## Key claims

### Chris Do

- [2019-11-03] The illustrator was dropping out of school because she couldn't pay tuition; her animal paintings were, in Chris's words, exquisite — technical skill was not the problem. (paraphrase)
- [2019-11-03] The intervention was purely about *context*: he had her paint the same half-finished horse in a new environment — the Paddock Riding Club, "the most exclusive riding club in Los Angeles" — positioned near where members bring their horses out. (paraphrase)
- [2019-11-03] Chris predicted the exact outcome in advance ("watch what's going to happen"): about an hour later a woman with two children walked past, stopped, and asked if she painted it and whether she does commissions. (paraphrase)
- [2019-11-03] The artist's instinct was to charge $100 for a commission; Chris coached her to charge $1,000 for one sitting and $2,000 for two — "don't even take the paints out for $100." (paraphrase / partial quote)
- [2019-11-03] Result: the woman commissioned a two-sitting painting, and by the end of the day the artist had four commissions worth close to ten thousand dollars. (paraphrase)
- [2019-11-03] Underlying principle (stated via Wayne Dyer): "if you change the way you look at things, things you look at will change" — reframing perception/positioning changes what buyers and prices you attract. (quote, attributed to Dyer by Chris; used as Chris's own closing thesis)

> Note: The video title frames this as a "marketing tip," but the operative lesson is about **pricing through positioning/context** — place your work in front of the right audience in the right setting and the price it commands changes dramatically, with no change to the work itself.
