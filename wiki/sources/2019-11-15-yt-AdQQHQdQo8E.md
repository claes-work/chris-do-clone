## Summary

A guest talk (published on The Futur channel) arguing that empathy is a *hard* skill,
not a soft one — the deliberate practice of understanding what another person knows,
believes, and where they came from, so you can consciously choose how to connect with
them. The speaker applies empathy across four business contexts: marketing (help others
get what they want), spreading ideas / creating change (reframe your idea so others can
welcome it, and meet people where they are), negotiation (empathy as deep listening to
find the other side's core value/priority), and self-reflection (using empathy on
ourselves to counter confirmation bias and our own wiring). Peter Singer's drowning-child
"paradox" (proximity + gratitude) is used to explain why distant persuasion is harder.

## Key claims

### "Bob B..." — guest (context, attribution: uncertain)

_Guest-attributed; does NOT train the persona._

- [2019-11-15] Empathy is a hard skill, not a soft one — humans are wired to be
  "me me me," so walking in someone else's shoes takes deliberate work. (paraphrase)
- [2019-11-15] To understand why someone acts as they do, start with what they know,
  what they believe, and where they came from; then you can make conscious decisions
  about how to act and react to connect with them. (paraphrase)
- [2019-11-15] Cites Zig Ziglar (garbled in captions as "ding zinger"): "the best way
  to get what you want in life is to help others get what they want" — and empathy is
  how you figure out what those others want. (paraphrase of a Ziglar quote, via guest)
- [2019-11-15] It is far harder to get someone to change their story (tied to their
  beliefs and wiring) than for you to shift your own narrative to converge with them
  and meet them where they are. (paraphrase)
- [2019-11-15] Invokes Peter Singer's drowning-child thought experiment: people will
  wade in to save a child in front of them but won't PayPal $20 to save a distant
  child — the difference is proximity. (paraphrase, third-party thought experiment)
- [2019-11-15] Marketing takeaways from Singer's paradox: "close and now" (immediate,
  in-front-of-you action earns favor; distance kills the impulse) and "gratitude" (the
  promise of honors/accolades drives generosity and is linked to proximity). Reaching
  remote people requires a deeper application of empathy to overcome proximity.
  (paraphrase)
- [2019-11-15] The tool of empathy in negotiation is deep listening — hearing the thing
  underneath, the priority/value that is "the last stand" in the other person's mind, so
  you can move the discussion to where your values intersect with theirs. (paraphrase)
- [2019-11-15] Empathy as reflection: the easier an idea is to comprehend, the more
  likely we are to believe it's true (confirmation bias); we may not see the world as it
  really is, and empathy turned on ourselves opens us up to what's really going on.
  (paraphrase)
