# My Struggle To Find Self Acceptance

## Summary

A short, confessional monologue in which Chris Do describes how, growing up, all he ever wanted was to be *accepted* — not loved or respected, just accepted — and to remain invisible so he wouldn't be noticed and picked on at school. He says that constant repressing and hiding cost him his voice and his sense of who he was, because he kept trying to be someone he wasn't. The turning point, he says, was accepting that he just has to be himself — leaning into his own quirkiness, crankiness, and sardonic humor — and becoming okay with the haters that result. He closes by inviting viewers to "come into the light," let their full self shine, and stop showing only one part of their personality.
