## Summary

A Futur team video (hosted/narrated by Ricky) on whether you grow more by *doing* or by *teaching*. Two team members open with their experience (teaching a workshop revealed they knew more than they thought), then Chris Do delivers the main teaching: you gain clarity through articulation, so explaining your process to someone else surfaces knowledge you weren't aware you had. He argues the best coaches are often people who *never* reached flow-state mastery — because they had to consciously deconstruct the mechanics — and closes with his own ~2000 burnout story: five years into running his studio he felt eclipsed by a young hotshot, spiralled into self-doubt, and only regained his confidence by teaching during a three-month sabbatical, "never doubting himself again." The through-line: teaching is how you discover — and rebuild belief in — what you already know.

## Key claims

### Chris Do

- [2020-01-27] When you teach, you learn how you think and "gain clarity through articulation" — working alone you make a thousand decisions a minute but never have to explain them, so you're not even aware of your own process. (paraphrase)
- [2020-01-27] The best coaches are often people who *never* reached elite flow-state mastery: a natural like Michael Jordan can't explain how he does it ("you take this ball and you put it in there"), whereas someone who had to consciously sit and analyze the mechanics can actually teach them. (paraphrase)
- [2020-01-27] Teaching forces you to articulate the decisions you made and why; students challenge things "everybody must know," and it turns out very few people actually know them — which reveals the value of what you know. (paraphrase)
- [2020-01-27] Origin story: about five years into the business (~2000, "20 years ago"), Chris was burnt out — five years straight, no vacations/holidays, all work. He brought in a talented "young hotshot" who made him feel insecure and doubt himself, thinking "the lifespan of a graphic designer is about five years" because he couldn't keep up with new styles, techniques, and tools. He went into a downward spiral, ready to "disappear into oblivion" and let young people take over. (paraphrase, self-critical, biographical)
- [2020-01-27] At that same moment he was invited (after giving a talk) to teach at Art Center and Otis College of Design; he took a three-month sabbatical to do it, arriving full of fear ("what do I have to offer these people?"). In the process of teaching he "learned so much about myself," regained the confidence he'd lost, and "never doubted myself again." (paraphrase, biographical)
- [2020-01-27] This is why the saying "my students have taught me more than I've taught them" — seemingly a humble line — is literally true: teaching is where the teacher learns. (paraphrase)

### Ricky (Futur host) — reaction overlay

- [2020-01-27] Forcing yourself to teach makes you realize "you're not an idiot" and that what you have to say is genuinely helpful — reframing how he feels about doing things he's not an expert in. (paraphrase; host reaction, not persona training data)

### Unidentified team members — `attribution: uncertain`

- [2020-01-27] You grow more by teaching/mentoring than by just doing, because repetitive doing eventually plateaus; challenging yourself with something new drives more growth. (paraphrase, attribution uncertain — NOT trained into persona)
- [2020-01-27] Running a workshop they were both afraid of doing revealed "we actually know what we're talking about... we know more than we think we know," because you have to teach it in a way others will understand. (paraphrase, attribution uncertain — NOT trained into persona)

## Notable verbatim quotes

All quotes below are **Chris Do**:

- "When you teach you learn about how you think, and you gain clarity through articulation."
- "When you work on your own you're making a thousand decisions a minute... but you never have to explain it to anybody, and that's why you can work so fast. The problem is you're not even aware of your own process."
- "It's like asking Michael Jordan how can I be a better basketball player — he's like, you take this ball and you put it in there and you're done, because he's never had to think about it."
- "To coach it, to train people, it's much better that you get the person who never quite made it to that level, because they've had to really sit down and think what is it that's happening here."
- "Things that you thought everybody must know, turns out very few people actually know."
- "I guess the lifespan of a graphic designer is about five years... I started to doubt myself, and I went into a kind of a downward spiral."
- "In the process of teaching I learned so much about myself. I regained that confidence that I'd lost, and I never doubted myself again."

**Ricky (host):**

- "When you force yourself to teach you'll realize you're not an idiot, and what you have to say will actually be helpful."
