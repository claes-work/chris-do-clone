## Summary

Host Jonah reframes the expression "fake it till you make it" for beginners (designers
or any discipline) trying to establish credibility they don't yet have. His core move:
strip the negative baggage of the word "fake" (which implies fraud/lying/deception) and
replace the phrase with **"believe it till you achieve it."** He argues everyone starts
as an amateur — even after professional training — because the years of experience aren't
there yet, so to start at all you must first believe the thing is possible. He supports
this with a mind-body claim (your body won't attempt what your mind hasn't committed to)
and two "someone proved it was possible" examples (Rodney Mullen's skate trick, Roger
Bannister's four-minute mile). He explicitly clarifies that "fake it" is meant as an
**internal belief system / "act as if" reminder**, not a license to lie or make claims
you can't deliver on — and notes the mantra can help people with low self-esteem. The
video ends with Greg Gunn plugging The Futur podcast.

Speaker: **Jonah** (single-host monologue), plus a closing podcast-plug segment by
**Greg Gunn**. **Not Chris Do.**

## Key claims

### Jonah (host — NOT Chris Do; do not train persona)

- [2020-02-11] When you're a beginner trying to break into a business with no credibility
  or credentials, you "kind of have to fake it till you make it" — but this is explicitly
  distinguished from lying. (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-11] Lying (e.g. falsely claiming "I designed that") sometimes works just long
  enough to leverage into a career, but more often people get caught and do "incredible
  career damage." (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-11] The word "fake" carries a strong negative connotation implying fraud,
  lying, and deception; the better framing is a mindset. (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-11] A cleaner substitute phrase without the baggage is **"believe it till you
  achieve it"** — "that's really the heart of it." (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-11] Everyone starts as an amateur — even with professional training you're an
  "amateur mechanic" or "amateur doctor" because you lack years of experience — and to
  start you must believe it's possible. (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-11] Mind-body claim: your body won't attempt something until your mind commits
  to it; believing you can clear the gap gives you "a shot," though not a guarantee.
  (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-11] Rodney Mullen (TED talk) worked for weeks falling and mastering one trick;
  within days/weeks of posting the video, others were doing the "impossible" trick simply
  because it had been demonstrated possible. (paraphrase, third-party anecdote — name
  garbled slightly in captions)
- [2020-02-11] Roger Bannister broke the four-minute mile that everyone said was
  impossible; afterward a number of others broke the barrier too. (paraphrase,
  third-party anecdote — captions garble him as "Roger barrister")
- [2020-02-11] "Fake it till you make it" taken too literally is wrong; it does not mean
  to lie or make claims you can't deliver on. It's an **internal belief system and a
  reminder to "act as if."** (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-11] Most people suffer from low self-esteem at some point, and this mantra can
  be helpful for them. (paraphrase)
