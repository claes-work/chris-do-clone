**Beliefs read from the book (Chris Do):**

- [2020-02-13] On introversion / relationships: he used to be "really uncomfortable and awkward in social situations" because he was focused on himself and on impressing others / appearing smooth and intelligent. His turning point / "mental trick": deliberately move attention away from himself and onto the person he's talking to — learn their story, be fully engaged and present. This removes the stress of trying to impress, makes the other person feel heard and connected, and (ironically) leaves people crediting *him* with being the interesting one. Encapsulated as "To be interesting, be interested." (paraphrase, self-reported — from Ch.1 "Relationships") **[biography: his self-described introversion]**
- [2020-02-13] On creativity / "Fail Forward": we must build "a different relationship with the word failure." We're programmed to associate pain/negativity/criticism with failure and mistakes, which paralyzes us from taking action; the alternative (staying with what's known) is the enemy of a creative, adventurous, pioneering life. Failure is "part of growth and necessary to invention." (paraphrase — from Ch.2 "Creativity")
- [2020-02-13] On beliefs / "No victims, just volunteers": creative people will feel overwhelmed and feel the cards are stacked against them, and "no one did you any favors" — which "may very well be true." But the only move is to "take 100% responsibility for your life" and be "completely accountable for everything that happens." Accepting that you control your life is empowering and lets you make new decisions; there's no point dwelling on what you can't control. (paraphrase — from Ch.3 "Beliefs")
