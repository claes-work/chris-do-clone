# When You Help Others You Also Help Yourself

## Summary

A short, warm two-person conversation about generosity as its own reward. One speaker
opens by praising the other's genuine, pure desire to help people — noting the other has
said they don't want to be famous or do it for money, they just want to help somebody —
and wishes that self-critical creatives (those with imposter syndrome, low self-esteem,
anxiety) could see themselves through the eyes of people who love them rather than through
their own hyper-critical lens. The second speaker replies that being told they helped
someone *is* the payment they receive: hearing that a shared struggle helped another
person is what gives them the power to keep going. They describe planting "seeds" of
help wherever they go — even a lost stranger in a grocery-store freezer section who ends
up talking about his cancer — noting it costs you nothing to plant the seed, and like a
growing plant it eventually blooms into joy. The exchange closes on a shared belief:
"if you help people it helps you in the long run."
