## Summary

A short Futur clip in which team member Jonah argues that you should push a prospective
client into a live verbal conversation (phone/video call) rather than transacting over
email. His reasoning: forcing a conversation is a commitment test that gives you power
in the sales process; email is one-directional, easy to ignore, cannot carry tone or
intention, and most people are poor writers. If a client insists on email, he suggests
recording a video message instead, and sending a calendar link with an invitation to
book (offering to move your own schedule to accommodate them). Clients who won't make
that commitment can be dropped.

**Speaker:** single-presenter clip by **Jonah** (Futur team member), first person.

### Jonah (Futur team member)

- [2020-02-18] Forcing a client to have a live conversation is "one of the most powerful
  things you can have in a sales process" — it signals commitment and gives you some
  power. (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-18] Says most of his own work, including big jobs, happens over conversation —
  on a telephone call, "without video, without the technology that we're using today."
  (paraphrase, first-person about Jonah)
- [2020-02-18] Email is easy to ignore: recipients skip to the parts they want and ignore
  the rest. (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-18] Email "cannot communicate tone and intention" — your speaking, smile, and
  "late night FM DJ voice" don't come through in writing. (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-18] Email is one-directional / one-sided; it is not conversational. (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-18] If you must send an email, record and send a video message instead —
  "surprisingly effective"; your tone, sincerity, and authenticity come through.
  (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-18] "Professional writers write an email; the rest of us try to get on the
  phone" — most people, himself included, aren't good enough writers to rely on the
  written word. (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-18] Send the client a link to your calendar: ask them to find a time, and if
  none works, to send back a time and you'll move your schedule to accommodate them.
  (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-18] You can give a brief reason for wanting the call — e.g. "so much is lost in
  email, I don't want to risk it, you're too important to me." (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-18] If a client won't commit to a conversation, you can just drop them.
  (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-18] His seven takeaways: (1) get a conversation with your client; (2) email is
  easy to ignore; (3) email can't replace conversation; (4) email is directional /
  one-sided; (5) send a video if you have to; (6) don't write unless you're a good writer;
  (7) [context] this clip is a cut-down from a Pro Group call. (paraphrase)
