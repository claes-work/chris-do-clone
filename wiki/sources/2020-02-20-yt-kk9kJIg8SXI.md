## Summary

Chris Do explains how to find and vet your first freelancer/subcontractor and how to build a bench so you always have someone to hand work to. The core moves: judge them by whether their existing work looks exactly like the work you want (they'll deliver whatever style they show you); have a working conversation about their process ("talk me through how you work, tell me where it breaks"); trust the gut feeling they give you and move on if it's not there; build a database of at least 12 people spanning low/mid/high price so you can match the freelancer to the job; and de-risk the start with a very short, paid trial (a day or two of work — if it works you continue, if not, no harm no foul). This clip is a cut-down from a weekly Pro Group coaching call.

## Key claims

### Chris Do

- [2020-02-20] Judge a freelancer by their existing work: make sure it looks exactly like the work you want done, because "they're gonna do whatever it is they show you" — grunge portfolio → grunge work, techno style → techno work. (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-20] If they don't have enough examples in the style you want, ask to see more; if they still don't have them, move on — "it's just not there." (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-20] This is why Chris tells creatives to have consistency and do the same thing over and over — a consistent body of work is "what gets you hired." (paraphrase; connects hiring advice back to his portfolio/branding advice)
- [2020-02-20] Have a working conversation: "talk me through how you work, tell me where it breaks, tell me where it's been really successful." (quote)
- [2020-02-20] Learning to vet freelancers and subcontractors is "your power as an entrepreneur." (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-20] Note the reframe of language: once you hire them, "they're the freelancer, not you" — they're a subcontractor; the relationship shifts from you-as-doer to you-as-hirer. (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-20] Good subcontractor traits: they make you feel really good, they're responsive, they seem reasonable and level-headed, they have a clear process, and they can articulate that process to you. (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-20] Trust the feeling — "I got a good feeling / I don't have a good feeling" — and act on it; if it's not a good feeling, move on, because there are countless people who do that same thing. (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-20] Build a database of at least 12 people who do exactly what you want, spanning low to mid to high price, so you know when to "bring in the big guns" versus when that would be overkill and you just need someone competent. (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-20] De-risk the first engagement with a very short, paid commitment: pay for a day or two of work; if it works out you keep working together, and if not, "no harm, no foul, we just move on." (paraphrase)
