# How To Respond When Someone Can't Afford You

★ **L3-candidate** — Contains a self-contained, named framework ("charity vs. clients" + the
creative-barter menu for clients who claim they can't pay) that extends the existing pricing
corpus in a genuinely new direction (bartered/non-cash value, exposure-as-an-act, the "trade
must exceed the cash price" rule, equity-in-lieu-of-fee). Worth promoting into
`wiki/topics/pricing/` and `persona/beliefs.md` (dated), not just held at L2.

## Summary

A coaching clip: Chris Do live-coaches a young freelance designer (studio audience) who
struggles with peers/musicians who "can't afford" design and expect it for free or in exchange
for "exposure." Chris first forces a hard distinction — a **charity** has a genuine cause and is
truly broke; a for-profit **client** who can pay should pay fair market price, and someone who
exploits creatives' need for opportunity is "not broke, they're just broken." He then makes the
student name her market rate ($4,000), and — if a genuine charity-adjacent client truly has no
cash — walks through a **barter menu** of non-cash value (concert tickets, merch, referrals,
lessons, genuine social-media endorsement) that must add up to *more* than the cash price, plus
the option to take **equity/a percentage** instead of a guarantee. Core reframe: exposure and
portfolio pieces don't count as payment because you can get those yourself.

Speaker: **Chris Do** is the coach and delivers all the substantive material (first person,
teaching a live student). **Eric Sweet** (a then-new Futur intern) narrates a scripted
intro/outro only. The student(s) speak in the role-play but train nothing. Attribution to Chris
is clear and first-person; only Chris-attributed material below feeds the persona.

## Key claims

### Chris Do

- [2020-02-26] Draw a hard line between **charity and clients**: a charity has a genuine cause
  and is truly broke; a client is in commerce, can pay, and should pay fair market price for
  creative services. Don't treat a for-profit business with the same generosity as a charity.
  (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-26] The people who demand free/near-free work and hide behind "we're broke" mostly
  "have no cause — their cause is capital," and they aren't actually broke: "they're not broke,
  they're just broken," because they're willing to take advantage of people in the greatest need
  of opportunity. They rarely try this on big firms — only on the vulnerable. (paraphrase + quote)
- [2020-02-26] "A free client is not a client" — unpaid work for a non-charity is just charity
  mislabeled. (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-26] If you do give work away, cap it with a ratio you're comfortable with — e.g. a new
  rule of "I'm gonna do ten clients before I give one away." He cites a friend who runs 50%
  charity / 50% client but with "a whole auditing process about who's gonna be considered for
  charity." (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-26] You must **know your number**. He repeatedly presses the student to state a market
  value for her work and treats "$100" / not knowing as a red flag ("I'm starting to question
  your whole 12 clients"); the exercise lands on $4,000. (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-26] If a client genuinely can't pay cash, "go back to ancient times" — people traded
  things of value before currency. Ask what non-cash value they can give. (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-26] **Exposure and portfolio pieces don't count as payment**: "you can get yourself
  exposure, you can get yourself the portfolio piece, but that's on you — that doesn't count
  against" the debt they owe you. (paraphrase + quote)
- [2020-02-26] The **barter menu** (each item gets a dollar value on it): concert tickets
  (~cover charge, e.g. $15 — "that's gonna be a lot of concerts"), merch (put a dollar sign on
  it), lessons (~$150/hr), referrals, and genuine social endorsement. Their tools (the guitar)
  are off the table — they need them to work. (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-26] **Referrals are only valuable from valuable people.** A broke band refers other
  broke bands who pay nothing — "you're gonna be in debt forever." "I got four homeless friends,
  you guys want to help them?" (paraphrase + quote)
- [2020-02-26] Reframe **"exposure" as an act, not a word**: a vague promise of exposure is
  worthless, but a real endorsement they *perform* — a social post to their following naming you,
  tagging your handle, recommending you — has value that scales with audience size; ask for their
  metrics and measure it. (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-26] **A barter trade must add up to more than the cash price, not equal it**, because
  bartering is extra work: "I would not do an even trade here." (paraphrase + quote)
- [2020-02-26] Bartered goods are re-tradable — you're only ever "borrowing" value; e.g. give
  band merch to a niece/nephew instead of spending $150 on a birthday gift. (paraphrase)
- [2020-02-26] If the client has upside potential, take **equity instead of a guarantee**: "I
  don't want a guarantee on something — I want a percentage of this or that, and I'll do all your
  design and branding." Some bands will take that deal. (paraphrase + quote)
- [2020-02-26] Bottom line for the freelancer: get creative about how you get paid, and learn to
  charge more — value your work at more than $100. (paraphrase)

### Eric Sweet (intern, framing only — does not train persona)

- [2020-02-26] Frames the clip's thesis: don't treat a for-profit business with the same
  generosity as a charity, and "exposure shouldn't be some magical word that allows clients to
  get free stuff from artists." (paraphrase; scripted outro echoing Chris)

## Notable verbatim quotes

All quotes are **Chris Do**:

- "We have to distinguish charity from clients. Big difference. They have a cause and they truly
  are broke... [the others] have no cause — their cause is capital, and they're not broke,
  they're just broken."
- "They're willing to take advantage of people who are in the greatest need of opportunity...
  they rarely take advantage of the big firms."
- "Somebody who has money, who can pay, who's in commerce, should pay a fair market price for the
  creative services that you do."
- "Maybe your new rule has to be: I'm gonna do ten clients before I give one away."
- "It can't be exposure, because... you can get yourself exposure, you can get yourself the
  portfolio piece — but that's on you, that doesn't count against" what they owe.
- "Referrals are valuable from valuable people... they'll tell all their band friends that have
  no money, that paid nothing — you're gonna be in debt forever."
- "When we say 'give me exposure,' this isn't an act that they have to participate in" — a real
  shoutout to their following, naming you and your handle, "that's worth something."
- "I would not do an even trade here."
- "Maybe you think they got potential, and you can say: I don't want a guarantee on something, I
  want a percentage of this or that, and I'll do all your design and branding for you."
- "You have to learn how to charge more, value your work more than a hundred bucks."
