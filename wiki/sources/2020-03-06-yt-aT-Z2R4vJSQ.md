## Summary

A short interview clip in which branding author **Marty Neumeier** answers the question
"how do you measure the effectiveness of branding in terms of ROI?" His answer: the old
view is that you can't measure branding because it's "too soft" and "too amorphous," but

## The brand ladder (Marty Neumeier's framework — 4 levels, bottom → top)

1. **Satisfaction** — "are customers satisfied with the company and what they put out." A
   low bar: they did what they do, maybe I buy again.
2. **Delight** — "wow, I didn't know it'd be this good... I'm gonna tell my friends."
3. **Engagement** — the customer loves the company and feels they belong to the brand.
   Signalled by three markers: **automatic repurchase** (new launch → "I'm in"),
   **emotional attachment** (e.g. the vigils/eulogies when Steve Jobs died), and a
   **sense of belonging** (tribe identity — "I'm an Apple tribe guy").
4. **Empowerment** (top) — "you don't buy brands, you join them"; the customer feels their
   life would be worse without the brand ("I wouldn't be making as much money... my life
   wouldn't be as good if you took that brand away from me").

Measurement method: track how many customers have **moved up toward the top** of the
ladder, year over year. That distribution is the read on overall brand success.
