## Summary

A first-person essay-video by **Matthew Encina** (a Futur channel host, not Chris Do) on
where crushing creative expectations come from and how they sabotage creatives. Encina
opens with a satirical 7-step recipe for disappointment (get inspired online → decide you
can make something like it → pour heart and soul in → post it expecting it to blow up →
wait for likes → get disappointed → quit). He then interviews Melanie Whitney, who unpacks
expectations as layers of cultural/family/occupational conditioning we carry unconsciously,
and offers her core reframe: don't be attached to a specific outcome, be committed to the
journey and the growth. Encina grounds it in a personal case study: The Futur's 2019
series *Building a Brand* was a hit, so the team made a follow-up, *Design From Scratch*,
which got a lukewarm response and left him feeling ashamed and like a failure — until
distance let him reread the comments and see they "aren't that bad." The takeaway: bloated
expectations, inflated by past success, leave no room for failure or learning; you don't
have to be perfect, and "you are enough."

and interview guest **Melanie Whitney**. Chris Do is absent.

## Key claims

> All claims below are attributed to Matthew Encina or Melanie Whitney. **None are Chris Do
> material.** Recorded only so the corpus is complete and this video is not re-ingested.

### Matthew Encina (host — NOT the subject)

- [2020-03-20] Encina has repeatedly set unreasonably high expectations for the things he
  makes, and failing to hit them "crushes my spirit." (paraphrase)
- [2020-03-20] He measures his self-worth by his accomplishments — which he thinks is common
  "in our performance driven culture." (paraphrase)
- [2020-03-20] Case study: The Futur's 2019 series *Building a Brand* (an inside look at
  working with clients from first meeting to final delivery) was "an amazing success," each
  episode getting hundreds of thousands of views. The follow-up series *Design From Scratch*
  got a lukewarm response, "nowhere close" to *Building a Brand*, which left him feeling
  awful, ashamed, and like a failure. (paraphrase)
- [2020-03-20] Expectations become inflated by past success; we get fixated on an imaginary
  projected outcome and leave no room for failure or learning, so we're disappointed when we
  fall short. (paraphrase)
- [2020-03-20] With distance, rereading the *Design From Scratch* comments, "they aren't
  that bad actually" — lots of love and constructive criticism he couldn't hear at the time;
  bloated expectations made the experience worse than it was. (paraphrase)

### Melanie Whitney (guest — educator, founder of "the Common Collective")

- [2020-03-20] Expectations come in layers of conditioning — cultural, family/birth-order
  (e.g. eldest sibling), occupational (people project traits onto a nurse or lawyer) — and we
  operate by them unconsciously; fulfilling them makes us feel good about ourselves. (paraphrase)
- [2020-03-20] Her core reframe: "don't be attached to a specific outcome but be committed to
  the journey and the unfoldment and the evolution." (quote)
- [2020-03-20] Becoming *aware* of the expectations you place on yourself and others lets you
  reverse-engineer where each came from and whether it's worth carrying — one of the most
  powerful things you can do for self-growth. (paraphrase)
- [2020-03-20] Shame is paralyzing, especially for people for whom things came easily; it
  erodes self-esteem into self-doubt, so next time a creative "sticks their neck out" they
  fear falling flat again. (paraphrase)

## Notable verbatim quotes

**Matthew Encina (host — NOT Chris Do):**

- "I've had so many points in my career where my expectations of the things I make are so
  unreasonably high that it crushes my spirit when I'm unable to achieve the results I was
  hoping for."
- "I measure my self-worth by my accomplishments, which I think is pretty common in our
  performance driven culture."
- "You don't have to be perfect and you don't have to live up to the expectations of others...
  you shouldn't expect yourself to be more than who you are right now, because you are enough."

  and the evolution."
- "That shame is paralyzing... then you don't feel good enough, then it creeps into your
  self-esteem, and then now you have self-doubt."
