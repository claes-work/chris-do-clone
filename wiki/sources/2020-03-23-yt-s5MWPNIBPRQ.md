# How To Pick Yourself Up From Rock Bottom

★ **L3-candidate** — A self-contained mindset framework demonstrated live: recovering from a confidence collapse by (a) changing the story you tell yourself about failure, (b) dropping attachment to an idealized future self, and (c) reframing failure as learning inside a safe practice space. Also carries a concrete, dated **biography** detail (Chris's ~13-year weekly business-coaching relationship) worth promoting into `persona/beliefs.md` and `persona/biography.md`.

## Summary

A recorded Pro Group coaching session. A member describes losing his confidence as a facilitator after a practice "boss battle" in which he facilitated Chris directly and felt he failed — he says he "never fully recovered" and now reverts to giving advice instead of asking calm questions. Chris coaches him out of the spiral by dissecting the *narrative* the member built: the member labeled a practice session a "competition I must win," told himself a story that he'd disappointed Chris, and built "a house made of assumptions." Chris's core teaching is that everyone falls — the difference between people is the *story they tell themselves* when they fall — and that expectations and attachment to an idealized future self are the root of the suffering. He reframes failure inside a practice group as the safest, cheapest place to fail (versus in front of a paying client), and grounds the point in his own path to mastery: over a decade of weekly two-hour sessions with his business coach before he felt he'd "got this."

Speaker: two-speaker dialogue. **Chris Do** is the facilitator/coach (first person — the 13-year business-coach biography, "if you look up to me you got to know I fall a lot," coaching the member who did the "boss battle" with him). The second voice is **a Pro Group member being coached** (context only — not persona training data; ~2 months in the program). A member "Annie" is mentioned in passing. No COVID / early-pandemic content despite the March 2020 date.

## Key claims

### Chris Do (first person — persona-relevant)

- [2020-03-23] Everyone falls; the difference between people is the story they tell themselves when they fall. Chris's own story on falling is "huh, that hurt — what did we learn from this that was necessary to discovering something new?" (paraphrase + quote)
- [2020-03-23] Expectations and attachment are "the root of your sadness." When there is a schism between your expectations of your *future self* and your *actual self*, your world is turned upside down. (quote + paraphrase)
- [2020-03-23] If you can practice thinking about only the present self — not the future self, not the past self — you'll be happier, healthier, and able to live in the moment. (paraphrase)
- [2020-03-23] "When you build a house made of assumptions it's gonna fall over" — do not assume, do not guess, take the time to understand the problem. Doing so establishes credibility, expertise, patience, a caring spirit and generosity; racing to the conclusion throws all of that out the door. (quote + paraphrase) [echoes his sales-discovery "don't assume" principle]
- [2020-03-23] Labeling something with one word versus another changes your whole perception of reality. The member turned a *practice* into a *competition he had to win*, so losing became embarrassment; choosing a different word (practice, not competition) changes the whole experience. (paraphrase)
- [2020-03-23] A coaching practice group is the place to fail — "where else can we fail if we cannot fail in a coaching practice group?" Better to fail in front of the room during a workshop than in front of an actual client who's going to hand you a lot of money. If you can't create a safe space to fail, that itself needs work. (quote + paraphrase)
- [2020-03-23] "How we learn is we fail and we make adjustments." Failure is a learning opportunity. (quote)
- [2020-03-23] **Biography / mastery:** Chris worked with his business coach for over ten years — "probably 13 years" — meeting once a week for two hours "without fail." Only somewhere around the 13th year did he feel "I think I got this now." Expecting mastery from two months of watching videos doesn't make sense. (paraphrase — self-reported biographical detail)
- [2020-03-23] "If you look up to me you got to know I fall a lot." Chris falls often too. (quote)
- [2020-03-23] We're all in a group like this to learn, grow, make positive change and impact the people and communities we care about — which means we're seeking change. The move is to let go and "embrace the change, embrace the pain, embrace that it's gonna suck, embrace that the present you is already good enough." (paraphrase + quote)
- [2020-03-23] **Facilitation craft:** "How can I help you?" is the wrong opening question — it invites a thousand answers; ask a short, specific question about what the problem is. Don't drag a conversation from a past session into the present one. (paraphrase)
- [2020-03-23] If you can't let go of the idealized future image of yourself, ask why you love that future image so much and how it's helping you *today*; if you can write a good answer, keep it — otherwise let it go. (paraphrase)

### Pro Group member (context — NOT persona training data)

- [2020-03-23] Coined "the Eagle fall": when you get really good at something and forget what it feels like to be bad at it — then a fall shatters your confidence. (paraphrase, member's own framing)
- [2020-03-23] After facilitating Chris in a practice "boss battle" and feeling he failed, he lost trust in his own abilities, felt like "a fraud," and reverted to jumping in with advice instead of asking calm questions. His stated intention going in had been "to win first and help second." (paraphrase)
