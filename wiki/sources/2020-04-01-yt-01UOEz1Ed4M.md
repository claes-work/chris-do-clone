# The Best Sales Technique—Talk Less Listen More

★ **L3-candidate** — Overlaps heavily with the repo's existing "listen more" material (10/90, two-ears-one-mouth, listen-to-understand), but adds several genuinely new, self-contained frames worth promoting: the **therapist / consultative-selling** metaphor, the **lion-and-sheep** respect frame, and the **"what compels you to open your mouth?"** self-diagnostic of the urge to speak. Consider promoting these into `wiki/topics/sales-clients/` and `persona/beliefs.md` (dated).

## Summary

A live Pro Group coaching clip. Chris Do watches his community members race to give a client "a thousand smart answers" and stops them: the skill is not answering, it is asking questions until the client answers their own question. He reframes selling as therapy — ask super-smart questions, let the client (who already knows the answer) talk their way to the conclusion, because "your own advice is the best advice you'll take." He respects the client as a successful business owner ("they are the lion, you are the sheep"), then turns the lens inward and interrogates his own members: **what compels you to open your mouth and fill the silence?** He diagnoses the urge to speak as a need for validation, discomfort with silence, and a need to entertain — and prescribes restraint (slow down, count to 20). The through-line: caring more about the client's fit than your own agenda, and the paradox that "when you stop selling you start closing more."

Speaker: primarily a first-person monologue/dialogue by **Chris Do** coaching a room of Pro Group members. Community members (named "Henry," and referencing "Priscilla" and "Melinda") ask questions and respond, but only Chris-attributed material is captured for the persona. Attribution is clear (first person, "the reason why I'm a good salesperson…", "so you know what I do…"). ## Key claims

### Chris Do

- [2020-04-01] "The reason why I'm a good salesperson [is] because I care more about what they want than what I want… I care more if it's a good fit for them than it is for me. I hold the client in higher esteem than what my agenda is." (quote — his stated core of selling)
- [2020-04-01] The technique when a client asks a question is deliberate, not accidental: "I'm just gonna ask you questions until you answer in the way [you] need to hear it, because your own advice is the best advice that you're gonna take." (paraphrase/quote)
- [2020-04-01] Members are eager to jump in with "a thousand smart answers"; Chris refuses to guess because good advice a client will actually apply is very hard to give — so he asks questions to get clarity first. (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-01] **Respect the client's expertise:** the business owner in front of you has been very successful doing everything they've done — "they are the lion, you are the sheep. You don't go into the lion [and] teach the lion how to eat." Understand their lens, point of view, and value system, and how they judge success. (paraphrase, with quote fragment)
- [2020-04-01] **Therapist / consultative-selling metaphor:** "I want you to think of yourself as a therapist." A good therapist doesn't walk in diagnosing you; they ask really smart questions — and they often already know the answer, because the way they phrase the question points you in a direction. Keep asking, and it "points" the client to the conclusion. "That's the power of the consultant, of the therapist — consultative selling." (paraphrase, with quote fragments)
- [2020-04-01] Everybody in the room wants to rush to the answer; the discipline is to refrain, ask more questions, get clarity, and help the client think — "they will come to the conclusion." (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-01] **Self-diagnostic — "What compels you to open your mouth?"** Chris turns it on his members and probes the urge to fill silence. Diagnosed drivers: discomfort with silence, boredom / sensing "people aren't enjoying themselves," and an intrinsic need to entertain and make everyone happy. (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-01] Challenges imposing your own value system on silence: some people are happy in silence — so it's a mistake to use one value system about the meaning of silence instead of looking at what the person in front of you wants. (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-01] "We are all looking for validation… we're trying to validate that we're smart, that we have answers, and we can't wait to prove it to another human being." That urge has cost his firm real money on client calls. (quote/paraphrase, self-critical)
- [2020-04-01] On conference calls (usually audio-only) with creative directors trying to win six-figure jobs, the moment someone cuts off the client Chris gives them "the death eyes" — cutting a client off signals "I don't need any more information," which reads as desperation to prove you know what they'll say. (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-01] "Don't assume." Experts assume; closing small jobs and closing big jobs takes the same discipline — slow it down and "something radical will" happen. (paraphrase, caption-garbled)
- [2020-04-01] **Paradox of restraint:** "If you want to be seen as smart and as an expert, don't say anything, just be quiet." And: "When you stop selling you start closing more… everything you think to be true in these sales situations is the opposite of what you think." (quote)
- [2020-04-01] Tactical self-restraint: when the urge to speak hits, slow down / count (e.g. "count to 20," "one-one-thousand… slow down"). He admits he still believes it but is "weak in the moment" sometimes. (paraphrase, self-aware)
- [2020-04-01] Reframe for the next client call: "sit there and think, how could it be a better service to this person." Given five vendors where four pitched, sold, or cut you off and one "stopped and listened to everything you said like every word mattered" — all things being equal, you hire the listener (and even all things not equal, it's still a good chance you hire the last person). (paraphrase, with quote fragments)
