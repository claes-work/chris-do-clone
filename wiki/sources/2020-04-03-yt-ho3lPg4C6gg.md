# Work From Home Setup: Kids, Distractions, and Workspace

> ⚠️ ATTRIBUTION: This is a **multi-speaker Futur team compilation** made at the start of the
> COVID-19 lockdowns (April 2020). Most of the video is NOT Chris Do and is **do-not-train**
> (Futur context only). Chris Do speaks in first person **only** in the closing reading-tips
> segment (after the narrator says "now I'll hand it over to Chris Do the CEO"). Only that
> segment is Chris-attributed and eligible for the persona.

## Summary

A Futur staff round-robin of work-from-home advice, filmed just as pandemic quarantines began.
Team members share personal setups and coping tactics; Chris Do closes with a short method for
getting more out of books. Only Chris's reading method trains the persona — the rest is context
about how the Futur team works remotely.

Speaker: **multi-speaker compilation.** Segments in order — **Matthew** (narrator/host, and the
"introvert who loves isolation" productivity segment — he references "a post that Chris shared on
his Instagram," so he is NOT Chris); **Ben Burns** (COO) & **Elyse Burns** (customer support) on
working from home with two young kids; **Greg Gunn** (illustrator) on managing anxiety/mental
health; **Elle** (social media manager, working remotely from Vancouver) on workspace setup; and
finally **Chris Do** (CEO) on how to read a book more effectively. Chris's is the only
subject-attributed segment.

## Key claims

Chris Do (subject) — reading/learning method:

- [2020-04-03] To get more out of a book, first **read the table of contents** as a high-level
  preview of what you're about to read. (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-03] **Read the book twice.** First pass: skim quickly in one sitting by reading only
  the first sentence of each paragraph. Second pass: read slowly to analyze and understand, in
  90-minute blocks with breaks between so your mind can process and digest. (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-03] **Create a legend / key code** for your annotations — e.g. use a highlighter to
  mark key words and phrases, underline things you want to cross-reference with other material,
  and draw a box around ideas you can reuse and repurpose (a building block for a future talk,
  workshop, or presentation). (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-03] Illustrates cross-referencing with a pricing contrast: Chris Voss (*Never Split
  the Difference*) recommends saying the price **last** in negotiations, because people often
  guess incorrectly and underprice themselves; whereas Blair Enns (*Pricing Creativity*)
  recommends saying the price **first** — that's anchor bias, a heavy reliance on the first
  piece of information. (paraphrase; caption garbled "blends"/"Chris Voss" — Blair Enns is the
  author of *Pricing Creativity*)

Futur team (context only — do-not-train):

- [2020-04-03] Matthew (narrator): once his day's list of 3–5 tasks (written on a Post-it) is
  done, his workday is done; he uses 90-minute "focus sprints" (learned from neuroscientist Dr.
  Sahar Yousef at 99U) with 20-minute rests, citing ultradian rhythms; and recommends the
  Eisenhower matrix from a post Chris shared on Instagram. (context)
- [2020-04-03] Ben & Elyse Burns: with two young kids, a **flexible schedule** beats fixed
  hours — batch work in the morning and after the kids sleep, treat the 4–8pm "witching hours"
  as unworkable, and measure the day by tasks completed, not hours at the desk. (context)
- [2020-04-03] Greg Gunn: coping with pandemic anxiety by finding an escape (gaming), controlling
  what he can (meals, getting dressed, music), and allowing himself to feel whatever he feels.
  (context)
- [2020-04-03] Elle: create a bright, sufficiently large workspace, a chair that encourages good
  posture, shut doors to isolate, and tune lighting/music — "a positive mindset and attitude is
  the best place to start." (context)

## Notable verbatim quotes

**Chris Do:**

- "Read the book twice. Quickly skim through the book on the first pass — it's easier than it
  sounds, just read the first sentence of each paragraph. Do this in one sitting."
- "Create a legend, a key code for what you read."
- "Draw a box around ideas that you can use and repurpose. Think of this as a building block for
  something else — perhaps a talk, workshop, or presentation you plan to give."

**Matthew (narrator, context):**

- "Once this list is done, my workday is done."

**Ben Burns (context):**

- "Stop focusing so much on the time that I'm spending on work or the time that I'm sitting at
  that desk, and [focus] more on the things that I'm getting done."
