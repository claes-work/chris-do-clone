## Summary

Ricky (a Futur team member) presents part 2 of a 4-part Instagram series, covering how
to define your audience *before* you post. The core argument: stop chasing customers and
start serving an **audience** — that's where influence, money, and leverage live. He
gives a method for finding your niche: identify who your audience is and what they need,
then map "a day in their life" and list the **gaps** (unmet needs) — that gap list
becomes your content roadmap. He overlays the audience's gaps with your own strengths,
interests, and skills, then prioritizes with a highest-impact / least-effort filter.

Speaker: single-speaker monologue by **Ricky** (Futur team member; self-identified in
the intro). **Chris Do does not speak in this video.** Seth Godin is cited as a
third-party reference, not present.

## Key claims

_All claims below are **Ricky's**, not Chris Do's. Recorded as Futur-context; do not
attribute to the subject._

- [2020-04-21] Don't make content for yourself — no cake photos or selfies (unless you're
  "super beautiful," a joke aside). You have to serve an audience. (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-21] Move away from "customers" toward an "audience" — "The power, the
  influence, the money is here. The leverage is here." Chasing customers means chasing
  them all your life; an audience shows up for you and may buy merch (a hat, cap, badge).
  (paraphrase + quote fragment)
- [2020-04-21] Serving people well makes them tell others organically, and those others
  can hire you, pay you, or sponsor a post. (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-21] Two-step targeting: first identify **who** the audience is, then identify
  **what** they need. (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-21] Two prompts for finding audience needs — **task** and **gap**: think about
  the audience's day, what they're trying to do every day, then map it out. Cites Seth
  Godin's "can you pick out your audience from the crowd." (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-21] Creatives have rich imagination but usually waste it "thinking about how
  bad we are and how the world hates us" — redirect that imagination toward imagining
  someone else's day. (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-21] List the audience's **gaps**; that list becomes the content-strategy
  roadmap. (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-21] You can't solve every challenge, so also list your own side: interests,
  strengths, skills (his own example — "I like to teach, my background's in video, I
  enjoy helping people, I read a lot"). Then look for the **overlap** between the
  audience's gaps and your strengths — that overlap is your content strategy. (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-21] You know your audience well because you talk to them all the time "through
  the hub" (the Futur community). (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-21] Prioritize using an **impact/effort graph**: do the highest-impact,
  lowest-effort things first. People err by doing the hardest things that give the least
  results, then get depressed and quit. (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-21] "I'm super lazy — I just want to do the easiest thing possible. And if it
  doesn't work I'll make more effort and keep increasing effort till I get the results I
  want." (quote, Ricky)
