## Summary

A Futur team member recaps a four-part Instagram-growth series, condensing it into a practical
tactics checklist: post frequently and consistently (up to 2x/day), time posts by your
analytics, spend 1–2 hours engaging with every comment, feed reader questions back into new
carousel content ("generative"), keep an analog idea notebook, write witty/personal
story-driven captions, and use a balanced mix of high/medium/low-traffic hashtags (max 30) with
the goal of ranking in the "top nine" for a hashtag. Includes an iPhone text-replacement
shortcut demo for pasting a saved hashtag block.

## Key claims

_All claims are attributed to the unidentified Futur team-member speaker, NOT Chris Do. Retained
as Futur context only._

- [2020-04-23] Frequency and consistency are key — the speaker tested posting once every three
  weeks vs. once a day vs. up to five a day; more frequent posting got more followers. (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-23] Post when your Instagram analytics say your audience is active (day-of-week and
  time-of-day charts); the speaker's own best times were ~7:00 a.m. and ~2:00 p.m. (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-23] Engagement is critical — the speaker reserves ~1–2 hours to respond to every
  single comment (with a heart, a sarcastic answer, or a real answer), prioritizing the most
  engaged people, to "reward my audience for paying attention." (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-23] Reading comments is "generative": recurring questions in the comments become the
  next carousel, generating more content from audience feedback. (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-23] Keeps an analog idea notebook (field-notes style) divided into eight frames per
  page to plan carousels; a pre-planned carousel can be built in ~30 minutes. Leaves a gap
  between grids to add notes; the filled notebook becomes a shareable "artifact." (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-23] Write witty and personal captions — "tell me a story in the caption, it's very
  important." (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-23] Instagram caps hashtags at 30; using more than 30 causes something to break, so
  never exceed 30. (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-23] Use a balance of high (1M+ searches), medium, and low (~50–100k or less) traffic
  hashtags. A few low-traffic ones let you become well-known for something, build traction, and
  "teach the algorithm" what you're related to. (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-23] Shortcut with no fancy tools: find someone with a good-sized following you want to
  compete against, copy their hashtags, and research each. (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-23] Goal is to land in the "top nine" posts shown when you search a hashtag (via the
  magnifying glass) — if you're in there, "you're golden." (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-23] iPhone tip: Settings → General → Keyboard → Text Replacement → "+" to save a
  phrase; the speaker stores ~30 hashtags under a shortcut like "BIZHT1" to paste instantly.
  (paraphrase)
