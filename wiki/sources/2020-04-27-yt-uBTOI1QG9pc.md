## Summary

A short Futur clip from the early COVID-19 period. Host Ben Burns frames it: The Futur
invited CPA and business consultant Errol Garrison on to talk about surviving the
financial crisis, and one piece of his advice generalized beyond the moment. Garrison's
core idea: when revenues drop, proactively call every current and past client, but open
with **"What can I do to help you?"** rather than **"Have you got any work for me?"**
Asking for work puts the other person on the spot and triggers defensiveness (fear of
saying "no"); offering to help positions you as an asset someone wants to be involved
with. He illustrates with a student who got ignored on LinkedIn when she asked for a
meeting/help, then landed a same-day reply after pivoting to a specific value-offering
message (a Live Aid–style virtual concert idea pitched to a Las Vegas entertainment
company). Burns closes by tying it to positioning yourself as an asset and communicating
your value, and plugs the full extended version on The Futur's website.

## Key claims

### Errol Garrison (guest — NOT persona training data)

- [2020-04-27] When revenue drops, be 100% proactive: call every client you currently
  work with or have ever worked with. (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-27] Open the call with "What can I do to help you?" rather than "Have you got
  any work for me?" Asking for work psychologically puts the person on the spot, makes
  them defensive, and makes them less receptive to the request. (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-27] Offering to help makes you "seen in a completely different light" —
  psychologically you become someone the other person wants to be involved with; asking
  for something makes them raise their barriers because they're scared of having to say
  "no." (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-27] Illustrative anecdote: a student reached out on LinkedIn to a large Las
  Vegas entertainment company (virtual concerts) saying "I'd like to meet you, I need
  your help" — and got no response. On his advice she reframed to offer help, pitching a
  specific idea ("I have an idea how your company can create the first live concert that
  will help people with the coronavirus," referencing a Live Aid–style fundraiser); the
  company replied within less than a day. (paraphrase, third-party anecdote)
- [2020-04-27] At networking events, the person handing you a business card and asking
  "Have you got any work for me?" makes you want to turn and walk away. (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-27] Describes himself as holding "the contrarian view of management."
  (paraphrase)

### Ben Burns (host)

- [2020-04-27] Positioning yourself as an asset to others puts you on sure footing when
  approaching people for new work; it's about communicating your value to the potential
  client. (paraphrase)
- [2020-04-27] This clip is a small excerpt; a free full extended version and Errol's
  resources are on The Futur's website. (paraphrase, promo)

## Notable verbatim quotes

**Errol Garrison** (guest — NOT Chris Do):

- "You have to be a hundred percent proactive and you have to literally make a call to
  every single client you currently work with and/or have ever worked with and say to
  them a simple sentence: what can I do to help you — rather than saying have you got any
  work for me."
- "If you make a call and ask for work you immediately psychologically make a person feel
  as if they're on the spot and they become defensive."
- "When you offer to help you are seen psychologically as someone I want to be involved
  with. When you ask me for something I psychologically immediately put up all of my
  barriers because I'm dead scared of having to say that horrible word 'no' to you."
- "Please my friends, offer to help — don't ask for something."

**Ben Burns** (host):

- "Positioning yourself as an asset to others is always going to put you on sure footing
  when you're approaching people for new work — it's all about communicating your value
  to that potential client."
