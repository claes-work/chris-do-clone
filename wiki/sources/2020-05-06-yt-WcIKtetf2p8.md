## Summary

Matthew Encina reflects on 20+ years of his own portfolio work (1998–2020) and shares three lessons for sustaining a creative career. The through-line is that a creative career is an endless journey with no roadmap, sustained by embracing risk, reframing failure as instruction, and trusting that seemingly unrelated experiences compound over time. The video is a personal, autobiographical monologue and closes with a plug for The Futur's courses.

Speaker: single-speaker monologue by **Matthew Encina** (first person — self-identified twice by name; all anecdotes are his: pitching Coldplay in 2014, founding and closing a design studio 2007–2009, working in advertising/video games). **Chris Do does not appear or speak.** No material here is attributable to Chris Do.

## The 3 tips (framework — Matthew Encina, not Chris Do)

1. **Start before you're ready** — anything worth doing carries risk; most of us are risk-averse and hunt for reasons to delay. The "I'll figure it out" mentality is the creative's superpower.
2. **Failure is not the end** — it's a lesson to your future self. Accept the situation, learn from it, and ask what you'd do differently knowing what you now know.
3. **Things will add up / nothing is a waste of time** — careers rarely follow a plan; intuition, improvisation, and curiosity-driven pursuits compound into who you become.
