## Summary

An unnamed Futur coach answers a member — a solo logo designer — who asks how to systematize and
"automate" bespoke logo work so he can remove himself as the bottleneck. The coach reframes
"automate" as **internal process systemization**, not full automation: turn logo production into a
"conveyor belt" of documented stages so the right people sit in the right seats. He leans on the
book *Built to Sell* (an agency whittled down to only designing logos) and offers concrete tactics:
pre-selected brand-descriptor words that map to pre-built "style-scapes" (mood boards), templatized
branding worksheets (from a branding workshop he attended in Austin), and website structural
templates derived from the highest-converting layouts. The unifying idea he names is
**"pre-loaded / done-for-you"** — do 70–80% of the work before a project starts so turnaround and
per-project customization shrink.

## Key claims

_All claims below are attributed to the **unnamed Futur coach**, not Chris Do. Recorded for Futur
context only._

- [2020-05-11] "Automating" bespoke work really means systemizing the internal process — you're
  still designing the logo, but you turn its production into a repeatable "conveyor belt" so the
  team knows what to do and you can remove yourself via people and/or process. (paraphrase — coach)
- [2020-05-11] Recommends the book *Built to Sell*: a burnt-out full-service agency owner, guided by
  a mentor, narrows the business down to only designing logos; the lesson is finding efficiency in a
  narrowed, systemized offering. (paraphrase — coach)
- [2020-05-11] To remove yourself as the bottleneck, break the work into stages, map the skill sets
  each stage needs, then document what you personally do in your bottleneck phase so you can hand
  that document to someone else. (paraphrase — coach)
- [2020-05-11] Warns against the common creative-world mistake of hiring an expensive high-end
  consultant/expert to fulfill one piece — it eats into margins. (paraphrase — coach)
- [2020-05-11] Tactic: let clients pick ~3 words from a pre-selected list of the most common
  brand-descriptor words (e.g. energetic, vivid, organic); map those to a library of pre-built
  "style-scapes" (distinctive visual mood boards) built up over time, which removes guesswork and
  dials in direction fast. (paraphrase — coach)
- [2020-05-11] Anecdote: attended a two-day branding workshop by a brand strategist friend from
  Austin whose company is "Branding For The People"; the whole thing was templatized worksheets
  (pick 3 of 9 "personas" to combine), and he walked out with a customized branding/identity
  booklet he handed to his logo designer, who could then "get to work" almost immediately.
  (paraphrase — coach, third-party anecdote)
- [2020-05-11] For websites: after designing ~100 sites in a niche (e.g. chiropractors), identify
  the ~3 highest-converting layouts, turn them into structural templates, then just swap in the
  client's colors, logo, messaging and content — the client never needs to know there are only a
  few templates behind the unique-looking result. (paraphrase — coach)
- [2020-05-11] Names the principle "pre-loaded / done-for-you": do 70–80% of the work before a
  project even starts so customization effort and total turnaround time shrink. (paraphrase — coach)
