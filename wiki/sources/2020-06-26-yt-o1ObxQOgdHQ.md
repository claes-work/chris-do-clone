# How To Learn & Create New Visual Styles

## Summary

Answering a student who asks how to step out of their comfort zone and create work
different from their usual, Chris Do offers two concrete exercises. First: list five
variables you're uncomfortable with (time, tools, colors, subject matter, typeface,
etc.) and, on your next assignment, introduce exactly one of them as a constraint — a
low-risk way to explore without feeling you've betrayed your authentic self. Second:
the "master copy" — pick a piece of design you love and rebuild it as closely as
possible; you'll absorb a new visual vocabulary, tools, and techniques. Threaded
through is his belief that you should develop a clearly defined, uniquely-you point of
view rather than trying to be like everyone else. The clip ends with his standard
Futur note that this is a cut-down from a longer video he helped produce.

Speaker: single-speaker monologue by **Chris Do** (first person — "I've given this
assignment to my students before," "I happen to think it's important," and the
signature Futur outro "a full video that I helped produce"). A student's question opens
the clip (lines 1–4) but the student is not the subject. Attribution is confident:
this is Chris Do, not another Futur designer.

## Key claims

### Chris Do

- [2020-06-26] To break out of a comfort zone, make five variables you're uncomfortable with — "time, tools, colors, subject matter, typeface choices" — and on your next assignment introduce **one** of them as a constraint (e.g. "you can't use a typeface you've used before"). (paraphrase)
- [2020-06-26] Change one variable at a time so you don't feel you're "betraying your true authentic self" — it's not about reinventing yourself into a whole new person, just trying something different. (paraphrase)
- [2020-06-26] You should have a clearly defined point of view / worldview that's uniquely you; forget trying to be like everybody else "because those spots [are] already taken." (paraphrase)
- [2020-06-26] The "master copy" exercise: take a design you love and rebuild it as closely as possible, without using any of the original elements — you'll learn a new visual vocabulary and stretch your creative wings. (paraphrase)
- [2020-06-26] He has given the master-copy assignment to his students and gotten "mind-blowing" results — they learn new tools, techniques, and rendering processes; sometimes the copy is so good he can't tell it from the original. (paraphrase)
- [2020-06-26] Pick something that excites you to master-copy, and "see what happens." (paraphrase)

## Notable verbatim quotes

All quotes are **Chris Do**:

- "I want you to create five variables that you're uncomfortable with — whatever that might be, time, tools, colors, subject matter, typeface choices... make five variables. And then the next assignment that you take on, introduce one of those things as the constraint."
- "It's not about reinventing you to become a whole new person, but seeing like, hey, you've always liked spaghetti, but maybe you'll like pizza today."
- "You so don't feel like you're betraying your true authentic self."
- "I happen to think it's important for you to have a clearly defined point of view in the world, to have this worldview that's uniquely you, and forget about trying to be like everybody else because those spots [are] already taken."
- "Look at a piece of design that you love and just copy it as an exercise, just try to rebuild that design as closely as possible... you'll start to learn a new visual vocabulary."
- "I've given this assignment to my students before and they've come back with mind-blowing results... sometimes I can't tell the difference between the master and the copy."
