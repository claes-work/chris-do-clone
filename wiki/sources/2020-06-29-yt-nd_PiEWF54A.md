## Summary

(a "style guides kit"), covering how the team consolidates assets, builds a schedule,
communicates asynchronously, and assigns task ownership. The narrator (Ben Stroh) frames
the problem — coordinating a globally distributed team of writers, marketers, designers

## Key claims

_All claims below are **Futur team / Ben Stroh**, org-context only — not Chris-attributed,
not persona training data._

- [2020-06-29] The Futur launches products "like a Kickstarter campaign": they sell at a
  discounted rate before the product exists to validate demand; if the pre-launch goal
  isn't met, they don't make the product and refund everyone. (paraphrase, Ben)
- [2020-06-29] The team switched from in-person whiteboard meetings + a scattered software
  stack (word processors, cloud storage, a calendar app, a task app, chat tools) to Notion
  as a single hub; they claim they "cut our meetings by 75%" because meeting notes already
  live in Notion. (paraphrase, Ben — sponsor claim)
- [2020-06-29] Their launch playbook has four steps: (1) gather all assets/information in
  one place, (2) build a schedule and timeline, (3) open lines of communication, (4)
  assign tasks and ownership areas. (paraphrase, Ben)
- [2020-06-29] A launch Notion page uses four headings: Information (brief, campaign dates,
  pricing, goals), Resources (reusable assets, mockups, sales copy, tracking links stored
  in code blocks so URL parameters survive copy/paste), Schedule, and Tasks. (paraphrase, Ben)
- [2020-06-29] They always set a product **launch date on a Tuesday** because "our sales
  are highest on Tuesdays" — after the Monday rush. (paraphrase, Ben)
- [2020-06-29] Pre-launch campaigns run about **three to five weeks** (this one ~4 weeks,
  with ~2 weeks of team prep beforehand) — tight enough to create urgency, long enough for
  the audience not to forget. (paraphrase, Ben)
- [2020-06-29] Ben is "a big believer in **asynchronous communication**": most things don't
  need an immediate answer, and leaving comments directly in Notion lets a globally
  distributed team (members in different time zones, e.g. Romania) respond at their own
  pace instead of being interrupted by meetings and notifications. (paraphrase, Ben)
- [2020-06-29] Channel ownership at The Futur (circa 2020): paid Facebook/Instagram ads +
  organic Instagram = one marketer (name caption-garbled); email = Natalia; organic Twitter
  and Facebook = Alex (in Romania); YouTube = Matthew and Chris. (paraphrase, Ben)
- [2020-06-29] They use a Notion Kanban board (Trello-style) for task ownership, with custom
  card properties (due dates, review dates, statuses, assignments) and switchable views
  (calendar, list). Ben says he tries "not to micromanage" — surfacing tasks ahead of time
  lets people manage their own bandwidth. (paraphrase, Ben)
- [2020-06-29] The Futur "has been using notion now for years"; Ben has "personally migrated
  my entire life over to a personal notion." (paraphrase, Ben — sponsor context)
