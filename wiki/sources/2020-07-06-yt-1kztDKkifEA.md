# Don't Live Your Parent's Plan—Why The Past Is A Poor Predictor

★ **L3-candidate** — A compact, quotable statement of one of Chris Do's signature themes (the parents' outdated "operating system" and why the past is a poor predictor of the future). Worth promoting into `wiki/topics/mindset/` and a dated entry in `persona/beliefs.md` if a fuller source surfaces; the Atari-2600-vs-PS4 analogy and the "have them call me" offer are distinctive persona material.

## Summary

Chris Do argues that using the past to plan the future is a mistake because industries are created and destroyed faster than ever (the iPhone's 10+ years disrupting GPS units, paper maps, pagers; now AI, deep learning, deepfakes). Parents mean well — good-hearted parents just want to protect their children from pain — but the "rule book" they grew up with worked in *their* era; handing it to you now is like handing you an Atari 2600 when the PlayStation 4 is out. He urges viewers to choose work that both aligns with current/near-future opportunity *and* that they love, because people who love the work will outperform those who merely tolerate it. The clip is drawn from a podcast/show episode featuring a self-taught YouTube-tutorial creator as a "shining beacon" of going one's own path, and closes with Chris offering to personally call any listener's parents to vouch for the career. A producer outro (Greg Gunn) plugs The Futur podcast.

Speaker: predominantly single-speaker monologue by **Chris Do** (first person — "have them call me, I'll talk to them"; "I'm speaking from experience... I have a lot of cousins who..."). ## Key claims

### Chris Do

- [2020-07-06] We often look to the past to decide where to go in the future, "but that's not really where the future lives." The past is a poor predictor. (paraphrase / partial quote)
- [2020-07-06] In just over 10 years the iPhone created brand-new industries and decimated others — it displaced standalone GPS units, paper maps, and pagers/texting plans. Today the frontier is AI, deep learning, and deepfakes. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-06] "The rule book that your parents grew up with worked in their era" — you need to understand that, and hopefully they do too. (quote/paraphrase)
- [2020-07-06] Good-hearted, good-natured parents ultimately just want to protect you from a life of pain and misery — but they're giving you an Atari 2600 when the PlayStation 4 is out; it's not going to help you. (paraphrase, with quote fragment)
- [2020-07-06] The message to give parents: "Mom and dad, I do love you and I cherish you for trying to look out for me, but I need to figure out what I need to do." Your choice must align with current/near-future opportunities *and* with what you love. (quote/paraphrase)
- [2020-07-06] "If you show up every day and do the job that you hate, the people who show up and love it are going to outperform you every single day." (quote)
- [2020-07-06] He is "speaking from experience": he has many cousins who did the traditional path — studying law, dentistry, engineering, medicine — and a lot of them are not happy, which is why they "live for the weekends." (paraphrase — biographical, self-reported)
- [2020-07-06] If you look forward to the weekend as an escape from your everyday life, "it's telling you something that's wrong." (quote/paraphrase)
- [2020-07-06] Going your own path takes work, dedication, a little luck, and a level of commitment — and it *should* take that. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-06] Cites Seth Godin's *The Dip*: if something is easy to obtain, everybody will have it, and "scarcity is the biggest driver of value." (paraphrase, third-party attribution — Seth Godin)
- [2020-07-06] Contrasts the featured creator with a hypothetical kid in Mumbai who made similar videos but "gave up" when it didn't click; the featured creator kept going, pushed past temporary setbacks / pain / depression, and reached the top of the pyramid. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-06] Offer to viewers: if after all this you still need a parent's approval and support — "because your relationship is everything" — have them call me; I'll tell them about the career you could have. But you have to "hold up your end of the bargain because it's not a guarantee." (paraphrase, with quote fragments)
