## Summary

A short excerpt of a Futur interview in which the guest (Cynthia Kane) walks through a
technique for building emotional intelligence: learning to notice and precisely
**label** your emotions. Her method starts below the vocabulary layer — first notice
the raw *sensation* (good / bad / neutral), then name the "superficial" emotion (fear,
anxiety, anger), then interrogate it to reach the deeper "inner emotion" underneath.
She uses a cappuccino metaphor — most people stay "in the foam," but the real feeling
is in the coffee/core. Chris Do closes by reframing the technique as "teaching people
how to have a conversation with themselves."

## Key claims

### Cynthia Kane (guest — CONTEXT ONLY, does NOT train persona)

- [2020-07-10] Creative people are often visual, so words are "not their best friends";
  when asked how they feel, what they say often isn't what's actually in their heart —
  hence the value of learning to identify feeling in general. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-10] Start below the label: notice the *sensation* first — what is a good
  sensation, a bad sensation, and a neutral state — rather than immediately naming the
  emotion. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-10] Many people are uncomfortable in a neutral state and read it as
  something being "wrong" or "off" because they don't feel the exaggerated extremes.
  (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-10] There is a "superficial" emotion we can name (anxiety, fear, anger) and
  often a deeper emotion underneath it; the inner emotion is where it really resides.
  (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-10] Be specific when labeling — if a label feels vague or too general, it
  isn't specific enough, and you need to look back and dig deeper. Specificity matters
  because it leaves less room for others to misunderstand you. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-10] Excavate by questioning: "you felt fear, but what were you afraid of?
  What did you think was going to happen?" — peel back layers (e.g. fear → feeling
  stupid → feeling invisible). (paraphrase)

### Chris Do (outro framing — persona-eligible)

- [2020-07-10] Reframes the guest's technique as excavation: "you're teaching people

- [2020-07-10] We go through life "totally unaware," having bursts of positive,
