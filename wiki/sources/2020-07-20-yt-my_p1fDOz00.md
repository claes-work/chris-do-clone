# How to Sell Without Being Salesy—Sell Like Crazy

★ **L3-candidate** — This is a named, self-contained framework (Chris Do's **SALES = Serve, Ask, Listen, Empathize, Summarize**), plus his "conditional/hypothetical if-then close." Attribution is clean (solo Chris Do monologue), so it is safe to promote into `wiki/topics/sales-clients/` and `persona/beliefs.md` (dated) rather than holding it at L2.

## Summary

Chris Do reframes selling away from pitching/convincing/manipulation into a service-and-inquiry process built on a five-word acronym: **SALES — Serve, Ask, Listen, Empathize, Summarize.** The goal of a sale, he argues, is to *inspire the prospect to make a decision by helping them think through their challenges* — you ask, you don't tell. He walks each letter (serve like a fiduciary; ask neutral "what" questions to surface the real problem; listen more than you talk with note-taking and mirroring; empathize by labeling emotion; summarize/play back to confirm understanding), then teaches a low-pressure **conditional hypothetical "if-then" close**. He cites three books as further reading.

Speaker: single-speaker monologue by **Chris Do** (first person — "I'm gonna teach you," "here's how I would say"). No co-host or guest appears. Note: despite the title's "Sell Like Crazy" tag (the title of Sabri Suby's book), **Sabri Suby does not appear and the book is not mentioned in the transcript** — this is Chris Do teaching his own SALES framework, not a Suby interview. The opening segment is a paid sponsor read for Google Ads Smart Campaigns (ad copy, not Chris's own view).

## SALES framework (Chris Do)

- **S — Serve**: a mindset shift; not about what you get but what you give. Show up to help without expectation, acting in the other's best interest "like a fiduciary."
- **A — Ask**: don't give advice ("silence the advice monster" — from Michael Bungay Stanier's *The Coaching Habit*). Ask questions to surface the *real* problem, because the first problem you hear is usually not the correct one. Best questions start with **"what"** (neutral, unbiased); "how" rushes to tactics; "why" can sound accusatory.
- **L — Listen**: talk less, listen more. Use small affirmations ("yes," "right," "mm-hmm"), take notes "like a reporter" (preferred over constant eye contact), and **mirror** — repeat the client's last few words.
- **E — Empathize**: understand and help the client relive the pain; focus on the feeling, not the solution. Use **labeling** (Chris Voss) — "it sounds like…"
- **S — Summarize**: play back the major points to confirm you understood ("If I understand you correctly… is that correct?"). A human understands, a tape recorder only records. Close with "is there anything else?"

## Key claims

### Chris Do

- [2020-07-20] Sales has a bad reputation because most of our exposure is to manipulative, high-pressure salespeople; you can need to sell for your business yet not want to feel "slimy." (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-20] Selling is NOT pitching, presenting, convincing, or manipulation. "The goal of sales is to inspire the prospect to make a decision, not to tell them what to do… you don't tell, you ask." (paraphrase + quote)
- [2020-07-20] The reframe: **sales means to Serve, Ask, Listen, Empathize, Summarize** — the SALES acronym. (paraphrase, named framework)
- [2020-07-20] Serving means acting in the other person's best interest in the spirit of generosity, "like a fiduciary… taking care of somebody else's money." (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-20] Silence the "advice monster" (crediting Michael Bungay Stanier, *The Coaching Habit*) — the urge to prove you know something is the opposite of what you want. (paraphrase, attributed to Stanier)
- [2020-07-20] Questions are "magical keys" that open the door to the client's mind; asking gives the other person deference, makes them feel important, in control, and powerful, and gets you information others didn't get. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-20] Not all questions are equal: "what" questions open discussion and stay neutral/unbiased; "how" questions rush to tactics; "why" questions can sound accusatory if the tone is off. (paraphrase, attributed to Stanier)
- [2020-07-20] Favorite question: "What's the real challenge for you?" — because it presupposes a fake/smokescreen challenge is usually presented first. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-20] When a client transmits and gets no recognition, they're "transmitting blind" (pilot metaphor) — signal you're listening with small affirmations. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-20] Practice "full value listening" (crediting Kevin Daley) — everything the client says matters; take notes to prove it, which he prefers over constant eye contact. (paraphrase, attributed to Daley)
- [2020-07-20] Mirroring: repeat the client's last few words back to them. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-20] Empathy means helping the client relive the pain and focusing on the feeling, not the solution; use "labeling the emotion" (crediting FBI negotiator Chris Voss) with the stem "it sounds like…" (paraphrase, attributed to Voss)
- [2020-07-20] Summarizing/playback demonstrates understanding; a human understands where a tape recorder only records. Ask "did I get this right?" to get confirmation or clarification — both are valuable. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-20] Close with a **conditional/hypothetical "if-then" close**: because deciding means "to kill" / eliminate possibility, clients fear it — so lower the stakes by walking them through small hypothetical commitments. Example: "If a vendor could ensure a website that increased conversion from 2 to 4%… you'd consider this a home run?" → then "If we submit a proposal for $65,000 by end of business, then you're ready to move forward next week, is that correct?" (paraphrase + example)
- [2020-07-20] "If" is safe: it's hypothetical and requires low commitment; you move from hypothetical to real gently. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-20] Recommended reading: *Socratic Selling* (Kevin Daley), *Never Split the Difference* (Chris Voss), *The Coaching Habit* (Michael Bungay Stanier). (paraphrase)
