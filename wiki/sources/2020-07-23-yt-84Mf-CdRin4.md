# Share What You Know, While You Learn To Build An Audience

★ **L3-candidate** — Contains two named, reusable frameworks new to the corpus: the **"2X rule"** (make twice as much as you consume) and the **consume → reflect → implement → share** loop feeding a **virtuous cycle of confidence**. Also sharpens the "share *while* you learn" reframe of "teach what you know." Worth promoting into `wiki/topics/content-strategy/` and `persona/beliefs.md` (dated). Gate: main teaching content is cleanly first-person Chris Do; the recap/outro (see below) is a Futur editor and does NOT train the persona.

## Summary

A pandemic-era (mid-2020) cut-down of Chris Do speaking on a panel about where the opportunities are in a transformed world. His answer: stop looking in the rear-view mirror, detach from process and history, and move fast to serve an audience that is currently being ignored. The build mechanism is teaching — but he reframes the scary "teach what you know" into **"share *while* you learn"**: don't hoard information, document your discoveries, out-teach competitors who are busy guarding secrets, and convert your learnings into earnings. He gives two operational tools — the **2X rule** and the **consume-reflect-implement-share loop** — that compound into confidence, and closes with a motivational charge to act before the window of opportunity closes.

Speaker: **Chris Do** speaks the entire teaching body (roughly the panel remarks and the closing "final thought"), in first person, addressing a room of creatives and handing back to a moderator ("Jose, back to you"). The event is a "BOW event by Art Center" (caption; likely an Art Center College of Design event), a conversation Chris had with **Jose** and **Robbie**. The **video's opening teaser and the closing recap + sign-off ("here is the key takeaway from this video… that's where I cut down from a conversation that Chris had…") are a Futur editor/narrator**, speaking about Chris in the third person — faithful to Chris's points but NOT Chris-attributed, so not persona training data.

## Key claims

### Chris Do

- [2020-07-23] You don't get to the future by looking in the rear-view mirror; the windshield is large and the mirror is small "for a reason." Divorce yourself from history and detach your attachment to process — focus on outcomes. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-23] Dogma and tradition slow you down; in a seismic moment (the pandemic, a possible second wave) you have to move really fast. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-23] Where to start: **find an audience that is being ignored right now.** It doesn't take much to find a group who share a worldview and are asking "does anybody care about us?" Find the gaps to fill; move quickly and you can build a whole different business model. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-23] He names systemic racism, Black lives, and trans lives as who was being ignored at that moment and deserving attention (contextual, mid-2020). (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-23] Design is "to move from an existing situation to a preferred one." (quote — his working definition of design)
- [2020-07-23] Borrows a UX principle for branding: **know me, anticipate my needs, make my life simple, look out for me, and reward me** — and you deliver this through teaching. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-23] "Teach what you know" sends people into an existential crisis ("what does anyone know anyways?"), so he modifies it to **"share while you learn"** — don't be an information hoarder; put your discoveries out there and see what happens. (paraphrase — the core reframe)
- [2020-07-23] Cites the book *Rework*: **out-teach the competition**, because they're busy hoarding their secrets. (paraphrase, third-party citation)
- [2020-07-23] Cites Jim Rohn: wealth is the ability to convert and transform knowledge and experience — so **"convert your learnings to your earnings"** and **become a documentarian of your work.** (paraphrase + quote, citing Jim Rohn)
- [2020-07-23] Creatives are divergent thinkers who see and connect things non-creatives can't; you synthesize connections because "there's nothing new under the sun." But you must then *do* something — make the product, share it, post it, let go of the fear. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-23] **The 2X rule:** for every one piece of content you consume, go out and make two things — twice as much, at minimum. (paraphrase — named rule, NEW)
- [2020-07-23] **The loop:** consume → reflect (ask "what was most valuable to me?" / "how could I adapt this for a different, underserved audience?") → implement → share. (paraphrase — named loop, NEW)
- [2020-07-23] **Virtuous cycle of confidence:** the more you try, the more you're exposed; the more you're exposed, the less you have to be afraid of; the less afraid, the more likely you are to try — go around the triangle and confidence grows. (paraphrase — NEW)
- [2020-07-23] You don't need 100,000 or a million followers — you just need the few people who can validate that you know what you're doing to get where you need to go. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-23] Be brave, let go, get out of your own way, and understand you are worthy to share who you are. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-23] **Build something with someone** — a friend, a spouse, whoever; do the practices (yoga, meditation, visualization) needed to maintain a "higher frequency" and step into your purpose. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-23] Closing "final thought": in times of great uncertainty a handful of individuals and companies seize the moment and become "the titans of tomorrow"; **disaster often precedes opportunity.** The window is open but not for long — don't let "if only" (if only I'd believed / pivoted quicker / acted sooner / not let fear paralyze me) become future regret. "Tough times don't last, but tough people do." (paraphrase + quotes; delivered by Chris as event closing)

### Futur editor/narrator (NOT persona training data — third-person recap)

- [2020-07-23] Recaps Chris's thesis: creatives are good at connecting different things into a new product/service, but before creating you must find the people you can serve — a group being ignored, and *not* an over-competed niche. Go where they are, share what you learn to help them reach their preferred outcome, and after doing so 3–5 times they'll trust you and buy. (paraphrase of the editor's restatement of Chris)
