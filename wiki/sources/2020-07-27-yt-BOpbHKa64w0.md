# How To Get Known & Capture Attention in 2020

★ **L3-candidate** — Chris Do's giving / permission-marketing / "build community not conversions" thesis is a coherent, quotable belief cluster (advertising-pushes vs. content-attracts, the chef-who-shares-everything analogy, Neumeier's "strongest tribe wins", the anti-scarcity stance). Attribution is clear (first-person Chris), so this flag stands: worth promoting into `wiki/topics/content-strategy/` and `wiki/topics/branding/` plus dated entries in `persona/beliefs.md`. ## Summary

An edited cut-down (published on The Futur, 2020-07-27) of a conversation Chris Do had with Jose Caballer and Robbie at "the BOW event by Arts Singer." An interviewer asks Chris how he's learned to give away decks, toolkits, and content for free over ~8 years. Chris argues that giving is not transactional: advertising interrupts and pushes people away ("focus on me"), whereas permission/content marketing offers a gift and earns the know-like-trust sequence that precedes any purchase. He attacks the creative's scarcity mindset (giving away your knowledge is not a zero-sum loss), uses renowned chefs — who publish their recipes and build empires — as the model, and frames the goal as building the strongest tribe (citing Marty Neumeier) by delivering so much value that people pay just to keep you going. A Futur narrator closes with the takeaway: start sharing your knowledge now because people must trust you before they buy.

## Key claims

### Chris Do

- [2020-07-27] Many people treat giving as transactional and expect reciprocity "within the minute," so they post content deliberately designed to promote their services — and the agenda is transparent; people "can see right through it." (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-27] Early on at Blind he "didn't know what to post" and just shared projects ("hey cool, check this out") — part of the "press release machine." Posting as himself, "a human being trying to connect to other human beings," got a very different response: a conversation. (paraphrase, self-critical / biography — Blind era)
- [2020-07-27] Advertising is interruptive by nature — it says "I'm important, focus on me" — and pushes people away. Permission/content marketing instead offers a gift ("here's a gift for you today... I hope you find this valuable"). (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-27] Giving well starts a sequence: people show up, ask "what's next," and go through **knowing you, liking you, and then trusting you** — trust being the important part. (paraphrase — know/like/trust)
- [2020-07-27] Many creatives hold a **scarcity mindset**: they think giving their thing away is a zero-sum game where they lose and the other gains. "It doesn't really work like that." (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-27] Artists he invites on the show refuse to share their work/technique, fearing "a thousand clones" who could compete with them — so they "wind up becoming the best kept secret." (paraphrase, anecdote)
- [2020-07-27] **Chefs are the model**: the guarded "secret recipe / 11 herbs and spices" era is over — any capable chef can reverse-engineer your dish, so "there are no more secrets." The most renowned chefs got there by writing cookbooks and going on shows, showing exactly how to cook the perfect steak, and built multi-million/billion-dollar empires through generosity. (paraphrase, analogy)
- [2020-07-27] You don't build something great with force; you build it "through love," through sharing and paying your gifts forward. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-27] The next generation of creatives won't be validated institutionally (catalogs, AIGA, monograms) but by **how much content they put out and how well-known they are**. To the objection "you can't just be an Instagram star and be an authority," his answer is "watch them do it" — the internet democratized platform access and "changed the whole game." (paraphrase — 2020-dated view on authority)
- [2020-07-27] He's especially excited about the rise of young people of color and women, because platform access is democratized and "the new will rise"; his own motivation is that influencing a generation of creatives lets them "make some impactful change" together. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-27] Cites **Marty Neumeier**: "the brand with the strongest tribe wins." Chris says he's "all about building the strongest tribe." (quote attribution to Neumeier via Chris)
- [2020-07-27] He does not think "how much value can we extract from our customers" but "how much more value can we deliver" — to the point where people say "enough, we get it, we'll just give you money so you can keep doing this." (paraphrase — value-delivery over value-extraction)
- [2020-07-27] In the knowledge/creative economy there's no shortage of information (Google gives beautiful free answers), so people choose to spend money with one person because of **trust and reciprocity** — customers feel "I've gotten so much value from you, I want to give back." Some say they'd pay even if the download were empty / "zero kilobytes," just to support the work continuing. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-27] If you stop chasing the quick transactional conversion, you build something meaningful and sustainable, and people give back "in ways that you can't even imagine" — "that's where the real power is in building community." (paraphrase)

### Futur editor / narrator (NOT Chris — closing voiceover)

- [2020-07-27] Stated takeaway: start sharing your knowledge and wisdom with potential clients because people need to trust you before they buy — noting people check "more than three" websites/reviews before purchasing — and "you are not too late to enter the content game." (paraphrase, editor)

## Notable verbatim quotes

**Chris Do:**

- "When you're advertising to people they tune out. Advertising actually pushes people away because it's very interrupt[ive] by nature. It's saying 'I'm important, focus on me,' versus permission marketing or content marketing which is 'I'm going to give you something, here's a gift for you today.'"
- "They start to trust you and that's a really important thing... a lot of creatives have this scarcity mindset that if I give my thing away to somebody they're thinking zero-sum game — that I lose something while they gain something. It doesn't really work like that."
- "They wind up becoming the best kept secret."
- "The most renowned chefs in the world are there because they've written cookbooks, they've gone on shows, they show you exactly how to cook the perfect steak... and then they build a multi-million, multi-billion dollar empire by sharing — just this spirit of generosity, this act of just giving."
- "You're not going to build it with force, you're going to build it through love."
- "They're going to be validated by how much content they put out and how well-known they are... 'you can't just be an Instagram star and be an authority' — and I'm like, yeah, watch them do it."
- "Marty Neumeier says the brand with the strongest tribe wins, and I'm all about building the strongest tribe. In my mind I'm not thinking about how much value can we extract from our customers, I'm always thinking how much more value can we deliver."
- "I don't care if I download it and [it's] zero kilobytes, I just need to support you guys so I can see this continue on."
- "If you stop chasing the quick transactional conversion, you actually build something that's meaningful, sustainable, and people give back in ways that you can't even imagine."

**Futur narrator (editor, not Chris):**

- "The reason why you should start sharing your knowledge and wisdom with your potential clients is that people need to trust you before they make a purchase."
