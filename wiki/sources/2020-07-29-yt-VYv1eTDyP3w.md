# Teach And Grow Rich

★ **L3-candidate** — Attribution is clean first-person Chris Do, and this clip contains a named, self-contained model (the confidence loop) plus Chris's clearest articulation of "teaching is the highest form of mastery" and "learning is not consumption." Worth promoting into `wiki/topics/content-strategy/` and `wiki/topics/mindset/` and into `persona/beliefs.md` (dated). Held at L2 pending a synthesis pass.

## Summary

A cut-down of a conversation Chris Do had with Jose Caballer and Robbie at the BOW event hosted by Art Center. Chris argues that people are numb to their own story and talents ("it's almost invisible to us"), so they wrongly believe they have nothing to share. He reframes learning as action, not consumption — consuming books/courses/podcasts gives the "illusion of mastery" but only practicing, shipping imperfect work, and *teaching others* cements knowledge. He offers a confidence loop (the more you try → the more you're exposed to → the less you fear → the more you try) and urges everyone to just share what they know online and stop trying to prove they're an expert.

Speaker: predominantly a single-speaker monologue by **Chris Do** (first person — recounts a podcast guest calling him "chris," gives his own favorite movies, says "here's my formula"). An unnamed interviewer poses the opening question (lines 11–24). At the end **Jose Caballer** speaks *about* Chris in the third person (lines 159–190), and a **Futur team host** delivers the outro (lines 191–201). Only Chris-attributed material trains the persona; Jose's and the host's lines are context and are labeled as such below.

## Key claims

### Chris Do

- [2020-07-29] We are bad at recognizing our own talents — "it's almost invisible to us" — because we forget what we know and what we've learned. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-29] Recounts podcast guest Matt Essen's analogy: you become numb to your favorite movie after seeing it a thousand times; likewise "you become numb to your own story" and forget what you have to share with the world. (paraphrase, third-party analogy Chris endorses)
- [2020-07-29] Cites Greg Gunn's 10-slide carousel tutorial on picking colors (invert a favorite color with Cmd-I, or go 180° for the complementary) getting ~25,000 likes off one simple tip Chris didn't even know Greg knew — proof that "we just forget what it is that we know." (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-29] Learning is not consumption; consuming books, videos, courses, podcasts, audiobooks and webinars is "an intellectual exercise" that feels like progress but is "the illusion of mastery." (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-29] To actually master a skill you must practice and implement — apply what you learn, make something imperfect, be okay with it, and "risk exposing yourself to see the gap that might exist between your skill and your taste." (paraphrase; note the gap-between-skill-and-taste framing, an Ira Glass idea)
- [2020-07-29] Quotes Stephen Covey: "to know and not to do is really not to know." (quote, attributed to Covey)
- [2020-07-29] The highest attainable level of mastery is teaching others to do what you do — mastery "is more than just being exceptional at something." (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-29] People think they have nothing to share because they compare up, but "there are a lot of people out there who are well below your level" who need their guidance; share by tweeting, posting, making a carousel, video tutorial, podcast or infographic. (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-29] "You gain clarity through articulation" — teaching 10 people means they hear it once but you hear it 10 times, cementing your own knowledge while helping others; "one teaches to learn." (paraphrase + quote fragment)
- [2020-07-29] **The confidence loop / formula:** the more you try, the more you're exposed to; the more you're exposed to, the less you have to be afraid of; the less you're afraid, the more you're likely to try — going around the loop grows your confidence. (paraphrase — named formula)
- [2020-07-29] Begs the audience (says he "doesn't beg often") to just be brave enough to put something out there and see what happens. (paraphrase)

### Jose Caballer (context — not subject; do NOT train persona)

- [2020-07-29] When Chris shares his content it is "genuinely chris… his manifestation of his essence"; you start by sharing your journey and saying "I'm not a master, I'm going to share what I've been taught." (paraphrase)
- [2020-07-29] Framing sharing as proving you're right or an expert is "a 20th century colonial institutional mentality that is no longer relevant" — give others the same value you've received. (paraphrase, Jose's view)
