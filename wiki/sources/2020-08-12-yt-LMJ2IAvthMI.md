# Skills Everyone Needs To Succeed In The 21st Century

★ **L3-candidate** — Chris Do frames a named, quotable thesis (Alvin Toffler's "learn, unlearn, relearn") as the core 21st-century success skill and layers his own "informed opinion / open learning loop" method on top. Worth promoting into `wiki/topics/mindset/` and (dated) into `persona/beliefs.md`, alongside the existing learn-how-to-learn / 3-things-to-learn-in-school material.

## Summary

Chris Do reads a quote from futurist Alvin Toffler — "the illiterate of the 21st century are not those who cannot read and write, but those who cannot learn, unlearn, and relearn" — and unpacks it as a three-stage cycle. We live "information rich and time poor," so the constraint is no longer access (education is now democratized and mostly free/near-free) but your ability to process, discern, and evolve. His method for the middle stage: form your own informed opinion by comparing and contrasting conflicting ideas, then keep testing that hypothesis for the rest of your life — learning should never stop, like a recycle symbol. He closes with the proverb "the wind can't break the tree that bends." The clip is a re-cut excerpt from a longer whiteboard session called "How To Learn Anything."

Speaker: single-speaker monologue by **Chris Do** (first person — "you've heard me say this before," "this clip is from a whiteboard session I recently did," and a self-deprecating self-reference to "some Asian fool" giving conflicting pricing advice, i.e. Chris joking about his own past teaching). He addresses a team member, "Ricky," directly (light banter, not a second speaker with attributable claims). Attribution is clear first-person Chris.

## Key claims

### Chris Do

- [2020-08-12] The key skill for success in the 21st century is being able to **learn, unlearn, and relearn** (citing Alvin Toffler); those who cannot are the new "illiterate." (paraphrase; the Toffler line is quoted below)
- [2020-08-12] We live in a time that is "information rich and time poor" — there's no shortage of information, so your ability to gather, process, and learn determines how far you rise and how close you get to your goals. (paraphrase)
- [2020-08-12] Education/information has been **democratized** and most of it is now available free or nearly free — "this is incredible." (paraphrase)
- [2020-08-12] **Unlearn:** don't get stuck on the first piece of information you learned. When two credible ideas conflict, you must resolve the conflict in your own mind and decide which you agree with. (paraphrase)
- [2020-08-12] The best thing to do while learning is to hold an **informed opinion** you can compare and contrast — that's how you form your own idea; then you **relearn** and keep testing the hypothesis over and over for the rest of your life. (paraphrase)
- [2020-08-12] Learning should never stop — it's a cycle, "like this recycle symbol." (paraphrase)
- [2020-08-12] As information becomes available from many different sources, you'll need to **discern and evolve constantly** to create an informed perspective. (paraphrase)
- [2020-08-12] Illustrates the "unlearn" problem with his own conflicting **pricing** advice: at one point he ("some Asian fool," a self-deprecating self-reference) says you should state the price *first*, and later says state it *last* — leaving the learner to resolve the contradiction and form their own view. (paraphrase; self-referential example)
