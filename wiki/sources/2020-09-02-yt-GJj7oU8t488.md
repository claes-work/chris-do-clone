# How To Create Your Own Luck

★ **L3-candidate** — Strong mindset material on "luck = the byproduct of preparation" and taking ownership of what you have agency over. **Gated on attribution:** this is a multi-speaker panel, so ONLY the clearly Chris-Do-attributed material below (the ownership/preparation monologue and the "business as a sport" answer) may train the persona. The autobiographical HealthStream/Nashville coding story belongs to a co-panelist ("Marcus"), not Chris — do NOT promote it into Chris's `beliefs.md`/`biography.md`.

## Summary

A panel-style clip in which Chris Do argues that "being at the right place at the right time" is not luck but the visible byproduct of preparation and showing up. He answers an audience question about the daily habits of a great entrepreneur by framing "business as a sport" — take care of body, mind and spirit (sleep, diet, working out, martial arts, intermittent fasting, mindfulness) so you can perform. He then delivers his signature move on "right place, right time": weather, viruses and the marketplace are out of your control, but everything in your domain is your agency; obsessing over luck is "a total waste of time." He illustrates with a co-panelist ("Marcus") whose tech-career break came from months of self-taught coding plus doing an ordinary job (waiting tables) as if it mattered — so that when the opportunity came, his "bags were packed." The takeaway is a "message of hope": take ownership of what you control and take the step sooner rather than later.

Speaker: **multi-speaker panel/event.** (1) An audience member/host asks the opening question. (2) **Chris Do** gives the "business as a sport" answer and, later, the main ownership/preparation monologue (identified by his signature themes — martial arts black belt, intermittent fasting — and his voice: "I'm going to play my old classic role," "some degenerate like myself," addressing "Riyadh" and "Marcus"). (3) **"Marcus"** — a co-panelist — narrates his OWN biography in first person (waiting tables on the Frist family, getting a tech job at HealthStream in Nashville, teaching himself to code). Caption boundaries between speakers are fuzzy; the opening martial-arts answer is attributed to Chris with reasonable but not total confidence. Marcus's autobiography is context only.

## Key claims

### Chris Do

- [2020-09-02] Being at the right place at the right time in front of the right person is "more the byproduct of your preparation than it is just luck from the outside — it's going to look like luck." (quote/paraphrase)
- [2020-09-02] He sees "business as a sport" and the human body as a machine that performs at the level you prepare it to. Martial arts, intermittent fasting and daily workouts are part of what makes him better than his competition. (paraphrase; direct self-attribution of his own routine)
- [2020-09-02] He works out so that when he sits down to present he is clear-headed, energized, dopamine firing, and can deliver his best; if he weren't training you'd be able to tell — sluggish, not powerful. (paraphrase)
- [2020-09-02] To be great: take care of yourself — sleep well, eat well, work out, and do whatever mindfulness practice you can, so you can better control the thoughts coming in and out of your head. (paraphrase)
- [2020-09-02] There is "literally no other thing more important than showing up to class two to three times a week" to becoming a black belt — if you don't show up, you can't do any of it. The best way to be a great entrepreneur is to keep doing it every single day: show up and work on your business daily. (paraphrase; martial-arts analogy)
- [2020-09-02] The "right place, right time" thing is a hindsight thing you have no control over, so chasing it is "a total waste of time." (paraphrase)
- [2020-09-02] Caveat: there is a legitimate *market-analysis* component — you don't want to be in a market where the thing you're trying to do is impossible (e.g. you need technology companies that are actually hiring). (paraphrase)
- [2020-09-02] When people say "you had to be at the right place at the right time," they are usually trying to attribute success to luck, which is completely out of your control. Wisdom is distinguishing what's in your control from what isn't: weather, a spreading virus, and (unless you're a "trillionaire") the marketplace are out of your control; everything in your domain / that you have agency over is in your control. (paraphrase)
- [2020-09-02] People don't realize how much of the "right time" stuff they can control simply by showing up, being focused, and executing well — when someone says "if this hadn't happened…," the main ingredient was *you* and how much you brought. (paraphrase)
- [2020-09-02] It's your job to "show up in every moment that you can, ready to give your absolute best performance" — you never know which moment will matter. (paraphrase/quote)
- [2020-09-02] He has "a strong allergic reaction" to the luck framing: at some point you have to take ownership and say "I'm gonna be ready." Most (not all) successful people, when the call came, were ready — or they created the calling themselves and went out into the unknown to explore. (paraphrase)
- [2020-09-02] Retelling Marcus's story to make the point: if Marcus doesn't pick up the books, doesn't teach himself, or quits halfway, then when the job opening appears he isn't qualified — the preparation is what converts the opportunity. (paraphrase)
- [2020-09-02] Closing "message of hope": feel empowered to take control of the things you have agency over, and take that step sooner rather than later — "because it gets a lot harder later." (paraphrase)
