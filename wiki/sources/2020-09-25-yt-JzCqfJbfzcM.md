# Are You Charging Too Much? w/ Melinda Livsey

★ **L3-candidate** — Chris Do's "the buyer and the seller decide, full stop" framing of subjective value, plus the doctor-consult-vs-surgery counter to the "designers shouldn't out-earn a doctor" objection, are a sharp inversion of the usual charge-more material (can you charge *too much*?). Worth promoting into `wiki/topics/pricing/` and a dated line in `persona/beliefs.md`.

## Summary

Chris Do and Melinda Livsey answer a viewer who was told (via DM) that designers "overcharge" — especially that it's wrong for a designer to earn more in a day than a doctor does. Chris dismantles the objection with the principle of **subjective value**: nobody in society gets to decree what a creative service is "worth"; only the buyer and the seller do. He argues the price attaches to the *problem being solved* (and to the buyer's context), not to the *thing* (the deliverable) — the same logo can be worth wildly different amounts to different buyers. He reframes the moral charge ("greedy, immoral, unfair") as envy from people who "can't cope," and closes by telling creatives to stop policing their own prices and instead focus on the value/business growth the client receives.

Speaker: **two-person dialogue** — **Chris Do** (subject) carries the argument; **Melinda Livsey** (recurring Futur colleague; context-only, NOT the subject) hosts, reads the viewer's objection, and adds one reframe (creatives place value on the thing rather than the problem). Only Chris-attributed material trains the persona.

## Key claims

### Chris Do

- [2020-09-25] Who decides what a creative service is worth? "The buyer and the seller — that's it." No third party in society gets to decree a fair price. (quote / paraphrase)
- [2020-09-25] Apple analogy: Apple commands a hefty premium over competitors; if you think it's overpriced you simply don't buy — but it would be absurd to stand outside an Apple store calling it "immoral" that they sell above cost. Price above cost isn't unethical. (paraphrase)
- [2020-09-25] When a client comes to you, "they have a problem that they already know they have to spend money to solve" — the buyer has already decided the problem is worth paying to remove; they only decide if *your* price is worth it. (quote / paraphrase)
- [2020-09-25] "I could literally design the same logo for 10 different people and they will tell me it's worth different amounts of money." Value is subjective and buyer-dependent, not a property of the deliverable. (quote)
- [2020-09-25] Serve the customer, not your ego: "the sun does not orbit around you." Big problem → happily solve it; small problem → refer them out ("call Larry or Mary"). Don't force a small customer to inflate their small problem into a big one. (paraphrase)
- [2020-09-25] Doctor counter to the "designers shouldn't out-earn a doctor" objection: a doctor's *consult* costs very little (or is free), but operating on your eye or removing cancer costs a lot — same doctor, same hour of time, radically different price. So price tracks the problem and mostly the buyer, not the time spent. (paraphrase)
- [2020-09-25] The same subjective-value logic governs the airplane seat, bottled water, and luxury handbags/clutches: the buyer decides it's worth a lot, the seller happily agrees to take the money. Meanwhile the knock-off vendor complains "why won't people pay more for my poorly-made bags?" and cries "luxury goods are a scam." (paraphrase)
- [2020-09-25] Once you grasp "the subjective nature of value," the whole objection dissolves — everybody has a different need, worldview, and value system. (paraphrase)
- [2020-09-25] The critics "throw shade because they just can't cope"; they invent reasons you're an evil/greedy person so they can feel better about their own situation. If you internalize their judgment you've "fallen prey to this." (paraphrase; note: ad-hominem reframe of the critic, not an argument about price)
- [2020-09-25] Prescription: stop judging your own worth; instead "only think about what is it that the client [is] gonna get from you, how much the business is gonna grow if they were to work with us" — then you'll feel happy charging what you charge. (paraphrase)
- [2020-09-25] Many creative people "have a really unhealthy relationship with money and value and getting paid to do something that they love." (paraphrase — Chris agreeing/extending Melinda's framing)

### Melinda Livsey (context — does NOT train persona)

- [2020-09-25] Reads the viewer's objection: multiple DMs claim designers overcharge, that it's wrong for a designer to earn more in a day than a doctor, that "what we charge is inflated." (paraphrase — relaying viewer, not her own view)
- [2020-09-25] Reframe: the critics "are putting value on the thing itself and not the problem that it is solving" — the deliverable (the logo) never changed, so they can't see how it could be worth more or less; the *context* is what differs. (paraphrase)
- [2020-09-25] Agrees creative people often have "a really unhealthy relationship with money." (paraphrase)

## Notable verbatim quotes

**Chris Do:**

- "Who gets to decide? I'll tell you who gets to decide — the buyer and the seller. That's it."
- "When a client comes to you, they have a problem that they already know they have to spend money to solve."
- "I could literally design the same logo for 10 different people and they will tell me it's worth different amounts of money."
- "The sun does not orbit around you. I'm here for the customer."
- "A doctor doesn't charge you the same amount of money based on the time that they spend... the consult [is] done for very little money, but the time in which they're operating on your eye, that's a lot of money."
- "The buyer decides this is worth a lot of money, [and] the seller happily agrees to take that money."
- "Once you start to understand that, then you understand the subjective nature of value."
- "There are a thousand people out there who[se] seemingly only purpose in life is to throw shade because they just can't cope."
- "If you can understand the subjective aspect of value, and only think about... how much the business is gonna grow if they were to work with us, then you will feel really happy [charging] what you [charge]."

**Melinda Livsey (context):**

- "They are putting value on the thing itself and not the problem that it is solving... the thing's the same, how could it be worth more or less?"
