## Summary

A Futur team member fields a webinar Q&A on lead qualification: how to tell a lead is
unqualified, and what to send a lead that is not qualified yet. The presenter lists the
red flags that should make you "run away" from an engagement, then recommends sending a
**capabilities deck** (company overview + case studies + a wide price-range sheet)
instead of a custom proposal, so "price fishers" self-select out without you doing bespoke
work. Closes with a pitch for The Futur's redesigned "The Perfect Proposal" product.

Speaker: single presenter, a **Futur team member (unnamed, attribution uncertain)** —
not Chris Do. Chris Do and Jose Caballer are referenced in the third person as authors
of a separate red-flags video, and are not present in this clip.

## Key claims

### Futur team presenter (NOT Chris — does not train persona)

- [2020-10-12] Red flags that a lead is unqualified: (1) you're not sure of their goal
  or needs; (2) they won't tell you a budget, or don't respond at all to a range question;
  (3) they have no timeline for the project; (4) they open by asking for a proposal too
  soon, before anything is discussed. (paraphrase)
- [2020-10-12] If you see one or more red flags and "the hair on the back of your neck is
  raising," run away or kill the engagement quickly — it signals someone not serious about
  working with you. (paraphrase)
- [2020-10-12] What to send an unqualified lead: a **capabilities deck** — who you are as a
  company, what you do, what you've done for others (case studies), and a basic price-range
  sheet. It satisfies price fishers without you building a custom proposal. (paraphrase)
- [2020-10-12] Wide price ranges on the sheet do the screening: e.g. "logo design 15 to
  35k" gives room to adjust, but a $500 customer knows immediately you're too expensive for
  them. (paraphrase)
- [2020-10-12] The capabilities deck is reusable — pick the right case studies, delete the
  rest, type the client's name on the cover, and send a PDF in ~5 minutes; no per-client
  rebuild. (paraphrase)
- [2020-10-12] Cross-references a separate, more detailed "bad client red flags and warning
  signs" video by **Chris and Jose** as the deeper treatment of unqualifying a client.
  (paraphrase — attributes the deep material to Chris/Jose, elsewhere)
- [2020-10-12] If money is tight (esp. "with everything going on in the world right now"),
  build proposals yourself; the "Perfect Proposal" product is a paid shortcut. (paraphrase,
  promotional)
