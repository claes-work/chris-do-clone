# How To Get Followers on Instagram When Starting From Zero

★ **L3-candidate** — This is a dense, named-framework Instagram-growth playbook told entirely first-person by Chris Do (AIDA carousel structure, the ROT hook formula, the "heads or tails" headline game, the 12-post minimum, the guest-post/shout-out growth loop). It is his own documented content-strategy method and worth promoting into `wiki/topics/content-strategy/` and `persona/beliefs.md` (dated). Attribution is clean (see below), so the L3 gate is satisfied.

## Summary

Chris Do answers an interviewer's questions on how to grow an Instagram audience from zero. His core argument: content must serve the audience (teach/give value, not show what interests you), and the platform rewards using its newest features — which is why he leaned into 10-slide teaching carousels. He gives a concrete cold-start plan (right mindset, 12-post minimum, one narrow topic, a spirit of generosity, three colors and one–two fonts, keep the design simple), then the structural craft: build carousels on the AIDA marketing formula (with Desire re-cast as "Detail" for carousels) and open with a ROT hook (Result, Objection, Time). He closes with four extra tips: the "heads or tails" headline game, learning hashtags, writing guest posts for bigger accounts, and paying for shout-outs — plus a plug for his Thursday Nov 5 Instagram workshop.

Speaker: Q&A / interview format. An **off-camera interviewer** (unnamed, addresses him as "Chris"; "Benji" is named once as someone he's spoken with before) asks the questions; **Chris Do** answers every question in the first person ("once I learned that I was able to grow an audience," "I get about six and a half thousand a week," "I personally have not done this"). ## Key claims

### Chris Do

- [2020-11-02] The first thing to decide when making content is *who you're serving* — you're asking for the audience's scarce time, energy, and attention, so you must give them something worth more than they gave; educate/teach them something of value to *them*, not to yourself. (paraphrase)
- [2020-11-02] Posting pictures of things that merely interest you is not interesting to others "unless you're setting trends"; learning to serve the audience is what let him grow. (paraphrase)
- [2020-11-02] When a platform rolls out new features it wants you to use them, and the algorithm rewards you for it; he realized Instagram was pushing 10-slide carousels, and his friend **Michael Janda** showed him you could teach with them — a fit for him as a 15+ year teacher. (paraphrase)
- [2020-11-02] Have the right mindset: this is a long game, not instant gratification — if you're in it for instant gratification, stop. (paraphrase)
- [2020-11-02] The minimum is **12 posts**, because that fills the entire grid; someone who sees one post won't insta-follow — they check what else you do, and it must be consistent. (paraphrase)
- [2020-11-02] "Consistent" means focused on **one specific topic**; posting off-topic (his example: mostly toy cars, then one action figure) makes people not follow or unfollow. Be very narrow, focus on one topic. (paraphrase)
- [2020-11-02] Two ways to pick what to write about: things you're passionate about, or imagine one specific audience member (younger self, a cousin/sibling/parent/classmate) and speak to and help *them*. (paraphrase)
- [2020-11-02] Approach it with a spirit of generosity — give with no strings attached and no expectations; do that consistently and you're on the right path. (paraphrase)
- [2020-11-02] Design guidance: pick ~three colors to use in combination (not all at once), and one—two fonts at most. (paraphrase)
- [2020-11-02] People without a design background are insecure about their design and over-add elements, making it worse; he ran a "carousel clinic" and set a bar — one typeface, one weight, one color — and said if you can't beat that generic design, don't do it. (paraphrase)
- [2020-11-02] The secret to growing anywhere: assume nobody knows you, and create something a *stranger* would feel compelled to share — merely liking isn't enough; it has to be valuable enough that they go out of their way to hit share and tell others. His growth comes from people sharing his posts to their friends/family on and off Instagram. (paraphrase)
- [2020-11-02] He structures each self-contained carousel story on **AIDA** (a long-standing marketing formula): Attention → Interest → Desire → Action. For Instagram carousels he changes **Desire to "Detail"** — the slides where you deliver the goods you teased. Action = one single call to action (not 45 different asks). (paraphrase)
- [2020-11-02] The **ROT formula** for the attention hook: promise a **R**esult they want, name an **O**bjection they might have, and reference **T**ime. Example: "Learn how to speak Spanish [result] in two weeks [time] even if you don't know a foreign language [objection]." (paraphrase)
- [2020-11-02] The YouTube title claiming "how to grow 10,000 followers a week" is a bit clickbait but is "exactly what I was doing" with this strategy at full-time effort; he's not doing that anymore and now gets ~**6,500 a week** without working that hard. (paraphrase — self-reported growth numbers)
- [2020-11-02] Carousels are hard to write; the balance is "light but satisfying" — too heavy means you can't explain it simply enough (you don't understand it well enough); too light is "all foam and no beverage." The takeaway must not be 100% obvious (his counter-example: "if you breathe more you'll be alive longer"). (paraphrase)
- [2020-11-02] The **"heads or tails"** game for getting unstuck: heads = write 10 headlines for the same idea, then build the carousel to deliver on the chosen headline; tails = start from the key takeaways and work backwards. He personally starts with tails because writing the headline first is horrible for him. (paraphrase)
- [2020-11-02] Learn to use **hashtags** — they teach the algorithm what your post is about; many strategies exist, just Google one and use it. (paraphrase)
- [2020-11-02] Once you've met the 12-post minimum on one topic, **write a guest post** for someone you look up to in your space: spend an inordinate amount of time on one super-valuable, well-designed piece, give them your best post; if they like it they post it free and can send you hundreds—thousands of followers. A few people in his community grew by 20,000–30,000 from a guest kickstart. (paraphrase)
- [2020-11-02] Key caveat on guest posts: it only works if your own account is already good ("their account was already good, they just didn't have eyeballs"); a good guest post to a weak account only picks up a few hundred. (paraphrase)
- [2020-11-02] If nobody will host a guest post, you can **pay for shout-outs** — DM accounts that post others' content, ask their rate, offer to write and design the content in their feed's style and mention yourself briefly at the end. It's inexpensive; he hasn't done it personally but knows people who went from zero to 100,000 followers in under a year this way. (paraphrase — third-party outcome)
