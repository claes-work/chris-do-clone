## Summary

A short, edited excerpt from a Futur live stream on landing a design job, distilled into four
tips. Chris Do opens and closes as host/narrator; the actual advice is given by a guest
educator. The four tips: (1) use LinkedIn to find alumni of your design school who work at
target companies and reach out through that warm network; (2) build a portfolio that shows
*thinking* — how the work translates to a client's customers, especially in virtual/social
contexts — not just polished artifacts; (3) don't chase jobs in struggling industries (e.g.
restaurants in 2020) — "skate to where the puck is going"; (4) treat the cover letter and
résumé as storytelling and information-hierarchy exercises crafted for real people, never
generic templates. A bonus timing tip: December (especially after the first week) is the worst
time to job-hunt; the second week of January is the best, because people don't quit before
collecting holiday bonuses/time off, then resign the first Monday of January, leaving teams
scrambling to hire.

Speaker: **two-speaker** edited conversation. **Chris Do** narrates the intro (lines ~12–24)
and outro (lines ~237–253) and interjects once ("Chris, I think that is the next step..." is
the guest addressing him; his own reply is brief). The **guest** — Professor **Petrula
Vrontikis** (captions garble her name to "petru lebron tiges"), a Futur/ArtCenter educator who
ran her own design studio doing fast-casual restaurant branding and teaches the Futur Academy
course "Land Your Dream Job" — delivers all four tips and the timing advice in the first
person. The self-descriptions ("I know arts undergraduates," "restaurant design... in my

**[Petrula Vrontikis / guest] (job-hunting tips — attribution: uncertain for persona):**

- [2020-12-01] LinkedIn makes networking easy: search your design school + a target company
  (she uses ArtCenter College of Design + "Collins") and you'll surface a dozen-plus alumni
  affiliated with that company; read their profiles, see who still works there and in what
  role, and reach out. The network need not be ArtCenter — any design school works. (paraphrase)
- [2020-12-01] The goal is to offer what they're looking for *plus* more value; get insight
  into what value they need. (paraphrase)
- [2020-12-01] A portfolio of "just artifacts" (luxury packaging, posters) shows design skill
  but not thinking; employers want to see how the work translates for their customers in a
  virtual environment — social media, promotion — not just a high-end retail display. (paraphrase)
- [2020-12-01] Get off the "doom scrolling"; research which industries are doing well and
  whether you have connections to people in those industries. (paraphrase)
- [2020-12-01] Don't target struggling industries. She did fast-casual restaurant design years
  ago; if she still did, it "would not be going very well" — those businesses are restructuring
  and the last thing they need is heavy outside branding help. "Skate to where the puck is."
  (paraphrase; note: 2020 pandemic context)
- [2020-12-01] Cover letters and résumés are storytelling and hierarchy-of-information
  exercises — same facts positioned differently changes everything; canned/generic letters read
  as generic. Craft them for real people to get real jobs; it's not about templates, it's about
  designing communication that solves a problem — what a graphic designer does best. (paraphrase)
- [2020-12-01] Timing: December (especially after the first week) is the worst time to job-hunt
  — everyone's busy with the holidays and won't give you attention. Don't send letters out on
  Dec 18. (paraphrase)
- [2020-12-01] The second week of January is the best time: people don't quit before collecting
  holiday bonuses and time off, so they resign the first Monday of January; teams then scramble
  to hire. Aim to have your materials on their desk two-to-three days after they return (not
  day one, when there's a big pile). (paraphrase)
- [2020-12-01] Acknowledges it's not "super easy" — job seeking is emotional and can deteriorate
  your mood — but says she's just applying logic and being objective about something people get
  emotional about. (paraphrase)
