# How To Build A $1M Dollar Business Teaching

## Summary
A recorded conference/keynote-style presentation by Chris Do (solo) laying out his framework for building an income-generating teaching business from an audience of "true fans." It opens with his personal origin story (burnout/imposter syndrome at ~5 years into his studio, discovering teaching, then starting YouTube in 2014), then walks through four parts: why true fans matter, what people get wrong about social media, transactional-vs-transformational thinking (reciprocity), and a concrete blueprint for a six-figure course business. All content is Chris-Do-attributed and trainable; he cites several outside thinkers (Kevin Kelly, Seth Godin, Marty Neumeier, Robert Cialdini) as sources, not as speakers.

**Speaker:** Chris Do solo (keynote/talk). He narrates third-party stories (Dustin Lee, Ari Chung) but they are reported by Chris, not spoken by them.

## Key claims
- [2020-12-07] [paraphrase] Chris's turning point: about 5 years into running his studio he felt depressed, burnt out, and had imposter syndrome after hiring talented young designers; he took a sabbatical, was invited to teach for the first time, found his voice/confidence, and promised himself he'd never feel imposter syndrome again.
- [2020-12-07] [paraphrase] In 2014 his friend Jose Caballer (caption: "Jose Cababier" — likely garble) invited him to teach online via YouTube; despite being an introvert and "behind the camera" talent, teaching online changed his life and business.
- [2020-12-07] [paraphrase] Chris frames the "creative person's dream" as earning a living, being around people you love and are inspired by, without having to do any client work.
- [2020-12-07] [paraphrase] Invokes Kevin Kelly's "1,000 True Fans" (2008 Wired essay): you don't need millions of fans; 1,000 true fans each yielding $100 profit/year = $100,000/year. Math scales — $200 profit needs 500 fans; $50 profit needs 2,000 fans.
- [2020-12-07] [paraphrase] Two components (per Kelly) to make it work: a direct/peer-to-peer relationship with fans, and the ability to earn ~$100 profit per fan. True fans will drive 200 miles, buy the book + ebook + audiobook + best-of compilation.
- [2020-12-07] [paraphrase] Gatekeepers/intermediaries (publishers, labels, studios, galleries, manufacturers) that controlled distribution are gone thanks to crowdfunding, streaming, and social networks — the public now decides.
- [2020-12-07] [paraphrase] The Futur book Kickstarter: launched with a few sample pages and a funny title, goal $25,000 to validate before writing; funded in 16 days, finished at ~$75,000 (3x goal), with five backers pledging $1,000 each. A second-run Amazon launch (Aug 25) added 774 books / ~$27,000.
- [2020-12-07] [paraphrase] Chris made his first YouTube video at age 42 — "it's never too late," you just have to start.
- [2020-12-07] [paraphrase] Cites Seth Godin (Permission Marketing): interruptive marketing steals time/attention; permission marketing creates value. Reframes mass media (push, yelling, interruptive, tuned-out) vs. niche media (pull, listening, value-creating).
- [2020-12-07] [paraphrase] Niche media converts strangers → friends → customers → casual fans → true fans. Cites Marty Neumeier: best brand builders see greatness in customers and enable it — so answer who they dream of becoming, their pains, aspirations, and jobs-to-be-done.
- [2020-12-07] [paraphrase] "Riches in the niches." Cites Kevin Kelly's "long tail": real profit selling low volume to small markets. Even one-in-a-million appeal = ~7,000 people — the "smallest viable audience" (SVA, borrowing Godin's framing). Quotes James Victore: "the things that make you weird as a kid will make you great tomorrow."
- [2020-12-07] [paraphrase] Engagement rules: listen before you speak, learn your audience's language, share the person behind the brand, have a dialogue (stick around after posting), and create quick wins. "Helping one person might not change the whole world, but it could change the whole world for one person."
- [2020-12-07] [paraphrase] Short game = hunting (chasing clients, feast/famine, outbound, "eat what you kill"); long game = farming (build audience/fans, slow patient work, inbound/content marketing, compound effect, "eat what you grow").
- [2020-12-07] [paraphrase] Adopt a mindset of generosity — "a deposit in the karma bank." If you keep your secrets, "you'll be the best kept secret that no one hires," because people hire those they know, like, and trust. Cites Seth Godin (This Is Marketing): marketing is the generous act of helping others achieve their goals.
- [2020-12-07] [paraphrase] Cites Robert Cialdini (Influence): reciprocity is a deep-seated need to balance the scale. Candy-store study: free sample → customers 42% more likely to purchase.
- [2020-12-07] [paraphrase] The Futur stats cited: 800+ videos, 49.9M views, 7.6M hours watched, 900k+ subscribers. Fans reciprocate by giving emails freely, joining the "1 Billion Minus One" mission (teach a billion people to make a living doing what they love), sending $5/month, even Venmo-ing thanks.
- [2020-12-07] [paraphrase] Blueprint origin: a 2016 SlideShare typography manual (10 rules) got 107,000+ views. A dream of launching a $1,400 / 7-week typography class earning $42,000 in 24 hours "was a dream" — reality: only 51 people showed up. Lesson: everyone starts small.
- [2020-12-07] [paraphrase] Fix = make content out of content that already works: repurposed the SlideShare manual into a video that got 1.5M+ views, then built supporting content (critiques, process videos, keyboard shortcuts, mock posters, a Josef Müller-Brockmann grid breakdown) to build authority.
- [2020-12-07] [paraphrase] Course launch sequence: Sept 2017 pre-launch at $69 → $25,000; a month later full course at $99 → $173,000 (six figures). To date the course has earned $500,000+, including a single month at $28,000 — the meaning of "passive income": build once, keep bearing fruit.
- [2020-12-07] [paraphrase] Blueprint summary: build audience first, pre-launch to gauge interest, adjust if feedback is off, price very attractively then raise over time (rewarding early adopters), and (for The Futur) give 24-hour free access at launch.
- [2020-12-07] [paraphrase] Ari Chung case (children's book author/illustrator): a cancelled COVID workshop → free Facebook class, 3,000 signups → $47 3-week camp = $9,000 → rinse/repeat free-webinar-to-list model → $42,000 → contest-driven launch, $140 5-week course, 4,700 signups = $730,000 (50% sold in first 36 hours).
- [2020-12-07] [paraphrase] Ari's anti-webinar model: instead of a 90-min high-pressure webinar with fake scarcity, he ran a 5-day / 1-hour-per-day webinar with the rule "give before you ask." First two days pure teaching, no selling; the class was only revealed on day three; used early-bird discount and *real* scarcity (enrollment closing because live class starts). Also cites Kyle MacDonald's TED talk (red paperclip → house) as "bigger and better."
