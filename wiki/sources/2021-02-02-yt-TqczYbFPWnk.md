# What Is Brand Strategy And How To Do It (Step 1)

## Summary
Step 1 of a Brand Strategy teaching series. Chris Do defines strategy and then *demonstrates* rather than explains it, running a live strategic discovery conversation with a pro-group member ("Stanley" / addressed once as "Michael") who struggles to get clients. Chris asks a scripted series of questions to uncover the real problem, reframes the member's "failing" campaign as actually over-performing, and lands on a dollar value for the engagement so he knows what to bid. The clip closes with a Futur team voiceover confirming it was cut from a weekly pro-group call "that Chris did." This is genuinely Chris-taught material and trains the persona.

**Speaker:** Chris Do solo (with a member interlocutor). The closing narration is a non-Chris Futur team member promoting the pro group — that portion is context, not persona data.

## Key claims
- [paraphrase] Strategy is figuring out what the goal is, then figuring out how to get to that goal (2021-02-02). Goals can be increasing market presence, conversion, awareness, etc.
- [paraphrase] When a client says "we just want a website," dig deeper — there is always a reason behind what people say they want. If you can't surface it, it's a combination of the client not knowing what they want AND you not knowing how to ask the questions that help them (2021-02-02).
- [paraphrase] You don't explain strategy — you show it. The whole discovery is a series of questions to uncover the real problem, done *with* the client, not *for* them.
- [paraphrase] Chris reveals the method is not improvised: he literally read Daniel Montgomery's "seven essential questions" (posted in the pro group), derived from Michael Bungay Stanier's book *The Coaching Habit* ("say less, ask more, and change the way you lead forever"). Only the final part — "what does the business represent to you?" — was Chris's own layer for assigning a dollar value.
- [paraphrase] The funnel principle: if the top of the funnel isn't wide, there's very low chance of conversion at the bottom. Numbers game — 2 replies from 14 signups is actually a *good* response rate (~14%); at 100 signups you'd expect only 3-4 replies (2021-02-02).
- [paraphrase] To net 5 paid clients you need ~200 signups (not the member's guess of 50); nurture those leads to ~8-10 who correspond, roughly half of whom convert to paid. Goal for an email list should be thousands, not tens (2021-02-02).
- [paraphrase] Value/pricing logic: establish what one client is worth (member: ~$1,000; noted as lower due to Polish currency) → 5 clients = $5,000 → ask how much it makes sense to spend against that. The member said he'd spend up to $5,000 to get $5,000 in clients *if* results were assured; Chris uses this to size a real bid (~$3,000).
- [paraphrase] On guarantees (citing Jonathan Stark): charge on results, not time — willing to work on something for two years if necessary. You can't guarantee results for $3,000, but you could for $300k or $3 million; guaranteed outcomes require charging a lot more money (2021-02-02).
- [paraphrase] Scope boundary: "I was just brought in to help you fix your funnel." Getting qualified leads is the deliverable; if the member can't close them or the value prop is weak, that's the member's problem, not the strategist's.
- [paraphrase] The diagnostic work (auditing the campaign, offer, messaging, boost method, where conversions happen) is not free — "I'm not going to do that for free."
