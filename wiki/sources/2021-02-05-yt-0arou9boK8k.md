# 9 Tips For Content Creation

## Summary
Chris Do solo monologue distilling nine lessons on mindset, creativity, and the
practice of content creation, framed as what he learned making 800+ videos and
reaching one million YouTube subscribers. The list is delivered once as a walkthrough
and then repeated "rapid fire" at the end. The video closes with an unrelated podcast
promo read by **Greg Gunn** (producer of The Futur podcast) — context only, not Chris,
not persona training data.

**Speaker:** Chris Do solo (persona-eligible). Outro promo (lines 299–323) is Greg Gunn — context, do-not-train.

## Key claims
Dated 2021-02-05, paraphrased unless quoted.

1. [paraphrase] Put in the work — show up every single day, do the best you can with what you have and the time you have, and be patient; treat it as an "infinite game" of continuous self-improvement. The only metrics to measure yourself by early on are whether you're having fun and whether you're learning; stay out of the numbers for a while.
2. [paraphrase] The longer you play the game, the better you get. He frames it as "persist and resist" (a phrase credited to Ryan Holiday's *The Obstacle Is the Way*): persist = don't give up; resist = resist distractions, which show up as negative feedback, low views/likes, or low monetization.
3. [paraphrase] Ignore those who don't care about your success; "no opinion can hurt you without your permission." Don't hand a stranger the power to control your emotional state — whether their feedback is praise or criticism.
4. [paraphrase] Try new things and make mistakes. We're conditioned by education, parenting, and culture to avoid failure (seek pleasure, avoid pain), but we need to fail more. Reframe failure as "opportunity in disguise" and "the tuition you pay for success," with detailed instructions on how to succeed — so "don't waste a good failure."
5. [paraphrase] Done is better than perfect. Perfectionism (ingrained in design/creative training) is the enemy of execution and a form of procrastination; it prevents you from showing up daily. Small iterative steps are more likely to lead to real breakthroughs.
6. [paraphrase] Be generous — help others. He calls this the "secret to success"; it feels good and pays back. Example: The Futur's video with Petrula Vrontikis (caption garbles the name as "petrula run tigers") on interviewing helped a discouraged Instagram follower land a job in under 30 days.
7. [paraphrase] We are poor judges of our own value and talent; only by creating, expressing, and releasing ideas into the wild do we discover what we know. Aim to make a difference in one person's life; try seeing yourself through the eyes of someone who admires you.
8. [paraphrase] Plans are overrated. Cites Mike Tyson's "everyone has a plan until they get punched in the mouth." Plans blind you to unforeseen opportunities and trap people in "paralysis by analysis"; instead, move in a direction and adjust as you go.
9. [paraphrase] "What makes you weird makes you wonderful." Credits James Victore's book *Feck Perfuction* (caption garbles title as "perfection" and name as "james victoria"): what made you weird as a kid makes you great today. Being different is a competitive advantage; since ideas are largely recycled, what people tune in for is your personality and unique voice.
