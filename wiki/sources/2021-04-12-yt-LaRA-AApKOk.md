# Choose A Focus To Build Expertise

## Summary

A ~30-second excerpt in which Chris Do argues that the reason most creatives never build
deep expertise is not a lack of ability but an unwillingness to make two hard choices:
choosing a focus, and then doing the undesirable work required to earn that focus. He
reframes his own "make the claim first" positioning advice as a psychological engine —
put out a brave, slightly aspirational claim, then let pride force you to run and acquire
whatever is missing to back it up.

**Speaker:** Chris Do (solo monologue; no other speakers audible in this clip). Likely an
excerpt from a longer talk — see the companion Clubhouse recording below.

## Key claims

- (2021-04-12) Most creatives who fail to build deeper expertise do so because they will
  "live with the undesirable circumstances rather than do the undesirable work" — the block
  is willingness, not talent. *(paraphrase)*
- (2021-04-12) Building deep expertise requires two sequential hard moves: first the
  **business decision of choosing a focus**, and then **doing the work afterward** to build
  the expertise behind it. Most people won't do either. *(paraphrase)*
- (2021-04-12) Once you actually choose a focus, the work becomes *easier* — if you have any
  pride, you're motivated to live up to the focus you claimed. *(paraphrase)*
- (2021-04-12) His method: put out a "brave claim" that is "a little bit aspirational,"
  then, driven by a "proud streak," "run like hell" to acquire what's missing to support
  that claim. Claim first, build the proof after. *(paraphrase)*
  - This is the same **claim-first positioning move** he articulated in 2015 ("make the
    claim first and start to build over time, expertise that supports your claim"), here
    recast in 2021 as a self-discipline mechanism rather than a marketing tactic. Not a
    contradiction — a consistent, elaborated position.
