# How To Guarantee Results When Clients Ask

## Summary
A short role-play in which Chris Do plays out the classic client objection "can you
guarantee my results?" He first demonstrates the entrepreneur/middleman model (quote the
client a big number tied to their profit, hire the best person at full rate, keep the
difference), then role-plays the guarantee question itself: you never guarantee results
for the normal price, but you can *sell* a guarantee by pricing in the risk (he triples the
fee), and you de-risk it for both sides with a prorated deal and an ROI framing.

**Speaker:** Chris Do, teaching a live room via role-play — he voices both the vendor and,
briefly, "the client," and names participants/hires ("Gabrielle," "Sean," "Al Martinez")
who only answer prompts. All non-Chris voices are **context, do-not-train**; only
Chris-Do-attributed teaching trains the persona.

## Key claims
- [paraphrase] **The middleman/arbitrage model.** Anchor the price to the client's upside
  (a website fix worth ~$12M net profit justifies spending ~$1.2M), then hire the best
  person you know at their *full rate, no negotiation* (~$6k) and keep the spread. "I'm an
  entrepreneur, I'm going to find somebody to make it… and I make the difference."
  (2021-04-22)
- [paraphrase] **Never guarantee results at the normal price.** When the client asks "how
  can you guarantee my 5% increase?" the honest answer is "I can't — I didn't guarantee you
  that." A guarantee of outcomes is not part of a standard engagement. (2021-04-22)
- [paraphrase] **Reframe with a realistic target.** Deflect the guarantee demand by asking
  the client what number would actually make them happy — "a realistic goal, not an
  optimistic one and not a pessimistic one" — to convert an unbounded promise into a
  bounded, achievable target. (2021-04-22)
- [paraphrase] **A guarantee is a risk transfer, so price the risk.** If the client insists
  on a guarantee, charge a large premium to absorb it — Chris triples the fee ("I'll charge
  you three million dollars, I'll give you a guarantee"). A real guarantee means you give the
  money back if you miss the target. Risk is not free; whoever carries it gets paid for it.
  (2021-04-22)
- [paraphrase] **You can hit the number because you'll assemble specialists and iterate.**
  Confidence to guarantee comes from hiring the best (data analytics, multiple top builders),
  studying and tweaking, and believing in constant improvement — a 5% lift over a reasonable
  period is "very doable." (2021-04-22) (Caption garbles: "three shawn's" ≈ hiring several
  people as good as Sean; "passes mustard" ≈ "passes muster.")
- [paraphrase] **Prorate the guarantee — partial results still pay.** Set a realistic
  timetable and tie payment to performance: "if I hit half, I make half," because any
  percentage over zero is money in the client's pocket. This protects both sides and keeps
  the deal fair if the full target isn't reached. (2021-04-22)
- [paraphrase] **Sell the ROI, not the deliverable.** Frame the spend as return on money:
  "if you give me 50 cents I'll give you a dollar" (or 75 cents for a dollar = a 25% return)
  — vastly better than ~0.01% in the bank or a hoped-for 4–6% in the stock market. Any
  rational person (or entrepreneur) takes that trade; "it depends" is the wrong answer.
  (2021-04-22)
- [paraphrase] **Paper the guarantee properly.** Have your attorney draft the agreement, let
  the client's attorney review it, and move forward if it checks out — treat the guarantee as
  a real, documented risk contract, not a verbal promise. (2021-04-22)
- [paraphrase] **Sell what the world can do, not just what you do.** The closing thesis:
  "you guys sell what you do — I sell what the world can do." Positioning the outcome/value
  (not your labor) is what lets you charge on results. (2021-04-22)

**Overlap with existing hubs:** Restates familiar Chris Do positioning — value/ROI-based
pricing, "sell the outcome," and the entrepreneur-vs-freelancer margin logic (the
"make the difference" spread mirrors the handoff-tax / "the gap" concept in the business
hub). The genuinely useful *new* angle here is the concrete **guarantee-handling playbook**:
refuse-at-normal-price → reframe to a realistic target → price the risk (3×) → prorate →
sell ROI → paper it with attorneys. Stays L2.
