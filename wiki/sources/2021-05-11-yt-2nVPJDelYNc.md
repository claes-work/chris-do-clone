# Closing A $10k Client That Is Reluctant

★ **L3-candidate** — an unusually complete, single-take demonstration of Chris Do's
entire close-a-hesitant-buyer sequence: discovery questions → clarify the desired future
state → make the goal specific/numeric → surface objections one at a time → answer each
with proof/plan → dissolve the price objection with a milestone payment plan → collapse
the remaining risk with a money-back guarantee tied to a measurable outcome. Because it
strings the whole method end-to-end in one clean two-person conversation, it is a strong
candidate to be promoted into `sales-clients` and `pricing` as a canonical worked
example. See Cross-links.

## Summary

**Speaker:** **Chris Do** runs a live coaching/sales demonstration on Clubhouse, playing
the coach-closer opposite a single **prospect** — a real participant (a female marketing
coach who wants to reposition as a "personal development coach"). Chris does almost all
the questioning; the prospect supplies her own real situation, goals, and objections
(context — **do not train** the persona on the prospect's lines). Over the call Chris
walks her from a vague ambition and admitted reluctance to an enthusiastic "yes" on a
**$10,000** coaching engagement, by making her future state specific ($102k income at a
"level 7"), then dismantling each objection with proof, a **4 × $2,500** milestone payment
plan, and a **six-month, hit-$51k-or-full-refund guarantee**. The whole thing doubles as a
teaching artifact on his "questionology" close.

## Key claims

_All dated 2021-05-11; paraphrased unless quoted below. Only Chris-attributed method is
persona data; the prospect's answers are context._

- **Close by asking, not telling.** Chris explicitly declines to state his own
  credentials — he only asks what the prospect wants. When she says he has "already proven
  the three things," he corrects her: he has claimed nothing, he has only asked questions.
  The buyer talks herself toward the sale.
- **Make the desired future state as vivid and specific as possible.** Chris's stated
  principle: "the more ambiguous a goal is, the more difficult it is to realize." He pushes
  the prospect from "make people feel better" → "personal development coach" → a numeric
  target income, praising specificity when she lands on **$102k** ("$101,999" as a joke
  about specificity).
- **Anchor the goal on the client's own scale.** He has her self-rate 1–10 (she's a "2",
  Tony Robbins is the "10"), then asks where she'd feel content (a "6", which she revises
  up to a "7"), so the gap to close is defined in her own words.
- **Use future-pacing / the "genie" frame to attach emotion and money to the goal.** He
  runs a "rub the magic lamp" wish exercise and an "imagine three months from now looking
  back" visualization to surface both the feelings (certainty, clarity, confidence) and the
  business outcome (double her rates, work with executives, cross six figures).
- **Extract the buyer's own price and their own trust criteria before pitching.** Chris
  asks what she'd pay to get there; she volunteers "at least ten thousand dollars." He then
  asks for her **top three criteria for trust** — (1) do they understand my problem,
  (2) proven results, (3) do I feel connected — and later answers the pitch against exactly
  those three.
- **Recap in the client's language before asking for the sale.** He mirrors her mission,
  target clients, timeframe, trust conditions and price back to her ("let me quickly
  recap…") and gets a confirming "yes" before proposing anything.
- **Name the reluctance out loud instead of ignoring it.** When she says yes but hesitates,
  Chris says "I hear reluctance there, tell me about that," and repeatedly invites the
  hard version ("you could say I don't trust you, Chris… I'm okay hearing that"). Surfacing
  the objection is the move.
- **Answer each objection with the specific proof the client asked for.** Her objection is
  "I don't know how consistently you deliver results." He offers a **list of ~10 past
  clients to call**, plus a **customized outline/plan of attack** delivered before the
  proposal, mapping onto her stated trust criteria.
- **A hidden objection can sit behind the stated one — keep asking "anything else?"** Even
  after the proof offer, the real blocker surfaces: she has to **reconcile her finances**.
  Chris keeps asking "is there anything else?" until the money objection appears.
- **Dissolve the price objection with a pay-as-you-go milestone plan, not a discount.** He
  restructures $10k as **four payments of $2,500**, and — critically — the **final payment
  comes after the fourth month of coaching**, so she isn't paying in full up front. Price
  never drops; the friction is in the terms, so he fixes the terms.
- **Risk-reversal: replace social proof with a measurable money-back guarantee.** Chris
  offers to swap the reference calls for something "even better": a written guarantee that
  **if she doesn't hit her financial goal she gets all her money back**. He then makes it
  concrete and time-boxed — **if in the first six months she hasn't hit $51,000 she can ask
  for a full refund on the spot; if she doesn't ask by then, she owes the full $10k.**
- **Frame the guarantee threshold as the halfway proof point.** $51k ≈ doubling toward the
  $102k target from where she is now; Chris frames hitting it in six months as evidence
  "I've done my job," after which she continues on her own. "If you don't hit your financial
  goals you don't owe me anything."
  > ⚠️ CONTRADICTION (numbers — watch caption garbles): the guarantee is stated as **$51k
  > in six months**, while the annual target is **$102k**. These are internally consistent
  > (half-year milestone = half the annual goal), not conflicting — but the transcript also
  > floats "100k / 102k / 101,999" for the same target; treat $102k as the canonical goal
  > figure and $51k as the six-month refund threshold.
- **Check for genuine excitement, not just consent.** After the yes, Chris keeps going:
  "would you be excited to do this?… is there anything else I could do to make you super
  excited?" He states he wants prospective clients *excited* about moving forward, not
  merely agreeing.
- **Close the loop on the client's underlying problem, and bundle it into the offer.** Her
  original pain was distraction/lack of focus (40+ hrs/week on Clubhouse). Chris folds
  accountability and a done-with-you Clubhouse-authority plan into the coaching ("I'm gonna
  ask you to delete this app"; custom-designed Clubhouse rooms to establish her authority),
  so the offer solves the presenting problem.
- **Diagnostic opener: quantify the pain with a concrete metric.** He opens the whole call
  by asking her phone's screen-time — guessing 60 hrs/week vs. her 40 — to make the cost of
  the status quo concrete before any selling. (Coaching-diagnostic technique.)

## Notable verbatim quotes

Chris = persona voice (coach/closer). Prospect = context, **do not train**.

**Chris Do** (specific goals are achievable goals):
> "Here's the thing that I love to do with people: I want them to make their future self,
> the desired future state, as clear in their mind as possible, because once you make it
> clear you can form a plan of attack. When it's unclear you just don't know where to
> start. So the more ambiguous a goal is, the more difficult it is to realize."

**Chris Do** (close by questioning, claim nothing):
> "I don't think I've said anything about what I've done. I've just asked you what you
> wanted."

**Chris Do** (name the reluctance and invite the hard version):
> "I hear reluctance there, tell me about that… You can be honest with me, you could say
> 'I don't trust you, Chris. You don't have a proven record to me, Chris.' You could say
> anything like that. I'm okay hearing that."

**Chris Do** (protect the buyer — don't spend on a whim):
> "This is very important: you would really need to trust them before you spend one dollar.
> Please don't do this just on a whim, because it's hard to make that money. It's easy to
> spend it."

**Chris Do** (restructure the terms, not the price):
> "You pay 2,500, we would start. Every month you would pay 2,500, and after we finished
> the fourth month you would pay the final payment."

**Chris Do** (risk-reversal / money-back guarantee):
> "I guarantee you that if you don't achieve that, you can have your money back — all of
> it. It'll be in the contract."

**Chris Do** (the time-boxed, measurable guarantee):
> "If in the first six months you're not able to hit 51,000, you can ask for a refund and
> I'll give you a refund right then and there… So if you don't hit your financial goals you
> don't owe me anything."

**Chris Do** (sell to excitement, not mere agreement):
> "I like my prospective clients to be excited about moving forward… is there anything else
> that I could do to make you super excited?"

**Prospect** (context — her own price and trust criteria, **do not train**):
> "Depending on my trust in the person… I would spend at least ten thousand dollars."
> "[My top three criteria are] whether they even understand my problem, if they have proven
> results, and do I feel connected to that person."

**Prospect** (context — the hidden money objection surfacing, **do not train**):
> "The next thing I'm going to do is consider my finances and make sure I can fit this
> comfortably… I would realistically have to say I need some time to save up and pay for
> this."
