# Three Story Telling Tips (Story Formula)

## Summary

A short instructional clip in which Chris Do gives a compact formula for creating shareable content: build an informed opinion first, then tell a **personal story that elicits an emotional reaction with one clear takeaway**. He frames content creation as low-friction, regular practice on whatever platform suits you, and points to his own multi-platform output as a publicly reverse-engineerable blueprint. **Speaker:** the teaching (lines 1–127) is **Chris Do** (first person "how I do what I do," references *The Business of Expertise* and his own cross-platform content strategy). The closing 25 seconds (lines 127–150) are a **podcast promo read by Greg Gunn** (producer of The Futur podcast) — clearly marked below as context / do-not-train.

## Key claims

- [2021-05-13] Before creating content, form an **informed opinion** — know what the other options/positions are before declaring "this is the truth"; otherwise you just add to misinformation. Distinguish "your truth" from "the truth." (paraphrase)
- [2021-05-13] Do the reps off-camera first: your first time articulating an idea should **not** be on camera — sit down, build an outline/structure, find supporting information and quotes ("meat on the bones"), then publish so you sound more informed. (paraphrase)
- [2021-05-13] Cites an exercise from David C. Baker's book *The Business of Expertise* — "drop and give me 20": list the **20 things you know that are valuable enough that someone would pay you** to know them. (paraphrase; attributed to David Baker's book, surfaced by Chris)
- [2021-05-13] Pick the platform with the **least friction** for you to create on regularly (Twitter, Reddit, LinkedIn, Facebook) — remove excuses/pain and you're more likely to create consistently; over time you'll see the pattern of what people respond to. (paraphrase)
- [2021-05-13] **The story formula (the three tips / "golden formula"):** (1) tell a **personal** story, (2) that **elicits an emotional reaction**, (3) with **one clear takeaway**. Hit all three and you have the golden formula. (paraphrase — the core teachable)
  - Tip 1 — **Personal**: it must be *your* experienced story ("how did this impact you"); a story others have already shared ("I get that") won't land the same way. (paraphrase)
  - Tip 2 — **Emotion**: stories that make people *feel* something (despair, courage, fear, love, honor, duty) are more likely to be shared and talked about. (paraphrase)
  - Tip 3 — **One clear point**: many people tell stories with no point — solve it by **starting with the point**. Write one sentence stating the single thing you want the audience to know, then edit the story so every detail that doesn't add up to that point is removed. (paraphrase)
- [2021-05-13] A tightly edited, point-driven story connects emotionally, delivers value, and produces results "almost immediately." (paraphrase)
- [2021-05-13] Invokes the Jim Rohn line "success leaves clues" — his own content is on public display, so you can line up his Instagram/YouTube/LinkedIn/Twitter posts **chronologically** (align dates within a week) and **reverse-engineer** his content strategy; he says he's "not hiding anything." (paraphrase; quote attributed to Jim Rohn)

## Notable verbatim quotes

Chris Do (teaching):

> "Tell a personal story that elicits an emotional reaction with one clear takeaway — if you can hit these three things, this is the golden formula."

> "Have an informed opinion, because there are too many people who are throwing around uninformed opinions... Well, that's a truth, that's your truth, but it's not necessarily the truth."

> "Your first time articulating an idea should not be on camera. Really just sit down and build structure, build an outline to what it is that you want to say, find supporting information, go to research, find some quotes — and then now you've got some meat on the bones."

> "I recommend finding the space and the place that is the least friction for you to create content on a regular basis. If we remove the excuses, if we remove the pain of creating content, then we're more likely to do it."

> "You could tell thousands of stories, but if there's not this, you're missing a point... start with a point — write one sentence: what's the one thing I want you to know from this? And then when you tell the story, edit the story so all the extra details that don't add up to the point are taken away."

> "Success leaves clues [Jim Rohn]... you're literally just going to look at it chronologically and jump from platform to platform, align the dates within a week, and you'll see how my content strategy works. I'm not hiding anything."

Greg Gunn (podcast promo — **context only, do-not-train**):
