# Masterclass In Talking About Budget (w/ Blair Enns)

## Summary

**Speakers:** Chris Do (co-host, framing/interjecting) + **Blair Enns** (context — the primary teacher; author of *Win Without Pitching* and *Pricing Creativity*, NOT the subject, do-not-train) + live participants (Danny, "Kasaya", Jim Zazla — names caption-uncertain).

This is Day 9 of a 12-part live series walking through Blair Enns's *Win Without Pitching Manifesto*; the ninth proclamation is "we will address issues of money early." The bulk of the teaching is Blair's — his Minimum Level of Engagement (MLE) concept, the "answer the phone / hang up if you don't have $50k" model, the 10%-of-fee-target math, walking away, anchoring, and putting compensation at risk. Chris frames the session, reinforces that talking money early is non-negotiable, and encourages designers that it is legitimate to declare who they will and won't do business with financially.

## Key claims (2021-06-01, paraphrased unless quoted)

**Chris Do (trainable — the subject):**
- The ninth proclamation is "address issues of money early." We over-invest in the buying cycle only to discover the client can't afford us — every person on the call has lived this. We avoid the money talk because we're afraid of the tension it creates. *(Chris framing, ~L11–30)*
- You cannot "forget" to talk about money in onboarding — it has to be one of the primary objectives of an early call, not an afterthought. *(Chris, ~L36–40)*
- There is nothing wrong with telling prospects you're not for everybody: firms already say "we only work with certain types of clients / certain types of work," so why not "certain types of financial engagements / above a certain minimum level" too? *(Chris, ~L403–412)*
- You reinvent your firm one new client at a time — each new opportunity is "a new you": new attitude, new behavior, new pricing, new tactics. Figure out your MLE and state it. *(Chris riffing off Blair's line, ~L438–450)*
- Meta on charging: he reads a viewer objection ("you can't charge whatever you want without a proven ROI track record") and reframes it as fill-in-the-blank, teeing Blair up. *(Chris, ~L744–763)*

**Blair Enns (context / do-not-train — influence, WWPM & Pricing Creativity author):**
- Uncover objections early; money is the biggest objection. If there's a financial reason not to work together, find it out as soon as possible (no sunk-cost thinking). *(Blair, ~L51–60)*
- "Minimum Level of Engagement" (MLE) — declared early as a hurdle for the client to clear; both sides can walk away before wasting time. Model line: "answer the phone — ABC Agency — if you don't have $50,000, hang up." Facetious, but the point is it's very hard to talk money *too* early. *(Blair, ~L42–81)*
- The "it's impolite to talk about money in my region/culture" objection is almost always a personal issue, not a real cultural rule; the principle holds everywhere even if rapport/ceremony precedes it. Don't confuse what your parents taught you about money in *personal* life with business. In business, an inability to talk money at the right time reads as poor business acumen. *(Blair, ~L82–179)*
- MLE math: starting point for the minimum is **10% of your annual fee target**, because a healthy firm has ~8–15 (call it 10) clients, each averaging ~1/10 of revenue. $200k target → ~$20k MLE; want a $400k firm → raise MLE to $40k. *(Blair, ~L290–425)*
- You don't actually have to *hold* the client to the MLE — it's a guideline presented as a hurdle. State it, then "embrace silence, count to ten, admire your cuticles" and let the client speak next. *(Blair, ~L310–353)*
- If the client's project budget is below your MLE, keep fishing: distinguish one-off projects from a series of projects / relationship; ask if more work follows. You can proceed project-by-project while making clear you're "in the business of a series of projects." *(Blair, ~L354–394)*
- The "five constraints" / Blairtopia thought experiment: cap yourself at 10 (8–15) clients. A hard constraint first feels impossible, then survivable-with-pain, then becomes the *source* of the breakthrough — it forces the MLE conversation and forces you to drop your least-profitable client to add a better one. *(Blair, ~L454–509)*
- Your only real power is to withhold your expertise; if you can't walk away, you relinquish whatever power you had. Walking away isn't bluffing — it's genuinely being the professional you want to be. Clients can smell neediness (repulsive) and can smell discernment (attractive). *(Blair, ~L510–553)*
- "No" is not the end, it's the beginning of what happens next — everybody's buying; the only variable is time. Every "no," objection, or hurdle you place, you also reserve the right to remove — so you can drop the MLE and take the small project if you decide you want it (trial close: "if I waive the minimum, can we agree today?"). *(Blair, ~L529–743)*
- Ignore external conditions (bad economy, pandemic) as excuses to abandon a steely backbone around money. *(Blair, ~L694–743)*
- Anchoring effect: the first number skews the final decision — always start with the highest price / high end of the range. Story: a solo-consultant friend anchored at $30M, the client's stated budget was $30k, and he closed a $300k solution (10× the budget). "You have no idea what you can charge — it's an infinite universe" (bounded only by value). *(Blair, ~L764–806)*
- To charge far more than your experience justifies, put compensation at risk — performance/value-based pricing (small base + incentives on outcomes). Channeling Drucker: "all profit is derived from risk." *(Blair, ~L807–853)*
- Leading with budget/MLE reading as "they only care about money" is a rookie mistake rooted in your own sense of worth. Two buyer types: price buyers (want cheapest) vs. value buyers. Exuding confidence over money = exuding confidence over results; the best clients pay a lot for results, the worst pay by the hour for the task. *(Blair, ~L871–926)*

> ⚠️ CONTRADICTION (nuance, same session): The MLE is stated as a firm hurdle ("if you don't have $50k, hang up") yet Blair also says you needn't hold the client to it and can waive it to take a $1k project. Resolution per Blair: the MLE is a *guideline presented as a hurdle* — declared to filter and to anchor, but revocable at your discretion.
