# Get Qualified Leads, Bigger Projects, With No Ads

## Summary

**Speaker:** Guest-led episode. The presenter is **Katie Sandoval** (a.k.a. "Katie Sandell/Sandal" in the caption garble), CEO/creative director of Aventive Studio and business educator at Aventive Academy — **NOT** Chris Do. Chris Do is the **host/interviewer**; he introduces her, watches the presentation, and asks follow-up questions. Katie teaches a six-step formula for landing "dream projects" (bigger, better-fit clients) through hyper-targeted, SEO-optimized blog posts on your own site — no ads, no big audience, no writing expertise required. The core mechanic: pick the exact next project you want, get inside the ideal client's head, and write one blog post targeting the phrase *they* actually Google (e.g. "tea packaging design"), then let Google surface it over ~6–12 months.

> ⚠️ ATTRIBUTION: The formula and nearly all substantive claims are **Katie Sandoval's**, delivered as a guest. This is **context / do-not-train** material for the persona. Only the Chris-Do-attributed lines flagged below may inform the persona.

## Key claims (2021-06-22)

**Katie Sandoval (guest — context only, do NOT train persona):**
- (paraphrase) Chasing every possible client — logos, brochures, flyers, websites for anyone — kept her broke, overworked, and competing on price against every designer online; the fix was niching down (she chose health & wellness).
- (paraphrase) The formula needs only your own website with a blog; you don't need to be a good writer, an SEO expert, or have a big following.
- (paraphrase) Six steps: (1) imagine the exact next project/client you want; (2) research the industry, client persona, and SEO keywords; (3) write a hyper-targeted blog post; (4) tweak structure (H2 headings, alt text, meta description) for SEO; (5) publish with essentially zero promotion; (6) repeat for each new dream project.
- (paraphrase) Get inside the client's head: they Google what matters to *them* ("skincare packaging," "tea packaging design"), not the designer's internal vocabulary ("wellness branding," "brand strategy"). Meet them where they are.
- (paraphrase) Use Google's "People also ask" / "People also search" strings as blog subheadings; use Google Keyword Planner for search volume; target one primary keyword + 3–4 secondary keywords; top-ranking posts often run 2,000–3,000 words.
- (paraphrase) Provide value beyond your own services (e.g. including social-media tips even though her agency doesn't do social) to position as an expert.
- (paraphrase) Results are slow then compound: her skincare post got ~0–2 page views/week for the first six months, then rose to 50–100/week (~95 unique visitors) once Google understood it — with no changes and no promotion.
- (paraphrase) Better leads, not just more: recently fielded a $40k/month-budget client and an international skincare rebrand inquiry; the strategy took her from charging hundreds (e.g. for a logo) to $15k–$30k projects, and from solo freelancer to running a studio with a content developer plus freelance designers/developers.
- (paraphrase) A sports-drink-packaging post landed a meal-replacement-shake client — "not 100% what we thought we wanted," but an even better project.

**Chris Do (host — persona-eligible):**
- (paraphrase, 2021-06-22) The number-one challenge creatives have is getting discovered, and they don't want to do outbound sales; this blog/SEO approach is a very effective **inbound** strategy.
- (paraphrase, 2021-06-22) Don't expect miracle results right away — it can take six to twelve months to hit, but when it hits you attract the right leads/customers who are looking for you right now.
- (paraphrase, 2021-06-22) He references his own YouTube video on "specialize vs. generalize / niche vs. broad" — creatives can **specialize externally but stay broad internally**. (Cross-reference to an existing Chris Do framework.)
- (paraphrase, 2021-06-22) Someone with an existing social following can "put a little juice behind" a post by sharing it to jumpstart the SEO — helping "train the spiders" from Google to find it sooner; paid partnerships / creators sharing the blog are another accelerant.

> ⚠️ CONTRADICTION (mild, within-episode): Katie stresses "zero promotion" as what makes the formula powerful, while Chris probes whether boosting/sharing/ads could accelerate it. They reconcile: promotion is optional and can jumpstart SEO, but the organic post works without it.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "We went from charging hundreds of dollars to tens of thousands of dollars by implementing the strategy."
