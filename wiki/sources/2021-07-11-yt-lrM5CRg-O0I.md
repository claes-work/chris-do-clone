# Easy Way To Market Yourself and Why You Should Do It — Tell Your Story (Part 1)

★ L3-candidate — introduces a core Chris Do personal-branding framework ("tell your story" as the easiest on-ramp to content + the long/infinite game of building an audience). Strong feed for `wiki/topics/content-strategy` and `wiki/topics/branding`.

## Summary

Short (~3 min) solo talk by **Chris Do** on @thefutur. This is **Part 1** of a series; Part 2 (a later ledger item) promises "the secret formula for a great story" — stories that "win hearts and minds."

Chris answers a question raised by a member of his program ("Mahi") who worries that if she keeps telling personal stories instead of talking about brand strategy, people won't recognize her as a brand strategist. Chris reframes this: marketing yourself is a **long game / infinite game**, not a one-effort-equals-one-client transaction. The easiest, least time-taxing way to make content and find your voice is to **tell your own story** — sharing lessons learned in open, vulnerable, transparent ways. Doing so builds an organic audience over months, makes you easier to find than competitors, and makes people fall in love with you as a creative human before you ever sell them a service or product. He cites designer **Aaron Draplin** as a model (all he does is tell his personal story, and you get to know who he is). He offers a concrete prompt: think of every person who taught you a lesson and tell that story — they were "the mentor in your story."

He closes with a real example of the payoff: someone from **EO (Entrepreneurs' Organization)** found him via Instagram (searching something like "business influence") and invited him to build a paid 21-day influencer/social-influence program for EO members worldwide, opening at $10,000. He frames this as proof of the **"Johnny Appleseed"** approach — plant many seeds now, some bear fruit months or years later, but the work must be done today.

## Key claims

All dated **2021-07-11**, paraphrased, attributed to **Chris Do** unless noted.

- The secret to making content easier is to **tell your story** and embrace your own uniqueness. (Chris Do)
- Marketing yourself is a **long game / "infinite game"** — thinking "one effort = one client" pushes you into the game of advertising (only talking about your services/products/offerings), which won't build a community. (Chris Do)
- You do have to talk about your services "to a point," but that alone won't build a community. (Chris Do)
- **Aaron Draplin** is cited as one of the best branding people Chris knows, and "all he does is tell his personal story all the time," so audiences get to know who he is. (Chris Do, about Aaron Draplin)
- The goal over the next ~3–6 months is to **organically build an audience** that shows up for you as a person who tells their story in open, transparent, vulnerable ways — empowering and enriching others' lives. (Chris Do)
- It's about **transformation, not transaction**; that creates far more long-term value. (Chris Do)
- The long-term plan: make it **easier for people to find you than your competitor**, make people **fall in love with who you are** as a creative human, then hire you for the services/products you sell. (Chris Do)
- At the beginning stage the aim is simply to **find your voice** and get used to creating content in a way that isn't overly taxing on time — and the easiest thing is to tell your story. (Chris Do)
- Concrete content **prompt**: find parts of your life that are lessons learned and share them; specifically, think about every person who taught you a lesson and tell that story, because "they were the mentor in your story." (Chris Do)
- This is a **content game to develop marketing skills**; it's hard work, not an overnight scheme — and Chris wants it to be hard. He calls his method his "latest scheme." (Chris Do)
- **EO example**: EO (Entrepreneurs' Organization) requires members to make more than ~$1M/year, with the average member earning $5M+. A woman in charge of learning (with a master's/PhD in teaching, based in South America) found Chris via Instagram and invited him to teach an influencer/social-influence program for EO members worldwide. (Chris Do — self-reported anecdote)
- She proposed a **$10,000** start to create a **21-day program** with self-study plus ~3 lectures/meetings; she found him by searching something like "business influence" and he "popped up all over the place." (Chris Do — self-reported)
- The **"Johnny Appleseed"** principle: plant many seeds; some bear fruit, some wither; you collect the fruits months or years later, but the work must be done today. (Chris Do)
- Positioning yourself as someone who can teach something valuable in front of a global community of millionaires is powerful — normally people pay for that access. (Chris Do)
