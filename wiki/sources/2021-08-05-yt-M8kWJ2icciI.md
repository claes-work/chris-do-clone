## Key claims

All claims below are the trailer's marketing framing (2021-08-05), paraphrased; presenter unconfirmed, so none are attributed to Chris Do.

- Brand strategy is described as the high-level plan that sets the tone for everything and guides understanding of who you are, who you talk to, and why anyone should care. (2021-08-05)
- Brands are framed as being fundamentally about people — understanding audience needs, wants, behavior, and motivation. (2021-08-05)
- Learning brand strategy is positioned as a way to "get paid to think and solve business problems" and bring more value to clients. (2021-08-05)
- The advertised course covers: purpose, brand personality, target audience, positioning, and moving a brand from where it is today to where it's going. (2021-08-05)
- Everything is said to start with **facilitation** — because brand strategy is a collaboration *with* the client, not work done *for* them (working together, not on their behalf). (2021-08-05)
- Skills promised: leading meetings, asking good questions, digging deeper to understand problems and identify gaps. (2021-08-05)
- Course extras mentioned: a workbook with exercises/prompts, and an online student community. (2021-08-05)
