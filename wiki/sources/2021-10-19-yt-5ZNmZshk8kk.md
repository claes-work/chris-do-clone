# Work Life Balance – 90 min Whiteboard Session

★ **L3-candidate** — This is Chris Do's fullest, most self-contained articulation of **work-life *integration*** (his preferred replacement for "work-life balance"): the MFP / PSS model and the "look for the overlap" method. It **directly reframes and partially contradicts** his own 2019 "compartmentalize / walled-off sections" teaching ([[wiki/sources/2019-11-25-yt-VN_0fy-jXYc]], §41 of the mindset hub). Worth promoting into `persona/beliefs.md` (dated) and `wiki/topics/mindset` as the canonical statement of the integration framework, with the contradiction reconciled and date-stamped. See ⚠️ callouts below.

## Summary

**Speaker:** Chris Do (whiteboard-session host; all persona-eligible material is his). A live-recorded 90-minute whiteboard session with a **Future Pro Group** audience whose members (Geo, Cashna, Michael, Paul, Ricky, Danae, Muhammad, Tom, Ian, Christian, Jonah, Arlie, Ivy, Jazz, Habib, Cameron and others) call out short answers — **context only, NOT training data**. Chris argues that "work-life balance" is a flawed, zero-sum, binary framing that forces tradeoffs, and proposes **work-life *integration*** instead: break work into **MFP (Money, Fulfillment, Purpose)** and life into **PSS (People, Self, Spirit)**, then deliberately look for **overlap** between the two — the bigger the overlap, the more joy. He works the model against his own life (25 years making commercials/music videos, 15 years teaching, then founding The Futur), gives worked examples of integration (bringing his sons on work trips, bartering sales/marketing for a fishing lodge into gifted trips for his father and uncles, the Pro Group content loop), and closes on take-action-then-reflect. Ricky, a departing Futur team member of ~3 years, is thanked at the end (context).

## Key claims

- [2021-10-19] Chris **dislikes the concept of "work-life balance"** — it is binary/zero-sum, treats work and life as opposing forces, and forces a compromise where taking time for one steals from the other (usually stolen from sleep/health). He prefers **work-life integration**. (paraphrase)
- [2021-10-19] The day-math proves balance is structurally near-impossible: 24h minus ~8h sleep, meals, ~1h commute, ~1h prep and ~8h work leaves only **~3 hours of free time** — not enough for relationships, self-care and chores. (paraphrase)
- [2021-10-19] He cites **Simon Sinek** as disliking the term "work-life balance" too, arguing the two must live in **harmony** and that the secret is the ability to **fluidly move between** work and life. *(His citation, not his origination.)* (paraphrase)
- [2021-10-19] **Work = MFP: Money, Fulfillment, Purpose.** Money is the primary, near-term reason to work; Fulfillment is personal growth/joy for *you*; Purpose is impact/service to *others*. All three must work "in unison and in harmony." (paraphrase — original framework)
- [2021-10-19] **The whole point of money is to buy options / freedom / time.** "If you make the money and you don't use it for what it's meant to do, which is to provide you options, then you've missed the entire point." Use money to buy help so you can spend time on what only you can do. (paraphrase → see quotes)
- [2021-10-19] **Life = PSS: People, Self, Spirit.** People = all relationships (family "does not have to be blood relation" — chosen family, friends, community, pets count); Self = body + mind (sleep, learning, rest — most people neglect it); Spirit = joie de vivre / faith / whatever fills you with joy. (paraphrase — original framework)
- [2021-10-19] **The integration method: look for the OVERLAP.** Take as many "ingredients" from work and blend them with as many from life; the more they overlap, the more joy — "As many of these things possible to overlap, that's the game. You win." Aim for the quadrant where money, people, purpose and joy all overlap. (paraphrase → see quotes)
- [2021-10-19] Do the **work you love first** as the precondition, then aspire to **work less while making more**, to maximize free time to do whatever you want. (paraphrase)
- [2021-10-19] **Early-career he did NOT integrate:** the first ~5 years of his business were all work — no vacation, heaviest weight of his life — and strained his marriage (his wife asked if they were "two roommates"). He felt it was necessary to establish the business, but the cost was clear. [self-reported, biographical] (paraphrase)
- [2021-10-19] Cites **Jim Rohn's "when you're at work, be at work"** (the man who leaves early but keeps thinking about unfinished paperwork, present nowhere) — but here he frames it as the *old balance model* he is moving **beyond** toward integration. *(His citation.)* (paraphrase)
- [2021-10-19] **Don't chase money first out of school** — prioritize fulfillment, purpose and skill-building, because "if you do more than what you get paid for, eventually you'll be paid more than what you do." A short-term money win with no growth is a long-term loss. (paraphrase; cites the maxim as not his own)
- [2021-10-19] **Blue-zone / Okinawa** has no concept of retirement; people work until they can't, and are among the longest-lived. People who retire and lose purpose/utility tend to die sooner. [self-reported, "not validated just yet"] (paraphrase)
- [2021-10-19] **Family integration examples:** bringing his sons on work trips (paying only a coach ticket, sharing his hotel room), one son doing part of his stage talk; doing sales/marketing for a Canadian fishing lodge paid partly in **trip credits** he gifted to his father and three uncles ("a trip of a lifetime"); the Pro Group generating content that becomes the marketing that recruits more members — a self-reinforcing loop. [self-reported] (paraphrase)
- [2021-10-19] **On boundaries (audience Q):** integration has "rough executions" (a work project with his son that backfired; a London trip his kids under-planned, causing a missed flight) — but "we shouldn't judge the idea based on the execution." He still endorses integration and being "fluid, not binary," while acknowledging some things are better kept separate. (paraphrase)
- [2021-10-19] He **began this integration journey at age 42** ("I'm almost 50"); sometimes you must live enough life first to have the skills and self-knowledge to even see the model. Younger people can now shorten the path with the roadmap. [self-reported] (paraphrase)
- [2021-10-19] **Take action, then reflect** — nothing changes without action; after acting, reflect on what to do better, then take more action, in a loop. (paraphrase; credits Aaron Garrison for framing input)

> ⚠️ CONTRADICTION (evolution / surface-level, resolvable): This 2021-10-19 session preaches **blurring the lines and merging work and life to maximize overlap** ("What if work could be more like life, and what if life could be more like work?"). Chris's 2019-11-25 Priscilla session ([[wiki/sources/2019-11-25-yt-VN_0fy-jXYc]]) preaches the opposite rhetoric — **compartmentalization into "walled-off sections"** (family days vs. work days; "you cannot pay me enough money to interrupt this") — and quotes the same Jim Rohn "when you're at work, be at work" line *approvingly*. Reconcilable: 2019 protects *presence* (be fully where you are), 2021 blends *domains* (bring the son to the shoot). But a persona answer should not present "wall off dedicated blocks" and "blur the lines together" in the same breath — date-stamp whichever is invoked, and note 2021 is the later, more developed position.

> ⚠️ Note (aligned, not contradictory): The 2021 "look for the overlap" method is the **same shape** as the 2018-08-24 five-prompt "superpower" inventory ("combine, don't segregate"; [[wiki/sources/2018-08-24-yt-VhztaSc4afE]], mindset-hub §28) and is explicitly framed as ikigai-adjacent (Chris references The Futur's "Ikigai one"). So 2018 and 2021 both preach *combine/overlap*; the 2019 *compartmentalize* framing is the outlier.

> "If you make the money and you don't use it for what it's meant to do, which is to provide you options, then you've missed the entire point."

> "This is why I personally don't love this concept of work-life balance. I would prefer something else… We're going to achieve work-life integration. That's the concept that I believe in."

> "What if work could be more like life, and what if life could be more like work?"

> "As many of these things possible to overlap, that's the game. You win."

> "I'm almost 50 years old. I didn't begin this journey until I was 42. Not because I'm a late bloomer cuz I'm dumb, but sometimes you have to live enough life to see different things… to be in a position where you can even see something like this."

> "Without action, without taking actual steps, nothing will happen. This will just be a lecture… You must take action for all things to work."
