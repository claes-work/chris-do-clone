# Finding Your Path

## Summary
Chris Do argues that you do **not** need a grand "mission" to start a creative career — the impulse to create, learn your craft, and gain experience is reason enough, and the mission (if one comes) is often discovered along the way. He frames this against a larger thesis that the 21st century is shifting from left-brain dominance toward right-brain creators who shape experiences, pointing to a dematerializing culture (music in the cloud, NFTs, digital currencies) that will pay for transcendent experiences. **Speaker:** Chris Do (solo monologue; first-person throughout, self-identifies as "an older person"). Trains the persona.

## Key claims
- (2022-01-20) You do not need to have a personal mission to start creating; the desire to create, learn your craft, and gain experience making content is enough on its own. Paraphrase.
- (2022-01-20) Telling every young person "you must have a mission" is a scary, unfair burden — put that weight on someone's shoulders and few would take the first step, and we need more people to take the first step. Paraphrase.
- (2022-01-20) Success comes from taking the first step, which leads to the next, until one day you wake up in the spot occupied by the people you used to admire. Figure it out as you go — but do eventually figure it out. Paraphrase.
- (2022-01-20) A mission, higher purpose, or calling — something you do beyond making money — is often found *along the way*, not required at the start. Paraphrase.
- (2022-01-20) Citing Simon Sinek: young people feel heavy pressure to find a mission bigger than themselves; you don't need your *own* mission — you can find a mission you believe in and apply your talents and energy to support it. Paraphrase (attributed to Sinek via Chris).
- (2022-01-20) The 20th century was dominated by left-brain thinkers (analytical, logical — engineers, computer scientists); they still rule, but the 21st century gives right-brain thinkers a much larger role in shaping experiences. Paraphrase.
- (2022-01-20) Culture is dematerializing — we spend more money for fewer physical things (music moved from CDs/LPs to the cloud). Chris notes he personally owns hundreds of CDs, bought mostly out of love for music and to support artists. Paraphrase (biographical detail).
- (2022-01-20) NFTs are changing physical representations of work; cites Mike Winkelmann (Beeple), described as "a friend of ours," selling a digital piece for $69.3 million at Christie's — the third-highest auction for a living artist and the first for a digital work. Paraphrase.
- (2022-01-20) We're moving toward digital currencies (ethereum, bitcoin, dogecoin, zcash) and will need creative people to build these new experiences. Paraphrase.
- (2022-01-20) Citing Jordan Peterson: humans yearn for a transcendent experience, and that's what we're willing to pay for (e.g. standing in the Sistine Chapel, museums, beautiful gardens). Paraphrase (attributed to Peterson via Chris).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Not everyone has a mission. Your desire, your impulses just could be to create, to learn your craft, to get experience making content — and that's totally okay." — Chris Do

> "One day you wake up and you realize you are now where you are relative to the people you used to admire — you're exactly in their spot, looking back." — Chris Do

> "It's okay to start to figure it out as you go. Eventually figure it out though, because that's where you will hit success — and maybe along the way you'll find your mission, a higher purpose, a calling if you will, something you do beyond trying to make money." — Chris Do

> "You don't need to have your own mission. You can find a mission you believe in and apply your talents and your energy to support that. It doesn't have to be yours." — Chris Do (relaying Simon Sinek)
