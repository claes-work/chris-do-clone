# Grow Your Business – Business Bootcamp 2022

## Summary

**Speaker:** Chris Do (first person, direct-to-camera). This is a **product promo / sales
video** for The Futur's "Business Bootcamp 2022" — an 8-week, 8-module cohort-based video
course — not a substantive teaching session. Chris pitches the offer using his origin story,
credentials, and a list of the pain points the course promises to solve. Because the content
is a scripted ad rather than framework instruction, treat it as **fence / promo**: biographical
and product-catalog facts are usable for the wiki, but the persuasive sales copy should **not**
be mined as persona voice-training or belief material.

## Key claims (2022-01-25)

- (Paraphrase) Chris describes himself as "a recovering graphic designer, a serial
  entrepreneur, author, public speaker, award-winning director, business coach and teacher."
- (Paraphrase) The course targets common creative-business pains: finding high-quality leads,
  deciding how much to charge, handling "you're too expensive" / "I thought you'd be less"
  objections, clients who ghost after a proposal, and clients who don't pay after delivery.
- (Paraphrase) Origin story: as a new graduate he believed good work plus a strong design
  pedigree were all he needed for a successful agency; he learned it "takes more than art and
  craft to build a sustainable business."
- (Paraphrase) He committed "the cardinal sin" of relying too heavily on **two big clients**;
  when they left he nearly went out of business and had to lay off his entire staff — who were
  close personal friends. He calls this a personal and professional embarrassment and says he
  vowed that day to "learn the business of design."
- (Paraphrase) To recover he spent years learning the things he hated — marketing, sales,
  bidding, project management — and **hired a business coach he met with weekly for over 13
  years, spending over a quarter million dollars.** (self-reported)
- (Paraphrase) 25-years-later claims (self-reported): his agency has "built over $80 million,"
  he started **two seven-figure businesses**, and worked with global brands/bands including
  Sony, Microsoft Xbox, Honda, Google, Snapchat, Toyota, Nike, Showtime, EA, Fox Sports,
  Quiksilver — and Coldplay, Gnarls Barkley, Justin Timberlake, Jet, The Raveonettes.
- (Paraphrase) Awards claimed: Emmys, the Art Directors Club, and British D&AD; he cites
  **15 years teaching** at Art Center and Otis College of Design, plus workshops worldwide
  (Milan, Barcelona, Dublin, Beijing, Amsterdam, London, New York, Taiwan, Geneva).
- (Paraphrase, product spec) Business Bootcamp is a video-based course of **8 core modules**
  run "bootcamp style" over **8 weeks** in a cohort. The module arc: mindset → lead generation
  and positioning → communication → objection handling → diagnosing → pricing → onboarding →
  delegating. Includes pre-recorded videos, prompts, exercises, worksheets, templates, an
  accountability partner, and **8 weekly group coaching calls** with Chris (hot-seat coaching
  and role-play possible).
- (Paraphrase, motivational close) "Without action there is no growth" and "if nothing
  changes, nothing changes"; enrollment is open for a limited time, and he prompts the viewer
  to ask whether their situation will get worse or improve 3, 6, or 9 months from now.

> Note: The $80M / two-seven-figure-businesses / $250k-coaching / 13-years figures are
> **self-reported** inside a sales pitch and are not independently verified here. No internal
> contradictions detected, but treat all numbers as claims-in-a-promo.
