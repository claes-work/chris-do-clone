# Rose, Thorn, Bud – Problem Solving Method

## Summary

A short instructional clip teaching **Rose, Thorn, Bud**, a structured observation method used inside a design-thinking / problem-solving process to move from empathy to insight. The teacher explains the method's origin (Boy Scouts of America wilderness observation) and how to apply the metaphor to a product or application: Rose = what's working / positive, Thorn = challenges or negatives to negotiate around, Bud = latent potential about to bloom.

**Speaker:** NOT verified as Chris Do. The voice is impersonal, curriculum-style instruction ("we're taking all of our empathy," "the one we're going to use") with no self-identification. ## Key claims

_Dated 2022-02-10. Paraphrased unless quoted. Do-not-train: retained as method reference only._

- Rose, Thorn, Bud is a method "for seeing the problem" — a bridge from **empathy to insight** in the problem-solving process (2022-02-10).
- The method originated with the **Boy Scouts of America**, where boys in the wilderness made observations using the rose/thorn/bud metaphor (2022-02-10). _(Self-reported origin story; not independently verified here.)_
- **Rose** = the positive things, what's working well, what makes you feel good about what you're observing (e.g., in a product or application) (2022-02-10).
- **Thorn** = a negative or a thing you must "negotiate around"; it need not be strictly negative — it can be "a challenge worth solving for" (2022-02-10).
- **Bud** = a latent potential "about to bloom" into another rose; an opportunity (2022-02-10).
- Bud and Thorn are **interchangeable** — an opportunity can live inside a challenge; the transcript jokingly coins "thud" / "born" for the hybrid (caption garble, clearly a play on thorn+bud) (2022-02-10).
- A Bud can also be a **previously-parked idea** — something you (or a client) thought of years ago but hadn't found the right place to apply, now surfaced as a candidate to bring forward (2022-02-10).
- The method can be run in three formats: **paper-based**, a **spreadsheet** (color the cells, log observations — handy when many people who can't observe in person are involved), and a **digital whiteboard** (the version used in the lesson) (2022-02-10).

## Notable verbatim quotes

_Attributed to the unidentified Futur presenter (do-not-train). Verbatim, minor caption cleanup only._

> "We're taking all of our empathy and trying to get some insight... so you're going to go from empathy to insight."
