## Summary

**Speaker:** Chris Do (whiteboard-session host; all persona-eligible material is his).
An edited/condensed cut of the Oct-2021 Future Pro Group whiteboard session on work-life
balance. Chris opens with the ideal ("do work you love, work less, make more, maximize
free time"), runs the 24-hour day-math to show that after ~8h sleep, meals, ~1h commute,
~1h prep and ~8h work there are only **~3 hours of free time** — proving "balance" is a
zero-sum trap where the two sides steal from each other (usually from sleep/health). He
then reframes to **work-life integration**: break work into **MFP — Money, Fulfillment,
Purpose** (Money = freedom/options for you and family; Fulfillment = growth/joy you feel;
Purpose = impact on others), and life into **People, Self, Spirit**. The method is to
draw the domains as overlapping circles rather than opposing halves and maximize the
overlap — "this is where you will find work-life integration." Closes with the
biographical note that he **began this journey at 42** and is "almost 50," that you
sometimes must live enough life first to even see the model, and that younger viewers now
have the roadmap to shorten the path. A brief audience call-and-response (members "Harley/
Arley" name Fulfillment) is context only, NOT training data. The video ends with a Futur
podcast promo read by Greg Gunn (context, NOT Chris).

## Notable verbatim quotes

All Chris Do (2022-02-17) — voice-training data (near-identical phrasing appears in the
2021-10-19 original):

> "We should aspire always to do the work that we love first — that's a condition of a
> work-life balance — and then we should aspire to then work less while making more,
> maximize our earning potential so that we have… the maximum amount of free time to do
> whatever it is that you want."

> "The problem… with work and life balance is it feels like when we're at work we're
> robbing from life, and when we're at home living our life we're neglecting work — the
> thing that affords us our life."

> "Rather than us continuing this conversation about work-life balance, we should be
> talking about work-life integration. And that's how we achieve the harmony and the
> ultimate balance."

> "This delicious overlap where we get all of it… that is where you will find work-life
> integration. And I hope that you get there."

> "I'm almost 50 years old. I didn't begin this journey until I was 42 — not because I'm
> a late bloomer, [but] because sometimes you have to live enough life to see different
> things, to be able to know who you are… to even see something like this."
