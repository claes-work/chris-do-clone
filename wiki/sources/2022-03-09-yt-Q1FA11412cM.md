# How To Get The Highest Quality From iPhone Video

## Summary

A team-led technical tutorial on shooting and transferring higher-quality video from an iPhone, covering automatic-capture pitfalls, a pre-shoot checklist, and moving footage into Adobe Premiere Pro via Premiere Rush. The advice is presenter-generic and gear-focused, not a Chris Do teaching.

## Key claims

_All dated 2022-03-09. Paraphrased from Nick Caraz unless quoted. Do-not-train — recorded for corpus completeness only._

- The best camera is the one you have with you; an iPhone is a viable storytelling device, but pressing record does not automatically produce great content (2022-03-09).
- iPhone auto-exposure brightens the image based on where you tap and shifts as light or environment changes; lock exposure by tapping and holding on a chosen object to prevent fluctuations that create problems in post (2022-03-09).
- The iPhone 11 quick "click-hold-and-swipe-right" to record from camera mode is convenient but does not capture at maximum/standard video frame size — clips come out below standard formats like 1920x1080 (transcript garble "1920x10a" = 1920x1080) (2022-03-09).
- iPhone 12 HDR video captures a wider color range, but HDR may be unnecessary if your audience lacks HDR devices, you lack a calibrated HDR monitor, and your effects/transitions/motion graphics aren't optimized for that color space (2022-03-09).
- iPhone 13 Pro Cinematic Mode adds an artificial depth-of-field look via machine learning with head-tracking focus, editable on-phone or in post (e.g., Final Cut Pro); but the blur isn't always accurate, and a manual camera app like Filmic Pro or Mavis may give more control and realism — Cinematic Mode is also limited to HD and a frame rate not always suited to filmic content (2022-03-09).
- Pre-shoot checklist: (1) launch and update your shooting apps over Wi-Fi the night before to avoid password/update delays on set; (2) keep a backup device — use a Lightning storage device with its app, or AirDrop to a Mac on set; (3) kill background apps; (4) enable Do Not Disturb / Airplane Mode to block interruptions; (5) manage batteries — start fully charged, use Low Power Mode when low, and bring a supercharger/USB chargers (2022-03-09).
- Post-production transfer tip: use Adobe Premiere Rush on the iPhone (add clips, set order, match project settings, enable "Sync to CC") as a gateway to open the same project in Premiere Pro on the desktop, provided both use the same Creative Cloud account (2022-03-09).
