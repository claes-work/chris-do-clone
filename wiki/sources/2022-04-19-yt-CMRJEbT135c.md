# Local Clients – The Best Way To Grow Your Business

## Summary

A short cut-down from a Futur Pro Group call in which a **Pro Group member** (a
Bakersfield, CA-based creative/agency owner — female, married, with a child; name
not stated in the transcript) recounts how she built her business on **local
clients**. She left a salaried job in May 2017 after finding her first client
through in-person networking, applied The Futur's brand-storytelling ideas to a
local lofts/townhomes developer that then sold out its inventory, and argues that
local clients are under-rated and financially viable. The clip closes with a
promo for the Futur Pro Group.

**Speaker:** NOT Chris Do. The entire teaching is a first-person member

## Key claims

_All dated 2022-04-19; paraphrased from the member-speaker unless quoted. Not
Chris Do's positions._

- (Member) There is a **misnomer that local clients don't have money** — they
  absolutely do, and can be enough to sustain a business if you have an offering
  they genuinely need and can stand behind. (2022-04-19)
- (Member) The **digital-services world glosses over opportunities in your own
  backyard**; local business is a different model — different systems, possibly a
  local office instead of a coffee shop — but equally viable, and you still need a
  digital presence alongside it. (2022-04-19)
- (Member) A **hybrid model** (clients both far-away and local) is under-documented;
  she couldn't find content on running it without strain and is "still figuring it
  out." (2022-04-19)
- (Member) Her growth came from **networking / putting herself out there** — she
  approached a local developer offering design, video and photo, they needed all of
  it, and telling the brand story drove a real sales result. (2022-04-19)
- (Member) She **refuses one-off "logo or flyer" work**; she educates clients that
  branding has impact "bigger and wider" than a single deliverable — for the whole
  company and its culture. (2022-04-19)
- (Member) Being the **first locally to put "branding" in their name** is a
  differentiator; she qualifies leads by whether they need branding guidance —
  if not, educate them or treat them as a poor fit on values. (2022-04-19)
- (Member) A stranger at a conference told her to **"put a date on"** leaving her
  job — a psychological trigger that got her to actually quit (May 2017). (2022-04-19)
- (Member, promotional) The **Futur Pro Group** offers a global network, bi-weekly
  calls with Chris Do, subject-matter-expert talks, and 300+ hours of private
  coaching-call recordings covering pricing, onboarding/off-boarding, etc. (2022-04-19)
