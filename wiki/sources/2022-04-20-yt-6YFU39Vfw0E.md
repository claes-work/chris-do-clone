# How I Got 800K+ Followers on Instagram Organically — @thefutur (2022-04-20)

## Summary

A short first-person piece (essentially a promotional talk/course trailer for The Futur's "Instagram Carousel Design Course") in which Chris Do lays out how he grew his Instagram following organically. The through-line: growth comes from giving generously and teaching, not from pitching or selling. He recounts his own doubt and slow early growth, an "epiphany" watching guest Michael Janda grow rapidly with teaching-style 10-slide carousels, and how repurposing his 100+ keynote presentations into carousels accelerated his growth dramatically. He frames Instagram carousels as the low-talent-requirement format that captures attention, positions the creator as an expert, and — combined with consistent daily posting — drives audience growth. The video ends by pitching the course (24 lessons, templates, etc.).

Speakers: **Chris Do** (host, sole speaker throughout — persona-relevant, first person). Michael Janda is referenced as a third party (context), not present.

## Key claims
_All dated 2022-04-20; paraphrased unless in the quote bank. All attributed to Chris Do unless noted._

- **[Chris] Give-first philosophy:** The key to growing a social following is to give generously without expectations — stop pitching and selling, start serving. (2022-04-20)
- **[Chris] The value test:** Before creating any content, ask whether you are "stealing time and attention" or "creating value for others." Content should aim to create transformative moments for the audience. (2022-04-20)
- **[Chris] Teaching is the growth mechanism:** "If you want to reach more people, teach more people." Teaching is the best way to give value at scale. (2022-04-20)
- **[Chris] Relatability / no special talent needed:** He positions himself as unremarkable for social media (felt too old, can't sing/dance/do backflips, no six-pack, no unique skill or cute animal, "another creative in a sea of creatives") to argue growth doesn't require those things. (2022-04-20)
- **[Chris] Early struggle:** He struggled to optimize his writing process for Instagram, to make on-brand carousels, to get engagement, and "really wasn't having any fun." (2022-04-20)
- **[Chris] The Michael Janda epiphany:** While prepping a livestream he noticed guest **Michael Janda** had grown rapidly (tens of thousands of new followers in months) by using 10-slide carousels that read like mini presentation decks, pulling bite-sized content from his book. (2022-04-20)
- **[Chris] Repurposing keynotes:** Chris realized he had 100+ keynote presentations (hundreds of slides) he could mine the same way, turning existing material into carousel content. (2022-04-20)
- **[Chris] Testing/iteration:** Over a few weeks he tested layouts, writing styles, typefaces, graphics, textures, concepts, color harmonies, and transition devices; many ideas failed before he settled on a repeatable style. (2022-04-20)
- **[Chris] Growth-rate claim (number uncertain):** The refined style helped him gain "over 10,000 followers per week" — versus 2.5 years to reach his first 10,000. He states he grew faster in a week than in two years of prior content creation. (2022-04-20; figures as stated in transcript)
- **[Chris] Algorithm insight:** Instagram wants to keep users on the app and rewards creators who capture attention; carousels are the format that does this without special talent — "carousels connect, carousels engage, carousels position you as the expert." (2022-04-20)
- **[Chris] Barriers to carousels:** From talking to The Futur's "feature pro group," the common obstacles are lack of process, creativity, and design — which create resistance. Cites Tony Robbins: "complexity is the enemy of execution." (2022-04-20)
- **[Chris] Consistency is vital:** Consistent execution is essential to growth. (2022-04-20)
- **[Chris] Paid help didn't work:** He tried hiring a social media manager (stated $3,000–$4,000/month) and a social media agency; neither worked for him. (2022-04-20; figure as stated)
- **[Chris] The 2019 challenge (numbers uncertain):** In July 2019 he ran a two-week challenge aiming for 10,000 new followers by posting one carousel daily, "with the full intention to fail." He missed the goal, but it kicked off steady growth leading to big spikes — citing October 2019 net growth of "over 30,000 followers," more than his entire prior following. Since then he has posted carousels consistently. (2022-04-20; figures as stated)
