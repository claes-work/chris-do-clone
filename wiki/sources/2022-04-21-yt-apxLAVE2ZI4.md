# All You Need To Know About Sales In Under 3 Minutes

## Summary

A tight ~3-minute single-speaker monologue in which Chris Do compresses his entire
sales philosophy into the **SALES acronym** (Serve, Ask, Listen, Empathize, Summarize)
and ends with a scripted "ask for the sale" close. He opens by demolishing the common
picture of sales (pitching, presenting, convincing, pressure tactics) and reframes it as
service. **Speaker:** Chris Do — solo, first person ("I have made a whole video on this,"
"here's what I heard you say"). Persona/voice data — no guest or co-host present. Nothing
new versus the corpus: this is a condensed restatement of the serve-don't-sell / SALES
material already fully covered in [[wiki/topics/sales-clients/sales-clients]] §1–§2, but
it is unusually clean, punchy voice material and gives a compact spoken version of the
SALES acronym predating the 2023-12-23 keynote statement.

## Key claims (2022-04-21, paraphrased)

- Sales is nothing like what most people think: it is **not** pitching, presenting,
  selling, or convincing, and **not** using secret language/techniques to pressure a
  client into a decision they're uncomfortable with — that pressure form is "a horrible
  form of selling."
- Sales runs on the acronym **S-A-L-E-S**: Serve, Ask, Listen, Empathize, Summarize.
- **Serve** = be of service, hold the client's needs above your own, put what they want
  ahead of your own best interest — even to the point of referring them elsewhere if they
  need something you don't do (e.g. packaging when you do websites). Serving comes from
  generosity and authenticity; "you need to give."
- **Ask** = ask lots of open-ended questions that help the client gain clarity on what
  they want and need, and surface deeper motivations, feelings, and emotions attached to
  the outcome. Attributes to **Brian Collins** the idea of "finding the hidden dragon"
  — go find the dragon.
- **Listen** = many people ask questions but don't actually listen. Cites the book
  *Socratic Selling* and its concept of **"full-value listening"** — everything the client
  says matters; you take lots of notes, ask follow-up questions, stay fully present, and
  listen for both verbal and non-verbal cues about their concerns.
  - NB caption garble: the transcript renders the book/author only as "the book socratic
    selling" — no author named on-screen; do not fabricate one.
- **Empathize** = try to feel the client's pain, understand what they're going through,
  and help them through it.
- **Summarize** = once you've listened and asked, stay objective and neutral and play it
  back: "here's what I heard you say — you need X, Y, Z because of this pain point, and
  you're willing to invest $25,000 to solve it, is that correct?" Confirm, then check "is
  there anything else?"
- **Ask for the sale = an if/then close.** After the summary is confirmed, close with a
  conditional next-step: "if I were to send you a proposal by Wednesday with these caveats
  for $25,000, is that something you'd be able to make a decision on?" — get the yes, then
  "fantastic, that's it. That's sales."
- Consistency check: this matches, not contradicts, the fuller SALES treatment in
  [[wiki/sources/2023-12-23-yt-B-NxAZiDkKo]] and the serve-don't-sell line in
  [[wiki/sources/2019-04-12-yt-ivKnj9ffcmE]]. The if/then confirming close ("is that
  something you'd be able to make a decision on?") is the same shape as the §14
  "confidence close" quick-reference script.

## Notable verbatim quotes (Chris Do — voice data)

> "The most important thing that you need to know about sales is that sales is nothing
> like what you think it is."

> "Sales is not pitching, sales is not presenting, sales is not selling, and it's not
> convincing — it's none of those things."

> "Sales is not using secret language and techniques to pressure the client to make a
> decision that they're not comfortable with. That's one form of selling, but it's a
> horrible form of selling."

> "First is to serve — to be a service to your client, to hold their needs above your own,
> to put what they want ahead of your own best interest."

> "The way that you serve people is your giving, and it comes from a place of generosity
> and authenticity — and that's what you need to do: you need to give."

> "A lot of people ask questions, but they're not really listening — they're not paying
> attention to what is being said."

> "Now that you understand, now that you've listened, now that you've asked questions...
> you should summarize to the client: here's what I heard you say — you need X, Y, and Z."

> "If I were to send you a proposal by Wednesday with the following caveats... for
> twenty-five thousand, is that something you would be able to make a decision on? ...
> Fantastic. And that's it. That's sales."
