# What To Do When Someone Copies Your Work

## Summary

**Speaker:** Chris Do (solo monologue answering an audience question — verified by first-person references to his own brand "the futur," his team members Matthew and Mo, and his own Instagram carousel experiments). Train on this.

Chris addresses how to react emotionally and strategically when competitors copy your work — right down to your typeface, colors, and stock illustrator. He reframes being copied as confirmation you are the leader ("follow the leader"), advises out-executing rather than policing imitators, and lays out a two-phase response: (1) put distance between you and them by executing fast, then (2) monetize the imitation by teaching/selling templates. He also gives a diplomatic "slip them a note" tactic for close-circle copycats.

## Key claims (2022-05-05, paraphrased)

- **Copying is how humans learn.** We learn to speak and walk by copying; early on we don't even call it copying, we call it "paying homage" or "drawing inspiration." It only becomes a problem when it enters the professional realm — especially inside a small circle. (paraphrase)
- **Being copied is proof you're the leader.** If you're the leader, there are followers — that's why it's called "follow the leader." If nobody copied or emulated you, you'd be on "a very lonely island." (paraphrase)
- **He openly admits he copies too.** His Instagram carousels were an idea he got from Michael Janda — he freely admits it and talks about it all the time. "We all are stealing from somebody else." (paraphrase)
  > ⚠️ CONTRADICTION (apparent, not real): This "we all steal / copying is how we learn" stance sits alongside his frustration at being copied. Chris resolves it himself: copying is fine and universal at the *learning* level, but becomes a problem when imitators (a) are direct competitors in a tight circle, (b) copy so exactly they blur brand distinction, and (c) deny it ("pretending like it was a coincidence"). The line is honest attribution + adding your own layer vs. exact, deceptive duplication. This nuance is worth reconciling in topics against any "be good, not original / remix everything" positions elsewhere in the wiki.
- **You can't stop people from copying — don't try.** Trying to police every imitator burns your energy while they take shortcuts and you stop growing. He invokes the (mis)attributed-to-Gandhi line about hating people being like drinking poison expecting the other to die. (paraphrase)
- **Turn the negative into a positive.** His counter to copycats: make templates and sell them / teach people how to do it — "look for the opportunity in the obstacle." Monetize the very thing being copied. (paraphrase)
- **Phase 1 — out-execute.** Put maximum distance between what you do and what they do by executing really fast; you win by out-running them. (paraphrase)
- **Phase 2 — teach and monetize.** "I can't stop you copying me, so I'll teach you how to copy me and make money off you" — leveraging your authority position. (paraphrase)
- **They can copy the output, not the operator.** They can copy your typefaces, colors, even your language — but they can't copy *you*, your process, or how you think and solve problems. That's where you win. (paraphrase)
- **The "slip them a note" tactic** for close-circle copycats: politely point out the resemblance and ask if they're aware, putting them "on notice" so they can't claim ignorance and giving them a chance to "bow out gracefully." He claims ~8/10 apologize and change; ~2/10 refuse and "get what they deserve." (paraphrase; the 8-of-10 figure is a self-reported anecdote, not data)
- **Naming/brand copying is unavoidable at scale.** At least two other companies targeting designers are also called "the future/futur"; people flag them to him constantly. "I can't own every version of it" — some copying is coincidental, some opportunistic. (paraphrase)
- **He deliberately shifts his own style to stay ahead**, e.g. adopting garish/unusual colors (salmon pink, dark sage-green) he assumed copycats wouldn't use — only to watch them adopt those too, down to the same Shutterstock illustrator (findable via reverse image search). (paraphrase)

## Notable verbatim quotes (all Chris Do)

> "If you're gonna be the leader there are followers — that's why it's called follow the leader."

> "We learn by copying, and at first we don't think it's copying, we think it's paying homage, drawing inspiration as designers do often... we're copying all the time."

> "My pressure was just to create such a gap between where they're at and where I'm at... that everybody's going to falsely attribute all of this to me anyway, so I get the benefit of that. I'm gonna ride that wave."

> "I don't want to spend my life trying to use all my energy trying to stop others versus trying to do what I'm doing."

> "I think Gandhi said this: hating people is like drinking poison and expecting them to die. It's not gonna help."

> "This is confirmation that you're on the right trail. I mean, imagine if you did something nobody cared, nobody copied, nobody emulated — it's like, oh, you're on a very lonely island then."

> "They may be able to copy your work, the typefaces, and even your language, but they can't copy you. They can't copy your process and how you think and solve problems. That's where you win."

> "I'll just make templates and sell them. I'm going to turn a negative into a positive... look for the opportunity in the obstacle."
