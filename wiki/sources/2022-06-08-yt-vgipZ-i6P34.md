## Summary

A short, single-speaker piece in which Stephanie Owens lays out her "Make it / Market it
/ Monetize it" framework for landing dream projects, drawn from running her own studio
(Gold Sheep Design, which specializes in music, mysteries, and pop culture). It is a
promo lead-in for her "Dream Project Workshop" linked in the video description. **Speaker:
Stephanie Owens** (Gold Sheep Design co-owner / Futur copywriter) — **not Chris Do**, so
the whole source is context / **do-not-train**.

## Key claims

All below are Stephanie Owens (context only — do-not-train), dated 2022-06-08:

- [2022-06-08] Her studio booked "dream" projects **without being Insta-famous or a thought
  leader** — the framework, not clout, did the work. (paraphrase, context)
- [2022-06-08] **Step 1 — Make it.** "The proof is in your portfolio." Take control of your
  portfolio by creating the exact kind of self-initiated work you want to be hired for
  *before* you have paying clients; don't let a cheap job or a bad client define the
  trajectory of future projects. Dig deep to identify the precise type of work you want,
  then do that work for yourself. (paraphrase, context)
- [2022-06-08] **Step 2 — Market it.** Get the work out there via a mixture of SEO, social
  media, and "good old-fashioned outreach." Stop relying on an algorithm; run both inbound
  and outbound marketing on a daily / weekly / monthly schedule to stay on track. (paraphrase,
  context)
- [2022-06-08] **Step 3 — Monetize it.** There's more to a living than client work alone —
  art licensing, teaching, publishing, digital products, physical goods. The trick is to
  pick **one at a time**, give it full attention, and add more as you go. (paraphrase, context)
- NB — dream-client "love letter" note: this is a **different** angle from the Dan Mall
  love-letter tactic already covered elsewhere in the wiki. Owens does **not** mention writing
  a personalized letter to a target client; her Step 1 is self-initiated portfolio work to
  attract the dream client. No overlap/contradiction with the Dan Mall material.
