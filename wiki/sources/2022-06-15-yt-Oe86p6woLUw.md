# Finding Your Creative Voice

## Summary

A short, solo-hosted The Futur video in which the host lays out a "creative cocktail"
framework for developing a distinctive creative voice / brand: mix your **craft** (base),
your **character and culture** (secret ingredient), and your **conquests** (chosen niches /
target clients) to produce your **content**. The advice: narrow your craft, lean into your
personality and interests to differentiate, pick specific industries and dream clients, and
let every brand asset (portfolio, web copy, bio, contact page) be a mashup of all three.

> **Speaker:** **Stephanie Owens** — co-owner of Gold Sheep Design and a copywriter at The
> Futur. **This is NOT Chris Do.** Chris Do does not appear or speak in this video.
> **CONTEXT / DO-NOT-TRAIN:** none of the material below is Chris Do-attributed and it must
> **not** flow into `persona/` (voice, beliefs, biography). Retained only as The Futur
> catalog context and for topic reference.

## Key claims

_All dated 2022-06-15. Paraphrased unless quoted. All attributed to **Stephanie Owens**
(context only — not persona training data)._

- **(Owens)** Ingredient one — **your craft**: what you actually do must be totally apparent
  in everything you make; a visitor should immediately grasp your craft from your Instagram.
- **(Owens)** Don't claim "every creative service" as a solopreneur — that's like a Long
  Island Iced Tea, only good for getting drunk, and refined clients with real budgets aren't
  looking for it. Narrow in on your craft.
- **(Owens)** More people and experience on a team let you offer more services; her studio
  (Gold Sheep, 20+ years) offers three primary services — branding, illustration, hand
  lettering. Writing is a personal skill (17 years teaching rhetoric/writing) but not the
  studio's primary service, so she doesn't offer it standalone.
- **(Owens)** Develop a "healthy obsession" with being the best at one thing rather than
  so-so at a bunch of things ("skill stacking" is a perk of getting older, but focus first).
- **(Owens)** Ingredient two — **your character and culture**: your interests, personality,
  and backstory are the secret ingredient that sets you apart, because there's only one you.
  Her studio's work/socials/site show she's into true crime ("murderino"), sci-fi, hard
  rock, and 80s cartoons — and they've won clients because her bio names those interests.
- **(Owens)** Ingredient three — **your conquests** (niche + target clients): you can't talk
  to everyone at once with the same creative voice, and you probably don't want every client.
  She flips the familiar line — instead of "your work is not for everyone," it's "not
  everyone's business is for me."
  > ⚠️ CONTRADICTION (framing, not substance): This flips the common creator maxim "your
  > work is not for everyone" to "not everyone's business is for me." It's a deliberate
  > rhetorical inversion by Owens, not a rebuttal of the original idea — noted only so the
  > phrasing isn't mistaken for a contradiction of Chris Do positioning material.
- **(Owens)** Once you know the industries/market segments you want, you know how to write
  copy, plan marketing, and fill portfolio gaps. You can have more than one conquest, and the
  list should include **specific named dream clients** so you can strategize outreach to them.
- **(Owens)** Combine craft + character/culture + conquests and you get your **content** —
  used loosely: everything you create for your brand should be a mashup of all three. Example:
  illustration (craft) + loving Star Wars (culture) → a dedicated Star Wars illustration page
  that can be sent to Star Wars licensees as outreach (conquest).
- **(Owens)** The framework applies beyond portfolio work to web copy, content, and site
  graphics; her contact page reads "for those about to rock we salute you," and her bio shows
  her with a guitar in an AC/DC skirt because she works with musicians and instrument brands
  and plays guitar — "nothing phony here."
- **(Owens)** CTA: her Dream Project workshop (link in description) walks through the creative
  voice cocktail over a month.
