# Find Your Creative Focus

## Summary

A short Futur explainer on how creatives can find focus (a gentler framing of "niching") across three axes: industry, skill, and market. The presenter argues that attracting "big league" brands and budgets requires deliberately defining the "playground" you work within, and proving capability through a targeted portfolio built on passion projects. The video closes with a promotion for her Dream Project workshop.

**Speaker:** Stephanie Owens — co-owner of Gold Sheep Design and a copywriter at The Futur. This is **NOT Chris Do.** Chris does not appear or speak in this video. **Context / do-not-train:** none of this material is attributable to Chris Do and it must NOT flow into `persona/` (voice, beliefs). Recorded here only as Futur-channel context and as a topical cross-reference to Chris's own positioning/specialization ideas.

## Key claims

_All dated 2022-06-23; paraphrased from Stephanie Owens unless quoted. Do-not-train — attribute to Owens, not Chris Do._

- Owens reframes "niching" as "focus," preferring to "design my own playground" and set her own boundaries rather than being told to "stay in your lane." (paraphrase)
- **Method 1 — industry focus:** pick an industry (or two or three) you are genuinely excited about; if you're not excited about it, it's a no-go. (paraphrase)
- Owens's studio first focused on musicians (a passion) but widened to "music and pop culture brands" because musicians "don't always have budgets" that pay the bills — a focus can be broadened when the original niche can't sustain the business, as long as the portfolio doesn't lose overall coherence. (paraphrase)
- **Method 2 — skill focus:** clients with large budgets don't want a "one-stop shop" (unless it's a large agency of experts); develop "a healthy obsession with one skill" and be "so good they can't ignore you." (paraphrase)
- Start with one skill; stack additional skills as the team and experience grow — but one skill may be all you need to start. (paraphrase)
- **Method 3 — market focus:** beyond industry, choose a design market (editorial/publishing, art licensing, consumer licensed goods, consumer packaged goods, etc.); your portfolio must unambiguously prove you can do the specific work you want to be hired for. (paraphrase)
- The more markets you want to enter, the more extensive your portfolio must be; "don't make clients think too hard." (paraphrase)
- **Passion projects** are how the studio landed illustration gigs with major entertainment brands and five-figure branding jobs as a home-based team of two. (paraphrase)

> ⚠️ CONTRADICTION: not a true contradiction, but note the **speaker mismatch** — these focus/specialization claims are Owens's, not Chris Do's. They rhyme with Chris's own "specialize externally" positioning theme (see [[wiki/topics/branding/branding]]), but Owens frames specialization primarily as a portfolio/skill-marketing tactic, whereas Chris's canonical framing is "specialize externally, diversify internally" (present one clear focus to the market while remaining broad in-house). Do not conflate the two or let Owens's phrasing stand in for Chris's position.
