# Making Money With No Clients

> ⚠️ **CONTEXT / DO-NOT-TRAIN** — Chris Do does **not** appear or speak in this video. It is a solo short delivered entirely by **Stephanie Owens**, a Futur team member. Nothing here trains the persona; this page exists only to catalog the source and its ideas. No first-person Chris Do material.

## Summary

Short promotional video for a Futur "Dream Project" workshop, presented start-to-finish by Stephanie Owens (co-owner of Gold Sheep Design, copywriter at The Futur). She argues creatives should diversify beyond client work — since work is cyclical and some clients "suck the soul from your body" — and walks through non-client income streams: art licensing, physical/digital products, and teaching. The recurring hook is that these require "no clients" but do require doing the heavy lifting of audience building and marketing.

**Speaker:** **Stephanie Owens (Futur team, not Chris Do)** — full video. **Context / do-not-train.**

## Key claims

_All attributable to Stephanie Owens, not Chris Do. Listed for cataloging only — none train the persona._

- [2022-07-01] Client work should be only **one** income stream, not the whole business, because work slows cyclically and some clients drain you. (paraphrase)
- [2022-07-01] **Art licensing** is a viable stream: you license existing artwork to be reproduced on products (she cites a Joker shirt her studio licensed to Six Flags theme parks); crucially, **you don't need a following** — you just reach out to licensors and show your work. (paraphrase)
- [2022-07-01] **Products** — physical goods, digital downloads, and teaching — put the creative "totally in charge" with "no clients," but require being ready to do the "heavy lifting" of **audience building and marketing.** (paraphrase)
- [2022-07-01] The workshop CTA ("Dream Project workshop," linked in description) promises a plan to add your next income stream and build "a business you love." (paraphrase — promotional)

**Novelty check:** Not new to the wiki. The multi-income-stream / products-and-passive-income model is already Futur/Chris Do canon; this short adds no Chris-attributed material.
