# How To Charge For Design Thinking

## Summary
A short (~50s) explainer in which Chris Do walks through how he prices design-thinking engagements: scoping them by duration (one week to a year+), pricing on value/outcome rather than deliverables, and unbundling the process into billable stages (prep, workshops, post-workshop analysis). He shares two concrete price points and closes with a pitch for The Futur's Design Thinking Guidebook.

**Speaker:** Chris Do — first-person throughout ("I typically charge...", "let me give you an example... where I charged..."). TRAIN.

## Key claims
_All dated 2022-07-15, paraphrased unless quoted._

- (2022-07-15) Design-thinking project scope varies widely — from as little as one week up to a whole year and beyond; price scales with scope.
- (2022-07-15) It is "not uncommon" to charge $50,000 or more for larger design-thinking projects.
- (2022-07-15) Chris typically charges **$150,000 for eight-to-ten-week projects** that bundle research, a workshop, and a design strategy. _(Caption is clean here — "150 000" for 8–10 weeks.)_
- (2022-07-15) Understanding the flow of the design-thinking process lets you see where the deliverables surface, which in turn lets you elevate (raise) your design pricing package — i.e. pricing follows from mapping the process, not the other way around.
- (2022-07-15) Worked example — a short two-week project priced at **$50,000**, billed as three chargeable phases:
  1. **Workshop prep** — aligning client + stakeholders on the creative brief / problem to solve, plus light research on the company.
  2. **The workshops themselves** — one to multiple days, in person or virtual; used to uncover deep insights on the customer base, market conditions, and the client's perceptions of their own business.
  3. **Post-workshop analysis & reporting** — at least four to five days unpacking learnings into a findings report that determines whether more research is needed or the project can move on.
- (2022-07-15) Promotes The Futur's "Design Thinking Guidebook" as the paid resource for applying this pricing process. _(Product plug, not a teaching claim.)_

> NB — the get-paid-to-think / strategy-pricing theme is already covered elsewhere in the wiki. What is **relatively new/specific here**: (a) the two hard numbers tied to durations ($50k for 2 weeks; $150k for 8–10 weeks), and (b) the explicit unbundling of a design-thinking engagement into prep / workshop / post-workshop-reporting as separately-justified line items. No contradiction with existing pricing pages detected — it is a concrete instantiation of the same value-pricing philosophy.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "It's not uncommon to charge 50,000 or more for these larger projects. I typically charge 150,000 for eight to ten week projects that include research, workshop and a design strategy." — Chris Do

> "Once you understand the flow of the design thinking process you'll start to see where the deliverables surface from this process, and it can help you elevate your design pricing package." — Chris Do

> "Let me give you an example of a short two-week project where I charged fifty thousand dollars. First I charge for the workshop prep..." — Chris Do

> "Second are the actual workshops... this helps me uncover deep insights about their customer base, their market conditions, and to see what their perceptions are on their own business." — Chris Do

> "Third is all the post workshop analysis and reporting. I spend at least four to five days uncovering and unpacking all of the things that I've learned and putting them together as a findings report." — Chris Do
