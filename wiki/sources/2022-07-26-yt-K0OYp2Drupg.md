## Key claims

_All dated 2022-07-26. Paraphrased unless quoted. **All claims below are the GUEST's (Daniel
Priestley), context-only — do not train.**_

- The core idea is that **"prolific beats perfect"**: nearly everyone hugely successful is
  prolific rather than perfect. `[guest, do-not-train]`
- Examples of prolific output: Muse took ~25 years of output to build a concert of hits; The
  Beatles wrote a new song roughly every 12 days for eight years, producing a *prolific*
  catalogue, not a *perfect* one. `[guest, do-not-train]`
- **Dunbar's number** (Prof. Robin Dunbar): the brain organizes people into concentric spheres —
  ~5 (family), 15 (close friends), 50, 150, 500, 1500. To move someone into the "acquaintance
  sphere" takes roughly **seven hours** of time together, ideally clocked within about three
  months. `[guest, do-not-train]`
- Google's "Zero Moment of Truth" research: buyers average about **11 touchpoints** (cited as
  ~10.7) with a brand before purchasing. `[guest, do-not-train]`
- Combined rule of thumb: people need roughly **7 hours + 11 interactions** to feel they know,
  like, and trust you enough to buy. `[guest, do-not-train]`
- You can build that time **analog** (dinners, coffees — synchronous, same place/time) or
  **digital** (publishing — asynchronous, scalable "across time and space"). Publishing lets one
  person accrue those hours with many people at once. `[guest, do-not-train]`
- **Asynchronous trust example:** the guest wrote a book in 2010; the interviewer read it in 2022
  and arrived at the conversation already "warmed up" — 12 years of asynchronous connection.
  `[guest, do-not-train]`
- **A hit is not required.** Trust is built by clocking up consistent time, not by landing one
  viral piece — like a best friend, built through ongoing time together, not a single great
  moment. You may never have a "hit" and still build the relationship. `[guest, do-not-train]`
- The "friend across the street" illustration: you forget the hundreds of strangers you pass, but
  a familiar face lights up your brain — not because they're remarkable, but because you've
  clocked up time with them. Same mechanic drives audience connection. `[guest, do-not-train]`
