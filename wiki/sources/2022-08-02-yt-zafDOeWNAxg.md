# Personal Branding Masterclass (w/ Daniel Priestley)

## Summary

A long-form masterclass in which Chris Do (host, interviewer) draws out Daniel Priestley (guest, primary teacher — author of *Key Person of Influence* and *Oversubscribed*) on how to build a personal brand and a scalable business around it. Daniel delivers virtually all of the framework content: his "5 P's" of a Key Person of Influence (Pitch, Publish, Product, Profile, Partnership), the four-part product ecosystem, the "know-like-trust" math (7 hours / 11 touchpoints), and the coming shift from social-media content to data-analytics/personalization. Chris's role is Socratic — restating, pressure-testing, and translating Daniel's ideas for a creative audience — plus one genuinely-new disclosure that The Futur has launched its own 40-question scorecard tool. **Speaker:** Chris Do (host — only his lines train) + Daniel Priestley (guest, primary teacher — context / do-not-train).

## Key claims

> ⚠️ ATTRIBUTION: Daniel Priestley is the guest expert; the vast majority of the ideas below are HIS (context / do-not-train). Only Chris-Do-attributed bullets train the persona; they are marked **[Chris — trains]**.

- (2022-08-02) **[Daniel — context]** Being a "Key Person of Influence" (KPI) matters because digital erased geography: you can now do business with anyone in the world, but anyone can also compete for your local customers — so a personal brand becomes the way to gain traction inside an industry. [paraphrase]
- (2022-08-02) **[Daniel — context]** People buy into the person behind the business before the business itself — e.g., Tim Cook has ~2x Apple's followers; Richard Branson (~12M) dwarfs Virgin (~250k). Human brains connect with faces, not logos (his party trick: most people can't name the color of the "G" in Google — it's blue). [paraphrase]
- (2022-08-02) **[Daniel — context]** Becoming a KPI shifts you from "functionality" (ability to perform a task) to "vitality" (irreplaceable + life force): inbound opportunities flow to you and you get energized by the work rather than worn down. [paraphrase]
- (2022-08-02) **[Daniel — context]** The 5 P's, done in order: (1) **Pitch** — enroll people into a new way of thinking/behaving (not answering questions or telling your backstory); (2) **Publish** — prolific content along a narrative that reinforces the pitch; (3) **Product** — build a product ecosystem so you don't sell your time; (4) **Profile** — guard reputation, amplify across media, but the truly great ones become a spotlight ("look at that") rather than chase it ("look at me"); (5) **Partnership** — access resources beyond your control. [paraphrase]
- (2022-08-02) **[Daniel — context]** "Prolific beats perfect": Dolly Parton wrote ~3–4k songs for a dozen hits; the Beatles wrote a new song every 12 days for 8 years. You may never have a hit — trust is built by clocking up consistent time, not by one perfect piece. [paraphrase]
- (2022-08-02) **[Daniel — context]** The know-like-trust math: ~7 hours of attention (within ~3 months) and ~11 touchpoints (Google's "Zero Moments of Truth" found an average of 10.7) before people buy; publishing lets you build that asynchronously and at scale. Cites Dunbar's numbers (5 / 15 / 50 / 150 …). [paraphrase]
- (2022-08-02) **[Daniel — context]** The four-part product ecosystem: **Gift** (free, no strings — e.g. a podcast), **Product for Prospects** (easy first engagement — scorecard/quiz, book, free workshop), **Core Offering** (the main thing), **Product for Clients** (extended, high-margin back end). BMW makes ~40% margin on finance/insurance vs. ~3–4% on cars — it's the ecosystem, not any one product, that produces profit; removing one piece collapses the others. [paraphrase]
- (2022-08-02) **[Daniel — context]** He dislikes the word "funnel" as dehumanizing ("I wouldn't want someone to funnel my grandma"); every step should deliver standalone value. Reframes it as an ascending exchange of value. [paraphrase]
- (2022-08-02) **[Daniel — context]** "Dent" (his company) is named after the Steve Jobs line about making a dent in the universe; leadership means getting others clear even on days you aren't clear yourself ("directionally correct at best… feeling for the light switch"). [paraphrase]
- (2022-08-02) **[Daniel — context]** Partnership overcomes scarcity thinking: rather than spend 3 years + £90k on a PhD, a client instead recruited 2–3 existing PhDs to an advisory board (~£4k/person/year) in weeks. Most partnership structures already exist and are well-worn; overwhelm is usually not lacking resources but not knowing how to structure the deal. [paraphrase]
- (2022-08-02) **[Daniel — context]** US presidential elections predict marketing trends (the "Formula 1 of marketing"): FDR's fireside chats → radio, JFK/Nixon → TV, Obama '08 → social media, and 2016/Cambridge Analytica → data analytics + hyper-personalization, which he calls the dominant rising trend ("friendship is a data problem"). [paraphrase]
- (2022-08-02) **[Daniel — context, self-reported bio/business]** He was employee #3 at a fast-growth startup (0→$6M in 2 years) from age 19–21, then built an introduction-events agency in Australia (0→A$1.3M year one, A$10.7M year three); today runs the Dent entrepreneur accelerators (Sydney/London/Toronto, ~3,500 alumni, ~£3k startups / ~£7k scale-ups) plus a group of owned agencies and ScoreApp (spun out, ~2,000 clients, raised at a ~$15M valuation). [paraphrase — self-reported; not registry-verified]
- (2022-08-02) **[Chris — trains]** The Futur has just launched its own self-diagnostic scorecard — coincidentally also 40 questions — that outputs one of four result types telling a person which product/service they likely need; Chris credits COO Ben Burns and calls the approach a game changer. [paraphrase — genuinely-new Futur/product disclosure]
- (2022-08-02) **[Chris — trains]** Chris's own stated mission, which Daniel points to as an example of a clear "game worth playing," is to get a billion people to do what they love; Chris affirms it and the "make a dent" framing. [paraphrase — note: mission is voiced by Daniel about Chris, then confirmed by Chris]
- (2022-08-02) **[Chris — trains]** Chris's interviewing method on display: he restates each concept back in a creative-friendly metaphor (e.g., the leader "blazing a path" through murky waters so followers know it's safe), tests it against adjacent frames (is "pitch" like a coach/therapist? is the ecosystem just a marketing funnel?), and mines the guest's off-stage behavior for lessons — modeling how he learns and teaches. [paraphrase — voice/method, not new doctrine]
