# 3 Networking Strategies That Landed A $50,000 Contract

## Summary

**Speaker:** NOT Chris Do — this is a guest/member designer telling her own success story,
with The Futur as publisher. Speaker attribution is unambiguous: she addresses Chris in the
second person ("when I asked this to my friends... Chris guess what they say"), references
"my operations manager," being "away for 4 months," and "my first year freelancing... back in
2014." She recounts building a personal relationship with publicity expert **Selena Soo** and
meeting Jim Kwik and Lewis Howes at Selena's business-anniversary party. The speaker is female
("she invited me... as a guest instead of her assistant"). **This is CONTEXT / DO-NOT-TRAIN:**

The talk lays out a referral-first client-acquisition system: after a paid agency funnel
(~$30k build + $3k ads) produced zero clients, the speaker's agency discovered nearly all its
business came from word-of-mouth and deliberately turned referrals into the marketing strategy.
Three concrete tactics are given, the third of which ("big fish strategy") is credited with a
career-changing network jump and, per the title, a $50,000 contract.

## Key claims

All dated 2022-08-30, paraphrased, attributed to the **guest speaker (not Chris Do)** unless noted.

- Most freelancers/agencies get the majority of clients from word of mouth, yet marketers
  frame that as a "dangerous" over-dependency and push Instagram, Facebook ads, and funnels
  instead. (paraphrase, guest)
- Her agency spent ~$30,000 having an agency build a funnel plus ~$3,000 on ads and got zero
  clients from it; reviewing the data showed clients were nearly all word-of-mouth referrals.
  (paraphrase, guest — self-reported)
- Cites the statistic that "91% of all B2B buyers are influenced by word of mouth." (guest,
  quoting an unnamed statistic — unverified)
- Strategy decision: make word of mouth the deliberate strategy so leads keep coming even
  during a 4-month absence. Philosophy: "always be planting seeds and watering them." (guest)
- Tactic 1 — reactivate cold leads: with the operations manager, pulled past inquiries who
  never became clients from the sales pipeline (~900 of ~9,900 leads over ~6 years) and sent
  the **nine-word email** (credited to **Dean Jackson**): "Hey [name], are you still interested
  in [brand identity]?" (paraphrase, guest)
- Tactic 2 — the "dream client" / referral-ask email: thank a past ideal client, then ask if
  they know "just one person" who'd benefit. The ask sentence is credited to **Phil Jones** and
  works because it (a) challenges — "you wouldn't happen to know" triggers "try me," (b) makes
  the ask reasonable — "just one person," (c) flatters — "someone just like you," and (d)
  reminds them of the benefit delivered. (paraphrase, guest)
- Tactic 3 — the "big fish strategy": connect with a well-networked person (not unreachable but
  knows many people) to elevate your whole network. She did this with **Selena Soo** (2014):
  joined the newsletter, replied to the welcome email, offered free work, pulled an all-nighter
  on a free sales page, then volunteered as an assistant for Selena's 2-year anniversary
  party/mastermind. Selena upgraded her from assistant to invited guest, introducing her to
  Jim Kwik and Lewis Howes, who became clients. (paraphrase, guest — self-reported)
- Takeaway: connect with a "big fish" and you can suddenly elevate your entire network.
  (paraphrase, guest)
