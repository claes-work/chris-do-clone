# Design Services as a Subscription Model? (w/ Ron Baker)

## Summary

**Speaker:** **Chris Do** (host, The Futur) interviewing guest **Ron Baker** (guest —
value-pricing authority, "recovering CPA," VeraSage Institute founder, co-host of *The
Soul of Enterprise*). This is a short clip on the **subscription business model** as
"value pricing 2.0." Ron does virtually all the teaching; Chris's only spoken
contribution is a brief reaction and a request for examples.

**Attribution note — this is CONTEXT, not core persona data.** The subscription
frameworks (transactional→relational, the concierge/annuity model, recurring
frictionless "peace of mind" value, the Porsche Drive / Summit CPA / hassle-free home
services examples) belong to **Ron Baker** and do NOT train the Chris Do persona. The
only persona-relevant line is Chris's reaction that his own bodily resistance to an idea
signals that it is powerful — a recurring Chris self-heuristic.

## Key claims

_All dated 2022-09-20. Paraphrased unless in the quotes section. Attributed per speaker._

**Chris Do (persona — his own framing):**
- Chris reacts that he can "feel inside my body some concepts of resistance," and that
  he reads that internal resistance as a signal that the idea is powerful and worth
  leaning into. _(Chris self-heuristic — persona-relevant, but not new enough for L3.)_
- Chris frames the subscription concept for his creative-services audience and asks Ron
  for concrete examples of creative/professional-service providers moving to it. _(host
  framing only)_

**Ron Baker (guest — CONTEXT / influence, NOT Chris persona):**
- We live in a "subscription economy"; within ~5 years you'll have the option to
  subscribe to nearly everything, and even non-subscription businesses will be confronted
  with it via competitors.
- Subscription moves a firm from a **transactional** to a **relational** relationship —
  everything centers on the relationship rather than "the math of the moment."
- Model on **concierge medicine / direct primary care**: subscribe to the firm, and it
  covers "whatever you need" within a defined scope, referring out beyond it.
- A subscription creates an **annuity** — makes the firm more valuable, commands a higher
  sale price, is more profitable and as scalable as you want, and gives the customer peace
  of mind, convenience, and a frictionless relationship.
- Examples: **Summit CPA** (Jody Grunden) moved from ~$600K fixed-price revenue to $7M+
  via weekly-billed subscription CFO services for marketing agencies; **Porsche Drive**
  (~$3,500/mo for swappable access to multiple models — you subscribe to Porsche, not a
  car, giving them a one-to-one data relationship); **hassle-free Home Services** (~$250/mo
  franchise handling home-maintenance chores, earning ~50% of revenue from one-off
  projects).
- Applied to creative firms: tell the client "anything you need — a logo, etc. — you're
  covered" within boundaries/options, replacing change-request/scope-creep friction with
  "serial transformations" of continuous help.
- "Common services don't command uncommon prices" — subscription is about **recurring
  value / recurring frictionless peace-of-mind value.**
