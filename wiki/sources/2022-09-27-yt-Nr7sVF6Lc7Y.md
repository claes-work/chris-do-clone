# The Longer You Wait, The Less Opportunity There Is (w/ Sean Cannell)

## Summary

**Speaker:** This is a **guest interview**. **Chris Do** hosts (asks the questions,
frames the theme); the storyteller is **Sean Cannell** — YouTube-growth expert, founder
of Think Media, co-author of *YouTube Secrets*. Sean's story and frameworks are
**context / DO-NOT-TRAIN** for the Chris Do persona; only clearly Chris-Do-attributed
lines could ever feed `persona/`.

The clip is Sean recounting his decision to book **Gary Vaynerchuk** as a keynote for a
Think Media event, paying a **$75,000 commitment / ~$150,000 total fee** — framed as a
deliberate "brand play" rather than a profit play. Sean walks through his risk math (worst
case wouldn't be fatal to the business; years ago the same bet *would* have been fatal),
the "lead Domino" leverage effect (booking Gary made other speakers like Vanessa Lau say
yes instantly and drew attention to Think Media before, during, and after the event), and
the payoff (profitable on ticket sales *plus* two years of content). He closes on Benjamin
Hardy's *Be Your Future Self Now* — entrepreneurs must keep making bigger and bigger bets.
The through-line, matching the title: waiting only makes high-trajectory people more
expensive and less reachable, so act now.

  marketing), profitability, or brand building; brand building was the goal.
- (Sean Cannell, guest, paraphrase) Risk discipline: he sized the bet so the worst case
  (down $50K–$100K) would *sting but not be fatal*; years ago a $100K loss would have been
  fatal, so this was an "intentional risk," not betting the whole farm.
- (Sean Cannell, guest, paraphrase) The **"lead Domino"** effect: once Gary was confirmed,
  the promotion alone drew attention to Think Media, and other speakers (e.g., **Vanessa
  Lau**) said yes instantly on seeing Gary attached — she "stopped thinking" and said yes
  before even checking her availability.
- (Sean Cannell, guest, paraphrase) Having Gary + Alex (Hormozi) + PBD at one event created
  pre-event buzz; the back-end content compounded the value.
- (Sean Cannell, guest, paraphrase) In hindsight it was ROI-positive on ticket sales alone,
  but the real return is roughly two years of content — "one of my best investments I've
  ever made in my entire career."
- (Sean Cannell, guest, paraphrase) Caveat: he'd do it again but would encourage others to
  "count the cost" first.
- (Sean Cannell, guest, paraphrase) Cites **Benjamin Hardy, *Be Your Future Self Now***: to
  keep being pulled into the future you're called to, keep making bigger and bigger bets.
