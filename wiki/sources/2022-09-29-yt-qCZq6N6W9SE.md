# If You're Scared To Spend, You'll Never Profit (w/ Sean Cannell)

## Summary

**Speaker:** Chris Do (host) + **Sean Cannell** (guest / context — YouTube-growth expert,
founder of Think Media). A short clip in which Sean tells the story of making
progressively "bigger and bigger bets" in his business — starting from a scary $997
Kajabi founder's-deal purchase before he even had the product, up to funding a large
in-person event years later — and credits the mindset shift to mentorship and proximity
to higher-level operators.

**Attribution note — this is CONTEXT, not core persona data.** Sean does virtually all
the talking. The narrative, frameworks, and mantras here (make bigger bets, "scared
money don't make money," things are "more caught than taught" through proximity,
David's Mighty Men / "courage transfers," seasons of risk) belong to **Sean Cannell**
and his mentors (**Chalene Johnson** and **Brett Johnson**) — they do NOT train the
Chris Do persona. Chris's only contribution in the clip is the interviewer question.

## Key claims

_All dated 2022-09-29. Paraphrased unless in the quotes section. Attributed per speaker._

**Chris Do (persona — his own framing):**
- As interviewer, Chris characterizes Sean as sounding like "a thoroughbred
  entrepreneur… built for this," and asks whether a coach or mentor helped change his
  mindset "about making small bets to making big bets" — probing for the source of the
  risk-taking shift. _(Chris's contribution is the question/frame only; no original
  Chris doctrine stated.)_

**Sean Cannell (guest — CONTEXT / do-not-train):**
- His first terrifying bet was buying Kajabi's $997 founder's deal to host the online
  course before he "even had that stuff yet"; he saw it as a good deal and a "domino"
  that would build the education side of the business. Today a $1,000 decision carries
  "zero financial friction" — so you have to keep making bigger and bigger bets.
- Making a big bet on a large event pushed both him and his team to their limits ("it
  almost" killed them) but he trusted it wouldn't break them: "we were built for battle
  and we need greater challenges… bigger mountains to climb, Giants to slay — within
  reason." Rationale: build the brand, stretch the team, and "if we lose we will survive
  and we'll still be better for it."
- He did not develop this mindset alone ("no man is an island"). Beyond books and
  podcasts, "proximity to people at a higher level than you that challenge your thinking
  is incredibly helpful." Cites a Monday men's Christian Zoom prayer group and a
  peer-led mastermind as critical communities.
- Some things are "more caught than they are taught" — head knowledge from a book vs.
  what you catch through a trusted relationship and proximity to people placing bigger
  bets. Invokes the Biblical example of David's Mighty Men (Benaiah fighting a lion in a
  pit) and "courage transfers."
- Credits **Chalene Johnson** (whose Marketing Impact Academy he was a student of, and
  who gave him his first shot speaking at a large event) and her husband **Brett
  Johnson** as powerful mentors who helped him "think bigger." Brett has "scared money
  don't make money" tattooed on his arm; the phrase became a Think Media mantra.
- Growth is uneven: $100k felt terrifying in year one, but by year five it "wasn't even
  the entirety of one individual's keynote fee." The numbers get bigger but it does not
  get easier — you must stay self-aware of your own and your team's mental health and
  "the season of life you're in." Not every season is for going "down in a snowy pit to
  pick a fight with a lion"; deliberately putting yourself in pressure situations forces
  personal growth in the right seasons.
