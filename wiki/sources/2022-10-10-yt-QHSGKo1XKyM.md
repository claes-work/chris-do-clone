# Selling: Why You Aren't Closing Any Clients

## Summary

**Speaker: Chris Do.** He is coaching a mentee ("Phil") through a cognitive
framework in the classic Chris Do teaching style (Socratic questioning, then
synthesis). Attribution is confirmed by the outro, in which he refers to "my
best-selling book," "our clients," and helping "grow your creative business" —
consistent voice/business markers for Chris Do / The Futur. **This is Chris-
attributed teaching and may train the persona (voice + beliefs).** Phil's replies
are the coachee's; they are not doctrine.

The throughline is a diagnosis of *why deals don't close* that ignores tactics and
targets the seller's underlying belief. Chris's chain: **the belief you hold about
an activity determines how you feel about it → how you feel determines the action
you take → the action determines the result.** He runs the same scenario (LinkedIn
outreach) through two people with opposite beliefs and shows they can get identical
raw results yet *interpret* them oppositely — the believer counts the yeses as proof
it works, the skeptic dismisses the yeses as luck and the no's as proof it doesn't.
The practical punchline: changing the *action* (different script, more outreach)
barely matters if the belief underneath is unchanged, because a hostile belief will
sabotage or dismiss any tactic you hand the person. So the only variable that
"really changes anything" is the belief itself. He closes by extending the same
logic to self-worth and revenue: if you believe to your core you should generate
half a million a year, you will.

## Key claims (2022-10-10 — Chris-attributed unless noted; paraphrased)

- (Chris, paraphrase) Belief → feeling → action → result: your belief about a thing
  determines how you feel about it, your feeling determines the action you take, and
  your action determines the result you get. He calls it an "oversimplified framework
  of human behavior" but "one of the most important things you can ever learn."
- (Chris, paraphrase) **Diagnosis of why you aren't closing:** two sellers can run the
  identical activity and get identical raw outcomes (some yeses, some no's, some
  ignores), yet get opposite *net* results — because they interpret the outcomes
  through their belief. This is the mechanism behind not closing clients: it's a
  belief problem masquerading as a tactics problem.
- (Chris, paraphrase) Person A believes outreach is the best way to get business AND
  that reaching out *is being of service*; they feel confident/trusting, so they reach
  out consistently, and they read the yeses as confirmation it works — focusing on the
  evidence that reinforces the belief.
- (Chris, paraphrase) Person B believes LinkedIn outreach is "salesy, spammy, and
  bothering people"; they feel uncomfortable/reticent, so they take little or half-
  hearted action, and they dismiss any yeses as luck and the no's/ignores as proof it
  "doesn't work."
- (Chris, paraphrase) **You can change many variables — the action, the script, the
  volume — but if the belief stays the same, the person will keep concluding "this
  doesn't work."** Handing a skeptic a better tactic doesn't fix the outcome; the
  belief is "the only variable that really changes anything."
- (Chris, paraphrase) The framework generalizes beyond sales to self-worth: if you
  believe to your core that you are worth (and should generate) half a million a year,
  you will — belief drives the revenue outcome the same way it drives the sales outcome.
- (Chris, business/CTA, paraphrase) Outro offer: grab a free copy of his best-selling
  book, or apply to a live workshop where he shows "what's working behind the scenes
  for our clients" and gives a custom roadmap to grow a creative business.

**Serve-don't-sell / objections — genuinely new?** The "reaching out *is* being of
service" framing touches the serve-don't-sell theme already in
[[wiki/topics/sales-clients/sales-clients]], but this clip's actual contribution is
different: it is a **belief-first diagnosis of non-closing** — the claim that mindset
(not script/volume/technique) is the binding constraint, illustrated with a clean
two-person self-fulfilling-prophecy demonstration. The belief→feeling→action→result
chain applied specifically to *why deals don't close* is the new, promotable angle;
the serve framing itself is not new.
