## Key claims (2022-10-11 — attributed per speaker; only Chris Do lines may train)

**Chris Do (HOST — trainable):**
- (Chris Do, paraphrase) **"Being an entrepreneur is synonymous with being a great
  teacher — not leader, teacher."** To grow a business you must teach other people your
  systems, process, and way of seeing; a client complimenting your trained editor's work
  ("nice edit") is proof you taught successfully.
- (Chris Do, paraphrase) Pricing craft: **anchor high, use price bracketing, then stay
  silent and let the client speak.** If the client says "yes" too quickly, the ceiling
  was too low — you want them to feel a little pain ("that's a lot of money... but I
  think you're worth it") before you know you've reached your ceiling *for now*.
- (Chris Do, paraphrase) **"Closing the gap"** delegation model: train someone to 60% and
  you take the 40%, then push them to 80/90/95% until you only spec, hand off, and QA —
  the way to scale output beyond your own hands.
- (Chris Do, paraphrase) The **"30%" budget rule** (referencing a ~$120k Google
  commercial): "We do everything for 30%. If it can't get done for 30%, it needs to get
  done for 30%" — build the profit-first discipline into how you run each job.
- (Chris Do, paraphrase) **The outside manifests to match the inside** — people with an
  abundance/optimism mindset tend to see external markers line up with how they already
  feel; people "rotten to the core" who blame society, culture, parents, or their city
  and never take responsibility stay stuck in a negative spiral. A person merely "waiting
  for the external markers to line up with how they already feel inside" is "unbeatable."
- (Chris Do, paraphrase) **Caution against partnerships:** "Do not get into a
  partnership." People often seek a partner as a surrogate to handle the thing they don't
  want to work on in themselves, then "check out" — both parties usually benefit from
  becoming independent and learning what they needed to learn about themselves.
- (Chris Do, paraphrase) **Adversity as gift, not curse:** financial challenge, uncertain
  future, and an emotional low reveal "the stuff we're made of"; the low point is a gift
  "when you look at it through the right lens." Recognize the fire that emerges and never
  slip into cruise control — "keep hungry, no matter what."
- (Chris Do, paraphrase) Learning money lessons early is better than late: while young,
  "the amount of damage you can do financially is finite" — in your 30s/40s "the toys get
  bigger, the habits don't change."
- (Chris Do, paraphrase) Value-based framing: a solo operator in a small town can command
  ~$20k for the "same product" because he **found clients with a bigger problem to
  solve**, to whom the work is worth more (interview prompt that lands the point).

**Chris Franklin (GUEST — context / DO-NOT-TRAIN):**
- (Chris Franklin, biographical) Started college in biological sciences (chem minor)
  aiming to be a neurosurgeon for the money; switched to graphic design, graduated,
  pivoted to video. Motivated by watching his father work three jobs.
- (Chris Franklin, biographical/emotional) Father's phone call — "I'm proud of you" after
  Chris Franklin booked a $5,000 wedding — was a turning point that unlocked his
  confidence to fight for his creative choices.
- (Chris Franklin, self-report/financial) Ended college ~$22k credit-card debt + ~$30k
  student loans, largely spent on gear, travel, and "double-dipping"; now essentially
  debt-free using the **Profit First** technique ("run my business on less").
- (Chris Franklin, biographical) ~2.5-year partnership with "Mo" dissolved when Mo decided
  to end it; Chris Franklin took a ~2-week processing break, then went solo. Solo revenue:
  **$91k (2020) → $147k (2021) → $165k in ~10 weeks of 2022.**
- (Chris Franklin, self-report) Post-breakup he asked to keep the ~300-subscriber YouTube
  channel to conquer his fear of the camera; day rate climbed toward ~$20k/day.
- (Chris Franklin, self-report) Applied Chris Do's teachings — anchoring, silence,
  delegation ("closing the gap" with an editor) — and credits "turn your brain off and
  just listen" as his operating posture toward Chris Do's advice.
