# Think Media's $1M Branding Bet To Get Gary Vee

## Summary

**Speaker split:** Chris Do is the HOST/interviewer. Sean Cannell (Think Media,
co-author of *YouTube Secrets*) is the GUEST and the source of nearly all substantive
content. The episode is Chris interviewing Sean about Think Media's ~$915K "Grow With
Video Live 5" event in Las Vegas — a deliberate brand-building bet anchored on paying
GaryVee's ~$150K keynote fee (~$155K with travel) as the "lead domino" that unlocked
Alex Hormozi, Layla Hormozi, Patrick Bet-David and other speakers. Sean's story
(the $1M event economics, the GaryVee negotiation, partnership with Brett Knutson /
Monopolize, the challenge-funded marketing engine, ~$1.25M revenue / ~$350K profit)
is **context / do-not-train** — it is Sean's business and Sean's philosophy, not Chris's.

Only Chris-Do-attributed lines below train the persona: his own risk philosophy
("steady as she goes" vs. Sean's "make bigger bets"), his critique of GaryVee's
event conduct, his approach to hosting/running a Q&A, and his one-word synthesis
of the lessons ("focus"). This is a light L2: Chris is mostly extracting Sean's story
and adds only a handful of genuinely-Chris opinions.

## Key claims (dated 2022-10-13)

**Chris Do (host — TRAINS):**
- On risk/growth style, Chris contrasts himself with Sean: Chris describes his own
  approach as "tried and true… as steady as she goes," explicitly distinct from Sean's
  "make bigger bets / scared money don't make money" mantra. He frames the two of them
  as "alike but different" entrepreneurs worth studying as a case study. *(mindset —
  Chris's stated risk temperament: steady, incremental growth over big all-in bets.)*
- On GaryVee's conduct at the event: Chris says he feels "cognitive dissonance" — Gary
  preaches generosity, humility, and being nice, yet at the event was filming for his
  own crew, cutting Sean off, and making eye contact with his camera guy rather than
  his host. Chris's leadership view: earning the right to do whatever you want doesn't
  mean you should exercise it; "my respect would have gone through the roof if he had
  just stopped with the media and said 'hey man, I'm here to talk to you.'"
  *(branding — authenticity vs. performance; a leader/model's obligation.)*
- On running a Q&A: Chris says he is "more confrontational" than Sean — he would cut off
  a rambling/self-promoting audience member: "stop, what's your question, let's go…
  nope, you're selling, stop, we're done, next person." He notes the first audience
  member who gives a testimonial instead of a question "sets the tone" and breaks the
  rules for everyone after. *(content-strategy / hosting craft.)*
- On staying composed on stage: praises Sean for keeping cool ("like the Terminator")
  when Gary derailed the interview; notes he's watched speakers "lose it" when AV or
  their own deck fails. *(hosting craft.)*
- Synthesis of Sean's two biggest lessons into one word: **"focus"** — Sean tried to run
  a $1M event while keeping Think Media's day-to-day fully loaded; more focus would have
  surfaced the key decisions (e.g. hiring the event planner) earlier. *(mindset.)*
- Backstory anchor: Chris says a mutual friend (Jace) told him about "Sean's thing" a
  month prior at a mograph meetup in Vegas; Chris's reaction was "why would he do that?
  You're not going to be financially whole — this is the craziest thing ever," until he
  understood it as a brand play. Chris notes he and Sean have similarly-sized YouTube
  channels and started around the same time (Chris ~2014, Sean ~2015).

**Sean Cannell (guest — CONTEXT / DO-NOT-TRAIN):**
> The following are Sean's claims, recorded for context only. None train the Chris persona.
- The event cost ~$915K to run; GaryVee's keynote fee was ~$150K + $5K travel (Sean paid
  full fee "from a place of honor"). Gary's fee reportedly rose right after, making it a
  retroactive discount.
- "Scared money don't make money" (attributed by Sean to his mentor Brett Johnson,
  Chalene Johnson's husband) is Sean's/Think Media's mantra; also the "lead domino"
  concept (from Benjamin Hardy's *Be Your Future Self Now*).
- Marketing engine: challenges (e.g. "Tube 1K Challenge") carried the ad spend
  profitably; the event rode on the back of already-profitable challenges rather than
  cold-traffic event ads. Cites Dan Kennedy: selling out an event is "100x harder than
  you think."
- Results per Sean: ~1,200 tickets sold, ~800 max in room, ~$1M in ticket sales,
  a $5K offer × ~80 units, ending ~$1.25M revenue / ~$350K profit (~30–35% margin).
- Sean's origin: read GaryVee's *Crush It* in 2009 while his wife was hospitalized;
  it "lit the fire" to start his creator/affiliate business.
