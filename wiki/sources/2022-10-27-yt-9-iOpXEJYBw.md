## Key claims

All dated 2022-10-27, paraphrased, attributed to the guest CEO unless noted.

- Remote work is fundamentally about building **organizational resilience** — the ability
  to work from anywhere, anytime, so a company isn't crippled if its office disappears.
- Remote work is a *different approach to work*, not just "local work done far apart";
  it leans on **asynchronous communication** and trusting people more, especially across
  time zones (forcing real-time across zones makes people work longer and is unhealthy).
- Companies that relied solely on in-person meetings were "caught flat-footed" by COVID;
  remote capability simply gives you another option.
- Hire the best people **anywhere**, not just within ~30 miles — the guest rejects the
  tech "talent war" over the same 50 people; exceptional talent exists everywhere.
- Remote work is more respectful at a human level: it frees employees from having to live
  where they work, opening up life decisions (partners' jobs, where to live).
- Caveat (guest): remote doesn't fit manual-labor/on-site jobs — it applies to information
  and creative workers.
- Interviewer's framing: in 2013 this was a "radical philosophy"; the pandemic played out
  "the way that it was written."
