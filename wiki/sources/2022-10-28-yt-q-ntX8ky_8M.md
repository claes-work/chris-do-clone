# Dark Patterns—Designed For Manipulation

## Summary

Short promo/lesson clip from a Futur **UX-design course** ("User Experience Design and
Business"). The instructor defines dark patterns and contrasts ethical, behavior-
supporting UI design with manipulative design that benefits only the company.

**Speaker: an unnamed female Futur UX-course instructor (name not stated in the
transcript).** Not one of the previously-identified Futur staff and not a named guest in
this cut. This is **not Chris Do.**

> ⚠️ **CONTEXT / DO-NOT-TRAIN.** UX-course instructor material; none of it feeds the Chris
> persona.

## Key claims

Dated 2022-10-28, paraphrased, attributed to the UX instructor.

- **Dark patterns** = intentional design choices that sway a user toward something they
  may not want, benefiting the company rather than the user.
- The relevant "data" is qualitative — from **user interviews and contextual inquiry** (both
  covered in her course) — not just demographic data.
- Design can ethically use observed behavior (e.g. a color users dwell on) to *help* both
  user and business; it becomes a dark pattern when done "solely to help yourself as a
  company."
- Example of a dark pattern: adding an item to someone's checkout cart before they decide.
