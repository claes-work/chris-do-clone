## Key claims

Dated 2022-11-01, paraphrased.

- **Hick's Law** (instructor): decision time increases with the number and complexity of
  choices; leads to **decision fatigue**, where people default to the fastest (not best)
  choice.
- **Jam study** (instructor, citing the two-psychologist grocery experiment): a 24-jam
  display drew more lookers but a 6-jam display **converted far better** — more choice can
  reduce willingness to buy.
- Applied to UX: launching with *all* features at once overwhelms users and blocks task
  completion; keep it simple ("don't make me think," attributed loosely to Steve Krug,
  garbled as "Eric Morris"/"paradox of choice… Barry Schwartz" in the caption).
- Second voice (uncertain, maybe Chris): restaurant menus (Cheesecake Factory) and Gordon
  Ramsay's "make seven great dishes" illustrate that fewer, better options reduce regret
  and speed decisions.
