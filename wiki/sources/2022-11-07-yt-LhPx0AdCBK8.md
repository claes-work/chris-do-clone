## Key claims

Dated 2022-11-07, paraphrased, attributed to the guest expert.

- Goal is "**better, not perfect**." Bring in a dataset if you have (a) a legitimate
  hypothesis about how it matters and (b) the ability to act on it — don't wait to "clean
  everything up" first.
- Start from a **blank sheet / hypothesis** ("what leads to great relationships? what data
  would prove that?"), then test and iterate — not from collecting all data.
- **Bear-in-the-forest analogy**: you don't need to outrun the bear (perfect info), just the
  other campers (competitors). In an ad auction you win by being *slightly* better-informed,
  faster — not by having perfect information.
- The **same dataset yields different narratives** depending on the human interpreting it
  (his own COO Ben vs. himself reaching opposite conclusions).
- **Confirmation-bias experiment**: given contradictory data, people keep their original
  decision and merely grow *more confident*; the fix that actually changed minds was simply
  **reminding people up front that they're biased toward first impressions** — that
  disclaimer made a majority update to match the data.
- Example: companies convinced their mobile app drove loyalty found (in the data) app users
  spent the same as non-app users.
