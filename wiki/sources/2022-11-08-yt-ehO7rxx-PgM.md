# What Does A UX Designer Do?

## Summary

Short Futur **UX-course** clip framing the UX designer's role: not to be the center of all
information or to "convince" stakeholders, but to collect information from all sources and
synthesize it clearly for decision-makers.

**Speaker: the unnamed female Futur UX-course instructor** (same "User Experience Design and
Business" course; name not stated). **Not Chris Do.**

> ⚠️ **CONTEXT / DO-NOT-TRAIN.** UX-course instructor material; does not feed the persona.

## Key claims

Dated 2022-11-08, paraphrased, attributed to the UX instructor.

- The UX designer's job is **not** to convince stakeholders they're right or to be the
  "center" of the business — it's to **gather information** from internal stakeholders, users,
  and any relevant source.
- The value is **synthesis**: distilling that information into something simplified and useful
  for everyone involved in the decision.
- Credits an unnamed senior practitioner friend (met at a conference) for the framing.
