# Understanding Your Customer Needs Through Data

## Summary

Guest interview clip (same **data / online-advertising expert** thread as

## Key claims

Dated 2022-11-09, paraphrased, attributed to the guest expert.

- To learn customer needs, **just ask more questions** — curiosity dies inside companies;
  it needn't be a big survey.
- Ask on the **thank-you page**: trust peaks right after someone pays. Only ask when you
  know what you'll *do* with the answer.
- Social media gives "rules for conversation" and feedback, but what wins differs per
  platform (Twitter ≠ LinkedIn); virality = conforming to a community's rules, which he finds
  an uninteresting problem and one that subordinates your brand to the platform.
- **Volume ≠ relationship**: influencers with millions of followers often get near-zero
  conversion (e.g. ~15k of millions buying a book). There are "one set of rules for volume,
  another for good relationships."
- If you're in the influencer game, **care how many people *act*, not how many follow**.
- **Electronic Arts case**: of 16 display ads, the one driving the most downloads was the
  *least* attractive to the players who'd actually spend money — vanity metrics diverge from
  paying customers.
