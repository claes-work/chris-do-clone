# How & When To Raise Your Rates (The 3x Rule)

## Summary

**Speaker: Chris Do** (founder of The Futur), teaching in a live workshop/coaching
setting with students in the room (one named "Jared," one named "Drigo" who asks a
follow-up). All the pricing doctrine and the personal speaking-fee story are
Chris's own — this is **voice + belief training data**. The audience members only
ask questions; nothing substantive is attributed to them.

Chris gives a concrete decision rule for *when* to raise your prices: track your
close rate and your string of "yeses." If clients keep saying yes — especially a
close rate above ~60–70% — your ask is too small and you must charge more. His
shorthand: **three yeses in a row = raise your price.** He grounds it in
supply/demand (there is only one of you; if demand rises, you sit on the favorable
side of the curve) and illustrates it with his own speaking career: he arbitrarily
decided he wanted $10K/gig, couldn't get it for a long time, finally landed it
(oddly, over Zoom during the pandemic), got three yeses, jumped the price to $30K,
and started getting yeses again. When raising the price produces *too many* no's,
you either drop back to the supply/demand equilibrium **or** — the harder, better
answer — you fundamentally *create more value and become a different person* worthy
of the higher fee. Closes on a "growing or dying" mindset (borrowed from Hormozi/the
stock market's ~9% and Canfield/Rohn's "it's who you become in pursuit of the goal").

The title's "3x Rule" is a slight mismatch with the content: Chris's *named*
heuristic is the **three-yeses rule** (three yeses → raise), and his suggested
increment is actually "**2x is great**, you don't have to go 10x." The "3x" likely
alludes to his own 10K→30K speaking-fee jump. Flag noted below.

## Key claims

_All dated 2022-11-22, paraphrased unless quoted._

- **The three-yeses rule (named heuristic — NEW).** If you get three yeses in a row,
  raise your price. "Three yeses is all you need, ever, forever." This is a concrete
  *trigger* for raising rates that is not documented elsewhere in the wiki (existing
  raising-rates material — §8 raise-on-every-new-client, §28 double-don't-inch-up —
  covers *how much* and *the mindset*, but not the "three yeses" signal). Genuinely new.
- **A high close rate is a symptom of underpricing.** If you're closing more than
  ~60–70%, "your ask is too small" / "you're giving away too much." Either way, charge
  more. (Reinforces the existing **underpricing-signal** concept with a specific
  numeric threshold.)
- **Increment: 2x, not 10x.** When you raise, "2x is great" — you don't have to make
  the Jared-style 10x leap. But *don't* take tiny steps (Chris on his own gig: "I don't
  take little steps, I'm just gonna go" — 10K straight to 30K). Consistent with the
  existing **double-don't-inch-up** rule (§28).
- **Supply and demand justifies the raise.** Three yeses means "supply and demand is
  on your side." There is only one of you (supply = 1); if you increase demand for
  yourself, you're on the favorable side of the curve and can raise price. (Restates
  the existing **control-supply-and-demand** point, §8.)
- **When raising causes too many no's → two options.** (1) Lower back to the
  supply/demand "stasis," or (2 — the real answer) **create more value / fundamentally
  change and become the person worth the higher fee.** Chris re-illustrates with
  speaking: to move from "no's" at $10K to yeses at higher fees, "I grow my social
  following, I continue personal development, I write more talks, I become a different
  person." Rejects the passive "just wish for it."
- **"Find better clients" and "more marketing" are partial answers**, offered by
  students; Chris accepts them but redirects to the core lever: create more value.
- **Growing or dying (mindset frame).** Business is binary: you're either growing or
  dying. Cites Hormozi (*$100M Offers*) via the stock-market analogy — the market grows
  ~9%/yr, so merely raising revenue ~9% is "maintaining," which Hormozi counts as dying.
  Be binary: "piss or get off the pot."
- **It's who you become, not the goal.** Attributes (uncertainly — "Jack Canfield or
  Jim Rohn") the idea that setting big goals matters less than the person you become in
  pursuit of them. `attribution: uncertain` on which author.
- No contradictions with existing pricing pages; this source *sharpens* the
  raising-rates cluster with a named trigger and a numeric close-rate threshold.

## Notable verbatim quotes

_All Chris Do — voice training data._

- "If you're closing more than 60, 70 [percent] it's because your ask is too small…
  either way you need to charge more."
- "If you get three yeses, change your price. Three yeses is all you need, ever.
  Forever."
- "Three yeses, change the price. It means supply and demand is on your side. How much
  supply is there of you? One… and if you can increase demand for you, then you're on
  the right side of the supply-demand chain."
- "You don't have to go 10x like Jared is, but you just go up — 2x is great."
- "I don't take little steps. I'm just gonna go." _(on jumping his speaking fee 10K → 30K)_
- "If you get too many no's when you raise your price… you have to create more value.
  You have to fundamentally change."
- "What are you willing to do to become a person where people will pay you that much?"
- "You're either growing or you're dying. That's it."
- "It's the person you become in the pursuit of the goal, and not the goal itself, that
  is the important part." _(attributing to Jack Canfield or Jim Rohn — uncertain)_
