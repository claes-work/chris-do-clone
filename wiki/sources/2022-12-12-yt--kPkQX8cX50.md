## Key claims

_All dated 2022-12-12. Paraphrased. **Speaker unverified (likely not Chris Do) — do not
train; context only.** Recorded here for corpus completeness and dedup._

- **[Unverified speaker]** When someone says "I can't afford it," don't argue — acknowledge
  it ("okay, cool, I totally understand that"), then ask permission to probe: "do you mind
  if I ask a couple of questions around that?" (2022-12-12) — _mirrors Chris's documented
  embrace-and-pivot / ask-permission move, but not attributable to Chris here._
- **[Unverified speaker]** Diagnose what "can't afford" actually means: no money in the
  bank, no way to get the money, or it just _feels_ like a lot? The objection is often
  about belief, not literal funds. (2022-12-12)
- **[Unverified speaker]** Reframe from present to future income: ask what doing "everything
  we said" over the next 90 days would do to their income, then show that the price is a
  smaller percentage of their _future_ earnings than of their income today. The client is
  "thinking about this investment from where you are now rather than where you're going to
  be in 90 days, 6 months, 12 months." (2022-12-12) — _same "desired future state" logic as
  Chris's, unattributed here._
- **[Unverified speaker]** "You can't afford it right now — that's exactly why we're doing
  this." The inability to afford is reframed as the reason to engage, not the reason to
  walk. (2022-12-12)
- **[Unverified speaker]** Offer a graduated payment schedule (called "grandfathering")
  to ease cash flow: e.g. $1,000 month one, $500 month two, then $2,000/month after — start
  small so the client sees results while paying, then step the payment up. (2022-12-12)
  _NB: "grandfathering" is used loosely here to mean a graduated/step-up schedule, not the
  usual sense of preserving old terms._
- **[Unverified speaker]** "The good news about sales is there is no rule book" — you get to
  decide how you sell and how you show up to calls. (2022-12-12)
