# Using Customer Feedback To Build A Better Business

## Summary

**Speaker: This is primarily a GUEST presentation by John D. Saunders** — creative
director and founder of 5Four Digital (a Miami branding/design/development agency),
founder of blackillustrations.com, and former founder of Urban Loot (a financial
literacy blog he says was acquired in 2021). **The bulk of this video is John teaching,
NOT Chris Do — it is marked `do-not-train` for persona voice/beliefs.** Chris Do hosts,
introduces John, asks Socratic probing questions, and interjects a handful of his own
observations (flagged separately below). Only the clearly Chris-attributed material may
feed the persona; John's frameworks are context only.

John walks startup founders through a six-step journey to find product-market fit and
grow via customer feedback: (1) problem & solution, (2) determine the target customer,
(3) define the value proposition, (4) implement the product, (5) measure impact, and
(6) identify power users to "sell like crazy." He demos free tools (Airtable keyword
tallies from a request form, Wappalyzer, BuiltWith, SimilarWeb, Google Analytics,
Microsoft Clarity heatmaps/recordings) and books he draws on (*What's Your Problem?*
and *Show Your Work* by Austin Kleon). The through-line: low-hanging fruit — retention
and existing power users — is more profitable than always chasing the next sale.

The most persona-relevant portion is a mid-video digression where Chris reframes the
elevator-waiting problem, names "shiny object syndrome," and delivers an extended
analysis of the TikTok vs. Instagram/Facebook platform war.

## Key claims

Claims below are dated 2022-12-20 and attributed to the speaker who made them.

**John D. Saunders (GUEST — context / do-not-train):**
- Paraphrase: Product-market fit is the intersection of market, price, and product; with
  strong PMF you can succeed even without the ultimate product (cites Andy Rachleff).
- Paraphrase: Ask your audience directly what they want — 5Four/blackillustrations uses a
  public request form piped into Airtable, then tallies keywords (e.g. "healthcare")
  across submissions to decide which illustration pack to build next; 8 of 10 times the
  most-requested pack is a hit.
- Paraphrase: Study competitors with free tools — screenshot sites into a Figma inspo
  board with likes/dislikes, use Wappalyzer/BuiltWith for their tech stack, and SimilarWeb
  for traffic, demographics, and a competitor list — to borrow what works without copying.
- Paraphrase: Build 2–3 buyer personas grounded in real competitor/analytics data rather
  than pulled "out of thin air."
- Paraphrase: Value-proposition formula — "We help [target customer] do [need/problem] by
  offering [product feature/benefit]" (e.g. "We craft profitable brands and websites for
  startups").
- Paraphrase: Turn audience questions into evergreen content — YouTube is a search engine,
  so a video answering a common question keeps converting for years.
- Paraphrase: Measure impact with Google Analytics + Microsoft Clarity (heatmaps and
  session recordings); they moved the download button above the fold after watching real
  user recordings.
- Paraphrase: Power users (repeat/regulars, vocal insight-sharers, influential referrers,
  early adopters) raise average customer value and lower CAC; identify, incentivize, and
  keep collecting feedback from them.

**Chris Do (host — persona-eligible):**
- Paraphrase: Reframe the problem before solving it — people are frustrated *waiting* for
  the elevator, not that the elevator is slow; the elegant fix was mirrors/artwork in
  lobbies to distract waiters, not a faster motor (drawing on the book *What's Your
  Problem?*).
- Paraphrase: It is easier and more profitable to keep the customers you already have than
  to chase customers you don't; "shiny object syndrome" makes businesses neglect existing
  advocates who then mentally check out and stop promoting you.
- Paraphrase: There is a hidden platform war — TikTok has reportedly surpassed Facebook and
  Instagram combined in hours logged and is tracking ahead on revenue trajectory; Meta is
  reverse-engineering TikTok's "secret sauce" (the algorithm that hooks users), which is
  why Instagram pivoted from static images/carousels to a vertical video feed.
- Paraphrase: TikTok's algorithm is unlike anything before it — it uses machine learning to
  study the video and audio itself, so creators don't need captions/hashtags to find an
  audience; Chris finds it "super addictive" and caps himself at 25 minutes/day.
- Paraphrase: Static images and carousels require work from the user (reading, swiping),
  which yields lower time-on-platform than a one-to-two-minute video — a structural reason
  video is winning attention.
