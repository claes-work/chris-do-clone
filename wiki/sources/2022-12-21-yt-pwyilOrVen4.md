# How To Get Better Clients That Pay You More

## Summary

**Speaker: Chris Do** (host/coach). This is a short clip of a **live coaching demo** —
Chris interviews an unnamed workshop participant/business owner and walks him through a
sales/discovery conversation in real time, then debriefs what he just did. The
participant speaks too, but only as the coached party; all the teaching and technique is
Chris's, so Chris's lines are valid voice/beliefs training data. The participant's lines
are context only (do-not-train).

The through-line: to get better, higher-paying clients you must first make your *goal*
specific and measurable (not "I need help closing clients" but "close $30k/$100k clients
that do X, Y, Z"), and in the sales conversation you must get the *client* to name the
number and the value — because "when you say it, you're selling; when they say it, you're
closing." Chris demonstrates the trap of a vendor volunteering a price first, and how
asking "what would you pay to achieve this result?" flips it.

## Key claims

All claims paraphrased from Chris Do, dated 2022-12-21, unless quoted.

- **(Goal-setting)** Don't accept a broad goal like "I need help closing clients" — it's
  too vague to act on. Force it into something specific and measurable: e.g. "teach me
  how to close $30,000 clients that do X, Y, and Z." Only then can you "zero in on a
  problem." [paraphrase]
- **(Make better wishes)** Chris frames goal-setting as a genie/"one thing" exercise
  (referencing the book *The One Thing*): what is the one wish that would make everything
  else easier? A vague wish gets a useless grant ("I can help you close 200 clients —
  that's not going to make you happy"); you have to be specific about the client you
  actually want. [paraphrase; book reference is Chris's]
- **(Striking distance)** Anchor the target to what's realistically within reach of where
  the person is now (a $100k client, if that's within striking distance), not a fantasy
  number. [paraphrase]
- **(Sell the dream outcome, then qualify)** Start from the client's dream outcome; the
  budget/time specifics come *after*, not first. Establish that solving the problem is
  worth a lot to them and that the result recurs "for the rest of your life" before
  numbers enter. [paraphrase]
- **(The core move — let the client say the number)** As soon as *you* (the vendor) name
  a price, you've "walked into the trap." Instead, ask "what would you pay to achieve this
  result?" and let the client volunteer the figure. In the demo the participant himself
  says "$100k" and even starts negotiating against himself — all without Chris naming a
  number. [paraphrase]
- **(Selling vs. closing — reframe of an existing principle)** "When you say it, you're
  selling; when they say it, you're closing." Construct the conversation so the client
  does all the talking and states their own value/number. [paraphrase]
- **(The payoff)** If you master just this one thing — getting clients to say what they
  want by carefully constructing the conversation — Chris claims you'll close "twice as
  much, twice as often, for five times as much money." [paraphrase; figures are Chris's
  illustrative estimate]
