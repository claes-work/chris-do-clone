## Key claims

All claims dated **2023-01-03**. **All attributed to the GUEST (likely Dakota Robertson,
uncertain) unless noted. GUEST material is context only — it does NOT train the persona.**

- **(Guest — paid distribution)** Big Twitter creators are "buying and selling retweets";
  you can pay for retweets and for IG-story shout-outs. He pays accounts to retweet his
  clients' content because organic tactics (e.g. "commenting 20,000 times a day") don't
  scale. He frames it as effectively paid ads — buying amplification into other accounts'
  networks. [paraphrase]
- **(Guest — content still has to be good)** Paid retweets are not a shortcut to followers:
  bad content with 100 retweets is "whipped cream on dog [poop]." You need BOTH nailed
  content AND distribution — best content with no eyes doesn't matter, and vice versa.
  [paraphrase]
- **(Guest — three pillars of why people engage)** People want to be **validated**
  (confirm their opinions / confirmation bias), **educated** (value, "how you do X, Y, Z"),
  or **entertained** (memes, funny posts). Rotate between the three. [paraphrase]
- **(Guest — three content types to rotate)** (1) **Personal** — stories, opinions,
  worldviews (builds relationship); (2) **Authority** — educational/value, "how I did
  [achievement] step by step" (raises authority); (3) **Growth** — broad/general content
  ("10 Chrome extensions you'll love") that mainly gets eyes on the account. Mixing/rotating
  the three gives the best return. He keeps frameworks simple because "it scales better,"
  and tries to write "one actionable piece of advice every day." [paraphrase]
- **(Guest — storytelling)** "All stories revolve around change." Most great stories are
  about **transformation** (start point → end point), i.e. the hero's journey and overcoming
  struggle. People relate through emotions and the feeling of overcoming, even if the exact
  context differs. Advice for people who think they have no interesting story: look at where
  you are now vs. where you started to find the arc. [paraphrase]
- **(Guest — retweet price sheet)** Broad/"platitude" motivation accounts (100k–300k
  followers) are cheaper — roughly **$10–$35 per retweet** — because they're commodity
  audiences with less authority. Authoritative niche accounts (e.g. business niche, 100k–200k
  followers) run roughly **$40–$70 per retweet** for a higher-quality audience. At 500k+
  niche followers, prices spike — he ballparks **~$200 per retweet**, and cites a
  ~500k-follower creator charging "six thousand dollars for 20–30 retweets." [paraphrase;
  figures per guest, illustrative]
- **(Guest — selling from your own audience is "the easiest money ever")** Once you build an
  audience you "just hit a button" and make $40–$60 a retweet. [paraphrase]
- **(Guest — ghostwriting-agency origin, self-reported)** Started his ghost-writing agency
  **November 2021**; hit **$11k/month within 28 days** with only ~**5,000 followers**,
  charging **$3k/month out the gate** (then a $5k/month client, then another $3k/month). Says
  he never promoted his landing page — just a link in bio and a form. Attributes it to being
  in the right market and positioning: as a generic "copywriter" he couldn't charge that, but
  ghostwriters were rare, so it was easier. [paraphrase; self-reported]
