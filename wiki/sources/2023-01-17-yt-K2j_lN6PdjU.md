# Build A Bigger Following On Social Media (w/ Dakota Robertson)

## Summary

**Speaker:** Chris Do (subject, host) + Dakota Robertson (guest — Twitter/X
social-growth ghostwriter, founder of the agency "Growth Ghost"). This is a guest
interview: Chris **hosts and asks the questions**, and Dakota supplies almost all of the
substantive frameworks (social growth, ghostwriting, viral-copy formula, retweet
distribution, content pillars). **Dakota's material is context / do-not-train** — it is
recorded here for reference only. Only Chris-Do-attributed lines (his interviewing
craft and his own framing observations) are persona training data.

Dakota tells his arc — chubby/socially-anxious kid, electrician on Canadian oil rigs,
Vietnam trip, crypto (2018), dropped out of an English/psychology teaching degree
because "I was learning more from Twitter than in class" — then launched a ghostwriting
agency (Nov 2021) that hit ~$11k/month within 28 days at only ~5k followers. He walks
Chris through: what he does for clients (writing + paid distribution), pricing, his
process for "pretending to be people on the internet," a three-pillar content model,
a viral-headline formula, and five copywriting rules. Chris's own contributions are the
interview structure (drawing out the guest's story before tactics) plus a few framing
asides (the 1%-compounding math, the fitness/net-worth correlation, "start with what you
can control," and crediting Blair Enns).

## Key claims (all dated 2023-01-17 — ATTRIBUTED per speaker; only Chris trains)

**Chris Do (subject — trains):**
- Chris's interviewing philosophy on this show: get the person's **backstory before the
  tactics** — "it helps me to understand the person before we get into all the tactics."
  He repeatedly redirects from tactics back to Dakota's childhood, father, and turning
  points, on the premise that the story reveals the person (paraphrase).
- Chris restates the **1%-improvement compounding** idea: 1.01^365 becomes a large
  number while 0.99^365 shrinks — "just showing up every day looking for that small
  improvement" compounds into "abs, then muscles, then a whole new life" (paraphrase;
  Chris frames it, not original to him).
- Chris's observation on a **fitness ↔ high-net-worth correlation**: cites Jeff Bezos
  ("nerdy dude" → "yoked") and Dana White as examples that "really successful high net
  worth people" tend to take care of their physical "frame"; advises anyone reinventing
  their life to "start with the things you can control" (paraphrase — his framing, offered
  tentatively as correlation not proof).
- Chris credits the maxim **"all strategy is autobiographical"** to **Blair Enns** — "we
  recommend people do things that have worked for us, so this worked for us and therefore
  it might work for you." (Chris citing Blair Enns, not claiming it as his own.)
- Chris reframes Dakota's content model back to him as: **growth content = "the candy"**
  (broad reach), **education = builds authority**, **story/validation = affirms and
  creates community** — a restatement of Dakota's framework, not new Chris material.
- Chris admits he is **unfamiliar with the active/passive-voice distinction** in
  copywriting and asks Dakota to explain it (a candid on-camera knowledge gap, not a
  teaching point).

**Dakota Robertson (guest — CONTEXT / do-not-train; recorded for reference only):**
- Viral-content formula = four elements: **(1) make the benefit huge, (2) make the
  perceived effort really low, (3) make it relevant to your audience, (4) ensure good
  reach/distribution.** Example headline: "47 sentences that'll make you more money than
  a four-year marketing degree."
- Ghostwriting service = two parts: **writing + distribution.** Distribution's "big
  secret": you **pay for retweets / IG story shout-outs** (broad accounts ~$10–35/RT;
  niche/authoritative ~$40–70/RT; 500k-follower niche accounts ~$200/RT). "It's like paid
  ads." But paid reach on bad content is "whipped cream on dog [expletive]" — you must
  nail both content and distribution.
- Pricing/positioning: charged as high as **$25k (10k followers in 90 days) and $35k**;
  now does **month-to-month at ~$7k/month**. Launched Nov 2021, ~$11k/month within 28 days
  at ~5k followers. Attributes premium pricing to **category scarcity** — "if I was just a
  copywriter there's no way I could charge that; since there are so few ghostwriters it's
  easier."
- Client-voice process: a **1-hour call**, focused on the client's **story** (change/
  transformation arc, hero's journey) plus their **vibe/tone and speech patterns**; the
  story "does the heavy lifting" so he doesn't have to mimic voice "to the T."
- **Three content pillars:** personal (stories/opinions), authority (education/value), and
  growth (broad reach content) — rotate all three; growth is top-of-funnel to pull people
  into the ecosystem (newsletter → podcast) where you raise their awareness.
- Five copywriting rules: **simplify** (fifth-grade reading level; ProWritingAid /
  Hemingway app), **use stories**, **talk to the reader** ("you/your"), active over passive
  voice (with a victim-framing exception), and **use numbers** (odd numbers; get great at
  headlines — "if they don't read your first line they won't read your last").
- Book recommendations: *Writing Tools* (Roy Peter Clark), *Storyworthy* (Matthew Dicks),
  and a copywriting/psychology "beginner" book. Self-esteem turnaround credited to
  *The Six Pillars of Self-Esteem* (Nathaniel Branden): "your thoughts are not reality…
  recognize, challenge, reframe, rewire."
