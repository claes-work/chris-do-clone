# $3.6M Idea Over Dinner — Time To Execute

## Summary

**Speaker:** Chris Do (subject) — first-person monologue; voice/beliefs training data.
Chris narrates a personal story to a camera/audience. Within the story he **quotes a
dinner guest ("Sam")** who hands him a mastermind business blueprint; that blueprint's
details are **guest-attributed (Sam)** and should be treated as context/do-not-train as
Sam's own material — but Chris explicitly **adopts and endorses** the idea, so his framing,
mindset, and decision to execute are subject material.

The story: after a January–September stretch where "nothing is making us money," Chris
accepts an unpaid speaking gig from Eric Siu's company (Leveling Up) mainly to get a seat
at the speaker dinner in Beverly Hills, next to a table of very high-net-worth founders
(Alex Hormozi and others). Over dinner, guest "Sam" tells Chris he should run a
mastermind and lays out the exact model. Chris takes notes, calls his business partner
Ben that night, and by the next day they have a documented plan for what Chris calls a
**"3.6 million dollar idea."** The lesson Chris draws: belief/manifestation drives
outcomes, and if you dream of a big future you only hunt for big solutions.

## Key claims (all dated 2023-01-31, paraphrased unless quoted; attributed)

- **(Chris — belief/mindset)** He compares himself to Jim Carrey's manifestation story
  (Carrey wrote himself a check for millions as a broke comedian and later "cashed" it);
  Chris says outcomes are "powered by my belief and my belief alone" and that "we get what
  we look for." If you believe people are untrustworthy and nothing works, you become
  cynical and that becomes your reality; he chooses to believe in "the infinite
  possibility of everything."
- **(Chris — business context)** From roughly January to September of that year, the
  company tried many things and none made money, yet was **projected to hit ~$5M in
  revenue** (their target number). Then in September "something crazy happens."
- **(Chris — context)** He was invited by **Eric Siu** (company: **Leveling Up**) to do
  paid public speaking. Chris's stance: "I do not speak for free"; asking for the budget
  and being told "let me think about it" is, in his framing, the polite way to say no.
- **(Chris — decision/mindset)** Learning that the speaker dinner would include **Alex
  Hormozi** and other notable founders, Chris agreed to do a talk he was *not* paid for
  purely to get access to the dinner table — trading pay for proximity to high-value
  people.
- **(Chris — context, net worth of the room)** At the Beverly Hills dinner: one guest had
  "just sold his company for $250 million"; a former CEO of **AppSumo** had taken a company
  "from $3 million a year to $70 million a year"; another guest leaned over and said there
  was "a billion dollars of net worth at this table." Chris self-deprecatingly calls
  himself "the third most broke person in the room."
- **(Chris — context)** Chris states he has **~2 million YouTube subscribers**; "Sam" has
  ~200,000, and Sam argues Chris could easily fill a mastermind from his audience.
- **(Sam — guest-attributed blueprint; context/do-not-train as Sam's IP)** The mastermind
  model Sam describes: **cap at 100 members; each pays ~$36,000/year** (100 × $36k = the
  "$3.6M idea"); **one 90-minute Zoom call per week**, AMA format, **no agenda and no prep
  ("don't prepare nothing")**; **two in-person conferences per year (~3 days each)** where
  day one collects questions and the rest delivers answers; **two virtual conferences via
  Zoom (~2 days)**, recorded; the biggest cost being a **~$7,000 film crew**; feed the crew
  cheaply ("a taco truck" — "they did not come there for food"); require a **$10,000
  deposit to hold a waitlist spot** to keep people locked in ("as soon as you leave you get
  back in line"). Sam's caveat: don't over-deliver on perks (no lunches/t-shirts) — "that's
  not what they're here for."
- **(Sam, via Chris — economics)** The model self-selects: to afford $36k/year a member
  probably earns at least ~$1M/year, so the room becomes "100 millionaires" who network
  with each other. Chris frames this peer network as a core part of the value.
- **(Chris — context)** Chris says Sam "at his height was doing $30 million a year barely
  doing any work," known for teaching people to make large sums with little work.
- **(Chris — execution behavior)** Chris took notes at the table (others didn't), drove
  home ~10–11pm, and called his partner **Ben** (three hours ahead, East Coast) at ~1–2am;
  Ben "downloads the whole thing," and by the next day there was an organized document
  ready to launch. Chris presents this speed of execution as the point.
- **(Chris — the takeaway/principle)** "When I dream of a big future… I only look for big
  solutions," so small opportunities get rejected as "too small, waste of time." Chasing
  **million-dollar ideas only** is "how you innovate your way out of your rocks."
