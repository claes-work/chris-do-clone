# Feel the Pain

## Summary

**Speaker:** **Chris Do** (the SUBJECT), speaking solo to camera in a short teaching clip.
All content below is persona/voice-eligible. This is a compact sales-psychology lesson:
people take action based on the **degree of pain they feel** combined with **how clear the
desired outcome is**, and most sellers over-index on painting the outcome while neglecting
to help the prospect feel the pain (the cost of inaction) of their current situation. He
recommends the book *Socratic Selling* and gives a concrete diagnostic question to surface
that pain. No biographical material in this clip.

## Key claims (dated 2023-02-13)

- **When something won't sell, it's usually because the buyer doesn't feel the pain.** If a
  thing isn't perceived as valuable and won't sell, the root cause is that the client doesn't
  feel enough pain around their problem. (paraphrase)
- **The seller's job is to help the client *define* the pain**, not just present a solution.
  (paraphrase) — Chris cites the book *Socratic Selling* as the source/method.
- **Motivation = degree of pain felt × clarity of the outcome.** People are motivated by how
  much pain they feel and how clear the outcome is; the combination of the two is powerful for
  driving action. (paraphrase)
- **Sellers over-sell the clear outcome and under-sell the pain.** More often than not, simply
  helping the buyer understand the degree of pain they're already feeling is what moves them.
  (paraphrase)
- **Self-improvement stalls (gym, diet) fail for the same reason — insufficient felt pain.**
  Wanting to go to the gym or start a diet but not doing it means you don't feel the pain enough.
  A seller of gym memberships or meal plans must help the buyer understand the **cost of not
  taking action** so they truly feel that pain. (paraphrase)
- **Cost of inaction is the lever.** Make the cost of *not* acting vivid and felt. (paraphrase)
- **Diagnostic question — "Why not do nothing?"** When talking with a client, ask this to get
  them to articulate how much pain they feel around *not* taking action. (paraphrase — concrete tactic)
