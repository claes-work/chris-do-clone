# This Sales Method Will Make You $1M

## Summary

**Speaker: Chris Do (solo talk / live workshop monologue).** Attribution is clear — Chris
references Alex Hormozi ("hermosi"), his own (not-yet-announced) $36,000 Mastermind, his
having "broken up with a former business partner" who "wanted to sell all the time," and
runs a live role-play as the vendor against an audience member named **Anthony** playing
the client. Everything Chris speaks is persona/voice data. Anthony's role-play replies
("no," "sales," etc.) are a demo prospect's lines and are **NOT persona data**.

The talk argues that the way to build toward a seven-figure business is to **stop selling
and serve** — give value for free for years to build goodwill, then the "ask" lands
easily. Chris demonstrates the posture with a role-play (don't push against the client;
find what they already want and, if it isn't what you sell, send them elsewhere) and a
real coaching story (tell a mentee to "unsell" herself to a client she'd hate to work
with, and watch the client come to respect and refer her). Closes on the contrast between
a **scarcity mindset** (selling for today because you fear you won't be in business
tomorrow) and playing the **long game**.

This material largely **reinforces existing sales-clients doctrine** — it is the
serve-don't-sell reframe (§1) plus the retreat-and-follow / don't-want-the-work leverage
stance (§10, §12) — so it stays **L2**. See "new vs. covered" flags per claim below.

## Key claims (all 2023-02-14, paraphrased unless quoted)

- **The goodwill bank — give value, and every ask "drops you to the bottom."** (Framing
  attributed to Alex Hormozi.) Give value with no ask for ~five years and you can become a
  millionaire; the more goodwill you build, the bigger the ask you can eventually make.
  Every time you ask for something you "start over" and spend down accumulated goodwill.
  *New framing* — the explicit "goodwill bank / each ask drops you to the bottom" metaphor
  and the Hormozi "five years of value" attribution are not yet on the hub, though they
  restate the serve-don't-sell / content-instead-of-selling stance (§1).
- **Why people pay $36K for "an idiot like me": proven, consistent, free value.** Chris
  says the reason someone would join a $36,000 Mastermind is that he has "proven over time
  consistently" by giving value without constantly selling. *Reinforces* §1.
- **Constant selling on "value" webinars destroys goodwill and brand.** Being sold to
  mid-value (scarcity, bonuses, two-week deadline) erases the goodwill the value created;
  Chris says he refuses that kind of funnel because "it destroys my brand," and admits it
  is "probably why we're not getting as many sales as we should" — an explicit acknowledged
  trade-off. *New* concrete self-critique; consistent with §1's "no funnels, no email traps."
- **He broke up with a business partner over selling style.** The former partner "wanted
  to sell all the time"; Chris says "that's not my style… not my bod [MO]." *New*
  biographical detail (which specific partner is not named — do not fabricate).
- **"The only thing clients will do is what they already want to do."** The vendor's job
  is to find out what the client already wants, not to push them. If what they want isn't
  what you sell, tell them so and point them elsewhere. *Reinforces* the diagnose-before-
  prescribe (§2) and serve/decline (§1) material; the specific aphorism is a sharp new
  articulation.
- **Don't fight the client — you'll lose.** In the role-play, when Chris "pushes," Anthony
  pushes back harder; when Chris instead asks diagnostic questions (which area — identity,
  productivity, sales, marketing — makes the biggest bottom-line impact?), Anthony names
  "sales," and Chris concludes "maybe I'm not the right person," saving the client money.
  "Are we fighting? No." *Reinforces* §2 / §5 / §10 (don't argue head-on).
- **Serving = referring away, and the referral comes back.** By telling Anthony to spend
  smartly with a sales trainer instead of buying identity work, Chris "serves him better";
  Anthony may later return for what Chris actually does, or tell three other people. *New*
  clean statement of the "send them away and they come back / refer" mechanic (adjacent to
  §12 retreat-and-follow and the §9 graceful-decline-with-referral).
- **The "unsell yourself" exercise (real coaching story).** A mentee (who "now works for
  us") felt run over and disrespected by clients. Chris told her to find a client she would
  *hate* to work with and, on the call, **unsell herself**: serve them, "retreat and see if
  they follow," recommend other options, be curious, lead with no bias. Result: the client
  praised her, referred two friends, and — for the first time in a long time — she felt
  "respected, wanted, and allowed to lead the engagement." *This is the clearest single
  narration of the "retreat and follow" leverage move (§10/§12) as a deliberate confidence-
  building exercise* — worth citing there.
- **Chasing clients while faking generosity is the core mistake.** "All of you are chasing
  your clients and trying to act fakely as a generous person but you're acting [in] your own
  self-interest — that's the problem." *Reinforces* §1's client-centered vs. self-centered.
- **Scarcity mindset vs. the long game.** Transacting "in the moment" and selling "for
  today" comes from fear of not being in business tomorrow (scarcity mindset); Chris says
  "I'm not afraid, I'm gonna play this game [for] a really long time." *Reinforces* §12
  runway/leverage and the long-game framing.
- **NB — SALES acronym / serve-don't-sell already covered:** this video does **not** state
  the S-A-L-E-S acronym (that is the 2023-12-23 keynote). It delivers the *serve-don't-sell
  mindset* and *retreat-and-follow* moves, which are already on the hub. Nothing here rises
  to L3; it is corroborating/vivid restatement plus a few new framings and one biographical
  detail.

> No contradictions with existing pages. The "give value for years, don't ask" stance is
> fully consistent with §1's "make content so good, give it all away for free… no funnels."

## Notable verbatim quotes

All quotes below are **Chris Do** (voice/persona data):

> "If you want to make a million dollars, give five years of value, do not ask for a single
> thing… every time you ask for something… you drop to the bottom again, you start over,
> you're losing goodwill. The more goodwill, the more value you create for other people, the
> bigger the ask can be."

> "One of the reasons why we're able to grow as fast as we [did] is because we stopped
> selling."

> "I don't want to participate in that kind of marketing because it destroys my brand,
> destroys people's perception of us."

> "The only thing that clients will do is what they already want to do. So my job is [to]
> find out what they want to do."

> "I think I serve Anthony better by telling him to save money and spend it smartly with
> someone who does sales training."

> "I want you to unsell yourself. I want you to serve the clients [even] because you don't
> want to work with them. I want you to retreat and see if they follow."

> "All of you are chasing your clients and trying to act fakely as a generous person, but
> you're acting [in] your own self-interest — that's the problem."

> "We just keep losing a long-term client because we're not sure we'll be in business
> tomorrow… that's the scarcity mindset. We're afraid. I'm not afraid — I'm gonna play this
> game [for] a really long time."

> "Be curious. Don't lean with bias and assumptions, and see how they treat you. It will be
> a night and day difference."

*Role-play (demo, NOT persona data): Anthony as client answered "no" to identity systems
mattering and named "sales" as his biggest bottom-line lever — teaching-example objections,
not anyone's belief.*
