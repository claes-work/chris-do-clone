# How To Scale Your Creative Agency To A $1M Business

## Summary

**Speaker: NOT Chris Do — an unidentified GUEST presenter (context / do-not-train).**
This is a single-speaker monologue published on The Futur channel. Chris Do never
appears or speaks in the transcript. The presenter delivers a solo teaching segment on
scaling a creative agency to ~$1M, drawing explicitly on **Gino Wickman's EOS** model
(Visionary vs. Integrator / general manager) and **Daniel Priestley**, and closes with a
call-to-action for **his own live workshop** ("apply … for my live Workshop where I will
be showing you behind the scenes of what's working for other creative agencies"). Because
the material is a named third party's frameworks — not Chris Do's — none of it is persona
training data. It is filed as **context only**.

The framework presented: the entrepreneurial journey is predictable and moves through
stages. The first milestone is ~$100k (idea validated, people will pay). The next is the
"boutique business" — solo founder to a small team of ~10–12 people, revenue ~$250k–$1.2M,
which the presenter says both Daniel Priestley and Gino Wickman regard as an ideal
boutique lifestyle-business structure. The roles that must be filled: **Visionary**
(big-picture direction), **general manager** (keeps the business running, sense-checks and
grounds the Visionary), **admin/finance**, **sales & marketing**, and **operations/
delivery**. Founders fail by wearing too many hats: the moment they step away from a role
they're personally performing (e.g. sales & marketing), that function "falls off a cliff."
The fix is to **systemize** each function once it works so it keeps running without the
founder — "one brick at a time." The stated end-state: the founder does **no more than two
roles**, and the business survives a six-week founder absence.

## Key claims

All dated 2023-03-01. **Speaker = guest (NOT Chris Do) — context only, not persona.**
Paraphrased unless quoted below.

- **Growth stages are predictable.** First milestone ~$100k = idea validated / people will
  pay enough to grow. Next = "boutique" stage: solo founder → small team of 10–12,
  revenue ~$250k–$1.2M. (guest, context)
- **The boutique/lifestyle structure is an ideal target** — attributed to agreement
  between **Daniel Priestley** and **Gino Wickman** (EOS). (guest, context)
- **Five role areas must be filled:** Visionary, general manager, admin/finance,
  sales & marketing, operations/delivery. (guest, context — this is EOS-derived)
- **Visionary vs. Integrator dynamic:** the general manager grounds the Visionary and
  sense-checks their ideas; cites Steve Jobs (Visionary) and Steve Wozniak as an analogy
  for the pairing (explicitly noting Wozniak was not literally the "general manager").
  (guest, context)
- **Founders wear too many hats and do each role ineffectively.** Moving between Visionary,
  delivery, finance, admin and sales at once makes things break down. (guest, context)
- **When you stop personally doing a working function, it collapses** unless systemized —
  e.g. win clients by networking, then delivery pulls you away and sales/marketing "falls
  off a cliff." (guest, context)
- **Systemize what works so it runs without you** — "build a system around this … once
  something is working we then systemize it." Build like a house, one brick at a time;
  always ask "what's my next hire and what's the next system to build." (guest, context)
- **Founder should end up doing no more than two roles**, with a team that lets the founder
  choose which projects to work on and lets the business survive a six-week absence.
  (guest, context)
