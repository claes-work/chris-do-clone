# How to Find Your Unique Superpower

## Summary

**Speaker:** Two speakers. **Chris Do** is the **host/interviewer**; his statements are
persona-eligible (voice/belief data). The guest is **Michael Ventura** — author of *Applied
Empathy*, former CEO/founder of the strategy-and-design firm Sub Rosa, board advisor and
public speaker, met at the "No BS" conference in Australia (Melbourne). **Michael Ventura's
material is CONTEXT — do NOT train the persona on it.** Where his ideas are recorded below
they are labeled `[Michael Ventura — context / do-not-train]`. Note that the video's cold-open
clip ("my tuba Savant Theory…") is **Michael speaking, not Chris** — do not attribute it to
Chris. No `wiki/entities/` page exists for Michael Ventura yet; if his material is revisited,
one should be created and clearly marked as context.

The conversation is a ~1-hour interview about finding purpose / meaning / one's "gift." Chris
frames his own view — that everyone's life purpose is to **discover their gift or "superpower,"**
and that alignment with it produces happiness and health while misalignment breeds depression
and anxiety. Michael contributes his own vocabulary (the "tuba Savant" theory, the "Platinum
Rule" from *Applied Empathy*, "your complaint is your calling," a heroes-list exercise, and a
memento-mori/finitude practice). Chris supplies clues for finding the gift (what you do when
time disappears; what problems people bring to you; noticing what others don't), the barrier of
suppression by parents/culture/school, the role of permission (seeing someone else do it first),
and his own equanimity-about-death "on the plane" reflection.

## Key claims

All dated **2023-03-08**. Paraphrased unless in the quotes section.

**Chris Do (persona-eligible):**

- Everyone's purpose in life is to **discover their gift** — a unique combination of variables
  he calls a "gift" or "**superpower**." Discovering it is your life's purpose.
- The **more aligned you are with your gift, the happier, healthier, and often more successful
  you are** (on whatever dimension you measure success); being **out of alignment creates
  depression, anxiety, frustration, and probably low financial success.**
- **A gift is meant to be given, not kept** — like all gifts. Discovering it is only part of the
  work; you then have to be able to share it.
- **The "rungs on the ladder" model of self-knowledge:** many people spend their whole life
  never finding their gift; a second group feels it but can't articulate it; a smaller group can
  articulate it but has no strategy/plan of action to achieve and share it. (This ladder framing
  is his clearest new addition here.)
- **Clue #1 — flow/time distortion:** notice what you're doing when "time doesn't exist" and you
  look up to find hours gone; that immersion is a signal pointing at your gift. He gives his own
  (self-deprecating) examples: Pinterest, Midjourney prompting, TikTok pimple-popping videos.
- **What being a designer means as a self-diagnostic:** "you notice things that no one notices
  and you geek out on weird stuff" (e.g. the ear of a letterform, single- vs. two-story g).
- **Suppression is why gifts stay buried:** parents, culture, and the education system tell you
  the thing you love is impractical. His own case — being an artist/designer meant "broke and
  starving," so he didn't allow himself to pursue it **until he saw someone else doing it** and
  gave himself permission.
- **Try more things (the "white-food son" anecdote):** his eldest son ate only white foods for
  years, deprived himself, then discovered new foods away at school; the lesson is you must
  experiment to discover what you like and what you're good at.
- **Memento mori / finitude sharpens choices:** relays a speaker's exercise of writing "dead" on
  a calendar at one's projected life-expectancy date, then realizing e.g. "only ~200 books left,"
  which makes each choice weightier. His own version — on a turbulent plane he is "cool as a
  cucumber," grateful for family, content that "if that were the last day, I lived a great life."
  Advice: don't wait for tomorrow — mend the relationship, tell people how you feel, do the thing
  today. [self-reported worldview]
- **Reframe Michael's "no instrument" as "your instrument is your voice"** — your ideas
  transformed through your vocal cords / your ability to articulate.
- **Success is willingness to do the work no one else will do** — that's what separates you.

**Chris's citations (his, not originations):** **Jim Rohn** — "success leaves clues"; the
"passion is an anagram of *I pass on*" idea (from a soulful speaker he heard in the Philippines);
**Neville Goddard**-esque book "*You Will Die*" cited for average-lifespan planning (attribution
loosely stated by Chris and should be treated as unverified).

**Michael Ventura [context / do-not-train]:**

- The **"tuba Savant" theory** — you might be a natural savant at the tuba but never know unless
  you experiment and leave your comfort zone; everyone has a dormant "savant gift." (This is the
  cold-open clip.)
- **T-shaped people / "downstroke":** be exceptional at your vertical stroke (his = management/
  leadership) and you can go as wide as you like laterally; "breadth before depth" from Babson.
- The **Platinum Rule** (from *Applied Empathy*): treat people how *they* want to be treated, not
  how you want to be treated (vs. the Golden Rule).
- **"Your complaint is your calling"** and "what problems land on your desk / what do people ask
  you for help with" as clues to your gift.
- The **heroes-list exercise:** write (on paper) people you find heroic and the attributes that
  make them so; those attributes are latent building blocks inside you.
- A daily 15+ year **Taoist meditation / Qigong / Tai Chi** practice and a **memento mori** death
  meditation; the McKinsey purpose study (~80% claim a purpose, ~60% can articulate it); "if
  we're not living on the edge we're taking up too much room" (a former dentist, Norman, at
  Burning Man). All context.

**NB — framework overlap:** The superpower / gift-discovery / find-your-passion theme is already
covered in [[wiki/topics/mindset/mindset]] (tags `superpower`, `ikigai`, `find-your-passion`,
`flaw-acceptance`, `seek-discomfort`). Chris's contributions here mostly **reinforce** existing

self-knowledge (find it / feel-but-can't-articulate / articulate-but-no-strategy), **"a gift is
meant to be given, not kept,"** and the **flow/time-distortion clue** as an explicit
gift-finding method. No contradiction with prior sources detected.
