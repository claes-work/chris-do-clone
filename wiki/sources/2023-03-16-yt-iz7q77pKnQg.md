# 5 Things I Do Every Day To Win New Clients

## Summary

**Speaker: Chris Do** (solo first-person monologue — "every time I contact someone new,"
"when I'm thinking about new clients"). This is persona voice data; no other speaker
appears. The clip is a short, self-contained teaching piece that ends with a promo for
Chris's live workshop.

Chris lays out a **five-part checklist he runs on every new cold or semi-cold outreach**
(email, LinkedIn DM, etc.) designed to boost response rate and build authentic
connection: **(1) Reason, (2) Genuine compliment, (3) Anchor, (4) Value, (5) The ask.**
The reframe at the core: he does **not** approach outreach thinking "how do I get this
person as a client," but "how do I hit these five points and build authentic, valuable
connections." Winning clients is downstream of building rapport and giving value first.

## Key claims

All claims dated **2023-03-16**, paraphrased, attributed to **Chris Do**.

Every new outreach should hit all five points:

1. **Reason** — a clear reason *why* you are reaching out to this specific person. (Chris)
2. **Genuine compliment** — something you actually like about them (not flattery). (Chris)
3. **Anchor** — something that ties the two of you together / common ground: same school,
   a shared acquaintance, similar projects in the industry, shared passions. Common
   ground is "ultimately what builds rapport." (Chris)
4. **Value** — what value you're offering; it should be **about them, not you**, and is
   usually **free or relatively low-priced** with clearly articulated value. It can be as
   simple as an article you've read, a person you can introduce them to, or something
   you've created (a book, a checklist). (Chris)
5. **The ask** — a small, easy ask: "Would you like me to send it over?", "Would you like
   me to introduce you to that person?", "Would you like to hop on a quick call to see if
   there's any way we can collaborate?" (Chris)

- Having all five in any cold or semi-cold outreach makes you "almost guaranteed to boost
  your response rate" and the chance of connecting authentically. (Chris)
- **Mindset reframe:** when pursuing new clients or expanding his network, Chris is not
  thinking "how do I get this person as a client" but "how do I hit these five points and
  build authentic, valuable connections with people." (Chris)
- Worked LinkedIn example he narrates: *"Hey, the reason I'm getting in touch is because
  your content keeps popping up on my feed. I really like it because it's different… it
  touches on something I really care about"* — then link an anchor ("I also care about
  this topic / I've been researching this / I worked on this brand") before offering value
  and making the ask. (Chris)

**Relation to existing wiki material:** the underlying *give-value-first / lead-gen*
philosophy overlaps with already-ingested attraction-marketing and give-first content;
what is **new here** is (a) the packaged 5-slot outreach checklist and (b) the **"anchor"**
building-block for rapport. No contradictions with prior sources — this is consistent with
and operationalizes the give-first stance.
