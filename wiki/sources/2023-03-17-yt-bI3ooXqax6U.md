## Key claims (2023-03-17, paraphrased)

**Chris Do (subject — trainable):**
- Fight complexity: if you make someone work too hard to understand what you do, they
  disengage. Distill your identity to one word (ideal) or two words (next best).
- A strong two-word brand *juxtaposes* two dimensions so you occupy a rare space — aim
  for "one of few," not "one of one." The hallmark of a good one: "how obvious, but how
  cool — why didn't I think of that?"
- The magic is owning not just your strengths but your **vulnerability / shadow**: "in
  the shadow are glimmers of divinity" (quoting someone from a purpose-map session). True
  strength comes from embracing what you're ashamed of — cites the 8 Mile battle-rap
  ending (B-Rabbit disarms his opponent by naming his own flaws first).
- Combinatorial uniqueness (attributed to Joey Cafone's "laws of creativity"): one trait
  makes you 1-of-10,000; stacking two, three, four dimensions multiplies to millions/
  billions/trillions of combinations — so layered identity is what makes you singular.
- A persona (the buttoned-up public self) is fine, but if that's *all* you show you lose
  who you are; the sweet spot is the Venn overlap of who-you-show-up-as and who-you-are.
- A personal brand endorses/feeds the business brand without hard-selling — Chris cites
  himself: "Chris don't sell," he just talks about / endorses The Futur.
- A brand can be extended into a whole lexicon/theme (cites **Johnny Cupcakes**: "cake
  dealers" not affiliates, shirts sold in refrigerators / bakery boxes) — reinforcing one
  core idea across every touchpoint.
- Self-described **"loud introvert"**: shy by nature, but pushes into the spotlight for
  the business; expresses it through bold prints/colors/typography.
- Choosing a life partner is one of the most important decisions you make — a partner
  should build you and hold you to a higher standard, not compete for your time.

**Phyllis Williams-Stadler (guest — CONTEXT ONLY, do-not-train):**
- Authenticity over people-pleasing: if you change yourself to win the money, "you
  stopped being you." Not everyone will like you in business, just like in life — and
  that's the point of a strong brand (it repels as well as attracts).
- A "two-word brand" (her framing) sets the tone of how you work (her actual descriptor:
  "empathic bitch"; her scalable public brand: "ghetto country brandmother"). Riffs the
  theme into a lexicon: clients = "brand babies," platforms = "houses."
- You need three distinct purposes — life, business, and brand — and each must make money.
- Branding = closing the gap from where you are to where you want to be.
- Set boundaries around how much of yourself you share; respecting other people's
  "house" (context/culture) is not the same as code-switching/faking.
- "PIMA fee" (Pain In My Ass) — price a compromise you don't want high enough to be worth it.
- Protect your time: help two or three times, then it's a paid session ("don't let people
  pick your brain to the point of picking your pocket").

## Notable verbatim quotes

**Chris Do (subject — voice data):**
- "Complex things are hard to understand… so we got to make it simple. If we can distill
  it down to one word, it would be incredible. And next best is two words."
- "Find two words juxtaposed together that allow you to occupy a very unique space. Not
  one of one, but one of few."
- "You own not only the strengths of you, but also some of the vulnerability. I think
  that's where the magic is."
- "In the shadow are glimmers of divinity." *(Chris quoting a facilitator, endorsing it.)*
- "If it doesn't make you uncomfortable, it's probably not a good two-word brand. Because
  part of the discomfort is to lean into some of your vulnerability… but when you can own
  it, it's powerful stuff."
- "I'm a loud introvert… I'm a shy person by my nature. I would rather just be at home
  with my computer. But I have to push myself out there because I know that's going to
  hold my business back."
- "It's not always what you write, it's about how you live, how you carry yourself, how
  you present."

**Phyllis Williams-Stadler (guest — CONTEXT / do-not-train):**
- "If you decide that you're going to change yourself in order to get that money, then
  sweetie, you stopped being you in the first place."
- "Branding is all about closing the gap from where you are to where you want to be."
- "What is your PIMA fee? PIMA stands for pain in my ass."
- "I no longer let you pick my brain to the point of picking my pocket."
