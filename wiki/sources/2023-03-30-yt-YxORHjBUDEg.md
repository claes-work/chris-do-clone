# Customers, Not Companies, Create the Brand

## Summary

**Speaker: NOT Chris Do — this clip is dominated by a GUEST who refers to himself as
"Kevin" (line 86: "people say so how does that work Kevin and I genuinely say…").**

⚠️ **CONTEXT / DO-NOT-TRAIN.** The core framework claims below are the **guest's**, not
Chris Do's. Do not flow any of these into `beliefs.md` / `voice.md` as Chris material.
Only interviewer prompts could be Chris, and those are generic and attribution-uncertain.

The clip argues that a brand is not something a company declares about itself — a brand
exists only when **society/customers agree it exists**. It distinguishes **brand** (who
you are — the sum of promises, expressions, feelings, impressions people hold about you)
from **branding** (the tools, frameworks, visuals and language used to build it). It
extends this to ethics: toxic brands are still brands, but their toxicity erodes
relevance and revenue over time, and consumers' spending is treated as a "vote" for the
world they want.

## Key claims

All dated **2023-03-30**. Paraphrased unless quoted. **All attributed to the GUEST
("Kevin") unless noted — context/do-not-train for the Chris persona.**

- **(Guest)** A brand becomes a brand only when society says so, not when the business
  declares it. If a business claims "we're a brand," that claim itself is suspect — like
  someone calling themselves "cool." *(This is the title's thesis: customers, not
  companies, create the brand.)*
  - **NB — NOT new for this clone.** This is the same customers'/audience-owned brand
    idea already documented on [[wiki/topics/branding/branding]] as **Marty Neumeier's**
    "a brand is a gut feeling / result that lives in other people's heads… you're
    creating millions of brands." Here it is a **guest** re-stating that idea in his own
    words, not Chris originating anything. Adds nothing new to Chris's persona.
- **(Guest)** **Brand ≠ branding.** A *brand* is who you are over a long period (the sum
  of all experiences, feelings and impressions you create); *branding* is the toolkit —
  frameworks, visuals, language — applied at a point in time to build it.
- **(Guest)** Not every business is a brand, **but any business can adopt a "brand
  mindset."** (The interviewer calls this framing "empowering.")
- **(Guest)** **"Impact = relevance = revenue."** Negative impact lowers relevance and so
  lowers revenue; positive impact raises both. A toxic brand is still a brand, but fewer
  people show up for it over time.
- **(Guest)** Toxic brands can still be successful when (1) there is no viable
  alternative, or (2) customers are locked into an incentivized/systemized arrangement
  and accept the cost. Given an equivalent replacement, people "jump ship in a second."
- **(Guest)** Our relationship with brands mirrors our relationship with ourselves — we
  tolerate patterns we know are unhealthy; a brand that mostly behaves ethically is
  forgiven occasional "trip-ups," while sustained bad behavior *becomes who you are.*
- **(Guest)** Consumer spending is a vote: "every dollar we spend is a vote for the kind
  of world we want to live in" (attributed by the guest to **Anna Lappé**). Designers and
  advisers have a role in warning organizations that they must change or disappear.
