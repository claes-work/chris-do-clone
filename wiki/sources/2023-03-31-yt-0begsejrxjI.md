## Key claims (dated 2023-03-31)

**Guest-attributed (Micah Salidis — CONTEXT, do-not-train):**
- *"Authentic" is often a workaround word* (paraphrase): brands reach for "authentic" before
  they have actually figured out the character or spirit they claim to be true to; the real
  work is workshops and interviews to define that spirit so designers/writers have an
  actionable foundation.
- *The "we're X, not Y" exercise* (paraphrase): defining a brand by what it is *not* is as
  important as what it is — but the "not" word should not be a strawman nobody would choose
  (avoid "authentic not fake"); split hairs instead ("rebellious but not brash").
- *Brand strategy = making the intangible tangible / "intuition on paper"* (paraphrase):
  codifying a client's gut feeling and giving them the reason it is correct.
- *Take the client on the journey* (paraphrase): spend as much time on the setup/narrative
  (audience, culture, competition insights) as on the positioning statement; a recent deck was
  "70% what we decided NOT to do." Clients pay for the thinking, not just the deliverable.
- *Research is real research* (paraphrase): stakeholder interviews (once ~47 people before any
  strategy doc), workshops, desk research — not browsing Pinterest/Behance. Big-budget
  segmentation/focus-group work is outsourced to specialist firms.
- *Workshops* (paraphrase): held at the start (or end) of a project, pulling people from all
  departments and levels (C-suite to coordinators) onto whiteboards; one-on-one interviews
  substitute when juniors won't speak candidly in front of a CEO.
- *Marvel global-positioning pitch* (paraphrase): ~40% of winning it was the creative, ~60%
  was showing "we get it" — he immersed himself at WonderCon to live the fans' lifestyle.

**Chris Do-attributed (host reflections — restate existing positions, NOT new):**
- *Focus / market-splitting* (paraphrase): credits **Seth Godin** — keep splitting the market
  until you are clear who you stand for; pick words with power but that not everyone can claim.
  (Already documented as Godin influence in the branding hub.)
- *Bigger firms take clients on the journey, not a "creative-cave" reveal* (paraphrase): on
  high-stakes work the win is buy-in — many milestone meetings so at the end stakeholders say
  "of course," not "ta-da." Restates existing Framework 24/30 material (design solves a
  business problem; strategist takes the client through the reasoning).
- *Paul Rand reference point* (paraphrase): Chris cites Rand's process books — presenting a
  logo via a logical narrative of ideation, and Rand's "one logo, no revisions, you pay me
  either way" stance — as the model for showing decision logic.
- *"Wisdom is experience plus reflection"* (paraphrase, source unremembered) — a mindset aside.
