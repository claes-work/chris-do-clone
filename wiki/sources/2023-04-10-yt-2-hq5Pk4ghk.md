# 3 Common Business Mistakes That Entrepreneurs Make

## Summary

The guest argues that scaling a creative agency (design/animation studio) is blocked by
three recurring mistakes and offers a one-line fix for each: (1) the services are too
**complex** and ever-changing → **simplify the offer**; (2) the delivery know-how is all
**in the founder's head** → **document it / "unpack your IP"** into a followable system;
(3) the studio has the **wrong team** — top-heavy with more designers/animators but
missing the essential roles that build the business — → figure out the target roles and
structure, then hire for them.

## Key claims

_All dated 2023-04-10. Paraphrased unless quoted. **Speaker = unnamed guest, not Chris
Do — none of this trains the persona.**_

- **[Guest]** Three big mistakes he sees business owners make: services too complex,
  everything in the founder's head, and not having the right team.
- **[Guest]** Complex = ever-changing: a different thing for every client or brief, which
  makes it very hard to build systems around.
- **[Guest]** Over the last 12–18 months, in his own and his clients' businesses, the
  **simpler** things are made, the **faster** they grow, the more impact, and the easier
  it is to build systems. (Self-reported.)
- **[Guest]** He expected simplifying to reduce creativity, but the opposite happened —
  simplifying gave him **more** room to be creative because he understood the
  fundamentals.
- **[Guest]** Photography analogy: a mentor told him to boil photography down to three
  basics — **shutter speed, depth of field / aperture, and ISO** — and mastering those
  fundamentals unlocked far more creativity. Same principle applies to business.
- **[Guest]** "Everything in your head" is like driving a car on autopilot — you get
  great results but can't explain the blueprint, so you can't teach it or systematize it.
- **[Guest]** Fix: **"unpack your IP"** — get the intellectual property out of your head
  and onto paper into a system someone else can follow.
- **[Guest]** Growing agencies get **top-heavy**: as they scale they keep hiring more
  designers/animators to deliver work while missing key roles needed to build the
  business holistically.
- **[Guest]** Fix for the team problem: first define what the right team looks like — the
  roles, the structure, who sits where — driven by the systems you've decided to build,
  then make the hire to match.
- **[Guest — CTA]** Invites viewers to apply, via the link, for his live workshop showing
  behind-the-scenes of what's working for other creative agencies plus a custom growth
  roadmap.
