# Brand Principles For The 21st Century with Kevin Finn (some explicit language)

## Summary

**Speakers:** **Kevin Finn** (guest) — an Australian brand-identity designer/writer based in
Brisbane, ~30 years in the industry — delivers a recorded conference-style presentation

**Who trains the persona:** essentially **nothing** here trains the Chris Do persona. Per the

is a coherent branding framework a viewer would associate with the @thefutur brand.

The talk argues that **designers do not "build brands" — they build the "canvas" (the logo /
identity) onto which meaning is applied over time.** Kevin distinguishes a *brand* (who you
are, how people feel about you, the sum of experiences, a promise) from *branding* (how you
articulate and communicate that — where designers are useful). He shares 5 of his 15 "brand
principles," then pivots to a thesis about the **future of business being "impact"**, closing
with a personal story (losing both parents to cancer) that grounds his mantra of "positive
impact at scale."

## Key claims

_All dated 2023-04-11. Paraphrased unless quoted. Speaker prefix marks attribution; **all
non-Chris claims are context only.**_

- **[Kevin Finn / guest — context]** "The logo doesn't make the brand; the brand gives the
  logo its value." Designers who claim "we build brands" because they designed a logo are
  overclaiming — a logo/identity is a **canvas for meaning** that absorbs associations over
  decades. Illustrated with three cases: the **Chase Manhattan Bank** octagon (Chermayeff &
  Geismar — the symbol meant "nothing" and became a vessel for meaning), **Canterbury** (NZ
  apparel; three circles = three founders / three C's, kiwi silhouette = locale), and
  **Supreme** (1994 Brooklyn skate shop, logo appropriated from artist **Barbara Kruger**;
  ranked most powerful fashion logo 2018, valued ~$1B in 2018, ~$2B in 2019). Nike's swoosh
  (1973) "meant very little" then and is hugely valuable now.
- **[Kevin Finn / guest — context]** Designers can only **help** build a brand (not build it)
  in three cases: (1) as a **long-term strategic partner** engaged with the executive/board,
  possibly holding equity; (2) as the **founder/owner** using design daily across the whole
  organization; (3) working **in-house** with enough seniority and a genuinely design-led
  employer. All three are long-term, whole-of-organization efforts — not a six-week/six-month
  external logo engagement.
- **[Kevin Finn / guest — context]** His definitions: a **brand** = who you are, how you're
  perceived, how people feel about your business, its core value and promise, the sum of all
  experiences someone has with you — and it **cannot be manufactured by an external
  consultant** (or you'd be living up to someone else's vision). **Branding** = how you
  appropriately articulate/communicate that across channels — "this is where designers are
  useful."
- **[Kevin Finn / guest — context]** On **purpose-driven** business: "making money is a
  *function* / objective of business, not a purpose." Cites author **Anna Lappé** ("every time
  you spend money you're casting a vote for the type of world you want to live in") and
  **BlackRock CEO Larry Fink** (without a sense of purpose a company loses its "license to
  operate"; $9.5T AUM as of Oct 2021; annual CEO letters). His definition of purpose-driven:
  putting something with a bigger positive impact **above and before profit** — moving money
  from the *metric* to the *outcome*.
- **[Kevin Finn / guest — context]** The **five brand principles** shared (of 15): (1) a brand
  is "the most valuable real estate in the world — a corner of someone's mind" (Kevin credits
  the first three-quarters of this to adman **Sir John Hegarty**; Kevin adds "and a relevant
  place in their life"); (3) a brand is **internal before external** — staff are your greatest
  ambassadors, and if staff learn "who we are" only from external marketing, that's a mess
  "waiting to explode"; a brand is not a department but "from receptionist to CEO"; mission/
  vision/value statements matter **internally** but customers don't care about the statements,
  only the value they deliver; (12) a brand is a **long-term, evolving objective** that
  "cannot be outsourced." (Kevin numbers these 1, 3, and 12.)
- **[Kevin Finn / guest — context]** "**I don't think every business is a brand, and in fact
  most of them aren't — and that's okay.**" What matters is adopting a **brand mindset**.
- **[Kevin Finn / guest — context]** The **future is "impact."** *Purpose is motivational;
  impact is measurable* — you need both (purpose without measurement = "words on a poster in
  the staff kitchen"; impact without purpose = scattershot). Uses **Edward de Bono**'s
  (coiner of "lateral thinking"; whom Kevin calls "the forefather of design thinking")
  riverbank-factory example — swap a factory's water intake and outtake so it must clean its
  own wastewater — which "later became law in some countries."
- **[Kevin Finn / guest — context]** Evidence the shift is "here": a 2015 study (91% of
  millennials would switch to a cause-associated brand); a 2018 **Harvard Business Review**
  study (9 in 10 people willing to trade ~**20% of future salary** for meaningful work — Kevin
  says the 21st-century definition of essentials becomes "food, clothing, shelter, and
  meaningful work"). Corporate examples: **Lego** (plant-based/recycled bricks by 2030),
  **McDonald's** purpose pledges, **Aldi** (CEO "Charles Hurley" — likely garble, see captions
  — non-negotiable recyclable-packaging demand to suppliers), Australian startup **Great Wrap**
  (compostable cling-wrap alternative), the **European Central Bank** assessing climate risk on
  bank balance sheets, and Germany's 2022 **€9/month unlimited train** program (cut ~1.8M tons
  CO2, dropped air pollution ~7%, funded by an energy windfall tax).
- **[Kevin Finn / guest — context]** His incentivized model: **"impact = relevance = revenue"**
  (revenue swappable for "return": reputation, reach, measurement). Put money at the *end* where
  it belongs; positive impact makes you relevant, relevance drives transactions/revenue, and
  revenue reinvested in impact compounds. He predicts **ROI will evolve into "Return On
  Impact."**
- **[Kevin Finn / guest — context]** On storytelling for brands: "**no one cares about your
  well-crafted story; what they care about is where your story shows up in their story**" —
  relevance is showing the brand appearing in the customer's/community's story.
- **[Kevin Finn / guest — context, personal]** Closing personal story: he lost his **mother to
  cancer in 2013 (age 64)** and later his **father (age 70)**, both after four-year battles.
  The lesson — "we don't have time to [expletive] around" because we don't know how much time
  we have — drove his decision to work only with meaningful organizations and good people, his
  mantra "**have a positive impact at scale**," and to write a book (a "medium that could
  scale"). Closes: "every day we are designing our future… and history is watching."
