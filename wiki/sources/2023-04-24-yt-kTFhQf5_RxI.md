# Raising Your Graphic Design Rates from $2k to $5k

## Summary

Short clip (~2 min) in interview/coaching format. **Chris Do** questions a guest — a
designer running the productized subscription service **DesignJoy** (identity strongly
implied but not stated on-screen; likely Brett Williams — `attribution: uncertain`) —
about what happened to client quality and demand as the guest raised the monthly rate of
his design subscription. The core lesson Chris draws out: **raising prices attracts
better, more design-literate clients rather than pricing you out of the market.** The
guest recounts going from a $450/month start, to $2k/month, to a current $5k/month, and
reports that each rate increase counterintuitively *increased* demand while improving
client quality. Chris frames the takeaway as a psychological hurdle creatives must live
through to believe. Ends with a CTA to a pricing/value masterclass video.

**Speakers:** Chris Do = interviewer/coach (persona-eligible). Guest = DesignJoy founder,
context only (not persona; identity `attribution: uncertain`). The guest's business-result
claims are self-reported.

## Key claims

_All dated 2023-04-24. Paraphrased unless quoted below._

- **[Chris Do]** If you want higher-quality clients, charge more — the buyer is
  psychologically *different* once your price crosses a certain threshold.
- **[Chris Do]** Getting over the "charge more to get better clients" idea is a
  psychological hurdle many creatives struggle with; he credits the guest ("Brett") with
  having said the same.
- **[Chris Do]** Frames the logic as a testable ladder: if raising 2K→3K→4K→5K each
  produced better clients, then 7K/8K/9K/10K should, by the same logic, be better still —
  and gets the guest to agree "yes."
- **[Chris Do]** People can hear this taught for years but can't internalize it until they
  live it — likens it to not believing fire burns until you put your hand in it. ("The
  Human Condition.")
- **[Guest / DesignJoy — self-reported]** Started charging **$450/month**, later **$2k/
  month**, now at **$5k/month**; says he has "seen the full scale" of what clients look
  like across that range.
- **[Guest — self-reported]** At $2k/month, senior-level design work was "a steal"; he
  felt he was underutilizing his skills and undercharging for the value delivered.
- **[Guest — self-reported]** The jump to $5k/month was an "instantaneous turning point"
  in every aspect of the business — and demand did **not** slow; it *increased*.
- **[Guest — self-reported]** Higher-paying clients understand and are bought into the
  process, and treat design as a *core* part of their business, not a peripheral part —
  which he calls a game changer.
- **[Guest — self-reported]** He considers **$5k/month (~$70k/year)** still *low* for a
  senior designer, noting a normal-market senior rate of roughly **$100–120** (per hour,
  implied — `number: uncertain`); says he could charge more but keeps rates where his
  stress stays manageable, working ~six hours a day.
- **[Guest — self-reported]** His intent in raising prices was **not** to make more money
  or get more customers, but to *slow down* an overwhelming inflow of demand
  ("drinking out of a fire hose") — and each increase had the opposite effect, bringing
  *more* demand.
- **[Guest]** Advises designers who lack the skill/speed for premium work to still serve
  lower markets and do the best they can at their current level.
