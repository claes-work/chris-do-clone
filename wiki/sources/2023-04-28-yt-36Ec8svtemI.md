### Guest (context only — does NOT train the persona)

- **[Guest]** YouTube is arguably the most challenging platform because every skill set
  converges: a still image for the thumbnail, good copy for the title, and a
  thoughtfully produced multi-minute video — which is also where the opportunity lies.
- **[Guest]** There is no ideal video length "in today's world" — "as long as it needs
  to be but as short as possible" while delivering value.
- **[Guest]** Riffs on a sales phrase, "a confused mind never buys," extending it to
  "the confused entrepreneur doesn't take action" — people research strategy, feel
  overwhelmed, and stop. Remedy: "punch fear in the face, punch perfectionism in the
  face, and press record."
- **[Guest]** Lowest-friction start: flip your smartphone to landscape; you don't need
  portrait like other platforms.
- **[Guest]** **AVLS gear priorities** (audio, video, lighting, stabilization): audio
  matters most — a cheap lavalier/clip mic for iPhone or USB-C, wireless, "as cheap as
  $25 or less" from third-party brands (no need to pay more for a logo); sit by a window
  or use a simple ~$50 soft-box light; stabilize with a tripod/phone mount on a stool at
  a proper angle. You can walk behind the phone, hit record, sit, talk, trim the front
  and back, and upload straight from the phone with no editing.
- **[Guest]** **ASQ method — Answer Specific Questions:** as a professional you already
  field the top 10–20 questions in your field; turn one question into one video. Frame it
  like a coffee chat with a friend and just deliver value (e.g., a real-estate agent
  answering "how do I know how much house I can afford?").
- **[Guest]** Start with **beginner and basic answers**, not advanced material. The
  "curse of knowledge" stops most people because they assume a question is too obvious or
  already answered — but there are always new people seeking clear answers, and little
  real competition.
- **[Guest]** Example: a Snohomish County (north of Seattle) real-estate market has
  essentially one inconsistent creator posting updates — evidence the niche is far from
  saturated even in 2023.
- **[Guest]** Cites a favorite channel, "**Clear Value Tax**," as proof production value
  isn't the barrier: the host stands at a wall with good lighting/audio and a clipboard,
  delivers info in ~7–8 minutes, and pulls 200k–400k views — "not that charismatic," but
  clear, brief, and concise.
- **[Guest]** Advice to start: use the phone you already have, keep it simple, answer
  specific questions, "be brief, be bright, be fun, and be done," upload, and keep going
  — one thing and one upload at a time. "Your hesitation is why 99% of your competitors
  are not going to take action."
