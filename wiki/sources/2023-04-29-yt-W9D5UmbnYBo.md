## Summary

Short-form (~54-line) teaching clip on a single sales idea: in a sales/discovery
conversation, **don't be afraid to give away high-level strategy**. The unnamed speaker
opens with an anecdote — a well-crafted LinkedIn cold pitch to launch a podcast (the
pitcher's value proposition: they'd secure sponsors *before* launch, and that sponsor
money would fund production, a "win-win"). The speaker was sold on the idea until asking
"what's the strategy?" and hearing that strategy only comes *after* the prospect signs and
becomes a client. The lesson: the "light-bulb insight" wasn't followed by a visible
path from A to B, so the prospect couldn't yet commit. Had the pitcher sketched a
framework/outline of how it would actually work, the speaker says he'd have been "totally
on board." The takeaway: give away the high-level strategy so the prospect gains
**clarity on what's preventing them from reaching their goals**, uncover a "didn't think
about that" moment, and present high-level ideas about how to fix those problems. The clip
ends with a CTA to apply for the speaker's live workshop.
