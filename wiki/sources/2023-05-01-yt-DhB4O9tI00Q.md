# Will AI End Creative Professions? (AMA at Art Center College of Design)

## Summary

Short (~2.5 min) solo talk: **Chris Do** addresses a room of students during an AMA
session at **Art Center College of Design**, responding to fears that AI platforms
(ChatGPT, Midjourney) will displace creatives — artists, illustrators, photographers,
designers. **This is an early-2023 AI position** (published 2023-05-01, roughly the
ChatGPT-4 / early-generative-AI moment) and should be dated as such: Chris's AI views
evolve over time, so nothing here is his "current" stance.

**Speakers:** Chris Do is the speaker throughout and the sole persona-eligible voice.
The only other voice is an **unnamed student** who asks the opening question (context
only — see below); a former student who runs an AI company and Chris's software-engineer
brother are **referenced by Chris**, not present as speakers. There is no guest
co-instructor in this clip.

The student's premise: with AI, projects get done faster at good quality, so won't
creatives be pushed into smaller-scoped work and needing more clients (a volume shop)?
Chris **rejects the premise** ("your question has an assumption baked into it… I'm not
ready to resign that that is true") and reframes AI as leverage for first movers rather
than a race to the bottom. He tells the story of "**dobot**" — an AI clone of himself a
former student is building by feeding it his book, his Business Bootcamp course, and all
his videos — and sketches AI-powered product ideas (a video-title generator, a thumbnail
generator). His thesis: as tools get more sophisticated, demand rises for people who can
**talk to the machine** — those who understand art history, logic, art direction, and can
articulate what they want. He closes with his signature bear metaphor: "**AI is the bear;
don't be the slowest person.**"

## Key claims (paraphrased, DATED 2023-05-01, speaker-attributed)

_All Chris Do unless marked. Paraphrased unless quoted in the next section. This is an
**early-2023 AI stance** — flag as such wherever it flows onward._

### Context-only (NOT Chris; does not train persona)

- **[Unnamed student — context only]** Frames the worry: AI makes projects faster at good
  quality, so creatives will end up doing smaller-scoped work and needing more clients to
  stay afloat. This is the assumption Chris pushes back on, not his own view.

### Chris Do (persona-eligible)

- **[Chris Do]** **Rejects the baked-in premise.** He is "not ready to resign" that AI
  will force creatives into smaller scopes / more clients / a volume shop; he refuses to
  accept a future where "clients want more for less money and you're going to turn into a
  volume shop."
- **[Chris Do]** **"dobot" — an AI clone of himself** (self-reported): a former student
  who runs an AI company offered to "build an artificial you." Chris said yes explicitly
  *because* the student would build it with or without him — "**I'd rather be on the right
  side of that**" — and because the student would "ask for forgiveness later" (Chris's read
  of "how Art Center people work"). They fed it his book, his Business Bootcamp course, and
  every video, weighting some material as more important. Planned as a **question bot** in
  his community that members can talk to, test, and train — a "trainable model using
  ChatGPT-4" that "might get you 80% of the way there." (All self-reported; a 2023 project
  claim.)
- **[Chris Do]** **Anecdote on the clone's value:** describes a scenario where someone
  tells ChatGPT to answer an objection "the way you would" and replies to a client with it
  — Chris is skeptical it truly "sounds just like" him, but says if it gets someone "30%
  better than what you're doing right now" for the zero dollars they paid, that's a win.
- **[Chris Do]** **AI product ideas** (self-reported, aspirational 2023): asked his
  software-engineer brother about building "a suite of tools for social media" —
  version one auto-generates the best **titles** for your videos; version two makes the
  **thumbnail** from ~30 images of yourself — pitched at ~$40/month, justified because
  making these things takes "hours freaking hours" and your time is worth more than $40/hr.
- **[Chris Do]** **The future rewards first movers.** He sees "a beautiful potential
  future," and "the future tends to reward first movers" — hence engaging with AI now
  rather than resisting it.
- **[Chris Do]** **As tools get more sophisticated, demand grows for people who can "talk
  to the machine"** — those who understand **art history**, understand **logic**, can
  **articulate what they want**, are great at **art direction**, and have **great language
  skills**. Asked whether AI will ever be the art director, he answers "**probably**," and
  on what's next: "I don't know… when we get there, we'll solve that problem." (Note the
  hedge — even in this optimistic 2023 framing he concedes AI may take the art-director
  role eventually.)
- **[Chris Do]** **Mission restated:** "teach a billion people how to make a living doing
  what they love." He doesn't have the method yet, but a clear goal "magnetizes" the means
  and methods; the hardest first step is committing to believe it's possible and
  inevitable. dobot / scaling himself is what makes that billion-person goal look feasible.
- **[Chris Do]** **On effort in the face of mortality** (told to the student): you're going
  to die one day — so why bother learning, growing, trying? Because it's "just part of the
  human existence." (Reframe: mortality is not a reason to stop.)
- **[Chris Do]** **The bear metaphor:** "AI is the bear; don't be the slowest person" —
  you don't have to be the fastest, you just can't be the slowest.
