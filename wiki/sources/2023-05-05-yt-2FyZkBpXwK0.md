## Key claims (paraphrased, DATED 2023-05-05, speaker-attributed)

### Lisa Galea (GUEST — context only, does NOT train the persona)

_All below are Lisa's, dated 2023-05-05, paraphrased. Included for source completeness
only; not persona training data._

- **[Lisa Galea]** Self-introduction: "creative Alchemist" who helps people get what they
  want using behavioral science plus 30 years of design thinking and advertising; a
  business mentor for The Futur; also runs leadership/mentor work with the Australian
  government.
- **[Lisa Galea]** Core thesis: most people don't achieve goals because "they have no
  idea what they want"; getting specific (and knowing *why*) lets the brain's filter find
  opportunities. Cites a self-Googled "~6%" of people achieve their goals (she openly
  distrusts percentages as "made up").
- **[Lisa Galea]** "The secret the secret left out" is **action** — nothing happens
  "without an exchange of energy"; clarity of desire alone is not enough.
- **[Lisa Galea]** Personal narrative told as a fairy tale: a childhood traveling the
  world (London, Middle East, Africa, Europe, Australia), then an adult "dark forest" — a
  period of judgment/blame/online attacks centered on her daughter — that led her to "rock
  bottom," followed by a healer ("Emery," feathers/dreadlocks) telling her her energy was
  "too low," which triggered a reframe of herself as "cells vibrating" / "a mass of atoms."
- **[Lisa Galea]** The "three crystals/gems" framework: (1) a **Talisman of security /
  anchor** — feel safe and grounded first, and use anchoring as a behavioral hack; (2)
  **curiosity** as the driver ("we are learning machines"; the brain as an ever-growing
  "live wire"/city like New York); (3) **habits** — the thing that both traps and frees us.
- **[Lisa Galea]** Energy/vibration claims: fresh food is "alive" and "vibrating" and she
  says she "measured" that it "raises the vibrational level of your body"; the company you
  keep is "vibrating energy"; protect your energy, find "people where you belong" rather
  than trying to fit in. (These are pseudo-scientific claims stated as fact — flagged.)
- **[Lisa Galea]** Brain/behavioral claims: the subconscious runs "95–98%" of the system;
  the brain filters perception to confirm existing beliefs (reticular-activating-system
  style: "you suddenly see the car you want everywhere"); ~12,000–60,000 thoughts a day
  and "98% are the same as yesterday" (autopilot/energy-saving); emotions are "molecules
  moving" signaling novelty.
- **[Lisa Galea]** Practical hacks: reboot/rest the brain like a phone; drink water +
  glucose at 2pm for afternoon fog; "breath is your joystick" (short breaths energize,
  long breaths calm); blinking to stay awake; celebrate small wins; **"do less, not more"**
  — small consistent steps, one new habit at a time; change the *cue* rather than fight the
  habit; reframe fear as excitement ("they show the same in your body").
- **[Lisa Galea]** Uses **Apple** as a behavioral-science marketing example (three-tier
  choice, top-price anchor, brand bias removing the decision). This is the guest's
  example, not Chris's pricing teaching.
- **[Lisa Galea]** References the **marshmallow / delayed-gratification** study and a
  **1965 learned-helplessness "dogs with a button"** experiment (she frames it as building
  on Pavlov; the described study is Seligman & Maier's learned helplessness). Also
  "SMART goals" work for short sprints but not long-term goals; "you are a new human every
  seven years"; "it's only today that's all we have."
