# Use This Strategy to Grow Your Business on YouTube (with Sean Cannell)

## Summary

Long-form podcast interview on The Futur. **Chris Do** hosts and interviews **Sean
Cannell** — founder/CEO of **Think Media** (a YouTube-education company; Sean self-reports
20 W2 employees + 10 contractors) and co-author (with Benji Travis) of the book *YouTube
Secrets*, here promoting its second edition. This is Sean's **second appearance** on the
show (an earlier episode covered live production; referenced but not linked here). The
episode is framed as a "master class for entrepreneurs" on whether and how to invest in
YouTube.

**The substantive YouTube-growth frameworks in this video are almost entirely Sean
Cannell's** — the "content lives forever / Content Library vs. feed" thesis, "it's
irresponsible to ignore YouTube," the AVLS gear method, the ASQ ("Answer Specific
Questions") method, "saturation is a myth / business is a game of attrition," "don't create
content, be the content," "hire top-down / a director as CEO of your media company," the
top-videos teardown (broad appeal, beginners, utility, "free," ethical clickbait), and the
deliberate-practice / game-tape communication regimen are all **Sean, not Chris**. Chris
contributes hosting, active "playing back"/summarizing, probing questions, feedback on
Sean's speaking style, and a handful of his **own** reflections — most notably his own
viral-content formula and several original one-liners.

## Key claims

_All dated 2023-05-07. Paraphrased unless quoted in the next section. Speaker-attributed._

### Chris Do (persona-eligible)

- **[Chris Do]** YouTube is different from other social platforms and he treats it
  specially: he came to YouTube **before** doing any other social media, learned to create
  content there first, and considers it "for the foreseeable future our home base of
  operations." Notes one reason it isn't quite "social media" is **shareability** — when he
  loves a video he doesn't share it *on* YouTube, he takes it to Twitter/elsewhere where
  active sharing happens.
- **[Chris Do]** On the anxiety of creating content: uses the industry frame of **long-tail
  vs. short-tail** content — people get anxious because on feed platforms, if a post doesn't
  hit in the first 3–24 hours it disappears, whereas YouTube behaves more like
  "just-in-time learning" and a searchable library.
- **[Chris Do]** On monetization framing (crediting a conversation with **Alex Hormozi** at
  Sean's conference): the phenomenal idea is content that is effectively your marketing
  material but that *pays you* to be consumed — inverting cost-per-acquisition / return-on-
  ad-spend. Hormozi is paraphrased as having shifted ad-spend dollars into content for a
  claimed "10x return" (Hormozi's claim, reported second-hand).
- **[Chris Do]** Self-reported metrics (mark **self-reported**): his Instagram earnings are
  "not enough to buy a meal"; The Futur is at ~2.02M subscribers vs. Sean's ~2.2M, and he
  openly uses "catch Sean" as a **positive** competitive motivator for his team ("it's not a
  negative competitive thing, it's a positive competitive thing"). The Futur's single most-
  viewed video is a **short at ~49M views**, and one short (a segment squeezed out of a
  36-minute video) drove ~700,000 subscribers.
- **[Chris Do]** On learning communication by **modeling**: admires **Joel Osteen**'s
  storytelling — makes complex concepts simple/repeatable, and stays relatable by not
  putting himself on a pedestal (referring to "daddy always says"). Watches **comedians**
  for stage presence and storytelling; tells a story of studying **Kevin Hart**'s confident
  stage walk, rewinding and practicing the exact walk at home until his wife caught him and
  approved it — an illustration of "modeling / reverse-engineering greatness."
- **[Chris Do]** On knowing your audience (agreeing with Sean): says Kevin Hart "knows who
  he is" and plays to it; encourages consistency of a core comedic/personal style rather
  than chasing constant reinvention.
- **[Chris Do]** **His own viral-content formula for The Futur** (genuinely-new,
  Chris-attributed): the videos that "pop" combine (1) something **polarizing** — you can be
  for it or against it; (2) something that **feels real / high-emotion** (e.g., a genuine
  argument with a potential client, "like reality TV") that puts viewers on one side or the
  other; and (3) **a piece of value baked into it**. He believes this formula applies across
  niches and platforms, and stresses doing it *ethically* — not manufacturing conflict but
  surfacing real convictions. Caveat: "even truth is held in tension," and a viral short is
  "just the short of it" — the fuller nuance lives in the long-form.
- **[Chris Do]** On nuance vs. attention: the criticism that social media lacks nuanced,
  thorough analysis is fair, but "we have to stop someone... long enough so they can learn
  something they can take action on... that day." Over-hedged, nuanced phrasing "loses all
  the bite and the punch." Coins/uses his own line **"theory is lonely, it loves action —
  don't keep them apart"** as an example of a non-hedged, shareable statement. Introduces the
  **"Jenga effect"** of storytelling: the audience half-knows what will happen but can't turn
  away as you keep pulling pieces until it's about to fall.
- **[Chris Do]** On "be the content": his younger-self advice is **"get on the content game
  sooner"** and make content a part of who you are, not a separate activity — the best way to
  demonstrate expertise is to be filmed **doing** what you do (coaching a team, giving advice,
  a sales call), analogous to the addictive "watching someone do their craft" TikToks. "Just
  do what you do and be brave enough to bring the world into this little place."
- **[Chris Do]** The **cold-open thesis** (his own): on the far side of giving up the fear of
  other people's opinions — the need to be liked, to please everyone, to avoid offending —
  there is "Untold growth for your business, your personal brand, your reach," provided you
  put out "powerful messaging that you believe in." He also adopts/echoes the **"it's
  irresponsible for you as a business owner to not be on YouTube"** line as a *deliberately
  cultivated* polarizing hook ("so it stings a little," "taking a page from garyvee") — while
  privately acknowledging you can survive without YouTube; the sting is the point, meant to
  get attention (the statement's origin in this talk is Sean's, see below).

### Sean Cannell (GUEST — context only, does NOT train the persona)

- **[Sean Cannell]** YouTube backstory: small-town college dropout; first channel in 2007 for
  a church north of Seattle; freelance video business in 2009; later founded Think Media and
  discovered affiliate marketing / ranking videos.
- **[Sean Cannell]** Core thesis: **YouTube content "lives forever"** — a video posted today
  can still earn views/revenue 10+ years later. YouTube is a **Content Library**, not a feed:
  users express *intent* by behavior and the algorithm recommends to meet needs, so every
  business/creator should make strategic content that attracts an ideal client or solves a
  problem. It's "the Forgotten social media platform," and now the **#1 podcast destination**
  for consumption.
- **[Sean Cannell]** "It is **irresponsible** for any serious business owner / entrepreneur /
  creative to ignore YouTube." (Origin of the line Chris later adopts.)
- **[Sean Cannell]** Monetization advantages: Think Media's content is roughly **revenue-
  neutral** (AdSense passively exceeds production cost). Case study of "Erica Kohlberg" (the
  social-media lawyer; name spelling uncertain from captions): YouTube ~$36K vs. TikTok a few
  thousand, Facebook a few hundred, Instagram ~$0. HubSpot's CMO cited that **YouTube
  subscribers are the most valuable subscribers on the internet**.
- **[Sean Cannell]** Barrier to entry as opportunity ("a confused mind never buys... a
  confused entrepreneur doesn't take action"). Just start with your smartphone (flip to
  landscape). Gear priority = **AVLS: Audio, Video, Lighting, Stabilization** (a ~$25 lav
  mic, a $50 softbox, a tripod). "Punch fear in the face, punch perfectionism in the face,
  and press record." Rule of thumb for delivery: **"be brief, be bright, be fun, and be
  done."**
- **[Sean Cannell]** The **ASQ method — "Answer Specific Questions"**: turn the top 10–20
  questions you already get from clients into one video each; start with *beginner/basic*
  answers because "the curse of knowledge" stops most people. **Content value beats
  production value** (examples: a Vegas real-estate agent's echoey webcam video at 305K
  views; "Clear Value Tax"; "Zach McDonald"). Improve ~1% per upload.
- **[Sean Cannell]** **"Saturation is a myth"** and **"business is a game of attrition"**
  (attributes the sentiment to Steve Jobs): most competitors quit; consistency over 7–8–9
  years builds "an Empire." "The riches are in the niches."
- **[Sean Cannell]** How he built his communication skill: an intentional decision + a
  **PDP (personal development plan)**; **deliberate practice** (invokes Gladwell's 10,000-hour
  rule, corrected to *deliberate* practice); relentless **note-taking**; watching **game
  tape** (cites Kobe Bryant) of his own talks; a book (*Decoding Greatness*, Ron Friedman) on
  **reverse-engineering** the masters. "Repetition is the father of learning and the mother of
  Mastery." A good sermon ≈ a good YouTube video (hook, premise, structure).
- **[Sean Cannell]** Communication influences (guest bio, not Chris's): Joel Osteen (learned
  brevity as his dad's video editor), Dr. Dave Martin (humor timing), comedians (Kevin Hart,
  Dave Chappelle), TD Jakes, Benny Perez, gary­vee, Casey Neistat. His own style: vulnerability/
  transparency/"real talk," **clarity**, and a **teacher** frame (points-driven, uses a slide
  deck/notes as a "helpful crutch").
- **[Sean Cannell]** "The Creator who understands the viewer best wins." Tells the **Social
  Media Marketing World** story of *bombing* (lowest speaker score) by misreading the audience
  — took full ownership, returned and scored well — as proof that knowing your audience
  (education level, background) is the number-one question.
- **[Sean Cannell]** For a busy owner *with money*: **"don't create content, be the content"**
  (credits Brad Lea's talk; ties to garyvee's "document, don't create") — hire a videographer
  to film you *doing your work* (meetings, coaching, instruction) and let the team distribute
  it. Start a **video podcast** — "kill 36 birds with one stone" (video + audio + clips).
- **[Sean Cannell]** Hiring: **hire top-down** — first hire a **director / "CEO of your media
  company"** (a ~$70–100K role) who then sources editors, designers, schedulers (invokes the
  book *Who Not How* and Ryan Pineda). Plan to lose money on a hire for ~6 months (credits
  Ezra Firestone). Alex & Leila Hormozi reportedly spend ~$125K/month on organic content.
  Grant Cardone's "raising money" brand strategy: "if they don't know you, they can't flow
  you." Start with the end in mind — get clear on what "winning" looks like in 3/5/10 years.
- **[Sean Cannell]** Top-videos teardown of Think Media (define "winning" before chasing
  views): most views come from **broad appeal** ("how to make YouTube videos on your phone"),
  **beginners** (always the largest market), **utility/search** ("best cheap cameras," "best
  copyright-free music"), and the word **"free."** Advocates **ethical/honest clickbait** (bait
  the click, then deliver). Warns that his most-viewed video (4.6M views, "30+ funny sound
  effects") attracts the **wrong** audience — "it's not a race to who gets the most
  subscribers, it's who gets the *right* subscribers." Cites *The Pumpkin Plan* (cut the small
  pumpkins) and self-reports **sunsetting a ~$24K/month program** because it starved the
  "prize-winning pumpkin." "Good is the enemy of the great." Jim Rohn: the person you become in
  pursuit of the goal matters more than the goal.
- **[Sean Cannell]** On polarizing/viral content: credits **Ryan Deiss** (Traffic & Conversion
  Summit) on the "five elements of creating a movement" and that **"movement leaders speak in
  absolutes"** (examples: Grant Cardone, garyvee — the garyvee "search.twitter.com or you're
  out of business" anecdote); ties to Jim Collins's *Built to Last* "cult-like" cultures.
  **"Vanilla doesn't work in 2023 — pick a side, pick a fight"** (but a fight you genuinely care
  about, not to be a jerk). The best businesses have a **good enemy** (his: the "money and
  Ferraris" lifestyle marketing; his stand: "family and impact"). Credits Patrick Bet-David for
  urging creators to state where they stand (Elon Musk vs. Jeff Bezos contrast). Names Andrew
  Tate as a polarizing-reach example.
