# AVOID THIS MISTAKE To Achieve Your Full Potential

## Summary

Short solo motivational/mindset clip. **Chris Do speaks alone throughout** — there is no
guest and no interviewer. All other people named (Jordan Peterson, Bert Kreischer, Terry
Crews, Elon Musk) are cited as *illustrations/examples*, not as speakers; none of their
statements are attributed to this repo as their own voice. The entire piece is
**Chris-Do-attributed and persona-eligible.**

The through-line: the biggest mistake artists and creative entrepreneurs make early in
their careers — the thing that separates "the starving artist" from "being paid to do
what you love" — is trying to do something *so big* that they end up doing nothing at
all. Chris's antidote is radical **incrementalism**: take really small steps, solve the
problem in front of you (your neighbor, your neighborhood) before trying to change the
world, and just aim for the next mile marker. He introduces a compact **"three M's"**
sequence — **Mastery → Media → Monetize** — as the correct order of operations, and
argues creatives fail by rushing straight to monetize. He anchors the message in stories:
Terry Crews' "I hate working out" routine (put on the clothes, drive to the gym, do a few
reps), his own channel journey (happy at 250 views, now ~2 million subscribers), an Adidas
ad ("just to the mailbox, just to the corner, just to the stoplight… for the love of
sport"), and his own education mission (teach one person, then five, then fifty; "suck a
little less each time").

## Key claims

_All dated 2023-05-18. Paraphrased unless quoted in the next section. Speaker-attributed._

- **[Chris Do]** The **biggest mistake** artists and creative entrepreneurs make starting
  out is the difference between being a starving artist and being paid to do what you love:
  they get stuck trying to do something *so big* that they don't do anything at all.
- **[Chris Do]** He is a "big proponent" of taking **really small steps** — his maxim:
  **"big things start small and small things start big."**
- **[Chris Do]** It's noble to try to solve the world's problems, but the better move is to
  **solve the problem with your neighbor and in your neighborhood first.** He cites Jordan
  Peterson's "clean your room first before you try to change the world" — do the basic small
  things (clean your room, show up to a job on time) rather than reaching for big when you
  can't do the basics. (Peterson noted as "a controversial figure.")
- **[Chris Do]** Introduces the **"three M's"** framework (which he says he'd been thinking
  about after talking to Bert Kreischer): **(1) Mastery** — go learn a skill and do it well,
  just focus on that; **(2) Media** — once you start to master something, learn to publish
  and produce content that *demonstrates* your mastery (mastery and media work together);
  **(3) Monetize.** The common failure is rushing straight to *monetize* / making money
  instead of learning a skill first and getting really good.
- **[Chris Do]** Retells **Terry Crews'** anti-motivation routine as the model for small
  steps: Crews (former NFL athlete) reportedly said he *hates* working out, so he tricks
  himself in stages — put on exercise clothes, feel stupid standing in the kitchen, so put
  on shoes; feel like he should go somewhere, so drive to the gym; feel stupid sitting in
  the parking lot, so go inside for "a few reps" — and three hours later he's done a full
  workout. The point: you can't wake up and *be* Terry Crews tomorrow; you take the small
  steps.
- **[Chris Do]** Uses his **own channel** as proof: he was happy with **250 views** and just
  wanted to reach the next mile marker; the channel now has **~2 million subscribers**. Growth
  came from chasing the next marker, not the end state.
- **[Chris Do]** Cites an **Adidas ad** as the visual for incrementalism: a street in
  single-point perspective with type reading "just to the mailbox, just to the corner, just
  to the stoplight," going on and on — tagline **"for the love of sport."** Just the next
  marker.
- **[Chris Do]** On his **education mission**: he wants to change education, but he's not
  going in declaring "I'm going to change education" — he'll **teach one person, then five,
  then maybe fifty, and "suck a little less each time I speak."** Little steps. "I just want
  to see what's in front of me."
- **[Chris Do]** Reserves world-changing ambition for the exceptions: **"God bless you if
  you're Elon Musk, go do it"** — but for the rest of us, just take the next step.
- **[Chris Do]** Frames the whole talk from a place of security: says he is **51**, his life
  is fine, he and his wife are "set," and it doesn't matter to him what the viewer does — he
  cares about them and it "kills" him to watch people skip the next step. _(Biographical:
  self-reports age 51 as of 2023-05-18; consistent with a 1971 birth year.)_
