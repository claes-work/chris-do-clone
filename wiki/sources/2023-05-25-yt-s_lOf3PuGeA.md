## Summary

An unnamed Futur instructor presents the **"one-page sales framework"** — a structured
sales-call script pitched at boutique agency owners and creative studios who lose deals
after spending hours writing proposals that get ghosted. The core argument: stop writing
long custom proposals sent by email and instead run a live **presentation** that walks
the prospect through a fixed sequence and asks for a decision **on the call**, so the
seller gets a fast yes/no plus the real objections. The framework's stages: build
**Rapport** (2-3 min max), set a **pre-frame** (tell them the structure/aim and do
"housekeeping" on time and distractions), establish **authority** (ask "why us?" so the
prospect states their own reason for choosing you), map the **two islands** (Island A =
where they are now, Island B = where they want to get to), surface the **obstacles**,
explore the **impact / cost of staying the same** vs. upside of change, **play back**
what was heard (doctor-style diagnosis), then **prescribe** steps/strategies, present
**packaged offer options** (preset pricing, not custom), and **ask for the decision**
using a **traffic-light** close (red = not a fit, amber = good fit but has
questions/concerns, green = ready to move forward). A downloadable one-page template is
promoted via a link below the video.

## Key claims

_All dated 2023-05-25. Paraphrased unless quoted. Speaker-attributed to the **unnamed
Futur instructor**, NOT Chris Do. Recorded as Futur-context; do not attribute to the
subject._

- [2023-05-25] Agency owners get disheartened writing hour-plus proposals that then get
  ghosted or met with vague reasons (chose someone else, project shelved). (paraphrase)
- [2023-05-25] The remedy is a **tool + framework** that gets a decision quickly —
  ideally on the call — so you know if it's going forward and, if not, exactly why, giving
  you feedback to improve the offer. (paraphrase)
- [2023-05-25] The goal is to relate to the client as a **partner** they respect, not a
  "worker or creative monkey." (paraphrase)
- [2023-05-25] The whole thing is the **one-page sales framework** the coaching business
  teaches its clients (small boutique agencies / creative studios); its purpose is to
  "get rid of being ghosted" and close more deals. (paraphrase)
- [2023-05-25] In an ~45-60 min sales call, spend no more than **2-3 minutes on
  rapport** (pets/kids/common ground) or it turns into chit-chat instead of a sale.
  (paraphrase)
- [2023-05-25] The **pre-frame** tells the prospect what to expect: the structure, the
  aim of the call, plus **housekeeping** (time limits, shut down email/distractions).
  Most people skip it because they're anxious about getting the sale. (paraphrase)
- [2023-05-25] **Authority** reframe: the seller "has the cookie jar" — the client needs
  them as much as they need to be paid, and can get paid by multiple clients. Ask
  "why us?" so the prospect articulates why they chose you; this can be looped back later
  if they mention competitors. (paraphrase)
- [2023-05-25] **Two islands**: Island A = where the business currently is (revenue,
  average client, traffic/exposure); Island B = the ultimate result they actually want,
  not the service. Most sellers over-focus on their services rather than the client's
  desired outcome. (paraphrase)
- [2023-05-25] Ask what's **standing in the way** of Island B, then examine the
  **long-term impact** — "what's the cost of these things staying the same?" and, on the
  flip side, "what would be possible if you overcame these obstacles?" People under-invest
  because they think short-term (money leaving the account) rather than long-term cost.
  (paraphrase)
- [2023-05-25] **Play back** what was heard, doctor-style: "would you like me to give my
  perspective on what I think is going on here?" then repeat their situation, obstacles
  and stakes and ask "have I missed anything?" before **prescribing** a few
  steps/strategies to get from A to B and getting feedback. (paraphrase)
- [2023-05-25] **Package your services** into a few preset options with known prices
  rather than building a custom proposal every time — that removes the laborious part and
  lets you check fit live. Present the steps, price, timeframe and logistics. (paraphrase)
- [2023-05-25] **Ask for a decision on the call.** Most sellers avoid this, but it's where
  the opportunity is: a forced yes/no surfaces the real objections that clients otherwise
  hide to keep you happy. (paraphrase)
- [2023-05-25] **Traffic-light close:** "most people are in one of three stages" — red
  (not a good fit), amber (good fit but has questions/concerns), green (ready to move
  forward). "Where are you right now?" The common answer is between amber and green,
  which opens the chance to explore and resolve what's blocking a "green." (paraphrase +
  quote fragments)
- [2023-05-25] Building **presentations rather than proposals** and treating objections as
  opportunities speeds the sales cycle (yes/no, move on either way) and teaches you what
  concerns to fix in the offer. A one-page template is linked below the video. (paraphrase)
