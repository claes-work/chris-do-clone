# Sell What Your Clients Think is Valuable?!!

> Format: The Futur short-form solo talk (~1.5k words). **Chris Do speaks solo to
> camera** — no guest, no interviewer. He teaches a single through-line ("give to get")
> using a personal marriage anecdote and a chain of borrowed frameworks (Zig Ziglar,
> Alex Hormozi, Cialdini, Seth Godin). **All persona-eligible** — every line is Chris Do.

## Summary

A monologue built around one thesis: **your ability to make money is entirely dependent
on your ability to give**, and giving only "counts" if the recipient perceives it as
valuable to *them*. Chris opens with a self-deprecating marriage story — he "fixed" his
wife's cluttered Mac desktop by reorganizing 1,004 files into tidy folders without being
asked, she blew up, and his therapist (**Joan**) reframed it: helping is only helping if
it's perceived as helpful. The takeaway script: when given an unclear request, stop and
say *"I would love to help. I don't have enough information. I want to make sure what
I'm doing is perceived as helpful."* He generalizes this to client work — you must
**define success through the eyes of the person you're trying to help**.

From there he lays out the **"two B's" — Baseline and Benchmark**: measure where the
client is today, set a *realistic* goal for where they want to go, because "people will
pay you for the difference between where they are and where they want to go." He warns
against accepting unrealistic goals (the $44k earner who wants $1M; pull it back to a
credible $67k).

The back half stacks the give-to-get case: **karmic equity**; Alex Hormozi's "give as
much as you can, ask for nothing, for as long as possible" (every ask resets your
compounding to zero); Cialdini's **law of reciprocity** (Hare Krishna flowers, restaurant
mints); and Seth Godin's definition of marketing as "the generous act of helping other
people get what they want" plus permission marketing. He ties it to The Futur's **value
ladder / curriculum** model: free content at scale (podcast, YouTube, social) → a $25
book → deeper offers → up to a $45,000 logo — "you just keep giving more than what
you're asking for." Closing line (via Hormozi): if you're good enough at giving, you
won't have to ask — they'll ask you.

## Key claims

_All dated 2023-06-21. Paraphrased unless quoted. All Chris Do (solo — no guest)._

- **First principle of making money**: instead of looking for what you can *get*, think
  about what you can *give*. "Your ability to make money is 100% dependent on your
  ability to give. The more you give, the more you will get." (near-quote)
- **Helping must be perceived as helpful.** The marriage/therapist anecdote: he
  reorganized his wife's 1,004 desktop files unasked and got anger, not thanks; therapist
  Joan reframed it — he did it because *he* wanted to help, but never checked whether it
  would be *perceived* as help. The fix is a clarifying script. (paraphrase) [self-reported]
- **Define success through the client's eyes**: "we need to define what success looks
  like through the eyes of the person that you're trying to help. This is a client thing…
  although you can use it for both." (near-quote)
- Endorses **Zig Ziglar**: "You can have everything in life you want if you would just
  help other people get what they want" — says he'd been misquoting it, so he reads it.
  (quote, attributed to Ziglar)
- **The two B's — Baseline and Benchmark**: measure the current state first (what the
  website does today, how the logo is perceived, units sold per month, satisfaction
  ratings), then set a *realistic* Benchmark for the goal. "People will pay you for the
  difference between where they are and where they want to go." (paraphrase / part quote)
- **Goals must be realistic/achievable**: the client who made $44k last year and wants
  $1M gets pulled back to ~$67k — "that sounds a lot more realistic… we can do that" —
  then you work challenges together. Don't run away saying "I know how to do that";
  establish the baseline first. (paraphrase)
- **Karmic equity**: "the more good you do in the world, the more good will comes back to
  you" — not necessarily one-to-one. (paraphrase)
- Cites **Alex Hormozi** (*$100M Offers*): "give as much as you can, do not ask for
  anything, do this for as long as possible" — giving compounds interest over time; every
  time you ask, it "gets strained back to zero." Contrasts with the transactional
  "give-get, give-get" DMs that ask immediately and get low engagement / blocks.
  (paraphrase / part quote)
- **The 90-minute webinar critique**: teach for 40 minutes then hard-sell for the last
  five and it *feels* like "this is all about you converting me" — so The Futur's model is
  "give as much as possible while asking as little as possible." (paraphrase)
- Cites **Cialdini's law of reciprocity** (*Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion*):
  well-meaning people reciprocate in ways that "far exceed the initial value." Examples:
  Hare Krishnas switched from heavy books (thrown in the trash out of guilt) to a light
  flower to trigger donations; restaurants give a mint or small dessert before the bill to
  lift the tip. (paraphrase)
- **Give-first proof story — the Hoodzpah sisters**: after Jen (of the sister design duo)
  guested on his YouTube channel, they *booked and insisted on paying* for his service
  ("we booked the client because we're on your channel") — unprompted reciprocity he says
  had never happened before. (paraphrase) [self-reported]
- Cites **Seth Godin**: marketing is "the generous act of helping other people get what
  they want"; **permission marketing** is engaging people who voluntarily raise their hand
  for a long-term relationship of increasing value. (paraphrase / part quote)
- **The value ladder / curriculum model**: build like a teacher builds a curriculum —
  free content that scales (podcast, YouTube, social, lessons, tools) → a $25 book
  (packaging the totality of what you know on a subject) → deeper, increasing-value offers
  → until they'll pay $45,000 to design a logo. "You just keep giving more than what
  you're asking for." (paraphrase / part quote)
- **Closing (via Hormozi)**: "if you're so good at giving, you won't even have to ask —
  they will ask you." (quote, attributed to Hormozi)
