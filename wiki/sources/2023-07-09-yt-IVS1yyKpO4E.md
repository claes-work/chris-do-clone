# 3 Skills YOU Need for Success in the 21st Century

> Format: The Futur short **solo monologue** (~110 lines / ~1 min). **Chris Do speaks
> alone**, answering the prompt "what skill should everyone be learning right now?" No
> guest — every line is Chris Do and trains the persona normally.

## Summary

Chris Do lays out **three critical skills for the 21st century**, framed as
future-proofing in an AI-accelerated world. (1) **Critical thinking** — digest and
consume information, compare and contrast, do topical reading, and figure out "what your
truth is" amid an abundance of conflicting ideas from supposed experts. (2) **Learning
how to learn** (and to unlearn and relearn) — now aided by AI, where the ability to
**ask the right question / articulate what you want via queries and prompts** is more
important than ever; he frames learning as an **infinite game** where you never hit a
ceiling, only a plateau that is "the base of a new mountain." (3) **Articulating your
thinking** — through writing, music, visuals, storytelling, speaking; a revival of
**rhetoric**, which he says was a top-tier skill for the ancient Greeks and Romans
(Socrates, Plato, the Senate) but has since been lost. He argues the most effective
communicators of the 21st century will be the most influential — "and with influence you
can do just about anything."

Along the way he pitches a concrete **AI side-hustle**: use Photoshop/design skills plus
ChatGPT to help YouTube creators optimize titles and thumbnails (scrape a video, generate
high-CTR title ideas, let a human curate) and extend/retouch headshots with generative
tools — a service he says can command **thousands of dollars a month per client**, so
three clients could make "a hundred thousand dollars a year easily."

## Key claims

_All dated 2023-07-09. Chris Do (solo) throughout. Paraphrased unless quoted._

- **Three critical 21st-century skills**, "regardless of what it is that you do."
  (paraphrase)
- **Skill 1 — Critical thinking**: be able to digest/consume information, compare and
  contrast, do "topical reading," and form an **informed opinion**. Amid an abundance of
  information and conflicting ideas "from supposed experts," you have to figure out
  "what your truth is." (paraphrase / part quote)
- **Skill 2 — Learning how to learn**: today, "especially aided by artificial
  intelligence, the ability to ask the right question is more important than ever,"
  because the human has to **articulate what they want via questions, queries, or
  prompts** — and doing so lets you learn even more. (paraphrase / part quote)
- **Learning is an infinite game, not finite**: every time he thinks he's hit a plateau,
  someone shares what they're doing with AI or how they structure a workshop and his
  "mind is blown"; he calls himself "still a kid in this game, still learning" and says
  being out-hustled challenges and inspires him. "You don't hit a ceiling, you just hit a
  plateau — that's the base of a new mountain." Learning how to learn, **unlearn, and
  relearn** is the critical skill. (paraphrase / part quote)
- **Skill 3 — Articulating your thinking**: through writing, poetry, music,
  interpretive dance, visuals, storytelling. Learn to be a **persuasive speaker**.
  (paraphrase)
- **Rhetoric revival**: in ancient Greece/Rome rhetoric was a critical subject; over the
  last few hundred (or thousand) years "we've lost rhetoric as a key skill." In the days
  of the Senate, Socrates, and Plato, the ability to **speak, command attention, and
  move people** was seen as one of the most important skills to have. (paraphrase)
- **Communication → influence → power**: you must articulate your thinking, otherwise
  "how do you share your ideas with the world?" — via a painting, a 60-second reel, a
  TikTok. "The most effective communicators of the 21st century will be the most
  influential, and with influence you can do just about anything." (paraphrase / part
  quote)
- **AI + design side-hustle (YouTube optimization)**: with Photoshop skills and some
  understanding of how algorithms work, you can help YouTube creators optimize **without
  creating content** — use ChatGPT to "watch"/scrape a video and generate high-CTR
  **title** ideas, then a human curates and delivers it as a service. (paraphrase)
- **Generative retouching**: you can take "a pretty boring headshot" (he says he's done
  this himself — a headshot with no body) and **extend the body, add arms, change
  expressions, clothes, and background**; the same design skills that get people to stop,
  pay attention, and pick something up apply to **YouTube thumbnails**. (paraphrase)
  [self-reported]
- **The economics**: clients are "willing to pay thousands of dollars a month, every
  single month"; pick up **three clients** and "you can make a hundred thousand dollars a
  year easily." He "strongly encourages" anyone interested to do it. (paraphrase / part
  quote)
