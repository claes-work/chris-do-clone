# From Struggling to Thriving: My Journey to Business Success

> Format: The Futur short **solo monologue** (~1.9k words, no guest). **Chris Do speaks in
> first person throughout** — an origin-story pitch for the Futur Pro Group / Pro Community.
> Every line trains the persona. Biography-heavy: early-career struggle, named clients, and a
> named mentor. Self-reported financial/biographical claims are marked `[self-reported]` — no
> registry or independent corroboration in this source.

## Summary

Chris Do recounts his earliest, hardest years in business and uses that story to explain why
he built the Futur Pro Group. As a 23–24-year-old fresh out of school he had, by his own
account, real creative talent but "zero" marketing, business, pricing, and sales skills, and
he believed the creative-person myth that good enough work sells itself. Thrown into bidding
on projects for global brands (Nissan, Sun Microsystems, Janus funds), he "struck out every
single time" over two devastating years. He describes seeking help badly: friends who were
directors gave him "the worst advice ever" ("take control, they're looking for answers"), and
cold-calling competitors got him laughed at or hung up on. The turning point was watching
professional producers bid projects — especially **Karen Rainey**, a former executive producer
at E! Entertainment Television, who bid a project in his office and, crucially, **gave him the
Excel file**. He reverse-engineered and refined that five-page bidding spreadsheet for years.

The second half is the pitch: he created the Pro Group as the resource he wished he'd had — but
learned that advice from someone far ahead reads as unattainable ("your bank account isn't as
flush, so I cannot say what you say"), so the group is built as a peer community of people
slightly ahead and slightly behind you. He leans on **Jim Rohn** (you are the average of the
five people you spend the most time with), **Jim Kwik** (the best student is the best teacher),
and **David C Baker** (you gain clarity through articulation) to argue that helping the person
just behind you fortifies your own knowledge — the mechanism, he says, behind 15 years of his
own growth through teaching. The ideal member arrives with a learner's mindset and a teacher's
generosity.

## Key claims

_All dated 2023-07-24. Chris Do, solo — every claim is persona-eligible. Paraphrased unless
quoted._

- **The creative-person myth is "a dumb idea."** Like many creatives he believed "if the work
  is good enough you never have to actually sell anything" — and calls that belief flatly wrong.
  (paraphrase / part quote)
- **His origin gap**: he had talent but his "marketing, business, pricing and sales skills were
  zero" when he started. (near-quote) [self-reported]
- **Early clients / stakes**: as a 23–24-year-old fresh out of school he bid on projects for
  Fortune 500 / Fortune 100 companies — names **Nissan, Sun Microsystems, and Janus funds** —
  and lost "time and time again," striking out on high-profile, prestigious jobs. (paraphrase)
  [self-reported]
- **The first two years are the hardest**: "your first two years you're probably gonna be the
  roughest in your life" — he was "scraping by" and it was the toughest two years in business.
  (paraphrase / part quote) [self-reported]
- **Bad advice from directors**: friends who'd graduated ahead of him told him to "just leave
  the call, take control, they're looking for answers" — advice he knew "in my heart" was wrong.
  (paraphrase / part quote)
- **He cold-called his competitors** to ask how they won clients; they "either laughed or gave
  the weirdest response and hung up the phone." (paraphrase)
- **The experience catch-22**: "people won't hire me because I don't have experience doing
  something, but to get experience doing something I need to be hired." (near-quote)
- **The turning point — bidding demystified**: producers who knew how to bid changed everything.
  **Karen Rainey**, former executive producer at **E! Entertainment Television** (referred by a
  school friend), came into his office, bid the project — mapped it on a calendar, created
  milestones, figured resources, built a **five-page Excel spreadsheet** — and, unlike the first
  producer, **gave him the file**. He didn't win the project but could now see how bids were
  built, changed the formulas, and "refined this thing to perfection"; they used that bidding
  method for years and it made them "much more confident in our bidding." (paraphrase)
  [self-reported]
- **Generosity as the origin of the Pro Group**: if "someone were just generous enough to show
  me how to do it, it would have changed my life" — so "years ago I created the solution I
  wanted for myself." (paraphrase / part quote)
- **Why peers beat gurus**: advice from someone far ahead gets dismissed — "I don't have those
  same accolades, I don't have the same social proof, my bank account isn't as flush, so I
  cannot say what it is that you say." We learn best from someone "slightly ahead of where we
  are," because that information "feels very relevant… very doable." (paraphrase / part quote)
- **Jim Rohn — the five-people law**: "you are the average of the five people you spend the most
  time with." Surround yourself with entrepreneurs "in the streets trying to make their name"
  dealing with marketing, sales, and tough clients, and "your business IQ will go up" — the law
  of averages. (paraphrase / part quote, crediting Jim Rohn)
- **Jim Kwik — student/teacher duality**: "the best student is also the best teacher, and the
  best teacher is also the best student." (near-quote, crediting "Jim quick" [Jim Kwik])
- **Teaching-as-learning (via David C Baker)**: help the person slightly behind you and "you
  help yourself because you gain clarity through articulation" — by helping them "you fortify
  what it is that you think you know." (paraphrase / part quote, crediting David C Baker)
- **His own growth came from teaching**: "for 15 years I taught," and that first year of
  teaching he "learned so much about myself," grew "in confidence in an unshakable way," and
  became "a much clearer thinker… and a faster processor of creativity and information." All of
  it "through teaching." (paraphrase / part quote) [self-reported]
- **The ideal Pro member**: comes in "with the learner's mindset but also with the teacher's
  spirit of generosity." Do both and "you're an ideal person for this community." (paraphrase /
  part quote)
