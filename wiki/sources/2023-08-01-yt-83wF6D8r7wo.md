## Summary

A short Futur panel on **how a small/up-and-coming studio actually lands big-brand clients**
(Red Bull, Nike, Asics). Two guest designers, **Jen and Amy**, run the practical advice: they
reverse-engineered how they won their biggest clients and found the throughline was
**visibility + relationships + fearlessness**, not cold prestige. Red Bull came from an
Instagram DM after they posted work non-stop and hashtagged into the surf/moto niche; Asics
came through a friend met at a local creative meetup who first hired them for his wife's ice
cream company; other leads came from cold emails, Twitter outreach to admired brands, and
online-only friendships they'd "never even met." Recurring themes: **put yourself out there**,
**do right by your clients** so referrals compound, and don't assume big brands mean big
budgets (capsule collections / sub-events want fresh, relevant, experimental studios and often
run lean). A geography thread: being in Southern California ("living tax," high rent, but dense
Enterprise/entertainment work) helps, but **location is not a hard limit** — conferences,
trade shows and remote/online outreach let anyone in a secondary market reach the same people.

**Chris Do's role appears to be host/moderator** (he is addressed as "Chris," and the framing
question "how do you get to work with the Big Brand?" reads like host framing), but this cannot

### Guests — Jen / Amy (studio designers) — CONTEXT ONLY, does NOT train the persona

_Paraphrased; attributed to the guest studio (Jen/Amy). Recorded for corpus context, NOT for
`beliefs.md` / `voice.md`._

- They **circled back over their biggest clients to reconstruct how each was won** — a
  deliberate exercise — and the common thread was visibility and relationships, not cold prestige.
- **Red Bull** came in through an **Instagram DM**: they posted work non-stop, hashtagged into
  the local **surf/moto** arena, and an art director searching hashtags found them.
- **Asics** came through a **friend from a local creative meetup they started**: they did a logo
  for his wife's ice cream company, he loved it, and it led to an Asics event logo. Takeaway:
  **take the small, "this may not be huge but it'll be fun" job** — "everybody knows somebody a
  little bit higher up… with a little bit more clout."
- **Cold emails and Twitter/Instagram outreach** to people at admired brands produced "some of
  our greatest leads… friends we literally only know on the internet, we've never even met."
- A **cold email from an agency** offering subcontractor help worked out because they built a
  reputation; a good portfolio plus willingness to put yourself out there brings work back.
- **Big brands don't always have big budgets:** Nike / Red Bull have many sub-projects, capsule
  collections and events; art directors "are on the hunt for what's new and relevant" and want
  an **experimental, up-and-coming studio look**, so reach out "never fear."
- **Geography helps but isn't required:** Southern California (Hollywood, LA, SF a flight away,
  Newport/Orange County) is dense with Enterprise, entertainment and retail/fashion work — you
  pay a "living tax" (high real estate) to be near it — **but don't let location be a limiting
  belief**; conferences, trade shows and remote outreach reach the same people from secondary/
  third markets. Their own studio works **remote** with no office to "wine and dine" clients.
