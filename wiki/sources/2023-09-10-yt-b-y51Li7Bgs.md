## Summary

Chris Do hosts digital-marketing/analytics consultant Amanda Webb (spiderworking.com) for a
website-conversion and analytics teardown aimed at creative/service businesses. Amanda
walks through three common website mistakes (weak/impersonal About pages, one catch-all
services page instead of one page per service, missing "thank you" pages), heat-mapping
tools (Microsoft Clarity) to see real visitor behavior, contrasting call-to-action button
colors, UTM tracking for attribution, and her own two-metric focus (LinkedIn direct messages
and lead-magnet sign-ups). Chris interjects with clarifying questions, isolates variables
(stripping out bad design/copy to test the "false data" idea), names the **paradox of
choice** when Amanda describes trimming her service list, and closes with a first-person
belief that too much of business is run on "instinct and emotion" and that data should
inform decisions instead. Biographical asides: Chris mentions he is scheduled to **speak at
Adobe MAX**, and that he and Amanda met at **Atomicon** (Daniel Priestley's conference).

## Fenced — Amanda Webb (guest, spiderworking.com; does NOT train the persona)

_Attributed to Amanda Webb; recorded as context only, not for `beliefs.md`/`voice.md`._

- Self-reported: digital-marketing ROI consultant/trainer/mentor, ~14 years in the field,
  based in Ireland; runs spiderworking.com; prefers working with service-based businesses
  over e-commerce because measurement is "a little bit more difficult... I like the
  challenge."
- **Three common website conversion mistakes** she flags for creative/service businesses:
  (1) a thin or missing About page without a photo — "there's nothing more distinctive than
  your face"; (2) one catch-all services page instead of a dedicated page per service; (3)
  no dedicated "thank you" page after a contact form / lead magnet signup, which she frames
  as both a measurement opportunity and a personal-branding opportunity.
- Recommends **Microsoft Clarity** (free heat-mapping/session-recording tool, GDPR
  compliant, integrates via Google Tag Manager) to see real visitor behavior; describes it as
  "addictive" — she reviews it daily.
- **Call-to-action color advice**: reserve one distinctive, high-contrast color exclusively
  for CTA buttons so it doesn't blend into brand colors; don't use a CTA color that matches
  your existing brand palette.
- Uses **UTM tracking** (via Google's URL Builder or the paid tool Agora Pulse) to attribute
  leads/sales back to individual social posts; tags leads by source in ConvertKit to trace
  a (sometimes multi-month) sales journey.
- Her own two headline metrics: LinkedIn direct messages received, and lead-magnet sign-ups
  from LinkedIn — the two channels (LinkedIn + email list) she deliberately narrowed her
  focus to after previously spreading herself across blogging, live-streaming, YouTube,
  podcasting, and all social platforms simultaneously.
- Personal turning-point story: burned out working 16–18 hour days despite "loving" the
  work, until she began applying her own analytics discipline to her own marketing —
  narrowed channels, redesigned her lead magnet (a "GA4 phrase book" replacing an
  under-converting DIY audit sheet), and now reports better sleep, a marriage, and
  weekends off.
- Offers: a six-week group program "Analytics Ace" (€795) and 1:1 Google Analytics training
  (€995 for three one-hour sessions + kickoff call), both self-reported pricing.
