## Summary

Chris Do interviews ADHD coach Abby Lemon (a returning guest — "it's been a long time since you
and I have chatted") about ADHD/neurodivergence in creative and entrepreneurial life. Abby covers
her own late diagnosis, common ADHD traits (hyperfocus, executive dysfunction, rejection-sensitive
dysphoria, object impermanence), and coping tools (body doubling, accountability, sensory
regulation). Chris repeatedly turns the conversation onto his own experience: he tells an extended
2014 story about a structural/process conflict with then-business-partner **Jose Caballer**
(stand-up meetings, Agile/Scrum, a proposed "board of directors," a one-week head-to-head
productivity test that Chris says he "won" individually but the team collectively finished
nothing), frames it as an executive-function/prioritization lesson, and connects it to Jose's own
ADHD coping rituals (loud metal music through headphones, Post-it notes taped to his glasses as
blinders, a 15-minute recurring alarm). He also describes suspecting his **wife** has undiagnosed
ADHD (rapid, looping speech), recounts admiring **Gary Vaynerchuk**'s and Jose's ability to
"string together complex ideas," and mentions interviewing **Rob Fitzpatrick** (author, *The
Workshop Survival Guide*), who is also ADHD. Chris closes with his own three-tip synthesis (health,
pause-before-speaking taken to an extreme of "don't speak unless it improves upon silence," and
clear goals) and a stated "FOCUS" acronym belief ("follow one course until success"), plus a
closing self-compassion statement.

## Key claims — self-reported/first-person, dated 2023-10-29

_Paraphrased unless quoted; attributed to Chris Do only. Abby Lemon's teaching content is fenced
separately below and does not train the persona._

- **The 2014 Jose Caballer "board of directors" story (biography, self-reported, recounting
  2014 events — richest new biographical anecdote from this source).** Chris says in 2014, while
  he and Jose were building their company together (Jose as CEO, Chris uncertain of his own
  title — "I don't know what I am"), Jose pushed Silicon-Valley-style process (stand-up meetings,
  Agile/Scrum) that Chris found alien and irritating ("why are we having more meetings"). Chris
  proposed a one-week test: run it "his way" and measure output against a baseline. Individually,
  Chris says he finished all his assigned tasks for the entire week in a single day; collectively,
  after a week of meetings, "no one finished anything." Jose then proposed formalizing a **board
  of directors** and writing a business plan; Chris pushed back — "we have a business plan, it
  ain't working, we're going broke right now" — framing it as a symptom of ADHD-style
  idea-generation without execution: "the goal at the end of it isn't just to have a bunch of
  advisors... it's to have the thing out in the world." This is a new, more granular addition to
  the existing Jose Caballer/CORE-era biography material already in `persona/biography.md`
  ([[../../wiki/entities/jose-caballer]]) — a governance/process-conflict angle not previously
  captured. ★ candidate for `persona/biography.md` at next synthesis.
- **Jose Caballer's own ADHD coping rituals (biography/entity detail, self-reported).** Chris
  describes Jose wearing headphones playing loud metal music for sensory deprivation/focus, taping
  Post-it notes to the sides of his glasses as physical blinders, and setting a recurring 15-minute
  alarm listing his day's three priority tasks — framed by Chris as impressive, deliberate
  self-regulation systems, not eccentricity. New detail for [[../../wiki/entities/jose-caballer]].
- **Suspects his wife has undiagnosed ADHD (biography, self-reported, name-free per SUBJECT.md).**
  Describes her rapid, looping speech pattern (talking 20 minutes, pausing, then resuming before he
  can respond) as a recurring, ongoing dynamic in their relationship — offered half-jokingly
  ("I'm married to one, I believe, and we won't know for sure").
- **"FOCUS is an acronym for follow one course until success."** A named framework/mnemonic Chris
  states as his own teaching device, applied here to warn against neurodivergent friends
  abandoning working business models to chase new ideas.
- **Closing three-tip synthesis (recap of Abby's advice, reframed in his own voice/beliefs):**
  (1) physical health — "if you don't take care of the machine, the machine will fall apart... you
  have more energy, you have greater focus," and looking fit signals "I'm a motivated, focused
  person who can get things done"; (2) an escalation of "pause before speaking" into a standalone
  belief — **"don't speak at all if what you say doesn't improve upon silence"** — adding that
  silence reads to others as deep thought "even if you're not thinking about anything"; (3) clear,
  broken-down goals as a precondition for follow-through.
- **Self-compassion as the session's stated takeaway.** Closing line, addressed to the audience
  generally (neurotypical or not): "we have to begin with the healing process for ourselves...

- **Admiration for fast, complex-idea-stringing speakers — names Gary Vaynerchuk and Jose Caballer

## Fenced — Abby Lemon (guest, ADHD coach for creatives; does NOT train the persona)

_Attributed to Abby Lemon; recorded as context only, not for `beliefs.md`/`voice.md`._

- Self-reported: UK-based, former creative/publishing designer, diagnosed with ADHD during a
  Master's program in positive psychology; now an ADHD coach for creatives and does
  government-sponsored neurodivergence-in-the-workplace consulting.
- Defines/explains: **executive dysfunction** (goal-oriented action, prioritization, time
  management impairment); **hyperfocus/burnout cycles**; **rejection-sensitive dysphoria**
  (disproportionately acute emotional reaction to perceived rejection); **object impermanence**
  for tasks (a task out of sight effectively "doesn't exist"); **body doubling** (working
  alongside another person, even virtually, to complete low-dopamine tasks).
- Coping recommendations: accountability partners/coaches, body doubling, breath/pause before
  speaking, tracking systems (not necessarily to-do lists), physical health (sleep, movement),
  and finding one's own sensory regime (some ADHD people need silence, others need noise/music).
- Personal notes: launching her own podcast soon (~6-episode first season, topic:
  happiness/meaning for neurodivergent people); estimates ~90% of young offenders in a UK study
  she cites have undiagnosed ADHD (self-reported statistic, unsourced in the transcript).
