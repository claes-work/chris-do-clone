## Key claims

_All dated 2023-12-30. Paraphrased unless quoted. Attributed per speaker._

### Guest (Yasin / "Hey J") — CONTEXT, not persona
- A hook is ~90% of a good post, but only a promise; the rest of the post must deliver
  on it. To write one, answer one question: "What is the one thing I want my reader to
  learn from this post?" and put that answer in a single line. (context)
- The "rehook" (the second line) keeps readers to the end, written two ways: add "heft"
  by crushing a likely objection, or state something seemingly opposite to the hook.
  Retention matters more than attention. (context)
- Images matter on LinkedIn; use your own face, tie the image conceptually to the text,
  and prefer a **4x5 aspect ratio** so your post fills the screen without neighboring
  posts showing. (context)
- Treat the LinkedIn profile as a landing page: the headline (sweet spot ~45 characters)
  must be clear with an obvious next action, and it must align with the banner. (context)
- The "Dear Son" writing frame: draft every post as if writing to one dear person (his
  son), then delete the "Dear Son" / "Love, Dad" bookends — this enforces a consistent,
  simple, warm voice. (context)
- Commenting is the biggest LinkedIn growth lever: posting daily = seen once/day (the
  "paradox"), while commenting 20-50x/day multiplies visibility. Comment for the whole
  audience, not just the author; his "1+3" rule = one strong comment plus three replies
  to others. (context)
- Warm outreach: rack up 10+ genuine comment interactions with a creator before sending
  a DM or connection request. (context)
- His model is 100% inbound (no cold outreach); he claims ~90% of people who book with
  him never exchanged a single message first. Tools he uses: Authored Up (analytics/
  scheduling/formatting), Secta Labs (AI headshots), and the "edit anything" Chrome
  extension for live copy demos. (context — third-party tools)
