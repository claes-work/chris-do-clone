- **Born 1972, Saigon, Vietnam.** Year and place are consistent across independent bios (https://en.everybodywiki.com/Chris_Do, https://grokipedia.com/page/Chris_Do, https://www.scalehouse.org/artist-index/chris-do, https://www.brighterfuture.studio/blog/origin-story-series-interview-w-chris-do-the-futur). [press-corroborated]
- **Exact date "January 13, 1972"** appears ONLY on celebrity aggregators (https://www.famousbirthdays.com/people/chris-do.html, https://allfamous.org/people/chris-do-19720113.html, https://platinumlist.net/artist/3286/chris-do); some aggregators even list a conflicting 1986 birth year. **LOW CONFIDENCE — no primary or press source found for the exact day.** Curiosity: Vietcetera's identity profile of him ran on January 13, 2024 (https://vietcetera.com/en/chris-do-on-mastering-the-power-of-embracing-identity), which may or may not be a birthday feature — do not treat as confirmation. ## 2. Origins: escape from Vietnam, Kansas City, San Jose (1975–~1990)

- Family fled Saigon at its fall, **April 30, 1975**, when Chris was three; his father had served in the South Vietnamese military. Reasons he gives: "communism, political oppression and ongoing war". (https://www.brighterfuture.studio/blog/origin-story-series-interview-w-chris-do-the-futur; https://en.everybodywiki.com/Chris_Do) [self-reported, widely repeated]
- Arrived in the US as refugees; **first landed in Kansas City, Missouri**, sponsored/hosted through a Catholic-church refugee program. (https://www.brighterfuture.studio/blog/origin-story-series-interview-w-chris-do-the-futur; https://en.everybodywiki.com/Chris_Do) [self-reported]
- **Moved to San Jose, California ~1977** ("two years later") to join extended family; grew up in San Jose until college. (same sources) [self-reported]
- **Father's arc**: started as a busboy, rose to "chief engineer at a semiconductor company in Silicon Valley" — family reached upper-middle class in one generation. (https://www.brighterfuture.studio/blog/origin-story-series-interview-w-chris-do-the-futur) [self-reported]
- **Family contrast** he cites as formative: father's side technical/stable Silicon Valley engineers; mother's side "super like emotional, beautiful, artistic people. Photographer, painter, artists." He is the **middle child**. (origin-story interviews surfaced via https://thefutur.com/watch/chris-do-origin-story-the-futur and https://www.brighterfuture.studio/blog/origin-story-series-interview-w-chris-do-the-futur) [self-reported]
- Childhood personality: introverted immigrant kid, felt a cultural outsider, found comfort in **drawing and comic books**. [self-reported] (same sources)
- **Path into design — skateboarding**: skate brands' graphics were his entry point — "The companies I found the greatest connection with had the best graphics." At ~17 he recognized graphic design as "the perfect outlet for my many passions, like drawing, letterforms, sub-culture, music, packaging, design and typography." (https://www.brighterfuture.studio/blog/origin-story-series-interview-w-chris-do-the-futur) [self-reported]
- **College rejections**: rejected from UCLA, UC San Diego and Cal Poly San Luis Obispo; around then worked freelance for a **silk-screening shop** (known locally as a kid who could illustrate), where meeting a working graphic designer showed him design was a profession. (https://thebusinessofgraphicdesign.com/an-interview-with-chris-do-part-1/ and origin-story interviews) [self-reported]
- **Gap**: his specific San Jose high school is not named in any source found. Do not invent.
- **Father's resistance**: his conservative Vietnamese father initially opposed the design career, fearing poverty; his mother intervened in support; the father took ~10 years to come around. (https://vietcetera.com/en/chris-do-on-mastering-the-power-of-embracing-identity, 2024-01-13) [self-reported]

## 5. Blind, Inc. (1995–~2022)

- **Founded December 1995** ("12/1995 – Present" on resume PDF [self-reported]; "started his own design business back in December of 1995 called Blind" — https://www.thelogocreative.co.uk/designer-interview-with-chris-do/; 20-year retrospective: https://motionographer.com/2015/10/22/blind-looking-back-at-20-years/ [press-corroborated]). Title: President / Founder / Executive Creative Director; later commonly CEO/Chief Strategist.
- **Legal entity: "Blind Visual Propaganda, Inc.", Santa Monica, CA (1702 Olympic Blvd.)** — appears in Dun & Bradstreet (https://www.dnb.com/business-directory/company-profiles.blind_visual_propaganda_inc.f8e442563aae5b6705a4447d01d680ff.html) and Bloomberg company profiles (https://www.bloomberg.com/profile/company/0710971D:US). **[registry-verified]** (commercial registries; California SOS record not directly pulled).
- **Name evolution** [self-reported]: began print-focused; he dissolved "Blind Visual Communication" and renamed it **"Blind Visual Propaganda"** out of infatuation with Russian constructivism; loves ironic names — designers doing visual communication called "Blind" provokes dialogue. (https://www.thelogocreative.co.uk/designer-interview-with-chris-do/)
- **Positioning**: bi-coastal, multi-disciplinary design firm emphasizing motion graphics; part of the "first and a half generation" of motion design pioneers. (resume PDF [self-reported]; https://motionographer.com/2015/10/22/blind-looking-back-at-20-years/ [press-corroborated])
- **Clients** (resume PDF + Behance + press, [self-reported] unless noted): Microsoft, Nike, Xbox, Sony/PlayStation, Showtime, Mitsubishi Motors, Kodak, Ford, Toyota, Warner Brothers TV, 20th Century Fox, Fox Sports Net, MTV, NYSE, Merrill Lynch, Amnesty International; music artists Justin Timberlake, Coldplay, Cinematic Orchestra, Gnarls Barkley, The Raveonettes. "$80M lifetime billings" remains [self-reported] only.
- **~2018 pivot**: Blind relaunched as a **"brand strategy design consultancy"** — Do: "I saw a gap in the market… a hybrid between… business management consultancy and design firm… the strategic divide that exists between concept and realization – where good ideas go to die." (https://blind.com/blog/blind-relaunches-as-brand-strategy-design-consultancy/) [self-reported]
- **Wind-down**: Brighter Future interview says he operated Blind "until 2018" (https://www.brighterfuture.studio/blog/origin-story-series-interview-w-chris-do-the-futur) [press-corroborated]. In Creator Science #111 he says in **December 2018** he challenged the team to walk away from client work — six-figure assignments included — and they stayed "client-free" thereafter (https://podcast.creatorscience.com/chris-do/) [self-reported]. By **~April 2022** he publicly offered the blind.com domain for sale (LinkedIn post #blind #domainforsale, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/thechrisdo_blind-domainforsale-activity-6924017326073806848-7G2M) [self-reported]. blind.com still presents the consultancy brand.
  > ⚠️ Nuance: "founded 1995, ran ~23 years, client work ended Dec 2018, entity/brand lingered into the 2020s" is the reconcilable reading. Exact dissolution date of the corporation: **not established**.

## 8. The Skool → The Futur (2013/2014–present)

- **The Skool: CEO/Co-Founder, 10/2013 – 06/2016. "Cofounded The Skool with Jose Caballer."** Launched 3 seasons of programming, 2 in-person conferences, co-authored content and kits. (resume PDF) [self-reported — this settles Phase-0 open item (b): Caballer co-founder status confirmed in the subject's own words]
  > ⚠️ CONTRADICTION (minor): resume says the Skool partnership began 10/2013; press and The Futur's own framing say "2014" with the first video published August 2014 (first episode w/ Caballer: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Gd83jOPPm8c; Motionographer https://motionographer.com/2017/09/28/the-future-of-the-futur/). Reconcilable: partnership formed late 2013, public content launched 2014.
- **Caballer's role**: he "initially spearheaded the concept, convincing Do to join forces while Do was considering launching 'Blind U'" (Motionographer, 2017-09-28: https://motionographer.com/2017/09/28/the-future-of-the-futur/ — article renders it "Bind U", almost certainly "Blind U"). [press-corroborated]
- **The split (2016)**: creative differences. Do, quoted by Motionographer: "Jose and I had differing ideas about content, direction, and leadership styles. I knew that if our friendship was going to survive, we both needed to do our own thing." Amicable, facilitated by mutual friends. Caballer went on to his own ventures (later TheSystm.co); the two publicly reunited on stage in London 2018-03-03 (https://medium.com/@moustachedesign/recap-of-the-future-is-here-london-b6c1e5fd917d). [press-corroborated]
- **The Futur founded July 2016** ("CEO/Founder/Host, 07/2016 – Present", resume PDF [self-reported]; LinkedIn company page says founded 2016, Santa Monica, 11–50 employees: https://www.linkedin.com/company/the-futur [self-reported/platform]). Content education platform; by Sept 2017 ~175K YouTube subscribers, expanding into podcasting/publishing/online education with ~3 FT staff + interns, staff overlap with Blind (Matthew Encina, Greg Gunn, Ben Burns) (Motionographer 2017) [press-corroborated].
- **Podcast** "The Futur with Chris Do": weekly since 2016-09-08; 445 episodes as of 2026-07-08 (https://www.listennotes.com/podcasts/the-futur-with-chris-do-the-futur-URywKcNj2TZ/). [press-corroborated/platform data]
- **Mission**: "teach 1 billion people to make a living doing what they love" (https://thefutur.com; Entrepreneur feature: https://www.entrepreneur.com/growing-a-business/chris-do-of-the-futur-on-his-1-billion-mission/448134). [self-reported, press-echoed]
